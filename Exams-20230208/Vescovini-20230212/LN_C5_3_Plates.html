Buckling of Thin Plates

Energy Approach and Solutions for Various Loading Conditions

1. Introduction and Problem Formulation

Consider a thin plate subjected to axial compression (the analysis can be extended to the case of multi-axial loading conditions). The goal of this section is to derive the equilibrium and buckling equations by means of the energy approach used for the Euler column.

1.1 Strain Tensor Components

The strain tensor components in the Donnell-von Kármán formulation are:

\[\epsilon_{ik} = \frac{1}{2}\left(u_{i/k} + u_{k/i} + w_{/i} w_{/k}\right) - w b_{ik} - z w_{/ik}\]

For flat plates \(b_{ik} = 0\), and the strain can be written as:

\[\epsilon_{ik} = \mathscr{E}_{ik} + z \kappa_{ik}\]

with:

\[\mathscr{E}_{ik} = \frac{1}{2}\left(u_{i/k} + u_{k/i} + w_{/i} w_{/k}\right) - w b_{ik}\] \[\kappa_{ik} = -w_{/ik}\]

2. Total Potential Energy

The total potential energy is given by:

\[\Pi = U + V\]

with the strain energy:

\[U = \frac{1}{2} \int_V \epsilon_{ik} \sigma_{ik} \, dV\]

and the potential of external forces:

\[V = \hat{N}_x b \left(u(a) - u(0)\right)\]

Derivation of Strain Energy

Recalling that \(\epsilon_{ik} = \mathscr{E}_{ik} + z \kappa_{ik}\), the strain energy reads:

\[U = \frac{1}{2} \int_V \left(\mathscr{E}_{ik} \sigma_{ik} + \kappa_{ik} z \sigma_{ik}\right) dV\] \[= \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left(\mathscr{E}_{ik} N_{ik} + \kappa_{ik} M_{ik}\right) dA\]

with the stress resultants defined as:

\[N_{ik} = \int_t \sigma_{ik} \, dz \quad ; \quad M_{ik} = \int_t z \sigma_{ik} \, dz\]

Thus the total potential energy is:

\[\Pi = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left(\mathscr{E}_{ik} N_{ik} + \kappa_{ik} M_{ik}\right) dA + \hat{N}_x b \left(u(a) - u(0)\right)\]

3. Perturbation Analysis

Introduce now a perturbation:

  • \(u \rightarrow u + \delta u\)
  • \(v \rightarrow v + \delta v\)
  • \(w \rightarrow w + \delta w\)

3.1 Variation of Strain Components

The perturbed membrane strain becomes:

\[\mathscr{E}_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik} = \frac{1}{2}\left(u_{i/k} + u_{k/i} + w_{/i} w_{/k}\right) - w b_{ik}\] \[+ \frac{1}{2}\left(\delta u_{i/k} + \delta u_{k/i} + \delta w_{/i} w_{/k} + \delta w_{/k} w_{/i}\right) - \delta w b_{ik}\] \[+ \frac{1}{2} \delta w_{/i} \delta w_{/k}\]

The strain variation can be decomposed into linear and nonlinear parts:

\[\delta \mathscr{E}_{ik} = \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^{NL}\]

with:

\[\delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L = \frac{1}{2}\left(\delta u_{i/k} + \delta u_{k/i} + \delta w_{/i} w_{/k} + \delta w_{/k} w_{/i}\right) - \delta w b_{ik}\] \[\delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^{NL} = \frac{1}{2} \delta w_{/i} \delta w_{/k}\]

3.2 Variation of Stress Resultants

The variation of the membrane stress resultant (assuming no coupling between in-plane and out-of-plane behaviour):

\[N_{ik} + \delta N_{ik} = A_{ikrs}\left(\mathscr{E}_{rs} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{rs}^L + \delta \mathscr{E}_{rs}^{NL}\right)\]
\[\delta N_{ik} = \delta N_{ik}^L + \delta N_{ik}^{NL}\]

with:

\[\delta N_{ik}^L = A_{ikrs} \delta \mathscr{E}_{rs}^L \quad ; \quad \delta N_{ik}^{NL} = A_{ikrs} \delta \mathscr{E}_{rs}^{NL}\]

For the curvature and bending moment:

\[\delta \kappa_{ik} = -\delta w_{/ik}\] \[\delta M_{ik} = D_{ikrs} \delta \kappa_{rs} = -D_{ikrs} \delta w_{/rs}\]

3.3 Variation of Total Potential Energy

The perturbed total potential energy:

\[\Pi + \Delta\Pi = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left[\left(\mathscr{E}_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}\right)\left(N_{ik} + \delta N_{ik}\right) + \left(\kappa_{ik} + \delta \kappa_{ik}\right)\left(M_{ik} + \delta M_{ik}\right)\right] dA\] \[+ \hat{N}_x b \left[u(a) + \delta u(a) - u(0) - \delta u(0)\right]\]

After expansion and collecting terms by order:

\[\Delta\Pi = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left(\delta N_{ik}^L \mathscr{E}_{ik} + \delta N_{ik}^{NL} \mathscr{E}_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L N_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^{NL} N_{ik} + \ldots\right) dA\]

The third and fourth order contributions are neglected.

The result can be written in the form:

\[\Delta\Pi = \delta\Pi + \frac{1}{2!} \delta^2\Pi\]

where:

\[\Delta\Pi = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left(\delta N_{ik}^L \mathscr{E}_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L N_{ik} + \delta M_{ik} \kappa_{ik} + \delta \kappa_{ik} M_{ik}\right) dA + \hat{N}_x b \left[\delta u(a) - \delta u(0)\right]\] \[+ \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left(\delta N_{ik}^{NL} \mathscr{E}_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^{NL} N_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L \delta N_{ik}^L + \delta \kappa_{ik} \delta M_{ik}\right) dA\]

4. Equilibrium Equations

The equilibrium condition requires:

\[\delta\Pi = 0\]

Symmetry Properties

Note that:

\[\delta N_{ik}^L \mathscr{E}_{ik} = A_{ikrs} \delta \mathscr{E}_{rs}^L \mathscr{E}_{ik} = A_{ikrs} \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L \mathscr{E}_{rs} = \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L N_{ik}\]

(by symmetry of \(A_{ikrs}\))

Similarly:

\[\delta M_{ik} \kappa_{ik} = D_{ikrs} \delta \kappa_{rs} \kappa_{ik} = D_{ikrs} \delta \kappa_{ik} \kappa_{rs} = \delta \kappa_{ik} M_{ik}\]

(by symmetry of \(D_{ikrs}\))

Therefore:

\[\delta\Pi = \int_A \left(\delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L N_{ik} + \delta \kappa_{ik} M_{ik}\right) dA + \hat{N}_x b \left[\delta u(a) - \delta u(0)\right]\]

Substituting the expressions for the variations:

\[\delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L = \frac{1}{2}\left(\delta u_{i/k} + \delta u_{k/i} + \delta w_{/i} w_{/k} + \delta w_{/k} w_{/i}\right) - \delta w b_{ik}\] \[\delta \kappa_{ik} = -\delta w_{/ik}\]

Integration by Parts

Observing that \(N_{ik} = N_{ki}\) and integrating by parts:

\[\delta\Pi = \int_A \left[\left(\delta u_{i/k} + \delta w_{/k} w_{/i} - \delta w b_{ik}\right) N_{ik} - \delta w_{/ik} M_{ik}\right] dA + \hat{N}_x b \left[\delta u(a) - \delta u(0)\right]\] \[= -\int_A \left[\delta u_i N_{ik/k} + \delta w \left(N_{ik} w_{/i}\right)_{/k} + \delta w b_{ik} N_{ik} + \delta w M_{ik/ik}\right] dA + \text{boundary terms}\]

The equilibrium equations are then:

\[\begin{cases} N_{ik/k} = 0 \\ M_{ik/ik} + N_{ik} w_{/ik} + b_{ik} N_{ik} = 0 \end{cases}\]

Or, in their extended form:

\[\begin{cases} N_{xx/x} + N_{xy/y} = 0 \\ N_{xy/x} + N_{yy/y} = 0 \\ M_{xx/xx} + 2M_{xy/xy} + M_{yy/yy} + N_{xx} w_{/xx} + 2N_{xy} w_{/xy} + N_{yy} w_{/yy} + b_{xx} N_{xx} + b_{yy} N_{yy} = 0 \end{cases}\]

Remarks

  1. The equilibrium equations are, in general, non-linear equations. Often the pre-buckling analysis is performed by considering their linearized counterpart but, in principle, the equilibrium condition can be found by solving the non-linear problem.
  2. In the non-linear case, the in-plane and out-of-plane response are coupled. In the linearized case, the coupling between in-plane and out-of-plane behaviour is due to the curvature. For flat panels (\(b_{ik} = 0\)) the two responses are uncoupled.

5. Buckling Equations

Consider the Trefftz criterion:

\[\delta\left(\delta\Pi\big|_{@equil.}\right) = 0\]

5.1 Second Variation of Potential Energy

\[\frac{1}{2} \delta^2\Pi = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left(\delta N_{ik}^{NL} \mathscr{E}_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^{NL} N_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L \delta N_{ik}^L + \delta \kappa_{ik} \delta M_{ik}\right) dA\]

Note that by symmetry of \(A_{ikrs}\):

\[\delta N_{ik}^{NL} \mathscr{E}_{ik} = \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^{NL} N_{ik}\]

Therefore:

\[\frac{1}{2} \delta^2\Pi = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left(2 \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^{NL} N_{ik} + \delta \mathscr{E}_{ik}^L \delta N_{ik}^L + \delta \kappa_{ik} \delta M_{ik}\right) dA\]

At equilibrium:

\[\frac{1}{2} \delta^2\Pi \bigg|_{@equil.} = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left[\delta w_{/i} \delta w_{/k} \bar{N}_{ik} + \left(\delta u_{i/k} + \bar{w}_{/i} \delta w_{/k} - \delta w b_{ik}\right) \delta N_{ik}^L - \delta w_{/ik} \delta M_{ik}\right] dA\]

5.2 Derivation of Buckling Equations

To take the first variation of \(\delta^2\Pi\), operate the following replacement:

  • \(\delta w \rightarrow w\)
  • \(\delta u \rightarrow u\)
  • \(\delta N \rightarrow N\)
  • \(\delta M \rightarrow M\)

Note: \(w, u, N, M\) should still be intended as variations!

This gives:

\[\frac{1}{2} \delta^2\Pi \bigg|_{@equil.} = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left[w_{/i} w_{/k} \bar{N}_{ik} + \left(u_{i/k} + \bar{w}_{/i} w_{/k} - w b_{ik}\right) N_{ik}^L - w_{/ik} M_{ik}\right] dA\]

Setting the Variation to Zero

Setting \(\delta\left(\delta^2\Pi\big|_{@equil.}\right) = 0\) and integrating by parts:

\[\delta\left(\delta^2\Pi\big|_{@equil.}\right) = \int_A \left[\delta w_{/k} w_{/i} \bar{N}_{ik} + \left(\delta u_{i/k} + \delta w_{/k} \bar{w}_{/i} - \delta w b_{ik}\right) N_{ik}^L - \delta w_{/ik} M_{ik}\right] dA = 0\] \[= -\int_A \left[\delta w \left(w_{/i} \bar{N}_{ik}\right)_{/k} + \delta u_i N_{ik/k}^L + \delta w \left(N_{ik}^L \bar{w}_{/i}\right)_{/k} + \delta w \, b_{ik} N_{ik}^L + \delta w \, M_{ik/ik}\right] dA\] \[+ \text{boundary terms}\]

The buckling equations are:

\[\begin{cases} N_{ik/k}^L = 0 \\ M_{ik/ik} + \bar{N}_{ik} w_{/ik} + N_{ik}^L \bar{w}_{/ik} + b_{ik} N_{ik}^L = 0 \end{cases}\]

5.3 Special Case: Flat Panel with No Pre-buckling Out-of-Plane Displacement

Consider the case of:

  1. Flat panel → \(b_{ik} = 0\)
  2. No pre-buckling out-of-plane displacements (\(\bar{w} = 0\))

The equations become:

\[\begin{cases} N_{ik/k}^L = 0 & \text{(2 in-plane buckling equations)} \\ M_{ik/ik} + \bar{N}_{ik} w_{/ik} = 0 & \text{(1 out-of-plane buckling equation)} \end{cases}\]

Remarks

  1. For isotropic, homogeneous plates or composite plates with symmetric lay-ups, the pre-buckling solution is characterized by \(\bar{w} = 0\). In the presence of coupled in-plane/out-of-plane response (e.g., non-symmetric laminates), \(\bar{w} \neq 0\). In this case, assuming \(\bar{w} = 0\) is an assumption which is worth checking.
  2. If \(\bar{w} = 0\) (or can be reasonably assumed \(= 0\)), then the last buckling equation can be solved independently from the previous ones.
  3. Although boundary conditions were not considered, it is clear from the expression of \(\Delta\Pi\) that the boundary conditions are:
    • a. homogeneous for the buckling case
    • b. non-homogeneous for the equilibrium case
  4. In the present approach, the formulation is aimed at deriving the buckling equations (so the problem is formulated in a strong form manner). However, it is worth observing that even the buckling problem can be formulated by means of a direct method (e.g., the method of Ritz), so in a weak form manner. In this case, the Ritz method could be applied to the functional: \[\frac{1}{2} \delta^2\Pi\bigg|_{@equil.} = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left[w_{/i} w_{/k} \bar{N}_{ik} + \left(u_{i/k} + \bar{w}_{/i} w_{/k} - w b_{ik}\right) \cdot N_{ik}^L - w_{/ik} M_{ik}\right] dA\] For the case of flat plates with no coupling between in-plane and out-of-plane response, the functional simplifies to: \[\frac{1}{2} \delta^2\Pi\bigg|_{@equil.} = \frac{1}{2} \int_A \left[w_{/i} w_{/k} \bar{N}_{ik} - w_{/ik} M_{ik}\right] dA\]

6. Solution for Axially Compressed Simply-Supported Plate

The equilibrium and buckling equations are now solved for the special case of simply-supported panel, subjected to axial load.

6.1 Equilibrium Solution

It is straightforward to verify that the solution of the pre-buckling equations can be found as:

\[N_{xx} = -\hat{N}_{xx}\] \[N_{xy} = N_{yy} = 0\] \[w = 0\]

→ equilibrium equations, as well as the boundary conditions, are satisfied

6.2 Buckling Analysis

The out-of-plane buckling equation is:

\[M_{ik/ik} + \bar{N}_{ik} w_{/ik} = 0\]

Expanding the components:

\[M_{xx/xx} + 2M_{xy/xy} + M_{yy/yy} + \bar{N}_{xx} w_{/ik} = 0\]

But \(\bar{N}_{xx} = -\hat{N}_{xx}\), so:

\[M_{xx/xx} + 2M_{xy/xy} + M_{yy/yy} - \hat{N}_{xx} w_{/xx} = 0\]

Constitutive Equation

Introduce now the constitutive equation (for an isotropic plate):

\[\begin{Bmatrix} M_{xx} \\ M_{yy} \\ M_{xy} \end{Bmatrix} = D \begin{bmatrix} 1 & \nu & 0 \\ \nu & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{1-\nu}{2} \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} -w_{/xx} \\ -w_{/yy} \\ -2w_{/xy} \end{Bmatrix}\]

So the buckling equation becomes:

\[D\left(w_{/xxxx} + 2w_{/xxyy} + w_{/yyyy}\right) + \hat{N}_{xx} w_{/xx} = 0\]

or equivalently:

\[D \Delta \Delta w + \hat{N}_{xx} w_{/xx} = 0\]

Boundary Conditions

The boundary conditions for the simply-supported plate are:

\[\begin{array}{ll} w = 0 & \text{on } \partial\Omega \\ w_{/xx} = 0 & \text{on } x = 0, a \\ w_{/yy} = 0 & \text{on } y = 0, b \end{array}\]

6.3 Solution by Fourier Series

The solution can be found by expressing \(w\) as:

\[w = \sum_{m=1}^{+\infty} \sum_{n=1}^{+\infty} q_{mn} \sin\frac{m\pi x}{a} \sin\frac{n\pi y}{b}\]

This solution (trial, so far) satisfies both the essential (\(w = 0\) on \(\partial\Omega\)) and the natural conditions (\(w_{/xx} = 0\) and \(w_{/yy} = 0\) on the transverse and longitudinal edges).

Substitution into Buckling Equation

The buckling equation becomes:

\[\sum_{n=1}^{+\infty} \sum_{m=1}^{+\infty} q_{mn} \left\{D\left[\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^4 + 2\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^2\left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)^4\right] - \hat{N}_{xx}\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^2\right\} \sin\frac{m\pi x}{a} \sin\frac{n\pi y}{b} = 0\]

The solution \(q_{mn} = 0\) \(\forall m, n\) is the trivial solution.

The buckled configuration is found by imposing:

\[\hat{N}_{xx}\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^2 = D\left[\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)^2\right]^2\]

Derivation of Buckling Load Expression

\[\hat{N}_{xx} = D\left(\frac{a}{m\pi}\right)^2\left[\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)^2\right]^2\] \[= D\left[\frac{m\pi}{a} + \frac{n^2\pi}{b^2}\frac{a}{m}\right]^2\]

Multiplying by \(\frac{b^2}{b^2}\) and defining \(r = a/b\):

\[= \frac{D\pi^2}{b^2}\left[m\frac{b}{a} + n^2\frac{a}{b}\frac{1}{m}\right]^2\]

Therefore:

\[\hat{N}_{xx} = \frac{D\pi^2}{b^2}\left(\frac{m}{r} + n^2\frac{r}{m}\right)^2\]

A minimum is reached for \(n = 1\), so:

\[\hat{N}_{xx} = \frac{D\pi^2}{b^2}\left(\frac{m}{r} + \frac{r}{m}\right)^2\]

The number of \(m\) that makes \(\hat{N}_{xx}\) minimum has to be found. To this aim, a graphical representation of the nondimensional parameter \(\left(\frac{m}{r} + \frac{r}{m}\right)^2\) is presented:

Remarks on the Buckling Solution

  1. The minimum value assumed by the nondimensional parameter \(\frac{\hat{N}_{xx} b^2}{D\pi^2}\left(\frac{m}{r} + \frac{r}{m}\right)^2\) is 4. This happens for integer values of \(r\). Whenever \(r\) is non-integer, the value of \(\left(\frac{m}{r} + \frac{r}{m}\right)^2\) is higher than 4. Asymptotically, i.e., for \(r \rightarrow +\infty\), the value is equal to 4.
  2. The buckling mode is always characterized by \(n = 1\). On the contrary, the value of \(m\) depends on \(r\). For integer values of \(r\), \(m = r\). This means that the buckling pattern is characterized by square buckles.

If \(r\) is non-integer, the buckles will be not exactly square, but mostly square.

6.5 Effect of Boundary Conditions

Different boundary conditions or loading conditions can be handled by means of approximate techniques (Ritz, Galerkin, Levy, ...). In general, the results can still be represented as function of a nondimensional parameter in the form \(\frac{\hat{N}_{xx} b^2}{D\pi^2}\) (for the compressive case).

7. Multi-axial Loading Conditions — Biaxial Compression

In many cases the loading condition is characterized by the presence of transverse and longitudinal compression/traction, shear, etc.

For simplicity, consider now the case of biaxial compression:

Consider again simply-supported boundary conditions.

7.1 Equilibrium

\[N_{xx} = -\hat{N}_{xx}\] \[N_{yy} = -\hat{N}_{yy}\] \[N_{xy} = 0\] \[w = 0\]

7.2 Buckling

\[D\left(w_{/xxxx} + 2w_{/xxyy} + w_{/yyyy}\right) + \hat{N}_{xx} w_{/xx} + \hat{N}_{yy} w_{/yy} = 0\]

+ Boundary conditions (same as previous)

7.3 Solution

The solution is still found by taking:

\[w = \sum_{m=1}^{+\infty} \sum_{n=1}^{+\infty} q_{mn} \sin\frac{m\pi x}{a} \sin\frac{n\pi y}{b}\]

After substituting \(w\) into the buckling equation and seeking for a non-trivial solution, the following equation is found:

\[\hat{N}_{xx}\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^2 + \hat{N}_{yy}\left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)^2 = D\left[\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)^2\right]^2\]

The pre-buckling loading condition can be parametrized by defining:

\[\gamma = \frac{\hat{N}_{yy}}{\hat{N}_{xx}}\]

so that:

\[\hat{N}_{xx} = \frac{D\left[\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)^2\right]^2}{\left(\frac{m\pi}{a}\right)^2 + \gamma\left(\frac{n\pi}{b}\right)^2}\]

and, after rearranging the expression:

\[\hat{N}_{xx} = \frac{D\pi^2}{b^2} \frac{\left[\left(\frac{m}{r}\right)^2 + n^2\right]^2}{\left(\frac{m}{r}\right)^2 + n^2 \gamma}\]

Notice that the expression is now function of \(\gamma\) (⇒ \(\hat{N}_{xx}\) depends on the loading condition, i.e., the ratio between the transverse and the longitudinal load).
