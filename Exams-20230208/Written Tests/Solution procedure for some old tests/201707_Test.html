Question 1

Discuss and illustrate mathematically the relation between the Principle of Complementary Virtual Works (PCVW) and the compatibility conditions.

Solution

Principle of Complementary Virtual Works (PCVW):

The PCVW states that for a system in equilibrium, the complementary virtual work done by equilibrated virtual stresses on actual strains equals zero:

\[ \delta W^* = \int_V \delta\sigma_{ij} \cdot \varepsilon_{ij} \, dV - \int_{\partial V_u} \delta T_i \cdot u_i \, dS = 0 \]

Relation to Compatibility:

The PCVW is the weak form of the compatibility equations. While the strong form requires:

\[ \varepsilon_{ij} = \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{\partial u_i}{\partial x_j} + \frac{\partial u_j}{\partial x_i}\right) \]

The PCVW enforces compatibility in an integral (average) sense. When PCVW is satisfied for all admissible virtual stress fields, it implies that:

  1. The strain field is compatible with a displacement field
  2. Geometric boundary conditions are satisfied

Mathematical Demonstration (as shown in Exercise 1):

The PCVW was applied with dummy load systems to determine the unknown redundants. The compatibility is enforced through equations such as:

\[ \left(\frac{65l^3}{24EJ} + \frac{l^3}{2GJ}\right)Y + \frac{2l^2}{EJ}W = \frac{8}{3}\frac{Pl^3}{EJ} \]

This equation represents the compatibility condition that ensures the structure remains continuous (no gaps or overlaps) at the points where the redundant forces act.

Key Points:

  • PCVW uses statically admissible virtual stress fields
  • The principle naturally enforces kinematic compatibility
  • It provides a systematic method for solving statically indeterminate structures
  • The compatibility equations emerge as the conditions for zero complementary virtual work
