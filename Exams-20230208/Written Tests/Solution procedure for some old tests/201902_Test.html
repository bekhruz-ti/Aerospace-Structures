Exercise 1

The two-cell beam in the figure is composed of five panels of thickness \(t\), and four stringers of areas \(A\) and \(2A\) (note: the area is the lumped one). The section is square and has dimension \(h\). The material is a light aluminum alloy, whose elastic properties are provided in terms of Young's modulus and Poisson's coefficient, denoted with \(E\) and \(v\), respectively. The beam is fixed at one end and free at the other one, and is loaded with a concentrated forces of magnitude \(P\). Determine the internal stresses at a distance \(l/2\) from the free end.

\(h\)

\(2A\)          \(p\)

Data
\(h = 100\, \text{mm}\); \(l = 1500\, \text{mm}\); \(t = 1.5\, \text{mm}\); \(A = 150\, \text{mm}^2\); \(E = 72000\, \text{MPa}\); \(v = 0.3\); \(P = 15\, \text{kN}\);

Solution

Section Properties

The principal axes are available from the symmetry of the section. The cross-section is a square rotated 45Â° with stringers at corners numbered 1-4.

Diagonal dimension:

\[a = h\sqrt{2} = 100\sqrt{2} = 141.42\, \text{mm}\]

Second moments of area (using lumped stringer areas):

\[J_{xx} = 2A\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 = 2 \times 150 \times \left(\frac{141.42}{2}\right)^2 = 1.5 \times 10^6\, \text{mm}^4\] \[J_{yy} = 4A\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 = 4 \times 150 \times \left(\frac{141.42}{2}\right)^2 = 3.0 \times 10^6\, \text{mm}^4\]

Static moments for each stringer:

\[S_{x_1} = 0, \quad S_{y_1} = -Aa\] \[S_{x_2} = A\frac{a}{2}, \quad S_{y_2} = -Aa\] \[S_{x_3} = A\frac{a}{2}, \quad S_{y_3} = 0\] \[S_{x_4} = 0, \quad S_{y_4} = 0\]

Shear Flows

Open section shear flows (cutting one web):

\[q_1' = \frac{P_x S_{y_1}}{J_{yy}} + \frac{P_y S_{x_1}}{J_{xx}} = -\frac{P_x}{a} = -\frac{10607}{141.42} = -75\, \text{N/mm}\] \[q_2' = 0\, \text{N/mm}\] \[q_3' = \frac{P_y}{a} = \frac{10607}{141.42} = 75\, \text{N/mm}\] \[q_4' = q_5' = 0\]

Equivalence condition (with respect to cell 1):

\[2\Omega_2 q_2' + 2\Omega_3 q_3' + 2\Omega_{c_1} q_1^* + 2\Omega_{c_2} q_2^* = 0\]

Where \(\Omega_2 = \Omega_3 = \Omega_{c_1} = \Omega_{c_2} = \frac{a^2}{2}\), so:

\[q_2' + q_3' + q_1^* + q_2^* = 0\] \[\boxed{q_1^* + q_2^* = -75}\]

Compatibility condition:

\[q_1^* - q_2^* = \frac{(q_3' - q_1')h}{2(h + a)}\] \[\boxed{q_1^* - q_2^* = 31.07}\]

Solving the system:

\[\begin{cases} q_1^* + q_2^* = -75 \\ q_1^* - q_2^* = 31.07 \end{cases} \Rightarrow q_1^* = -21.97\, \text{N/mm}, \quad q_2^* = -53.04\, \text{N/mm}\]

Total shear flows and shear stresses:

\[q_1 = q_1' + q_1^* = -96.97\, \text{N/mm} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \tau_1 = \frac{|q_1|}{t} = 64.65\, \text{MPa}\] \[q_2 = q_2^* = -53.04\, \text{N/mm} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \tau_2 = 35.36\, \text{MPa}\] \[q_3 = q_3' + q_2^* = 21.96\, \text{N/mm} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \tau_3 = 14.64\, \text{MPa}\] \[q_4 = q_1^* = -21.97\, \text{N/mm} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \tau_4 = 14.65\, \text{MPa}\] \[q_5 = q_2^* - q_1^* = -31.08\, \text{N/mm} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \tau_5 = 20.72\, \text{MPa}\]

Normal Stresses \(\sigma_{zz}\)

Referring to the principal axes, the bending moments at \(z = l/2\) are:

\[M_x = P_y \cdot \frac{l}{2}, \quad M_y = P_x \cdot \frac{l}{2}\]

The normal stress formula:

\[\sigma_{zz} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}}y - \frac{M_y}{J_{yy}}x\]

Stringers 2, 4: \(x = 0\), \(y = \pm\frac{a}{2}\)

\[\sigma_{zz} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}}\left(\pm\frac{a}{2}\right) = \pm 375\, \text{MPa}\]

Stringers 1, 3: \(x = \pm\frac{a}{2}\), \(y = 0\)

\[\sigma_{zz} = -\frac{M_y}{J_{yy}}\left(\pm\frac{a}{2}\right) = \mp 187.5\, \text{MPa}\]

Final Answers

Shear stresses:

  • \(\tau_1 = 64.65\, \text{MPa}\)
  • \(\tau_2 = 35.36\, \text{MPa}\)
  • \(\tau_3 = 14.64\, \text{MPa}\)
  • \(\tau_4 = 14.65\, \text{MPa}\)
  • \(\tau_5 = 20.72\, \text{MPa}\)

Normal stresses in stringers:

  • Stringers 2, 4: \(\sigma_{zz} = \pm 375\, \text{MPa}\)
  • Stringers 1, 3: \(\sigma_{zz} = \mp 187.5\, \text{MPa}\)

Exercise 2

\(A\)

The slender beam in the figure is fixed at one end, and is elastically supported by two springs of stiffness \(k_x\) and \(k_y\) at the other end. The beam axial stiffness is \(EA\), while the bending stiffnesses are \(EJ_{xx}\) and \(EJ_{yy}\) (note: \(EJ_{xy} = 0\)). A concentrated force \(P\), whose components are \(P_x\), \(P_y\) and \(P_z\), is applied at the middle. Determine the unknown reactions forces and the strain energy stored in the structure (including the springs).

Data
\(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\); \(EA = 7 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\); \(EJ_{xx} = 5 \times 10^9\, \text{Nmm}^2\); \(EJ_{yy} = 8 \times 10^9\, \text{Nmm}^2\); \(k_x = 5\, \text{N/mm}\); \(k_y = 2\, \text{N/mm}\); \(P_x = 150\, \text{N}\); \(P_y = 100\, \text{N}\); \(P_z = 10000\, \text{N}\);

Solution

The structure is 2 times statically indeterminate. Let \(X\) be the spring reaction in the x-direction and \(Y\) be the spring reaction in the y-direction.

XZ-Plane Analysis

Using the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work (PCVW):

Bending moments in the real structure:

  • Region 1 (\(0 \leq z_1 \leq l\)): \(M_y^1 = X z_1\)
  • Region 2 (\(0 \leq z_2 \leq l\)): \(M_y^2 = Xl + (X + P_x)z_2\)

Virtual bending moments (unit load in x-direction):

  • \(\delta M_y^1 = z_1\)
  • \(\delta M_y^2 = z_2 + l\)

PCVW equation:

\[\int_0^l \frac{M_y^1 \cdot \delta M_y^1}{EJ_{yy}} dz_1 + \int_0^l \frac{M_y^2 \cdot \delta M_y^2}{EJ_{yy}} dz_2 + \frac{X}{k_x} = 0\]

After integration:

\[X\left(\frac{8}{3}l^3 + \frac{EJ_{yy}}{k_x}\right) + \frac{5}{6}P_x l^3 = 0\]

YZ-Plane Analysis

By analogy with the XZ-plane:

\[Y\left(\frac{8}{3}l^3 + \frac{EJ_{xx}}{k_y}\right) + \frac{5}{6}P_y l^3 = 0\]

Reaction Forces

Solving for the spring reactions:

\[\boxed{X = -29.27\, \text{N}}\] \[\boxed{Y = -16.13\, \text{N}}\]

Strain Energy

Total strain energy:

\[U = U_{bxz} + U_{byz} + U_{ax} + U_{springs}\]

Bending energy in XZ-plane:

\[U_{bxz} = \frac{1}{EJ_{yy}}\left(\frac{2}{3}X^2 l^3 + \frac{1}{6}(X+P_x)^2 l^3 + \frac{1}{2}X(X+P_x)l^3\right) = 0.154\, \text{J}\]

Bending energy in YZ-plane:

\[U_{byz} = \frac{1}{EJ_{xx}}\left(\frac{2}{3}Y^2 l^3 + \frac{1}{6}(Y+P_y)^2 l^3 + \frac{1}{2}Y(Y+P_y)l^3\right) = 0.133\, \text{J}\]

Axial strain energy:

\[U_{ax} = \frac{1}{2}\int_0^l \frac{P_z \cdot P_z}{EA} dz = \frac{1}{2}\frac{P_z^2}{EA}l = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{(10000)^2}{7 \times 10^7} \times 1000 = 0.714\, \text{J}\]

Spring strain energy:

\[U_{springs} = \frac{1}{2}\frac{X^2}{k_x} + \frac{1}{2}\frac{Y^2}{k_y} = \frac{1}{2}\frac{(-29.27)^2}{5} + \frac{1}{2}\frac{(-16.13)^2}{2} = 0.150\, \text{J}\]

Final Answers

Spring reaction forces:

  • \(X = -29.27\, \text{N}\) (horizontal spring)
  • \(Y = -16.13\, \text{N}\) (vertical spring)

Total strain energy:

\[U = 0.154 + 0.133 + 0.714 + 0.150 = \boxed{1.151\, \text{J}}\]
