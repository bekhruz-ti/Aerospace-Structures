Exercise 1

The structure in the figure is composed of three beams of length \(l\), which are charaterized by the same elastic properties. In particular, the bending and torsional stiffnesses are denoted with \(EJ\) and \(GJ\), respectively. The structure is elastically supported by a torsional spring of stiffness \(k_t\) at one end. A linearly varying force per unit length is applied along one of the beams, as illustrateci in the sketch. The force per unit length is equa! to zero at one end, and is equa! to \(q\) at the other end. Determine the reaction forces and plot the internal actions.

\(l\)

Data \(EJ = 72e6\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(GJ = EJ/2\)
\(k_t = 72\, \text{Nmm}\)
\(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(q = 100\, \text{N/mm}\)

Solution

Reference System and Beam Numbering:

The structure is divided into three beams with local coordinates \((z_1, z_2, z_3)\). Let \(X\) be the torsional moment (unknown hyperstatic reaction) and \(Y\) be the vertical reaction force.

Load Distribution:

\[q(z_1) = \frac{\bar{q}}{l} z_1\]

Internal Actions (Real System):

Beam 1:

\[M_x^{(1)} = -\frac{\bar{q}}{6l} z_1^3\] \[M_z^{(1)} = X\]

Beam 2:

\[M_x^{(2)} = -Y z_2\]

Beam 3:

\[M_x^{(3)} = X - \left(\frac{\bar{q}l}{2} + Y\right) z_3\] \[M_z^{(3)} = \frac{\bar{q}l^2}{6} - Yl\]

Virtual Work Analysis:

Dummy System #1 (unit rotation at spring):

\[{}^1\delta M_z^{(1)} = 1, \quad {}^1\delta M_x^{(3)} = 1\]

Dummy System #2 (unit vertical displacement):

\[{}^2\delta M_x^{(2)} = -z_2, \quad {}^2\delta M_x^{(3)} = -z_3, \quad {}^2\delta M_z^{(3)} = -l\]

PCVW Equations:

Equation 1:

\[\left(\frac{l}{GJ} + \frac{l}{EJ} + \frac{1}{k_t}\right)X - \frac{l^2}{2EJ}Y = \frac{\bar{q}l^3}{4EJ}\]

Equation 2:

\[\frac{l^2}{2EJ}X - \left(\frac{2l^3}{3EJ} + \frac{l^3}{GJ}\right)Y = \frac{l^3}{3EJ} \cdot \frac{\bar{q}l}{2} - \frac{l^4\bar{q}}{6GJ}\]

Final Results:

\[\boxed{X = 28043.5\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}}\] \[\boxed{Y = 6255.3\, \text{N}}\]

Exercise 2

The beam in the figure is made of an isotropie materiai with modulus \(E\) and Poisson's coefficient 11. It is characterized by a square cross section of dimensiona, where \(a(x) = \gamma\sqrt{a_0 + (a_1 - a_0)y}\), and \(x \in [0, Z]\). The first end of the beam is fixed; the second one is free to transiate along the transverse direction, while the rotation is prevented. A concentrateci load of magnitude \(P\) is applied at \(x = l\).

Assume a Euler-Bernoulli beam model, and determine: the transverse displacement at \(x = l\); the maxi­mum stress at \(x = l/4\). To this aim, apply the method of Ritz and, using polynomial functions, and solve the problem by considering one single shape function.

Discuss how the problem can be formulateci if two shape functions are adopted (report the relevant equa­tions; no calculations are required).

Qla

\(a\)
\(l\)

Data \(E = 72000\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(\gamma\)
\(10\, \text{mm}\)
\(a_0 = 1\)
\(a_1 = 16\)

Solution

Variable Cross-Section:

\[a(x) = \gamma \sqrt[4]{a_0 + (a_1 - a_0)\frac{x}{l}}\]

Bending Stiffness:

\[EJ = \frac{1}{12}a^4 E = \frac{1}{12}E\left(\gamma^4 a_0 + (a_1 - a_0)\frac{x}{l}\right)\]

This can be written as:

\[EJ = EJ_0 + EJ_1\left(\frac{x}{l}\right)\]

where:

\[EJ_0 = \frac{1}{12}E\gamma^4 a_0\] \[EJ_1 = \frac{1}{12}E\gamma^4 (a_1 - a_0)\]

Trial Functions (Polynomial):

\[w = C_0 + C_1 x + C_2 x^2 + C_3 x^3\]

Essential Boundary Conditions:

\[w(0) = 0\] \[w_x(0) = 0\] \[w_x(l) = 0\]

Applying these conditions yields:

\[w = C_3\left(x^3 - \frac{3}{2}lx^2\right)\]

Non-dimensional Trial Function:

\[w = C\left(\left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^3 - \frac{3}{2}\left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^2\right) = C \cdot \phi\]

where \(\phi = \left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^3 - \frac{3}{2}\left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^2\)

Stiffness Matrix:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^l \delta w_{xx} \, EJ \, w_{xx} \, dx = \delta C \cdot k \cdot C\]

where:

\[k = \int_0^l \left[EJ_0 + EJ_1\left(\frac{x}{l}\right)\right]\phi_{xx}^2 \, dx = \frac{1}{l^3}\left(3EJ_0 + \frac{3}{2}EJ_1\right)\]

External Loads (Virtual Work):

\[\delta W_e = \delta w(l) \cdot P = \delta C \cdot f\]

where \(f = -\frac{1}{2}P\)

Solution for C:

\[C = \frac{f}{k} = -\frac{l^3}{2} \cdot \frac{P}{3EJ_0 + \frac{3}{2}EJ_1} = -32.68\, \text{mm}\]

Displacement at \(x = l\):

\[w = -32.68 \cdot \phi(l) = -32.68 \times \left(1 - \frac{3}{2}\right) = -32.68 \times \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)\] \[\boxed{w(l) = 16.34\, \text{mm}}\]

Stress at \(x = l/4\):

\[\sigma = E\varepsilon = E(-y \cdot w_{xx}) = E(-y \cdot \phi_{xx}) \cdot C\] \[\sigma\left(\frac{l}{4}\right) = -E \cdot y \cdot \phi_{xx}\left(\frac{l}{4}\right) \cdot C\]

The maximum stress is obtained at \(y = \frac{a}{2}\):

\[\sigma\left(\frac{l}{4}\right) = -E \cdot \frac{a}{2} \cdot \phi_{xx}\left(\frac{l}{4}\right) \cdot C\] \[\boxed{\sigma\left(\frac{l}{4}\right) = 26.05\, \text{MPa}}\]

where \(a = 14.76\, \text{mm}\) at \(x = l/4\).

Two Shape Functions Formulation:

If two shape functions \(\phi_1\) and \(\phi_2\) are adopted, the displacement becomes:

\[w = C_1 \phi_1 + C_2 \phi_2\]

The stiffness matrix becomes a \(2 \times 2\) system:

\[k_{ij} = \int_0^l EJ \, \phi_{i,xx} \, \phi_{j,xx} \, dx\]

And the equilibrium equations become:

\[\begin{bmatrix} k_{11} & k_{12} \\ k_{21} & k_{22} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} C_1 \\ C_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} f_1 \\ f_2 \end{bmatrix}\]
