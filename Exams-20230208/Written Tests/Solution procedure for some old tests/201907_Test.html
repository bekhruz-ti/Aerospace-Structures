Exercise 1

A thin-walled beam is fixed at one end and loaded at the free end with a distributed force per unit length \(q\). The total length is \(l\), while the geometry of the section is depicted in the figure. The lumped stringers have area \(A\), while the thickness of the panels is denoted with \(t\). By considering a semi-monocoque approximation, determine the shear and the axial stresses acting at the mid-span section.

\(q= q(x)\)

\(x\)-

Data \(l = 6000\, \text{mm}\); \(a = 200\, \text{mm}\); \(b = 200\, \text{mm}\); \(e = 400\, \text{mm}\); \(d = 50\, \text{mm}\); \(t = 1.5\, \text{mm}\); \(A= 1000\, \text{mm}^2\);

\(q = 150\, \left(\frac{x}{400}\right)^2\, \text{N/mm}\), \(x \in (0, 400]\, \text{mm}\);

Solution

Step 1: Calculate resultant force and moment

\[F_0 = \int_0^{400} 150 \left(\frac{\xi}{400}\right)^2 d\xi = 20\, \text{kN}\] \[M_0 = \int_0^{400} 150 \left(\frac{\xi}{400}\right)^2 \xi\, d\xi = 6 \times 10^6\, \text{N·mm}\]

Step 2: Determine shear flows and stresses

The shear flows and stresses are obtained as follows:

Shear Flows (Units: N/mm):

  • Top left panel: \(21.49\, \text{N/mm}\)
  • Top right panel: \(4.83\, \text{N/mm}\)
  • Left vertical panel: \(0\, \text{N/mm}\)
  • Right vertical panel: \(0\, \text{N/mm}\)
  • Bottom left panel: \(26.75\, \text{N/mm}\)
  • Bottom right panel: \(10.09\, \text{N/mm}\)

Shear Stresses (Units: MPa):

  • Top left panel: \(14.33\, \text{MPa}\)
  • Top right panel: \(3.22\, \text{MPa}\)
  • Left vertical panel: \(0\, \text{MPa}\)
  • Right vertical panel: \(0\, \text{MPa}\)
  • Bottom left panel: \(17.83\, \text{MPa}\)
  • Bottom right panel: \(6.73\, \text{MPa}\)

Step 3: Axial stresses in the stringers

The vertical position of the principal axis origin is at \(350\, \text{mm}\) from the bottom. The horizontal position is available from the symmetry of the section.

\[M_x = -F_0 \cdot z\]

At the mid-span section \(z = 3000\, \text{mm}\), so:

\[M_x = -6.00 \times 10^7\, \text{N·mm}\]

The stresses on the stringers are:

\[\sigma_{xx_1} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}}(600 - 350) = -78.95\, \text{MPa}\] \[\sigma_{xx_2} = \sigma_{xx_3} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}}(400 - 350) = -15.79\, \text{MPa}\] \[\sigma_{xx_4} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}}(-350) = 110.53\, \text{MPa}\]
Final Answers:
Shear stresses: \(\tau_{12} = 14.33\, \text{MPa}\), \(\tau_{23} = 3.22\, \text{MPa}\), \(\tau_{34} = 17.83\, \text{MPa}\), \(\tau_{41} = 6.73\, \text{MPa}\)
Axial stresses: \(\sigma_{xx_1} = -78.95\, \text{MPa}\), \(\sigma_{xx_2} = \sigma_{xx_3} = -15.79\, \text{MPa}\), \(\sigma_{xx_4} = 110.53\, \text{MPa}\)

Exercise 2

A structure is composed of two beams. The geometric and elastic properties, calculated with respect to the principal axes, are denoted as \(EA_i\), \(EJ_i\) and \(GJ_i\), with \(i = 1, 2\), as illustrated in the figure. Note, \(EJ_i = EJ_{xx} = EJ_{yy}\). The structure is fixed at one end, and elastically constrained by means of two linear springs of stiffness \(k_1\) and \(k_2\). Considering a loading condition with an axial force \(P\) applied at the free end and a linearly varying distributed load \(q = q(x)\), determine the horizontal and vertical displacement in correspondence of the two springs.

Data \(l_1 = 1000\, \text{mm}\); \(l_2 = 800\, \text{mm}\); \(EA_1 = 5.7 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\); \(EJ_1 = 9.0 \times 10^8\, \text{N mm}^2\); \(GJ_1 = 4.0 \times 10^8\, \text{N mm}^2\); \(EA_2 = 2.8 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\); \(EJ_2 = 7.0 \times 10^8\, \text{N mm}^2\); \(GJ_2 = 2.0 \times 10^8\, \text{Nmm}^2\); \(k_1 = 2.8\, \text{N/mm}\); \(k_2 = 3.5 \times 10^4\, \text{N/mm}\);

\(q = 6 \times 10^{-3}\frac{x}{800}\, \text{N/mm}\); \(P = 40\, \text{kN}\).

Solution

Step 1: Define the distributed load

The distributed load can be written as:

\[q(z_2) = a \cdot z_2 \quad \text{with} \quad a = 7.5 \times 10^{-6}\, \text{N/mm}^2\]

Step 2: Internal forces (Real configuration)

For beam (2):

\[M_x^{(2)} = -\frac{a \cdot z_2^3}{6}\]

For beam (1):

\[M_x^{(1)} = -\left(\frac{a \cdot l_2^2}{2} + Y\right) z_1\] \[M_y^{(1)} = -(F + X) z_1\] \[M_z^{(1)} = \frac{a \cdot l_2^3}{6}\]

Step 3: Dummy load systems for PVW

Dummy #1 (for vertical displacement):

\[^1\delta M_x^{(2)} = 0, \quad ^1\delta M_x^{(1)} = -z_1, \quad ^1\delta M_y^{(1)} = 0, \quad ^1\delta M_z^{(1)} = 0\]

Dummy #2 (for horizontal displacement):

\[^2\delta M_x^{(2)} = 0, \quad ^2\delta M_x^{(1)} = 0, \quad ^2\delta M_y^{(1)} = -z, \quad ^2\delta M_z^{(1)} = 0\]

Step 4: Apply Principle of Virtual Work (PVW)

\[\left(\frac{l_1^3}{3} + \frac{EJ_1}{k_1}\right) Y = -\frac{R \cdot l_1^3}{3} \quad \text{where} \quad R = \frac{a \cdot l_2^2}{2}\] \[\left(\frac{l_1^3}{3} + \frac{EJ_2}{k_2}\right) X = -\frac{F \cdot l_1^3}{3}\]

Step 5: Solve for spring forces and displacements

From the equations:

\[X = -40\, \text{kN}\] \[Y = -1.22\, \text{N}\]

The displacements at the springs are:

\[S_1 = -\frac{Y}{k_1} = 0.44\, \text{mm}\] \[S_2 = -\frac{X}{k_2} = 1.14\, \text{mm}\]
Final Answers:
Vertical displacement at spring 1: \(S_1 = 0.44\, \text{mm}\)
Horizontal displacement at spring 2: \(S_2 = 1.14\, \text{mm}\)
