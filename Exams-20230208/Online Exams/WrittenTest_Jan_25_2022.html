Force-based #3

Consider the structure in the figure. The two beams are connected at the middle and are constrained with four hinges. (rotation allowed around the dashed line)

Solve the problem exactly and determine the reaction force \(R\).

Note: Shearing deformation is negligible.

Data:
\(\ell = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
\(P = 9000\, \text{N}\)
\(EJ = 10^{11}\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)

Solution

The problem can be easily solved by noting that the two beams can be represented as a system of two linear springs in parallel.

The stiffness of a simply-supported beam can be obtained following the steps below:

Real System:

Beam with load \(P\) at center, reactions \(\frac{P}{2}\) at each support.

\[M = -\frac{P}{2}x\]
Dummy System:

Beam with unit load \(1\) at center, reactions \(\frac{1}{2}\) at each support.

\[\delta M = -\frac{1}{2}x\]

By application of the PCVW:

\[S = 2\int_0^L \delta M \frac{M}{EJ}\, dx = \frac{L^3}{6EJ} P\]

From which:

\[k = \frac{6EJ}{L^3}\]

For the problem at hand, the system is:

With:

\[k_1 = \frac{6EJ}{L_1^3} \quad \text{with } L_1 = \ell \quad \Rightarrow \quad k_1 = \frac{6EJ}{\ell^3}\] \[k_2 = \frac{6EJ}{L_2^3} \quad \text{with } L_2 = \frac{\ell}{2} \quad \Rightarrow \quad k_2 = \frac{48EJ}{\ell^3}\]

The total stiffness of the system is then:

\[k = k_1 + k_2 = \frac{54EJ}{\ell^3}\]

The displacement \(S\) is:

\[S = \frac{P}{k} = \frac{P\ell^3}{54EJ}\]

And so the load introduced in beam #2 reads:

\[F_2 = k_2 S = \frac{48EJ}{\ell^3} \cdot \frac{\ell^3}{54EJ} \cdot P = \frac{8}{9}P\]

From which the reaction force \(R\) is obtained as:

\[R = \frac{4}{9}P = 4000\, \text{N}\]

Force-based #5

Use a force-based approach to determine the displacement \(S\). The contribution of shear deformability is negligible.

Data:
\(\ell = 1200\, \text{mm}\)
\(q = 2\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(EJ = 10^{12}\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)
\(P = 2000\, \text{N}\)

Solution

Real System:

The only beam leading to a not null contribution in the PCVW is the fixed one. Then, the internal actions are reported for this beam only.

Only the shear force and the bending moment lead to a not null energy contribution. Then, the other forces are not reported.

Bending moment in the real system:

\[M = q\ell x - \frac{q\ell^2}{2} = q\ell\left(x - \frac{\ell}{2}\right)\]
Dummy System:

Bending moment in the dummy system:

\[\delta M = x\]

And by application of the PCVW:

\[\int_0^{2\ell} M \frac{\delta M}{EJ}\, dx = S\]

One obtains:

\[S = \frac{5}{3}\frac{q\ell^4}{EJ} = 6.91\, \text{mm}\]

Ritz #2

Use the method of Ritz to determine the displacement \(S\). Use the simplest approximation of the unknown fields, using polynomial trial functions.

Data:
\(\ell = 1300\, \text{mm}\)
\(d = 60\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 2000\, \text{N}\)
\(m_t = 20\, \text{N}\)
\(EJ = 10^{11}\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)
\(GJ = 2 \cdot 10^{9}\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)
\(k = 50\, \text{N/mm}\)

Solution

The essential conditions are:

\[w(0) = 0 \qquad v(0) = 0 \qquad \theta(0) = 0\] \[w_{,x}(0) = 0 \qquad v_{,x}(0) = 0\]

So the displacements are expanded as:

\[w = a_1 \left(\frac{x}{2\ell}\right)^2\] \[v = a_2 \left(\frac{x}{2\ell}\right)^2\] \[\theta = a_3 \left(\frac{x}{2\ell}\right)\]

The PVW reads:

\[\int_0^{2\ell} \delta w'' EJ\, w'' dx + \delta w(2\ell)\, k\, w(2\ell) + \int_0^{2\ell} \delta v'' EJ\, v'' dx + \delta v(2\ell)\, k\, v(2\ell) + \int_0^{2\ell} \delta\theta' GJ\, \theta' dx =\] \[= -\delta w(\ell)F + \delta v(2\ell)F + \delta\theta(\ell)Fd - \delta\theta(2\ell)Fd - \int_\ell^{2\ell} \delta\theta\, m_t\, dx\]

Note that the displacement \(w\) does not provide any contribution to the displacement \(S\).

Furthermore, there is no coupling between \(w\) and \(v, \theta\). So the problem can be solved by considering \(\theta\) and \(v\) only:

\[\int_0^{2\ell} \delta v'' EJ\, v'' dx + \int_0^{2\ell} \delta\theta' GJ\, \theta' dx + \delta v(2\ell)\, k\, \delta u(2\ell) =\] \[= \delta v(2\ell)F + \delta\theta(\ell)Fd - \delta\theta(2\ell)Fd - \int_\ell^{2\ell} \delta\theta\, m_t\, dx\]

Upon substitution of the approximation into the PVW one obtains:

\[\begin{bmatrix} k + \frac{EJ}{2\ell^3} & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{GJ}{2\ell} \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} a_2 \\ a_3 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} F \\ -\frac{Fd}{2} - \frac{3}{4}m_t\ell \end{Bmatrix}\]

From which:

\[a_2 = 27.49\, \text{mm}\] \[a_3 = -0.10\, \text{rad}\]

The displacement \(S\) is:

\[S = v(2\ell) - \theta(2\ell)\cdot d = a_2 - a_3 d\]

\[S = 33.69\, \text{mm}\]

Ritz #3

The axial stiffness varies linearly between \(EA_0\) at \(x=0\) and \(EA_1\) at \(x=\ell\).

Use a two-term polynomial approximation and the Ritz method to determine the axial displacement at \(x = \ell/2\).

Data:
\(\ell = 1500\, \text{mm}\)
\(\hat{h} = 10\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(EA_0 = 7 \cdot 10^{6}\, \text{N}\)
\(EA_1 = 12 \cdot 10^{6}\, \text{N}\)
\(k_1 = 2 \cdot 10^{3}\, \text{N}\)
\(k_2 = 2 \cdot 10^{2}\, \text{N}\)

Solution

The axial stiffness is:

\[EA(x) = EA_0 + (EA_1 - EA_0)\left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)\]

The boundary conditions are of natural type, so the displacement is expanded as:

\[u = a_0 + a_1 \frac{x}{\ell}\]

From which:

\[u' = \frac{a_1}{\ell}\]

The PVW reads: \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\) with:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^{\ell} \delta u'\, EA(x)\, u'\, dx + \delta u(0)\, k_1\, u(0) + \delta u(\ell)\, k_2\, u(\ell)\] \[\delta W_e = \int_0^{\ell} \delta u\, \hat{h}\, dx\]

And upon substitution of the polynomial expansion:

\[\delta W_i = \delta a_1 \frac{1}{\ell^2} \int_0^{\ell} EA(x)\, dx\, a_1 + \delta a_0\, k_1\, a_0 + (\delta a_0 + \delta a_1)\, k_2\, (a_0 + a_1)\] \[= \delta a_0 \left[(k_1 + k_2)a_0 + k_2 a_1\right] + \delta a_1 \left[k_2 a_0 + \left(k_2 + \frac{EA_0 + EA_1}{2\ell}\right)a_1\right]\] \[\delta W_e = \int_0^{\ell} \delta u\, \hat{h}\, dx = \int_0^{\ell} \left[\delta a_0 + \delta a_1 \left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)\right] \hat{h}\, dx = \delta a_0\, \hat{h}\ell + \delta a_1\, \frac{\hat{h}\ell}{2}\]

The equilibrium equations are then:

\[\begin{bmatrix} k_1 + k_2 & k_2 \\ k_2 & \frac{EA_0 + EA_1}{2\ell} + k_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} a_0 \\ a_1 \end{Bmatrix} = \hat{h}\ell \begin{Bmatrix} 1 \\ \frac{1}{2} \end{Bmatrix}\]

From which:

\[a_0 = 6.73\, \text{mm}\] \[a_1 = 0.94\, \text{mm}\]

And then:

\[u\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right) = a_0 + \frac{a_1}{2} = 7.20\, \text{mm}\]

Semi #3 - Exercise 13

Determine the shear stiffness \(GA^*_y\) for the beam section in the figure.

All the panels have thickness \(t\).

Data:
\(a = 300\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 160\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 1.5\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 450\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(\ell = 3000\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 72\, \text{GPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)

Solution

The coordinates of the centroid are:

\[x_c = 0\] \[y_c = \frac{4Aa + \frac{4}{3}Aa}{8A} = \frac{2}{3}a\]

The horizontal position of the shear center is available from the symmetry of the section.

The shear flows can be evaluated by exploiting the symmetry of the section.

Note: The jump of shear flow is zero in correspondence of the two stringers lying on the section neutral plane.

The equivalence between internal action and shear flows requires that:

\[q_1 \cdot 2a = T \quad \Rightarrow \quad q_1 = \frac{T}{2a}\]

From which:

\[A^* = \frac{T^2}{\sum_i \frac{q_i^2 \ell_i}{t_i}}\]

where:

\[\sum_i \frac{q_i^2 \ell_i}{t_i} = \frac{T^2}{4a^2} \cdot 2a \cdot \frac{1}{t} = T^2 \cdot \frac{1}{2at}\]

And so:

\[A^* = 2at = 900\, \text{mm}^2\] \[G = \frac{E}{2(1+\nu)} = 27692\, \text{MPa}\]

\[GA^* = 2.49 \cdot 10^{7}\, \text{N}\]

Semi #4

Starting from the previous exercise, determine the displacement \(S\).

Data:
\(a = 300\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 160\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 1.5\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 450\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(\ell = 3000\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 72\, \text{GPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(F = 12\, \text{kN}\)

Solution

The solution of the problem requires the evaluation of the section properties \(GA^*\), \(EJ\), \(GJ\).

The first one is available from the previous exercise.

1. Bending Stiffness EJ
\[J_{xx} = 4A\left(\frac{1}{3}a\right)^2 + 2A\left(\frac{2}{3}a\right)^2 = \frac{4}{3}Aa^2 = 5.4 \cdot 10^{7}\, \text{mm}^4\] \[EJ_{xx} = 3.89 \cdot 10^{12}\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\]
2. Torsional Stiffness GJ

The Bredt formula can be applied to determine the torsional constant.

\[J = \frac{4\Omega^2}{\oint_\Gamma \frac{1}{t}\, d\Gamma}\]

with: \(\Omega = ab \quad \Rightarrow \quad \Omega^2 = a^2 b^2\)

\[\oint_\Gamma \frac{1}{t}\, d\Gamma = \frac{2(a+b)}{t}\]

So:

\[J = \frac{4a^2b^2}{2(a+b)} \cdot t = \frac{2a^2b^2}{a+b} \cdot t = 1.50 \cdot 10^{7}\, \text{mm}^4\] \[GJ = 4.16 \cdot 10^{11}\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\]

The evaluation of the displacement is done by application of the PCVW:

Real System:
\[T = -F\] \[M = -Fx\] \[M_t = -\frac{Fb}{2}\]
Dummy System:
\[\delta T = -1\] \[\delta M = -x\] \[\delta M_t = \frac{b}{2}\]

The PCVW reads:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \left(\delta T \frac{T}{GA^*} + \delta M \frac{M}{EJ} + \delta M_t \frac{M_t}{GJ}\right) dx = S\]

And then:

\[S = \frac{F\ell}{GA^*} + \frac{F\ell^3}{3EJ} - \frac{Fb^2\ell}{4GJ} = 28.67\, \text{mm}\]
