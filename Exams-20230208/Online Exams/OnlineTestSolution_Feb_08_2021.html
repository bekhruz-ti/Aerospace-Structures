Exercise 1: Two-Beam Frame Vertical Displacement

The beams composing the structure in the figure have stiffnesses \(EJ\) and \(GJ\). Shear deformability is negligible.

Determine the vertical displacement \(v\) as shown in the figure.

(Unit for result: mm)

Data (solution for A = 0):
\(l = 1000 \times (1 + A/10)\); Units: mm
\(EJ = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\); Units: N·mm²
\(GJ = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\); Units: N·mm²
\(q_1 = 1.0\); Units: N/mm

Solution

Real System Analysis
Beam 1: \[M_y = -Xz_1 - 2q_1 \frac{z_1^3}{\ell}\]
Beam 2: \[M_y = -2q_1\ell^2 - (X + 8q_1\ell)z_2\] \[M_t = X\ell\]
Dummy System #1
Beam 1: \({}^1\delta M_y = -x_1\)
Beam 2: \({}^1\delta M_y = -x_2\), \({}^1\delta M_t = \ell\)
Application of PCVW

By application of the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^\ell {}^1\delta M_y \frac{M_y}{EJ}\, dx_1 + \int_0^\ell {}^1\delta M_y \frac{M_y}{EJ}\, dx_2 + \int_0^\ell {}^1\delta M_t \frac{M_t}{GJ}\, dx_2 = 0\]

From which:

\[\frac{\ell^3}{15EJ}(5X + 6\ell q) + \frac{\ell^3}{3EJ}(X + 11\ell q) + \frac{\ell^3}{GJ}X = 0\]

And so:

\[X = -\frac{61\, GJ\, \ell\, q}{15EJ + 10GJ} = -2440\, \text{N}\]
Dummy System #2 for Displacement

A second dummy system is introduced for evaluating the displacement.

Beam #2: \({}^2\delta M_y = -x_2\)

And by application of the PCVW:

\[\int_0^\ell M_y \frac{{}^2\delta M_y}{EJ}\, dx_2 = S\]

From which:

\[S = \frac{\ell^3}{3EJ}(X + 11\ell q)\]

Final Answer: \(S = v = 2.85\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 2: Three-Beam Frame with Spring - Strain Energy

The structure in the figure is composed of three beams with same length \(l\) and bending stiffness \(EJ\).

Shear deformability is negligible and so is the contribution due to the axial stiffness.

Determine the strain energy stored in the spring.

(Unit for result: N mm)

Data (solution for C = 0):
\(l = 1000 \times (1 + C/10)\); Units: mm
\(EJ = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\); Units: N·mm²
\(k = 1000\); Units: N/mm
\(P = 1000\); Units: N

Solution

Real System Analysis

Equilibrium equation:

\[-V\ell + X\ell - P\ell = 0\] \[V = X - P\]
Beam 1: \(N = +V\)
Beam 2: \(N = -X\), \(M = +Vx_2\)
Beam 3: \(N = -V\), \(M = +V\ell + (P - X)x_3\)
Dummy System
Beam 1: \(\delta N = 1\)
Beam 2: \(\delta N = -1\), \(\delta M = x_2\)
Beam 3: \(\delta N = -1\), \(\delta M = \ell - x_3\)
Application of PCVW

The PCVW reads:

\[\int_0^\ell \delta N \frac{N}{EA}\, dx_1 + \int_0^\ell \delta N \frac{N}{EA}\, dx_2 + \int_0^\ell \delta N \frac{N}{EA}\, dx_3 + \int_0^\ell \delta M \frac{M}{EJ}\, dx_2 + \int_0^\ell \delta M \frac{M}{EJ}\, dx_3 + \frac{X}{k} = 0\] \[\frac{\ell}{EA}(2V + X) + \frac{\ell^3}{3EJ}V + \frac{\ell^3}{6EJ}(P + 3V - X) + \frac{X}{k} = 0\]

And substituting \(V = X - P\):

\[\frac{\ell}{EA}(3X - 2P) + \frac{\ell^3}{3EJ} \cdot 2(X - P) + \frac{X}{k} = 0\]

From which:

\[X = \frac{2EA\,k\,\ell^3 + 6EJ\,k\,\ell}{2EA\,k\,\ell^3 + 9EJ\,k\,\ell + 3EA\,EJ} \cdot P\]
Simplified Solution (Neglecting Axial Stiffness)

If the energy contribution of the axial stiffness is neglected:

\[X = \frac{2k\ell^3}{2k\ell^3 + 3EJ} \cdot P = 400\, \text{N}\]

And the strain energy reads:

\[U_{spring} = \frac{1}{2}\frac{X^2}{k}\]

Final Answer: \(U_{spring} = 80\, \text{N·mm}\)

Exercise 3: Two-Span Continuous Beam - Stiffness Ratio

The structure in the figure is composed of two beams with bending stiffnesses equal to \(EJ_1\) and \(EJ_2\).

Determine the bending stiffness \(EJ_2\) such that the vertical displacement at the free end is equal to \(w\) when the structure is loaded with a uniformly distributed force per unit length.

Report the result as \(EJ_2 / EJ_1\).

(Unit for result: adim)

Data:
\(l = 1000\); Units: mm
\(EJ_1 = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\); Units: N·mm²
\(q = 1.0\); Units: N/mm
\(w = 0.5 \times (1 + A/10)\); Units: mm

Solution

Equilibrium Analysis

From moment equilibrium:

\[q\ell \cdot \frac{3}{2}\ell + V\ell = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad V = -\frac{3}{2}q\ell\]
Real System - Bending Moments
Beam 1 (span with \(EJ_1\)): \[M = -\frac{q x_1^2}{2}\]
Beam 2 (span with \(EJ_2\)): \[M = -\frac{q\ell^2}{2} - (V + q\ell)x_2\]
Dummy System
\[\delta M = -x_1 \quad (0 \leq x_1 \leq \ell)\] \[\delta M = +x_2 - \ell \quad (0 \leq x_2 \leq \ell)\]
Application of PCVW

By application of the PCVW:

\[\int_0^\ell \delta M \frac{M}{EJ_2}\, dx_1 + \int_0^\ell \delta M \frac{M}{EJ_1}\, dx_2 = w\]

From which:

\[\frac{\ell^3}{12EJ_1}(2V + 5q\ell) + \frac{q\ell^4}{8EJ_2} = w\]

And upon substitution of \(V\):

\[\frac{q\ell^4}{8EJ_2} + \frac{q\ell^4}{6EJ_1} = w\]

From which:

\[EJ_2 = \frac{q\ell^4}{8w - \dfrac{4q\ell^4}{3EJ_1}}\]

Final Answer: \(\dfrac{EJ_2}{EJ_1} = 0.375\)

Exercise 4: Thin-Walled Obround Section - Shear Stress

The thin-walled beam in the figure is subjected to an internal shear force equal to \(P\). The force is referred to the shear center of the section.

The area of the lumped stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is equal to \(A\); the panels have thickness equal to \(t\).

By using a semi-monocoque approximation of the section, determine the shear stress in the panel indicated in the figure.

(Unit for result: MPa)

Data (solution for A = 0):
\(a = 200\); Units: mm
\(R = 45\); Units: mm
\(t = 1.2 \times (1 + A/10)\); Units: mm
\(P = 9000\); Units: N

Solution

Shear Flow from Symmetry

Due to the symmetry of the structure, the shear flows are readily found as shown in the figure, where:

\[q \cdot 2R \cdot 2 = P\]

So:

\[q = \frac{P}{4R} = \frac{9000}{4 \times 45} = 50\, \text{N/mm}\]
Shear Stress Calculation

The shear stress in the panel is then:

\[\tau = \frac{q}{t_2}\]

Final Answer: \(\tau = 41.67\, \text{MPa}\)

Exercise 5: Two-Cell Section - Torsional Stiffness

Consider the two-cell section in the figure. The area of the lumped stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is equal to \(A\); the panels have thickness equal to \(t\). The shear modulus of the material is \(G\).

Determine the torsional stiffness \(GJ\). For this purpose, adopt a semi-monocoque approximation of the section.

Report the result as \(GJ / GJ_{ref}\), where \(GJ_{ref}\) is reference value available in the data.

(Unit for result: adim)

Data (solution of C = 0):
\(a = 100 \times (1 + C/10)\); Units: mm
\(t = 1.5\); Units: mm
\(A = 400\); Units: mm²
\(G = 27000\); Units: MPa
\(GJ_{ref} = 1.0 \times 10^{10}\); Units: N·mm²

Solution

Equivalent Moment Equation
\[2\Omega_1 q_1^* + 2\Omega_2 q_2^* = M_t \quad \text{with } \Omega_1 = a^2; \; \Omega_2 = 2\Omega_1\]

So:

\[q_1^* + 2q_2^* = \frac{M_t}{2\Omega_1}\]
Compatibility Equations
\[2G\Omega_1 t\, \theta_1' = q_1^* \cdot 4a - q_2^* \cdot a\] \[2G\Omega_2 t\, \theta_2' = q_2^* \cdot 6a - q_1^* \cdot a\]

Imposing \(\theta_1' = \theta_2'\):

\[8q_1^* - 2q_2^* = 6q_2^* - q_1^*\]

From which:

\[9q_1^* - 8q_2^* = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad q_1^* = \frac{8}{9}q_2^*\]
Solving for Shear Flows

Substituting into the moment equation:

\[\frac{8}{9}q_2^* + 2q_2^* = \frac{M_t}{2\Omega_1}\] \[q_2^* = \frac{9}{26}\frac{M_t}{2\Omega_1} \quad ; \quad q_1^* = \frac{4}{13}\frac{M_t}{2\Omega_1}\]
Twist Rate and Torsional Stiffness
\[\theta_1' = \frac{1}{2G\Omega_1 t}(4q_1^* - q_2^*)a\] \[= M_t \cdot \frac{23}{104} \cdot \frac{1}{Ga^3 t}\]

Therefore:

\[GJ = \frac{M_t}{\theta_1'} = G \cdot \frac{104}{23} \cdot a^3 t = 1.83 \times 10^{11}\, \text{N·mm}^2\]

Final Answer: \(\dfrac{GJ}{GJ_{ref}} = 18.31\)

Exercise 6: Thin-Walled Circular Beam - Torsional Rotation

The thin-walled beam in the figure is loaded with two concentrated forces \(F_1\) and \(F_2\). The lumped area of the stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is equal to \(A\). The thickness of panels is \(t\).

The shear modulus of the material is \(G\).

By using a semi-monocoque approximation, determine the rotation of the section at a distance equal to \(1/3\, l\) from the free end.

Report the absolute value of the rotation angle expressed in degrees.

(Unit for result: deg - absolute value)

Data (solution for E = 0):
\(l = 2500\); Units: mm
\(R = 25\); Units: mm
\(t = 0.6\); Units: mm
\(A = 500\); Units: mm²
\(G = 27000\); Units: MPa
\(F_1 = 2000\); Units: N
\(F_2 = 5000 \times (1 + E/10)\); Units: N

Solution

Shear Center and Torsional Moment

The position of the shear center is readily available due to the section symmetry. The torsional moment evaluated with respect to the shear center is:

The torque diagram shows \(F_1 R\) for the first segment and \((F_1 + F_2)R\) for the remaining portion.

Torsional Constant (Bredt's Formula)

The torsional constant of the section is evaluated referring to Bredt's formula:

\[J = \frac{4\Omega^2}{\oint_p \frac{1}{t}\, dn} \quad \text{where } \Omega = \pi R^2\]

So:

\[J = \frac{4\pi^2 R^4 t}{2\pi R} = 2\pi R^3 t\]
Twist Rate Calculation

Recalling that the torsion is \(\theta' = M_t / GJ\), it follows that:

\[\theta' = \frac{(F_1 + F_2)R}{GJ}\]
Rotation at \(x = 2\ell/3\)

And the rotation of the section at \(x = 2/3\) is:

\[\theta = \theta' \cdot \frac{2}{3}\ell\]

Final Answer: \(\theta = 10.51°\)
