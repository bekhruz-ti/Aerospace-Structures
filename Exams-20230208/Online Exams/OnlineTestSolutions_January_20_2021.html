Problem 1: Beam with Spring Support - Vertical Displacement

Consider the structure in the figure. It is loaded with a distributed load, which is introduced continuously. The section is square with dimension \(a\); all the panels have thickness \(t\), while the lumped area of the stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is \(A\). The Young and shear moduli of the material are \(E\) and \(G\), respectively.

Determine the vertical displacement \(v\) in correspondence of the spring by taking shear deformability effects into account. For this purpose, refer to a semi-monocoque model of the section.

(Unit for result: mm)

Given Data (solution for B = 0):
\(\ell = 200\, \text{mm}\)
\(a = 40\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 0.2\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 400\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(G = 28000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(E = 72000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(k = 2000 \times (1 + B/10) = 2000\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(q = 100\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(W = 4.0 \times 10^6\, \text{N·mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Beam Section Properties

The bending stiffness is:

\[EJ = E \cdot 2A \cdot \left(\frac{a}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 = EA \cdot a^2\]

Step 2: Shear Stiffness \(GA^*\)

Due to the symmetry of the section, the shear flows due to the shear force \(T\) (applied at the shear center) are:

\[q \cdot \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}} \cdot 4 = T\]

So:

\[q = \frac{T}{4a} \cdot \sqrt{2}\]

From which the shear stiffness is:

\[GA^* = G \cdot \frac{T^2}{\sum_i q_i^2 \ell_i / t_i} = G \cdot \frac{T^2}{4q^2 a/t} = G \cdot 2at\]

Step 3: Real System Analysis

Internal forces in the right portion (with coordinate \(x\) measured from the spring):

\[T = -\left(X + \frac{q\ell}{2}\right)\] \[M = -W - \frac{q\ell^2}{6} - \left(X + \frac{q\ell}{2}\right)x\]

Step 4: Dummy System

Applying a unit load at the spring location:

\[\delta T = -1\] \[\delta M = -x\]

Step 5: Application of the Principle of Virtual Work (PCVW)

\[\int_0^{2\ell} \left(\delta M \cdot \frac{M}{EJ} + \delta T \cdot \frac{T}{GA^*}\right) dx + \frac{X}{k} = 0\]

After integration:

\[\frac{\ell^2}{3EJ}\left(5q\ell^2 + 8X\ell + 6W\right) + \frac{\ell}{GA^*}\left(q\ell + 2X\right) + \frac{X}{k} = 0\]

Rearranging:

\[\left(\frac{\ell^3}{3EJ} \cdot 8 + \frac{\ell}{GA^*} \cdot 2 + \frac{1}{k}\right) X = -\frac{\ell^2}{3EJ}\left(5q\ell^2 + 6W\right) - \frac{\ell}{GA^*} \cdot q\ell\]

Solving for \(X\):

\[X = -12615\, \text{N}\]

Step 6: Calculate Displacement

The spring displacement is:

\[S = \frac{X}{k} = \frac{-12615}{2000} = -5.84\, \text{mm}\]

Note: \(S\) is positive in the downward direction.

Final Answer: \(v = 5.84\, \text{mm}\)

Problem 2: Rectangular Frame - Reaction Force

The structure in the figure is composed of three beams with same length \(L\), bending stiffness \(EJ\) and axial stiffness \(EA\). Shear deformability is negligible.

Determine the reaction force \(H\).

(Unit for result: N)

Given Data (solution for D = 0):
\(\ell = 1000 \times (1 + D/10) = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EJ = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)
\(EA = 1.0 \times 10^6\, \text{N}\)
\(P = 500\, \text{N}\)

Solution

Step 1: Real System Analysis

Beam 1 (right vertical beam):

\[N = -V\] \[M = -Xx_1\]

Beam 2 (top horizontal beam):

\[N = X\] \[M = -X\ell - Vx_2\]

Beam 3 (left vertical beam):

\[N = V\] \[M = (P + X)x_3 - (V + X)\ell\]

Step 2: Dummy System

Beam 1: \(\delta N = 0\), \(\delta M = -x_1\)

Beam 2: \(\delta N = 1\), \(\delta M = -\ell\)

Beam 3: \(\delta N = 0\), \(\delta M = -\ell + x_3\)

Step 3: Application of PCVW

\[\int_0^\ell \delta N \cdot \frac{N}{EA}\, dx_2 + \sum_{i=1}^{3} \int_0^\ell \delta M \cdot \frac{M}{EJ}\, dx_i = 0\]

This leads to:

\[\frac{X\ell}{EA} + \frac{5}{3EJ}\ell^3 X + \frac{5}{6EJ}\ell^3 P = 0\]

Rearranging:

\[X\left(\frac{\ell}{EA} + \frac{5\ell^3}{3EJ}\right) = -\frac{5}{6EJ}\ell^3 P\]

Solving:

\[X = -5 \cdot \frac{EA\ell^2}{6EJ + 10EA\ell^2} \cdot P\]

Final Answer: \(X = -156.25\, \text{N}\)

Problem 3: Two-Beam Hinged System - Ritz Method

Two beams are hinged through an inextensible joint (see figure). Determine the vertical displacement \(w\) in correspondence of the applied force \(F\) using the Euler-Bernoulli beam model in conjunction with the Ritz method. For this purpose, adopt a polynomial expansion of the displacement field where:

  • the displacement is expanded up to the third order for the first beam
  • the displacement is expanded up to the second order for the second beam

(Unit for result: mm)

Given Data (solution for E = 0):
\(\ell = 500\, \text{mm}\)
\(EJ = 1.0 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)
\(k = 80 \times (1 + E/10) = 80\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(k_t = 2.0 \times 10^7\, \text{N·mm}\)
\(F = 2500\, \text{N}\)

Solution

Step 1: Displacement Field Expansion

The displacement field is expanded as:

\[w_1 = C_1\left(\frac{x_1}{\ell}\right)^2 + C_2\left(\frac{x_1}{\ell}\right)^3; \quad w_2 = C_3\left(\frac{x_2}{\ell}\right)^2\]

The two expansions do not include the constant and linear terms to guarantee the fulfillment of the essential conditions:

\[\begin{cases} w_1(0) = 0 \\ w_2(0) = 0 \end{cases}\]

Step 2: Continuity Condition at Joint

The additional essential condition introduced by the joint is:

\[w_1(\ell) = w_2(\ell) = 0\]

So:

\[C_1 + C_2 = C_3\]

And then:

\[w_1 = C_1\left(\frac{x_1}{\ell}\right)^2 + C_2\left(\frac{x_1}{\ell}\right)^3\] \[w_2 = (C_1 + C_2)\left(\frac{x_2}{\ell}\right)^2\]

Step 3: Derivatives

\[w_1' = C_1 \cdot \frac{2x_1}{\ell^2} + C_2 \cdot \frac{3x_1^2}{\ell^3}; \quad w_2' = (C_1 + C_2) \cdot \frac{2x_2}{\ell^2}\] \[w_1'' = C_1 \cdot \frac{2}{\ell^2} + C_2 \cdot \frac{6x_1}{\ell^3}; \quad w_2'' = (C_1 + C_2) \cdot \frac{2}{\ell^2}\]

Step 4: Principle of Virtual Work

\[\int_0^\ell \delta w_1'' EJ\, w_1''\, dx_1 + \int_0^\ell \delta w_2'' \cdot 2EJ\, w_2''\, dx_2 + \delta w_2(\ell)\, k\, w_2(\ell) + \delta w_1'(\ell)\, k_t\, w_1'(\ell) = \delta w_2(\ell)\, F\]

After working out the integrals:

\[\delta C_1\left(k + \frac{12EJ}{\ell^3} + \frac{4k_t}{\ell^2}\right)C_1 + \delta C_1\left(k + \frac{14EJ}{\ell^3} + \frac{4k_t}{\ell^2}\right)C_2 +\] \[\delta C_2\left(k + \frac{14EJ}{\ell^3} + \frac{4k_t}{\ell^2}\right)C_1 + \delta C_2\left(k + \frac{20EJ}{\ell^3} + \frac{4k_t}{\ell^2}\right)C_2 = \delta C_1 F + \delta C_2 F\]

Step 5: Solve Linear System

Defining \(\hat{k} = \frac{k\ell^3}{EJ}\) and \(\hat{k}_t = \frac{k_t \ell}{EJ}\), the linear system is:

\[\begin{bmatrix} \hat{k} + 12 + 4\hat{k}_t & \hat{k} + 14 + 4\hat{k}_t \\ \hat{k} + 14 + 4\hat{k}_t & \hat{k} + 20 + 4\hat{k}_t \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} C_1 \\ C_2 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} \frac{F\ell^3}{EJ}\]

With numerical values:

\[\begin{bmatrix} 17.00 & 19.00 \\ 19.00 & 25.00 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} C_1 \\ C_2 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 31.25 \\ 31.25 \end{Bmatrix}\]

From which:

\[C_1 = 2.93\, \text{mm}\] \[C_2 = -0.98\, \text{mm}\]

Step 6: Calculate Displacement

The displacement at \(x_2 = \ell\) is:

\[w_2 = (C_1 + C_2)\left(\frac{\ell}{\ell}\right)^2 = C_1 + C_2\]

Final Answer: \(w = 1.95\, \text{mm}\)

Problem 4: Three-Cell Section - Torsional Stiffness

Determine the torsional stiffness \(GJ\) of the three-cell section in the figure. All the panels have thickness \(t\), while the lumped area of the stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is \(A\). The shear modulus of the material is \(G\).

To solve the exercise, use a semi-monocoque approximation of the section.

Report the result as \(GJ/GJ_{ref}\), where \(GJ_{ref}\) is reference value available in the data.

(Unit for result: adim)

Given Data (solution for B = 0):
\(a = 100 \times (1 + B/10) = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 1.5\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 400\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(G = 27000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(GJ_{ref} = 1.0 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)

Solution

Step 1: Set Up Shear Flows

Step 2: Moment Equivalence

\[2\Omega(q_1^* + q_2^* + q_3^*) = M_t \quad \text{with } \Omega = a^2\]

Step 3: Compatibility Equations

\[2G\Omega t\theta_1' = 4aq_1^* - aq_2^*\] \[2G\Omega t\theta_2' = 4aq_2^* - aq_1^* - aq_3^*\] \[2G\Omega t\theta_3' = 4aq_3^* - aq_2^*\]

From which:

\[\theta_1' = \theta_2' \Rightarrow 5q_1^* - 5q_2^* + q_3^* = 0\] \[\theta_1' = \theta_3' \Rightarrow q_1^* = q_3^*\]

Step 4: Solve System

\[\begin{cases} 6q_1^* - 5q_2^* = 0 \\ 2q_1^* + q_2^* = \frac{M_t}{2\Omega} \end{cases}\]

From which:

\[q_1^* = \frac{5}{16} \cdot \frac{M_t}{2\Omega}\] \[q_2^* = \frac{3}{8} \cdot \frac{M_t}{2\Omega}\]

Step 5: Calculate \(GJ\)

Recalling that \(M_t = GJ\theta'\), so \(GJ = M_t/\theta'\)

With \(\theta' = \theta_1'\):

\[2G\Omega t\theta_1' = 4aq_1^* - aq_2^*\] \[\theta_1' = \frac{1}{2G\Omega t} \cdot \frac{M_t \cdot a}{2\Omega}\left(\frac{5}{4} - \frac{3}{8}\right) = \frac{7}{32} \cdot \frac{M_t}{Ga^3t}\]

So:

\[GJ = \frac{M_t}{\theta_1'} = G \cdot \frac{32}{7} \cdot a^3t = 1.85 \times 10^{11}\, \text{N·mm}^2\]

Final Answer: \(GJ/GJ_{ref} = 18.51\)

Problem 5: Thin-Walled Beam - Shear Stress

The thin-walled beam in the figure is loaded with a uniform pressure \(p\). The section is modeled with a semi-monocoque approximation, where the lumped stringers (including the contribution of the panels) have area \(A\), and the thickness of the panels is \(t\). Assuming that the load is introduced in correspondence of the ribs, determine the shear stress in the panel indicated in the figure at the section A.

(Unit for result: MPa)

Given Data (solution for D = 0):
\(\ell = 2000 \times (1 + D/10) = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
\(a = 70\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(c = 30\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 1.2\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 400\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(p = 1.0 \times 10^{-2}\, \text{N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Convert Distributed Load to Concentrated Forces

\[F = p \cdot a \cdot \frac{\ell}{6} = 233.33\, \text{N}\]

Step 2: Calculate Shear Force at Section A

The shear force at the section A is:

\[T = \frac{5}{2}F = 583.33\, \text{N}\]

Step 3: Analyze Shear Flow

Due to the symmetry of the section, the torsional moment with respect to the shear center is null.

The shear flows are readily available by exploiting the symmetry of the section:

\[q = \frac{T}{2c} = 9.72\, \text{N/mm}\]

Step 4: Calculate Shear Stress

\[\tau = \frac{q}{t} = \frac{9.72}{1.2}\]

Final Answer: \(\tau = 8.10\, \text{MPa}\)

Problem 6: Cantilever Thin-Walled Beam - Rotation Angle

The thin-walled beam in the figure is loaded with a force per unit length \(q\). The lumped stringers, including the contribution of the panels, have area \(A\), and the thickness of the panels is \(t\). The shear modulus of the material is \(G\). Assume that the load is introduced in correspondence of the ribs. By using a semi-monocoque approximation, determine the rotation of the section at \(x = \ell/3\).

Report the absolute value of the rotation angle expressed in degrees.

(Unit for result: deg - absolute value)

Given Data (solution for E = 0):
\(\ell = 2500\, \text{mm}\)
\(a = 150 \times (1 + E/10) = 150\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(t = 1.2\, \text{mm}\)
\(G = 27000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(q = 100\, \text{N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Torsional Constant

This is a 1-cell section, so Bredt's formula can be applied:

\[J = \frac{4\Omega^2}{\oint \frac{1}{t}\, d\Gamma} = \frac{4a^4 t}{4a} = a^3 t\]

Step 2: Analyze Torsion

The torsion \(\theta'\) is evaluated by referring to the section's shear center, which is readily available due to the section's symmetry.

The loading can be decomposed: a force \(T\) at the corner produces a torsional moment:

\[M_t = T \cdot \frac{a}{2}\]

Step 3: Rate of Twist

\[\theta' = \frac{M_t}{GJ} \quad \text{with } M_t = T\frac{a}{2}\] \[\theta' = \frac{Ta}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{Ga^3t} = \frac{T}{2Ga^2t}\]

Step 4: Calculate Forces in Each Bay

\[F = \frac{q\ell}{6}\]

For the first bay:

\[T_1 = \left(5 + \frac{1}{2}\right)F\]

For the second bay:

\[T_2 = \left(4 + \frac{1}{2}\right)F\]

Step 5: Calculate Rotations

\[\theta_1' = \frac{T_1}{2Ga^3t}\] \[\theta_2' = \frac{T_2}{2Ga^2t}\]

Step 6: Total Rotation

The rotation at \(x = \ell/3\) is:

\[\theta = \theta_1' \cdot \frac{\ell}{6} + \theta_2' \cdot \frac{\ell}{6}\]

Final Answer: \(\theta = 6.82\, \text{deg}\)
