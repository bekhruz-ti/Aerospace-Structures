Problem 1: Beam Displacement Analysis

The hinges at points A and B constraint the three components of displacement, \(u\), \(v\) and \(w\). The cart at point B constraints only the displacement in the \(y\) direction, \(v\). Compute the displacement in the \(z\) direction of point D.

Given Data:
\(EA = 7.2 \times 10^7 \, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
\(E\bar{I}_{xx} = E\bar{I}_{yy} = 6 \times 10^{11} \, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
\(GJ = 2 \, E\bar{I}_{xx}\)
Beam cross-section stiffness in a local reference system.
\(l = 1200 \, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 2500 \, \text{N}\)
Unit for result: mm

Solution:

Step 1: Internal Forces in Each Element

For Element ①:

\[M_x = Fz\]

For Element ②:

\[M_z = Fl\] \[M_x = -Fz\]

For Element ③:

\[N_z = R_x + F\] \[M_x = Fl - \frac{F}{2}z_3\] \[M_y = -Fl + \frac{F}{2}z_3\]
Step 2: Apply Virtual Force Method

For element 3: \(\delta N_z = 1\)

\[\int_0^{2l} \frac{\delta N_z (R_x + F)}{EA} \, dz_3 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_x = -F\]
Step 3: Virtual Moment Expression

For element 3:

\[\delta M_y = \begin{cases} -\frac{1}{2}z_3 & 0 < z_3 < l \\ \frac{1}{2}z_3 - l & l < z_3 < 2l \end{cases}\]
Step 4: Calculate Displacement \[\int_0^{l} \frac{\delta M_y \cdot M_y}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \, dz_3 + \int_{l}^{2l} \frac{\delta M_y \cdot M_y}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \, dz_3\] \[= \frac{1}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \cdot Fl^3 \left(\frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{12}\right) = \frac{Fl^3}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \cdot \frac{1}{4}\]

Final Answer: \(w_D = 1.8 \, \text{mm}\)

Problem 2: Cantilever Beam Rotation

Compute the rotation \(\varphi_y\) of the beam at point A.

Given Data:
\(l = 4000 \, \text{mm}\)
\(E\bar{I}_{yy} = 6 \times 10^{11} \, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
\(F = 8000 \, \text{N}\)
Unit for result: rad

Solution:

Step 1: Bending Moment Expressions \[M_y = -Fz \qquad 0 < z < l\] \[M_y = -Fz + R_x(z - l) \qquad l < z < 2l\]
Step 2: Virtual Moment \[\delta M_y = \begin{cases} 0 & 0 < z < l \\ (z - l) & l < z < 2l \end{cases}\]
Step 3: Apply Virtual Work Principle \[\frac{1}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \int_l^{2l} \left[-Fz^2 + Fzl + R_x(z^2 + l^2 - 2lz)\right] dz\] \[= \frac{1}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \left(-\frac{1}{3}Fz^3 + \frac{1}{2}Flz^2 + \frac{1}{3}R_xz^3 + R_xl^2z - R_xlz^2\right)\bigg|_l^{2l}\] \[= \frac{1}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \left[Fl^3\left(-\frac{8}{3} + 2 + 1 - \frac{1}{2}\right) + R_xl^3\left(\frac{8}{3} + 2 - 4 - \frac{1}{3} - 1 + 1\right)\right] = 0\]
Step 4: Solve for Reaction \[-\frac{5}{6}F + \frac{1}{3}R_x = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_x = \frac{5}{2}F\]
Step 5: Calculate Rotation

Moment with known reaction:

\[M_y = -F(z + l) + \frac{5}{2}Fz \qquad 0 < z < l\]

Virtual moment: \(\delta M_y = 1\)

\[\int_0^l \frac{-F(z+l)}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} + \frac{5}{2}\frac{Fz}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \, dz = (-1) \cdot \varphi_y\] \[\varphi_y = \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{Fl^2}{E\bar{I}_{yy}}\]

Final Answer: \(\varphi_y = 0.05\overline{3} \, \text{rad}\)

Problem 3: Semi-Monocoque Beam Shear Stress

The semi-monocoque beam model in the figure is loaded by a pressure distribution that is constant along the beam span and varies linearly from 0 to \(p\) along the upper side with dimension \(a\). Compute the shear stress in panel ① at a distance \(2l - \xi\), with \(0 < \xi < l\), accounting for the fact that the external load is introduced by the ribs.

Given Data:
\(l = 2000 \, \text{mm}\)
\(p = 0.2 \, \text{MPa}\)
\(a = 800 \, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 150 \, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 500 \, \text{mm}^2\)
\(t = 4 \, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 72000 \, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
Unit for result: MPa

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate Resultant Force \[R = \frac{pa}{2} \cdot l \cdot \frac{5}{2}\]
Step 2: Calculate Moment \[M = \frac{pa}{2} \cdot \frac{5}{2}l \cdot \left(\frac{a}{2} - \frac{a}{3}\right) = \frac{5}{24}pa^2l\]
Step 3: Calculate Shear Flows

Shear flow from resultant:

\[q'_1 = q'_2 = \frac{R}{2b}\]

Antisymmetric shear flow:

\[q^a = \frac{M}{2ab}\]

Total shear flow in panel 1:

\[q_1 = q'_1 - q^a\]
Step 4: Calculate Shear Stress \[\tau_1 = \frac{q_1}{t_1}\]

Final Answer: \(\tau_1 = 277.78 \, \text{MPa}\)

Problem 4: Torsional Stiffness of Two-Cell Section

Consider the two-cells semi-monocoque cross section model sketched in the figure. Compute its torsional stiffness.

Given Data:
\(a = 200 \, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 100 \, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 1 \, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 1000 \, \text{mm}^2\)
\(E = 72000 \, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
Unit for result: N mm²/rad

Solution:

Step 1: Symmetric Shear Flows

Due to symmetry:

\[q_1^s = q_2^s\] \[q_1^s = \frac{M_z}{4ab}\]
Step 2: Rate of Twist \[\dot{\theta}_1 = \frac{1}{2abG} \cdot \frac{(2a+b)}{t} q_1^s = \left[\frac{1}{2ab} \cdot \frac{(2a+b)}{t} \cdot \frac{1}{4ab}\right] \cdot M_z\]
Step 3: Calculate Torsional Stiffness \[K_\theta = \frac{1 \cdot G}{\left[\frac{1}{2ab} \cdot \frac{(2a+b)}{t} \cdot \frac{1}{4ab}\right]}\]

Final Answer: \(K_\theta = \frac{1.77 \times 10^{11}}{6.46 \times 10^6} \, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}/\text{rad}\)

Problem 5: Rivet Force in Semi-Monocoque Beam

The semi-monocoque beam model in the figure is loaded by a pressure distribution that is constant along the beam span and varies linearly from 0 to \(p\) along the side with dimension \(a\). The rib at \(2l\) from the clamp is connected to the upper panel by means of a row of rivets, with pitch \(\hat{p}\) and diameter \(d\). Compute the force transmitted by one of these rivets.

Given Data:
\(l = 700 \, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 72000 \, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(t = 2 \, \text{mm}\)
\(\hat{p} = 30 \, \text{mm}\) (pitch)
\(A = 800 \, \text{mm}^2\)
\(a = 400 \, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 200 \, \text{mm}\)
\(p = 0.3 \, \text{MPa}\)
\(d = 3 \, \text{mm}\)
Unit for result: N

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate Resultant Force \[R = \frac{pal}{2}\]
Step 2: Calculate Moment \[M = \frac{pal}{2} \cdot \left(\frac{a}{2} - \frac{a}{3}\right)\]
Step 3: Calculate Shear Flows \[q'_1 = q'_2 = \frac{R}{2b}\] \[q^a = \frac{M}{2ab}\] \[q_3 = q^a\]
Step 4: Calculate Rivet Force \[P_i = q_3 \cdot \hat{p}\]

Final Answer: \(P_i = 525 \, \text{N}\)

Problem 6: Virtual Work of Parabolic Element

The three-nodes parabolic element sketched in the figure is loaded by a constant force per unit of length. Compute the virtual work of the load for a unit virtual displacement in the \(y\) direction of node ① (the node at \(x = -2\)).

Given Data:
\(p = 0.56 \, \text{N/mm}\)
Unit for result: N mm

Solution:

Step 1: Shape Function for Node 1 \[N_1 = \frac{x(x-2)}{8}\]
Step 2: Calculate Virtual Work Integral \[\int_{-2}^{2} \left(\frac{x^2}{8} - \frac{x}{4}\right) p \, dx = \left(\frac{x^3}{24} - \frac{x^2}{8}\right)\bigg|_{-2}^{2} \cdot p\] \[= \frac{2}{3} p\]

Final Answer: \(W = 0.3\overline{73} \, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}\)
