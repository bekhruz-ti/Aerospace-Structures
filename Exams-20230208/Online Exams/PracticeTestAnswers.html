Structural Mechanics Examination

Problem 1: L-Shaped Frame with Spring Support

Find the rotation \(\theta\) (in degrees) at \(x = L/2\)

Given Data:

  • \(L = 400\left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA = 7.2 \cdot 10^4 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 1.25 \cdot 10^6 \left(1 + C/10\right) \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(K = 60 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(P = 5000 \text{ N}\)

Solution (A = C = 0)

Step 1: Evaluate the reaction force at the spring

Using the virtual work method with "Real" and "Dummy" systems:

Real system: Force \(X\) at spring, force \(P\) at right end

Dummy system: Unit force \(1\) at spring location

Bending moments:

\[M_y = -(P + X)z \qquad \delta M_y = -z\]

Compatibility equation:

\[\int_0^L \frac{M_y \cdot \delta M_y}{EJ} \, dz + \frac{X}{K} = 0\]

Step 2: Solve for spring reaction force X

From which:

\[X = -\frac{P}{\left(1 + \frac{3EJ}{KL^3}\right)}\]

Step 3: Evaluate the rotation

Using a new dummy system with unit moment at the point of interest:

Dummy system: Unit moment \(1\) applied at \(z = L/2\)

\[\delta M_y = -1 \quad \text{for } z \geq L/2\]

The rotation is found from:

\[\int_{L/2}^{L} \frac{M_y \cdot \delta M_y}{EJ} \, dz = \theta\]

Step 4: Calculate the final rotation

From which:

\[\theta = \frac{3L^2(P + X)}{8EJ} = 12.23°\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta = 12.23°}\]

Problem 2: Triangular Truss - Shear Center Location

Find the vertical position of the shear center (measured from \(x = 0\)).

Given Data:

  • \(A = 500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(h = 150 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_1 = 1 \cdot (1 + F/10)\)
  • \(t_3 = 2 \cdot (1 + A/10)\)

Solution (A = F = 0)

Step 1: Calculate section properties

Apply force \(T\) at an arbitrary distance \(d\) from the reference position (\(T = 1000 \text{ N}\))

\[J_{yy} = 2A \left(\frac{h}{2}\right)^2 \cdot 2 = Ah^2\] \[S_{y_1}' = 2A \cdot \frac{h}{2} = Ah\] \[S_{y_2}' = Ah\]

Step 2: Apply shear flow equation

Applying the shear flow equation two times:

\[q_1' = -T \frac{S_{y_1}'}{J_{yy}} = -\frac{T}{h} = -6.67 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_2' = -T \frac{S_{y_2}'}{J_{yy}} = -\frac{T}{h} = -6.67 \text{ N/mm}\]

Step 3: Moment equivalence condition

Equivalence with respect to point 1:

\[2q_* \Omega + 2q_2' \Omega = -Td\]

Step 4: Impose zero twist condition

Impose \(\theta' = 0\):

\[\theta' = \frac{1}{2G\Omega} \sum_i \frac{q_i \ell_i}{t_i} = 0\] \[\frac{q_1 h}{t_1} + \frac{q_2 h}{t_1} + \frac{q_3 h}{t_3} = 0\] \[(q_1' + q^*) + (q_2' + q^*) + \frac{t_1}{t_3} q^* = 0\] \[\Rightarrow \quad q^* = \frac{2T}{h} \cdot \frac{1}{2 + t_1/t_3} = 5.33 \text{ N/mm}\]

Step 5: Calculate shear center position

\[d = -\frac{2\Omega (q_* + q_2')}{T} = 25.98 \text{ mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{d = 25.98 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 3: Rectangular Section with Diagonal Brace - Torsional Stiffness

Find the torsional stiffness of the section \(GJ/GJ_{ref}\)

Given Data:

  • \(A = 600 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(a = 400 \cdot (1 + B/10) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 1 \cdot (1 + A/10) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(G = 2.7 \cdot 10^4 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(GJ_{ref} = 1 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ N·mm}^2\)

Solution (A = B = 0)

Step 1: Identify symmetry

From the symmetries of the section it is immediate to note that the position of the shear center is in the middle of the section. Therefore the shear flow along the oblique panel does not provide any torsional resistance. Therefore, owing to the symmetry of the section, the flows on the four remaining panels is equal.

Step 2: Apply Bredt's formula for torsion

For the closed section with uniform shear flow:

\[M_t = 2\Omega_c \cdot q \quad \text{with } \Omega_c = a^2\] \[q = \frac{M_t}{2a^2}\]

Step 3: Calculate torsional stiffness

From which:

\[GJ = G a^3 t\]

And so:

\[\frac{GJ}{GJ_{ref}} = \frac{6 a^3 t}{GJ_{ref}} = 172.80\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\frac{GJ}{GJ_{ref}} = 172.80}\]

Problem 4: Circular Section Torsional Shaft

Evaluate the rotation \(\theta\) (in degrees) at the free end.

Given Data:

  • \(A = 500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(R = 80 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 1.2 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(L = 2500 \left(1 + F/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(G = 27000 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(P = 7500 \text{ N}\)

Solution (A = F = 0)

Step 1: Apply Bredt's formula

From Bredt's formula for thin-walled circular section:

\[GJ = G \cdot 2\pi R^3 t\]

Step 2: Calculate rate of twist

The torsional moment is \(M_t = 2PR\) (from the couple). The rate of twist is:

\[\theta' = \frac{M_t}{G \cdot 2\pi R^3 t} = \frac{2PR}{G \cdot 2\pi R^3 t} = \frac{P}{G\pi R^2 t}\]

Step 3: Calculate total rotation

The total rotation at the free end:

\[\theta = \theta' \cdot L\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta = 1.65°}\]

Problem 5: Cantilever Beam with Distributed Torsion - Ritz Method

Use the Ritz method and a 1-DOF polynomial approximation to determine the torsional moment \(M_t / M_{t_{ref}}\) at \(x = L/2\)

Given Data:

  • \(L = 3700 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(GJ = 2.2 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(k = 1 \cdot 10^4 \left(1 + D/10\right) \text{ N·mm}\)
  • \(m_t = 520 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ N}\)
  • \(M_{t_{ref}} = 3 \cdot 10^4 \text{ N·mm}\)

Solution (A = D = 0)

Step 1: Assume displacement field

Using a 1-DOF polynomial approximation:

\[\theta = x \cdot q_1 \qquad ; \qquad \theta' = q_1\]

Step 2: Write the energy equation

Applying the principle of virtual work:

\[\int_0^L \delta\theta' \, GJ \, \theta' \, dx + \delta\theta(L) \, k \, \theta(L) = \int_0^L \delta\theta \, m_t \, dx\]

Step 3: Substitute and solve

Substituting the assumed field:

\[\delta q_1 \left(GJL + kL^2\right) q_1 = \delta q_1 \frac{L^2}{2} m_t\]

Solving for \(q_1\) and the torsional moment:

\[M_t = GJ\theta' = GJ q_1 = GJ \frac{L^2 m_t}{2(GJL + kL^2)}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\frac{M_t}{M_{t_{ref}}} = 1.8}\]

Problem 6: Two-Span Beam - Ritz Method with Trigonometric Approximation

Use the Ritz method and a 1-DOF trigonometric approximation to determine the vertical displacement at \(x = L/2\)

Given Data:

  • \(L = 300 \left(1 + B/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 1.25 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(q = 0.2 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution (B = 0)

Step 1: Assume displacement fields for each span

For the left span (length \(L\), coordinate \(x_1\)):

\[w_1 = \sin \frac{\pi x_1}{L} \cdot q_1\]

For the right span (length \(2L\), coordinate \(x_2\)):

\[w_2 = \sin \frac{\pi x_2}{2L} \cdot q_2\]

Step 2: Apply compatibility conditions

Displacement continuity at middle support:

\[w_1(L) = w_2(0) \rightarrow \text{identically satisfied}\]

Slope continuity at middle support:

\[w_{1/x}(L) = w_{2/x}(0) \rightarrow \text{to be imposed}\]

After imposing slope continuity:

\[w_1 = \sin \frac{\pi x_1}{L} \cdot q_1\] \[w_2 = -2 \sin \frac{\pi x_2}{2L} \cdot q_1\]

Step 3: Apply virtual work principle

\[\int_0^L \delta w_{1/xx} \, EJ \, w_{1/xx} \, dx_1 + \int_0^{2L} \delta w_{2/xx} \, EJ \, w_{2/xx} \, dx_2 = \int_0^{2L} \delta w_2 \, q \, dx_2\]

Step 4: Solve for the generalized coordinate

From which:

\[q_1 = -\frac{8L}{\pi} \, q \cdot \frac{1}{\left(\frac{\pi}{L}\right)^4 EJ \cdot \frac{3}{4} L}\]

Step 5: Calculate displacement at x = L/2

The displacement in \(x = L/2\) is:

\[w_1\left(L/2\right) = \sin \frac{\pi}{2} \cdot q_1 = q_1 = -0.452 \text{ mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{w_1(L/2) = -0.452 \text{ mm}}\]
