Problem 1: Axial Force in L-Shaped Frame

Problem Statement: Determine the axial force at \(x = L/2\).

Given Data:
\(L = 1200\left(1 + \frac{D}{10}\right)\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA_1 = 2.0 \cdot 10^4\, \text{N}\)
\(EA_2 = 1.0 \cdot 10^4\, \text{N}\)
\(EA_3 = 2.9 \cdot 10^4\, \text{N}\)
\(EJ = 1.5 \cdot 10^{10}\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)
\(P = 1200\left(1 + \frac{F}{10}\right)\, \text{N}\)

Solution (D = F = 0)

Using the force method with the vertical reaction \(X\) at the pin support as the redundant force.

Internal forces and virtual fields:

For beam 1 (vertical segment):

\[N = -X \qquad \delta N = -1\]

For beam 2 (horizontal segment):

\[M_x = -(P + X)z_2 \qquad \delta M_x = -z_2\]

Compatibility equation:

\[\int_0^{L} \frac{N \cdot \delta N}{EA_3}\, dz_1 + \int_0^{L} \frac{M_x \cdot \delta M_x}{EJ}\, dz_2 = 0\]

Solving for X:

\[X = -\frac{PL^2/3}{\frac{EJ}{EA_3} + \frac{L^2}{3}}\]

Final Answer: \(X = -577.59\, \text{N}\)

Problem 2: Shear Flow in Arch Panel

Problem Statement: Evaluate the shear flow in the panel with thickness \(t_2\) at \(x = 0\).

Given Data:
\(A = 700\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(b = 600\, \text{mm}\)
\(h = 150\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_1 = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_2 = 1.5\, \text{mm}\)
\(L = 1800\left(1 + \frac{A}{10}\right)\, \text{mm}\)
\(p = 2.5 \cdot 10^{-2}\left(1 + \frac{B}{10}\right)\, \text{N/mm}^2\)

Solution (A = B = 0)

Step 1: Calculate the shear force at \(x = 0\):

\[T = p \cdot L \cdot (b - 2h)\]

Step 2: From the symmetry of the section, the shear flow is:

\[q = \frac{T}{2h}\]

Final Answer: \(q = 45\, \text{N/mm}\)

Problem 3: Strain Energy using Ritz Method

Problem Statement: Using the Ritz method and a 1-dof polynomial approximation for any displacement component, determine the strain energy.

Given Data:
\(L = 1200\, \text{mm}\)
\(d = 45\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA_0 = 1.15 \cdot 10^6\, \text{N}\)
\(EA_1 = 6.5 \cdot 10^5\left(1 + \frac{B}{10}\right)\, \text{N}\)
\(EJ_0 = 1.5 \cdot 10^9\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)
\(EJ_1 = 7.5 \cdot 10^9\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)
\(P = 800\, \text{N}\)
Note: \(EA\) and \(EJ\) vary linearly with \(x\) from \(EA_0/EJ_0\) to \(EA_1/EJ_1\).

Solution (B = 0)

Step 1: Assume displacement approximations:

\[u = q_1 x \qquad w = q_2 x^2\]

Step 2: Define stiffness variations:

\[EJ(x) = \alpha + \beta x\] \[EA(x) = \delta + \gamma x\]

where:

\[\alpha = EJ_0 \qquad \beta = \frac{EJ_1 - EJ_0}{L}\] \[\delta = EA_0 \qquad \gamma = \frac{EA_1 - EA_0}{L}\]

Step 3: Virtual work equation:

\[\int_0^{L} \left(\delta u_{,x} \cdot EA(x) \cdot u_{,x} + \delta w_{,xx} \cdot EJ(x) \cdot w_{,xx}\right) dx = -\delta u(L) \cdot P - \delta w_{,x}(L) \cdot P \cdot d\]

Step 4: After substitution of the approximations, the system becomes:

\[ \begin{bmatrix} \delta L + \gamma L^2/2 & 0 \\ 0 & 4(\alpha L + \beta L^2/2) \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} q_1 \\ q_2 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -PL \\ -2LPd \end{Bmatrix} \]

or:

\[\underline{\underline{K}} \cdot \underline{q} = \underline{f}\]

Step 5: Solving for \(\underline{q}\) and evaluating the strain energy:

\[U = \frac{1}{2} \underline{q}^T \underline{\underline{K}} \, \underline{q}\]

Final Answer: \(U = 599.47\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}\)

Problem 4: Bending Moment in Rectangular Frame Rib

Problem Statement: Model Rib #2 as a beam and evaluate the bending moment at \(x = a/3\).

Given Data:
\(a = 500\left(1 + \frac{A}{10}\right)\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 250\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 0.6\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(L = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
\(P = 1000\, \text{N}\)

Solution (A = 0)

Step 1: Determine the equilibrating shear flows:

The shear flows in the panels are found from equilibrium:

  • Left vertical panel: \(\frac{3}{4}\frac{P}{b}\)
  • Top panel: \(\frac{1}{4}\frac{P}{b}\)
  • Right vertical panel: \(\frac{1}{4}\frac{P}{b}\)
  • Bottom panel: \(\frac{1}{4}\frac{P}{b}\)

Step 2: Extract Rib #2 as a free body:

The rib experiences equal and opposite vertical forces of \(\frac{1}{4}P\) at each end from the shear flows.

Final Answer: \(M = 0\) at \(x = a/3\)
(The rib is in pure shear with equal end forces, resulting in zero bending moment along its length)

Problem 5: Torsional Stiffness Ratio

Problem Statement: Find the value of \(\frac{GJ_2}{GJ_1}\) such that \(S = S_{max}\).

Given Data:
\(L_1 = 300\, \text{mm}\)
\(L_2 = 750\, \text{mm}\)
\(EJ_1 = 2 \cdot 10^9\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)
\(EJ_2 = 3 \cdot 10^9\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)
\(GJ_1 = 9 \cdot 10^7\, \text{N}\cdot\text{mm}^2\)
\(P = 1200\, \text{N}\)
\(S_{max} = 15\left(1 + \frac{F}{10}\right)\)

Solution (F = 0)

Step 1: Define internal moments and virtual fields:

For beam 1 (horizontal segment - bending):

\[M = -Pz_1 \qquad \delta M = -z_1\]

For beam 2 (inclined segment - torsion):

\[M_2 = -2PL_1 \qquad \delta M_2 = -L_1\]

Step 2: Apply unit load method for displacement \(S\):

\[\int_0^{L_1} \frac{\delta M \cdot M}{EJ_1}\, dz_1 + \int_0^{L_2} \frac{\delta M_2 \cdot M_2}{GJ_2}\, dz_2 = S = S_{max}\]

Step 3: Solve for \(GJ_2\):

\[GJ_2 = \frac{2PL_1^2 L_2}{S_{max} - \frac{PL_1^3}{3EJ_1}}\]

Final Answer: \(\frac{GJ_2}{GJ_1} = 187.5\)

Problem 6: Shear Deformability in Cylindrical Tube

Problem Statement: Evaluate the contribution of shear deformability to the displacement \(S\).

Given Data:
\(L = 2300\, \text{mm}\)
\(R = 150\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 0.8\left(1 + \frac{A}{10}\right)\, \text{mm}\)
\(P = 1700\left(1 + \frac{B}{10}\right)\, \text{N}\)
\(G = 27000\, \text{MPa}\)

Solution (A = B = 0)

Step 1: Calculate the effective shear area for a thin-walled circular tube:

\[A^* = \frac{8tR}{\pi}\]

Step 2: Calculate the shear contribution to displacement:

\[S_{shear} = \frac{PL}{GA^*}\]

Final Answer: \(S_{shear} = 0.4739\, \text{mm}\)
