Exercise 1

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by the two concentrated moments \(M_1\) and \(M_2\). Compute the rotation \(\theta\) in the middle of the beam. (Unit for result: rad)

Data \(l = 400\, \text{mm}\)
\(M_1 = 50000\, \text{Nmm}\)
\(M_2 = 20000\, \text{Nmm}\)
\(EJ = 6.0 \times 10^6\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(EA = 7.2 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\)

Answer Solution:
Using the virtual work method (unit load method):

1. Compute reaction force: \[F_2 = \frac{M_1 + M_2}{l} = \frac{50000 + 20000}{400} = 175\, \text{N}\] 2. Actual moment: \(M_x = -M_1 + F_2 \cdot z\)

3. Virtual moment diagram for unit rotation at midpoint:
\[M'_{x_1} = \frac{z}{l} \quad \text{for } 0 < z < \frac{l}{2}\] \[M'_{x_2} = -1 + \frac{z}{l} \quad \text{for } \frac{l}{2} < z < l\] 4. Apply virtual work: \[\theta = \int_0^{l/2} \frac{M'_{x_1} \cdot M_x}{EJ}\, dz + \int_{l/2}^{l} \frac{M'_{x_2} \cdot M_x}{EJ}\, dz\] 5. After integration: \[\theta = \frac{F_2 \cdot l^2}{24 \cdot EJ} = \frac{175 \times 400^2}{24 \times 6.0 \times 10^6}\] \[\theta = \frac{175 \times 160000}{144 \times 10^6} = \frac{28 \times 10^6}{144 \times 10^6}\]
Final Answer: \(\theta \approx 0.19\, \text{rad}\)

Exercise 2

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by the concentrated force \(F\). Compute the beam displacement \(w\) in the \(z\) direction of point \(A\). (Unit for result: mm)

Data \(l = 1400\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 2500\, \text{N}\)
\(EJ_{xx} = EJ_{yy} = 6.0 \times 10^{10}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(GJ = 8.0 \times 10^{10}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(EA = 1.0 \times 10^5\, \text{N}\)

Note: the beam cross-section stiffness properties are given in a local reference system.

Answer Solution:
Analyzing the 3D frame with local coordinate systems:

1. Define local coordinates for each member and moment equations:
\[M_{x_1} = -F \cdot z_1, \quad M_{z_1} = F \cdot l, \quad M_{x_2} = -(F + R) \cdot z_2\] 2. Virtual moments: \(M'_{x_2} = -z_2\)

3. Compatibility condition: \[\int_0^{2l} \frac{(F + R) \cdot z_2^2}{EJ}\, dz_2 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R = -F\] 4. Virtual moments: \(M'_{x_1} = 0\), \(M'_{z_1} = -1\)

5. Rotation at joint: \[\theta(z_1 = l) = \int_l^{2l} \frac{-F \cdot l}{GJ}\, dz_1 = -\frac{F \cdot l^2}{GJ}\] 6. Since \(w_1(z_1 = l) = 0\) (because \(M_{x_1} = 0\) in virtual system):
\[w = \theta(z_1 = l) \cdot \frac{l}{2} = -\frac{F \cdot l^3}{2 \cdot GJ}\] 7. Substituting values: \[w = -\frac{2500 \times 1400^3}{2 \times 8.0 \times 10^{10}} = -\frac{2500 \times 2.744 \times 10^9}{1.6 \times 10^{11}}\]
Final Answer: \(w \approx -42.9\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 3

The semi-monocoque beam model in the figure is loaded at the free extremity by a concentrated moment \(M\). It has five stringers, each with concentrated area \(A\). The upper face is made by by two panels, that are connected through the stringer positioned in the middle at \(a/2\). Carefully check the detailed sketch on the right, and in case of doubt ask for a clarification! These two panels are connected to the stringer by means of rivets, spaced by \(p\), and with a diameter \(d\). Compute the shear stress of one rivet. (Unit for result: MPa)

Data \(a = 600\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_1 = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_2 = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_3 = 2.5\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_4 = 3\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_5 = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 100\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(M = 1 \times 10^5\, \text{Nmm}\)
\(p = 30\, \text{mm}\)
\(d = 2\, \text{mm}\)

Answer Solution:
For a closed section under pure torsion:

1. Shear flow in the panels: \[q = \frac{M}{2 \cdot A_{enclosed}} = \frac{M}{2 \cdot a \cdot b} = \frac{1 \times 10^5}{2 \times 600 \times 100} = \frac{10^5}{120000} = 0.833\, \text{N/mm}\] 2. Two panels connect to the middle stringer, so the total shear force per unit length on the rivet is \(2q\):
\[F_{rivet} = 2q \cdot p = 2 \times 0.833 \times 30 = 50\, \text{N}\] 3. Rivet cross-sectional area: \[A_{rivet} = \frac{\pi d^2}{4} = \frac{\pi \times 2^2}{4} = \pi\, \text{mm}^2\] 4. Shear stress in the rivet: \[\tau = \frac{F_{rivet}}{A_{rivet}} = \frac{50}{\pi} = \frac{50}{3.1416}\]
Final Answer: \(\tau \approx 15.9\, \text{MPa}\)

Exercise 4

Consider the semi-monocoque cross section model sketched in the figure. Compute the axial stress of stringer number 2 at a distance of \(l/2\) from the clamp. (Unit for result: MPa)

Data \(A = 400\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(a = 200\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(l = 6000\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 2000\, \text{N}\)

Answer Solution:
Using rotated principal axes (45° rotation for symmetry):

1. Centroid location: \(x_G = \frac{a}{3}\), \(y_G = \frac{a}{3}\)

2. Force components in rotated system: \[F_{x'} = \frac{F}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{2000}{\sqrt{2}} = 1414.2\, \text{N}, \quad F_{y'} = \frac{F}{\sqrt{2}} = 1414.2\, \text{N}\] 3. Moments of inertia: \[J_{x'x'} = \frac{1}{3} A a^2 = \frac{1}{3} \times 400 \times 200^2 = 5.333 \times 10^6\, \text{mm}^4\] \[J_{y'y'} = A a^2 = 400 \times 200^2 = 1.6 \times 10^7\, \text{mm}^4\] 4. Moments at \(z = l/2 = 3000\, \text{mm}\): \[M_{x'} = -F_{y'} \cdot \frac{l}{2} = -1414.2 \times 3000 = -4.243 \times 10^6\, \text{Nmm}\] \[M_{y'} = F_{x'} \cdot \frac{l}{2} = 1414.2 \times 3000 = 4.243 \times 10^6\, \text{Nmm}\] 5. Stringer 2 coordinates: \[x'(2) = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}} = 141.4\, \text{mm}, \quad y'(2) = \frac{a}{3\sqrt{2}} = 47.14\, \text{mm}\] 6. Axial stress: \[\sigma_{zz} = \frac{M_{x'} \cdot y'}{J_{x'x'}} - \frac{M_{y'} \cdot x'}{J_{y'y'}}\] \[\sigma_{zz} = \frac{-4.243 \times 10^6 \times 47.14}{5.333 \times 10^6} - \frac{4.243 \times 10^6 \times 141.4}{1.6 \times 10^7}\] \[\sigma_{zz} = -37.5 - 37.5\]
Final Answer: \(\sigma_{zz} = -75\, \text{MPa}\)

Exercise 5

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by the concentrated moment \(M\) at \(z = 2l\) from the leftmost hinge and by the concentrated force \(F\) at \(z = 7/2l\). By resorting to the displacement method, and using a trigonometric approximation with only one term, estimate the rotation \(\theta\) of point \(A\) (the point at \(z = l\) from the leftmost hinge). (Unit for result: rad)

Data \(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EJ = 6 \times 10^8\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(M = 4 \times 10^5\, \text{Nmm}\)
\(F = 2000\, \text{N}\)

Answer Solution:
Using trigonometric approximation with one term:

1. Assume displacement: \[v = c \cdot \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\] 2. Derivatives: \[v' = c \cdot \frac{\pi}{l} \cos\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right), \quad v'' = -c \cdot \frac{\pi^2}{l^2} \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\] 3. Virtual work equation: \[\int_0^{4l} \delta c \cdot \sin^2\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right) \cdot c \cdot EJ \cdot \frac{\pi^4}{l^4}\, dz = -\delta c \cdot \frac{\pi}{l} \cos(2\pi) \cdot M + \delta c \cdot \sin\left(\frac{7\pi}{2}\right) \cdot F\] \[\frac{2l \cdot \pi^4 \cdot EJ}{l^4} \cdot c = -\frac{M \cdot \pi}{l} - F\] 4. Solving for \(c\): \[c = \frac{-M \cdot \pi/l - F}{2\pi^4 \cdot EJ/l^3}\] 5. Numerical calculation: \[\frac{2\pi^4 \cdot EJ}{l^3} = \frac{2 \times 97.4 \times 6 \times 10^8}{10^9} = 116.9\, \text{N/mm}\] \[-\frac{M \cdot \pi}{l} - F = -\frac{4 \times 10^5 \times \pi}{1000} - 2000 = -1256.6 - 2000 = -3256.6\, \text{N}\] \[c = \frac{-3256.6}{116.9} = -27.86\, \text{mm}\] 6. Rotation at point A (\(z = l\)): \[\theta = -v' = c \cdot \frac{\pi}{l} = -27.86 \times \frac{\pi}{1000}\]
Final Answer: \(\theta \approx -0.088\, \text{rad}\)

Exercise 6

The nine-nodes parabolic element sketched in the figure is loaded by a traingular force per unit of length, going from \(0\, \text{N/mm}\) to \(p\, \text{N/mm}\). Compute the virtual work of the load for a unit virtual displacement in the direction of the load for the upper middle node (i.e. the node identified by an "X" in the sketch). (Unit for result: Nmm)

Data \(a = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 3\, \text{mm}\)
\(p = 10\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer Solution:
For a 9-node parabolic element with the middle node on the top edge:

1. Shape function for the upper middle node (at \(x = 0\) in local coordinates \([-b/2, b/2]\)): \[N_x = 1 - \frac{x^2}{(b/2)^2} = 1 - \frac{x^2}{2.25}\] 2. Triangular load distribution: \[q(x) = \frac{p}{b}\left(x + \frac{b}{2}\right) = \frac{p}{3}(x + 1.5)\] 3. Virtual work for unit virtual displacement: \[\delta W = \int_{-1.5}^{1.5} N_x \cdot q(x)\, dx = \int_{-1.5}^{1.5} \left(1 - \frac{x^2}{2.25}\right) \cdot \frac{p}{3}(x + 1.5)\, dx\] 4. Evaluating the integral: \[\delta W = \frac{p}{3} \int_{-1.5}^{1.5} \left(1 - \frac{x^2}{2.25}\right)(x + 1.5)\, dx\] Since \(\int_{-1.5}^{1.5} x\, dx = 0\) and \(\int_{-1.5}^{1.5} x^3\, dx = 0\) (odd functions): \[\delta W = \frac{p}{3} \left[\int_{-1.5}^{1.5} 1.5\, dx - \int_{-1.5}^{1.5} \frac{1.5x^2}{2.25}\, dx\right] = \frac{p}{3}[4.5 - 1.5] = \frac{p}{3} \times 3 = p\]
Final Answer: \(\delta W = p = 10\, \text{Nmm}\)
