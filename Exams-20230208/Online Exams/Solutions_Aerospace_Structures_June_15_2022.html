Structural Mechanics Exam Solutions

Problem 1: Cantilever Beam Reaction Force

Compute the y component of the reaction force at B

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 400 \, \text{mm}\)
  • \(EA = 9.2 \times 10^7 \, \text{N}\)
  • \(F = 5000 \, \text{N}\)
  • Unit for result: N

Solution

Step 1: Define stiffnesses

\[k_1 = \frac{EA}{\ell}\] \[k_2 = \frac{EA}{2\ell}\]

Step 2: Equilibrium equation

\[(k_1 + k_2) u = -F\]

Step 3: Solve for displacement

\[u = \frac{-F}{k_1 + k_2}\]

Step 4: Calculate reaction force Y

\[Y = -k_2 u = \frac{k_2 F}{k_1 + k_2} = 1666.7 \, \text{N}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{Y = 1666.7 \, \text{N}}\]

Problem 2: Simply Supported Beam Rotation

Compute the rotation \(\varphi_z\) of point A

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1400 \, \text{mm}\)
  • \(F = 2500 \, \text{N}\)
  • \(EI_{yy} = 6 \times 10^{10} \, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • Unit for result: deg

Solution

Step 1: Use symmetric structure and load

Due to symmetry, analyze half the beam with appropriate boundary conditions.

Step 2: Define moment expressions

For \(0 < z < \ell\):

\[M_y = M\]

For \(\ell < z < 2\ell\):

\[M_y = M + F(-z + \ell)\]

Step 3: Apply virtual work integral

\[\int_0^{\ell} \delta M_y \frac{M_y}{EI_{yy}} dz + \int_{\ell}^{2\ell} \frac{\delta M_y M_y}{EI_{yy}} dz = \phi\]

Step 4: Expand and solve

\[\frac{2M\ell}{EI_{yy}} \left( 1 + \frac{3}{2} \frac{F\ell^2}{EI_{yy}} - \frac{F\ell^2}{EI_{yy}} \right) = 0\]

Step 5: Solve for M

\[M = +\frac{1}{4} F\ell\]

Step 6: Virtual moment conditions

For \(0 < z < \ell\): \(\delta M_y = 1\)

For \(-\ell < z < 0\): \(\delta M_y = 0\)

Step 7: Moment expression in changed coordinates

For \(0 < z < \ell\):

\[M_y = M - Fz\]

Step 8: Evaluate integral

\[\frac{1}{EI_{yy}} \int_0^{\ell} \left( +\frac{1}{4} F\ell - Fz \right) dz = -\frac{1}{4} \frac{F\ell^2}{EI_{yy}}\]

Step 9: Convert to degrees

\[\phi_{z,\text{in deg}} = -\frac{1}{4} \frac{F\ell^2}{EI_{yy}} \cdot \frac{180}{\pi} = -1.1698°\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\varphi_z = -1.1698°}\]

Problem 3: Semi-Monocoque Structure Axial Stress

The semi-monocoque structure in the figure is loaded at point ④ by the concentrated forces \(F_x\) and \(F_y\). Compute the axial stress \(\sigma_{zz}\) of stringer ⑦ at a distance of \(\frac{\ell}{2}\) from the clamp.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 4000 \, \text{mm}\)
  • \(F_x = 5000 \, \text{N}\)
  • \(F_y = 1000 \, \text{N}\)
  • \(A = 1000 \, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(a = 200 \, \text{mm}\)
  • \(t = 1 \, \text{mm}\)
  • \(E = 72000 \, \text{MPa}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • Unit for result: MPa

Solution

Step 1: Transform forces to diagonal coordinate system

\[F_{x'} = -\frac{F_x}{\sqrt{2}} + \frac{F_y}{\sqrt{2}}\] \[F_{y'} = \frac{(F_x + F_y)}{\sqrt{2}}\]

Step 2: Calculate diagonal distance

\[d = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}}\]

Step 3: Calculate second moment of area in rotated system

\[\bar{I}_{y'y'} = 2A \cdot d^2 = A a^2\]

Step 4: Calculate moment

\[M_{y'} = -F_{x'} \cdot \frac{\ell}{2}\]

Step 5: Calculate axial stress

\[\sigma_{zz} = \frac{M_{y'}}{\bar{I}_{y'y'}} (-d) = \frac{(-F_x + F_y)}{4A} \cdot \frac{\ell}{a} = -20 \, \text{MPa}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\sigma_{zz} = -20 \, \text{MPa}}\]

Problem 4: Three-Cell Semi-Monocoque Shear Flow

The symmetric three cells semi-monocoque cross section in the figure is loaded by the force F in the plane of symmetry. Compute the flux q.

Given Data:

  • \(A = 2000 \, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(a = 200 \, \text{mm}\)
  • \(b = 100 \, \text{mm}\)
  • \(h = 100 \, \text{mm}\)
  • \(F = 5000 \, \text{N}\)
  • \(t = 1 \, \text{mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Apply symmetry condition for closed cell flows

\[q_2^0 = q_3^0\]

Step 2: Open section shear flows

\[q_1' = 0\] \[q_2' = q_4' = \frac{F}{2h}\]

Step 3: Twist rate equations

\[\dot{\theta}_1 = 0 \quad \text{(for symmetry)}\]

Cell area:

\[\Omega_2 = \frac{ah}{2}\]

Step 4: Twist compatibility equation

\[\dot{\theta}_2 = \frac{1}{ah} \left( -\frac{\ell_4 q_4'}{t} + \frac{(\ell_5 + \ell_4) q_2^0}{t} \right)\]

Step 5: Calculate member lengths

\[\ell_4 = \sqrt{\left( \frac{a-b}{2} \right)^2 + h^2}\] \[\ell_5 = \sqrt{a - \left( \frac{a-b}{2} \right)^2 + h^2}\]

Step 6: Solve for shear flow

\[q_2^0 = \frac{\ell_4 q_4'}{\ell_5 + \ell_4} = 9.5636 \, \text{N/mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{q = 9.5636 \, \text{N/mm}}\]

Problem 5: Beam with Linearly Varying Distributed Load

The beam in the figure is loaded by a linearly-varying force per unit of length. Resort to a displacement-based approach and using the simplest possible polynomial approximation in order to estimate the vertical displacement in the middle of the beam.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 3000 \, \text{mm}\)
  • \(\mu = 4 \, \text{N/mm}\)
  • \(E\bar{I}_{xx} = 6 \times 10^{11} \, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • Unit for result: mm

Solution

Step 1: Assume displacement function

\[v = c \cdot z(z - \ell)\]

Step 2: Define load distribution

\[p = \frac{\mu}{\ell} \cdot z\]

Step 3: Virtual displacement

\[\delta v = \delta c \cdot z(z - \ell)\]

Step 4: Calculate second derivatives

\[v'' = 2c\] \[\delta v'' = 2\delta c\]

Step 5: Apply virtual work principle

\[\int_0^{\ell} \delta c \cdot 4 E\bar{I} \cdot c \, dz = \int_0^{\ell} \delta c \left( z^3 - z^2 \ell \right) \frac{\mu}{\ell} \, dz\]

Step 6: Simplify equation

\[4 E\bar{I} \cdot \ell \cdot c = -\frac{1}{12} \ell^3 \mu\]

Step 7: Solve for constant c

\[c = -\frac{1}{48 E\bar{I}} \ell^2 \mu\]

Step 8: Calculate displacement at midspan

\[v\left( \frac{\ell}{2} \right) = \frac{1}{192 E\bar{I}} \ell^4 \mu = 2.8125 \, \text{mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{v\left( \frac{\ell}{2} \right) = 2.8125 \, \text{mm}}\]
