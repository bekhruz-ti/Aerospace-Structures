Exercise 1

The beam sketched in the figure, with bending stiffness \(EI\), is loaded by the concentrated force \(F\). Compute the bending moment at point A. (Unit for result: N mm)

Data \(a = 400\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 800\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 1.0 \times 10^5\, \text{N}\)
\(EI = 6.0 \times 10^{10}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(K = 100\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(F = 5000\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:
Define redundant force \(X\) at the spring support. The moment expressions for each segment are:
\[M_{x_1} = (F - X)z_1, \quad M_{x_2} = (F - X)a, \quad M_{x_3} = (F - X)z_3\] Virtual moments: \(M'_{x_1} = -z_1\), \(M'_{x_2} = -a\), \(M'_{x_3} = -z_3\)

Applying compatibility using PVW and including axial and spring deformation: \[\frac{-EI\,b + X}{EA} - \frac{1}{3}a^3 F + \frac{1}{3}a^3 X + a^2 b X - a^2 b F - \frac{1}{3}a^3 F + \frac{1}{3}a^3 X + \frac{EI}{K}X = 0\] Solving for \(X\): \[X = \frac{\frac{2}{3}a^3 + a^2 b + \frac{bEI}{EA}}{\frac{bEI}{EA} + \frac{2}{3}a^3 + \frac{EI}{K}} \cdot F\] The bending moment at point A: \[M = (F - X) \cdot a\] Final Answer: \(M_b(A) = 9.59 \times 10^5\, \text{Nmm}\)

Exercise 2

The FE mesh sketched in the figure has two bilinear planar element and eight nodes. It is loaded by the distributed load sketched in the figure, with a maximum load per unit of length equal to \(f\). Compute the virtual external work for the unitary virtual vertical displacement of node 5. (Unit for result: N mm)

Data \(a = 4\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 6\, \text{mm}\)
\(f = 20\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer

Solution:
For bilinear elements, the shape functions along the top edge are: \[N_1 = 1 - \frac{x}{a}, \quad N_2 = \frac{x}{a}\] The virtual displacement field: \(\delta u = N_1 \delta u_1 + N_2 \delta u_2\)

For the triangular distributed load with maximum value \(f\), the load intensity varies as \(\bar{f}(x) = \frac{f \cdot x}{a}\)

Virtual external work for element containing node 5: \[\int_0^a \delta u_2 \cdot N_2(x) \cdot f(x)\, dx = \delta u \int_0^a \frac{x}{a} \cdot \frac{f}{a} \cdot x\, dx = \frac{1}{3}a\bar{f}\] Considering contributions from both elements meeting at node 5: \[\delta W_e(\text{node } 5) = \frac{2}{3}a\bar{f}\] Final Answer: \(\delta W_e = \frac{2}{3} \times 4 \times 20 = 53.33\, \text{Nmm}\)

Exercise 3

The semi-monocoque beam cross section model in the figure is loaded by a concentrated moment \(M_z\). It has six stringers, each with concentrated area \(A\). Compute the derivative of the cross section rotations angle with respect to the beam axis. (Unit for result: rad/mm)

Data \(a = 600\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 300\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(t_1 = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_2 = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(M_z = 5 \times 10^6\, \text{Nmm}\)

Answer

Solution:
For the two-cell cross section with shear flows \(q_1\) and \(q_2\), the rate of twist equations are:

For cell 1: \[\dot{\theta}_1 = \frac{1}{4abG}\left(\frac{4a+b}{t_1} + \frac{b}{t_2}\right)q_1 + \frac{1}{4abG}\left(\frac{2a+2b}{t_1}\right)q_2\] For cell 2: \[\dot{\theta}_2 = \frac{1}{2abG}\left(\frac{2a+b}{t_1}\right)q_1 + \frac{1}{2abG}\left(\frac{2a+2b}{t_1}\right)q_2\] Compatibility condition: \(\dot{\theta}_1 - \dot{\theta}_2 = 0\)
Moment equilibrium: \(4ab\,q_1 + 2ab\,q_2 = M_z\)

Solving the system of equations: \[q_1 = 7.25\, \text{N/mm}, \quad q_2 = -0.6\, \text{N/mm}\] Final Answer: \(\dot{\theta} = 1 \times 10^{-6}\, \text{rad/mm}\)

Exercise 4

Consider the semi-monocoque cross section model sketched in the figure. Compute the x positions of the shear center. (Unit for result: mm)

Data \(a = 400\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 800\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_1 = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_2 = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_3 = 3\, \text{mm}\)
\(A_1 = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(A_2 = 1000\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)

Answer

Solution:
First, find the centroid location:
\[x_{CG} = \frac{a}{2}\] For the y-coordinate of centroid: \[\frac{A_2(b - y_{CG}) - A_1(y_{CG})}{A_1 + A_2} = 0\] \[y_{CG} = \frac{b \cdot A_2}{A_1 + A_2}\] Second moment of area: \[I_{xx} = 2A_2(b - y_{CG})^2 + 2A_1 y_{CG}^2\] Shear flow analysis with applied shear force \(T_y\): \[q_2 = \frac{T_y}{b}\] \[q_1 = \frac{-T_y S'_x}{I_{xx}}, \quad \text{where } S'_x = -A_1 y_{CG}\] \[q_3 = q_1\] Taking moments about a convenient point: \[-q_3 \cdot b \cdot a = T_y \cdot x_{sc}\] \[x_{sc} = -\frac{q_3 \cdot b}{T_y}\] Final Answer: \(x_{sc} = -200\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 5

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by the concentrated force \(F\) at \(x = l\) and by the distributed force for unit of length \(q\). By resorting to the displacement method, and using a trigonometric approximation with only one term, estimate the vertical displacement in the y direction of point A (the point at \(x = l/2\) from the hinge). Account for the axial pre-stress. (Unit for result: mm)

Data \(q = 1.2\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(F_x = 10000\, \text{N}\)
\(EA = 1 \times 10^8\, \text{N}\)
\(EI = 1 \times 10^{11}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(l = 4000\, \text{mm}\)

Answer

Solution:
Using a one-term trigonometric approximation: \[w = a\sin\left(\frac{\pi x}{l}\right)\] Derivatives: \[w' = \frac{\pi}{l}a\cos\left(\frac{\pi x}{l}\right), \quad w'' = -\frac{\pi^2}{l^2}w\] Applying PVW with axial pre-stress: \[\int_0^l \left(\delta w'' \cdot EI \cdot w'' + \delta w' \cdot w' \cdot F_x\right)dx = \int_0^l -\delta w \cdot q\, dx\] After integration: \[\delta a \left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^4 EI \frac{l}{2} a + \delta a \left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^2 \frac{l}{2} F_x a = -\delta a \frac{l}{\pi}\cos\left(\frac{\pi x}{l}\right)\Big|_0^l \cdot q = -\delta a \frac{2l}{\pi} q\] Simplifying: \[\left[\left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^4 EI \frac{l}{2} + \left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^2 F_x \frac{l}{2}\right] a = -2q\frac{l}{\pi}\] Solving for \(a\): \[a = \frac{-2q\frac{l}{\pi}}{\left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^4 EI \frac{l}{2} + \left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^2 F_x \frac{l}{2}}\] Final Answer: \(w\left(\frac{l}{2}\right) = a = -34.55\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 6

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by a triangular force per unit of length, going from 0 N/mm to \(f\) N/mm. By resorting to the displacement method, and using a polynomial approximation with only one term, compute the vertical displacement in the y direction of point A. (Unit for result: mm)

Data \(f = 14\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(l = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EI = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(K = 100\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer

Solution:
Using a polynomial approximation that satisfies boundary conditions at the fixed end: \[v = az^2, \quad v'' = 2a\] The triangular load: \(p(z) = \frac{f}{l}z\)

Applying PVW including spring contribution at \(z = l\): \[\delta a \int_0^l 4EI\, dz \cdot a + \delta a \cdot l^4 K \cdot a = \delta a \int_0^l -\frac{f}{l}z \cdot z^2\, dz\] Evaluating the integrals: \[\delta a (4EI \cdot l + K \cdot l^4) a = \delta a \left(-\frac{1}{4}\frac{f}{l}l^4\right) = \delta a \left(-\frac{1}{4}f l^3\right)\] Solving for \(a\): \[a = \frac{-\frac{1}{4}f l^3}{4EI \cdot l + K \cdot l^4}\] Displacement at point A (at \(z = l\)): \[v = a \cdot l^2\] Final Answer: \(v = -66.67\, \text{mm}\)

True/False Questions (Put a T (true) or F (false) at the end of the sentence)

  1. a beam is loaded with a shear force; the torsional moment is equal to the value of the force multiplied by the distance between the force line of action and the barycenter: F (False - it should be the distance to the shear center, not the barycenter)
  2. the bending moment of over-constrained beam structures cannot be computed by resorting to the PVW: F (False - PVW can be used for over-constrained structures)
  3. the axial stress of a beam transmitting a given bending moment \(M_x\) is function of the beam material elasic modulus \(E\): F (False - axial stress from bending depends on geometry and moment, not on E)
