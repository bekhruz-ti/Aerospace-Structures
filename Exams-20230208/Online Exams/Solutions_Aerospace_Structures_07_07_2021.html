Exercise 1

Determine the value of the concentrated force \(X\) such that the vertical displacement at the corresponding point of application is zero.

Data:

\(\ell_1 = 1000\left(1 + \dfrac{B}{10}\right)\, \text{mm}\)    \(q = 1\, \text{N/mm}\)

\(\ell_2 = 1500\, \text{mm}\)        \(P = 1000\, \text{N}\)

\(EA_1 = 1 \cdot 10^{6}\, \text{N}\)
\(EA_2 = 1 \cdot 10^{7}\, \text{N}\)
\(EJ_1 = 1 \cdot 10^{12}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)
\(EJ_2 = 1 \cdot 10^{12}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)

Solution

Real System

Beam 1:

\[N_1 = P\] \[M_1 = -\frac{q x_1^2}{2}\]

Beam 2:

\[N_2 = P\] \[M_2 = -\frac{q \ell_1^2}{2} - (X + q\ell_1) x_2\]
Dummy System

Beam 2:

\[\delta M = -x_2\]

By application of the PCVW (Principle of Complementary Virtual Work):

\[\int_0^{\ell_2} \frac{M \cdot \delta M}{EJ_2}\, dx_2 = 0\]

From which:

\[X = -\left(q\ell_1 + \frac{3}{4} q \frac{\ell_1^2}{\ell_2}\right)\]

Final Answer: \(X = -1500\, \text{N}\)

Exercise 4

Determine the vertical displacement \(w\) in correspondence of the spring.

Data:

\(\ell = 1000\, \text{mm}\)      \(q = 15\, \text{N/mm}\)

\(EJ = 10^{12}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)

\(k = 1500\left(1 + \dfrac{B}{10}\right)\, \text{N/mm}\)

Solution

Real System
\[M = -Yx - \frac{qx^2}{2}\]
Dummy System

Apply unit force at spring location:

\[\delta M = -x\]

By application of the PCVW:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M \cdot M}{EJ}\, dx + \frac{Y}{k} = 0\]

From which:

\[\frac{\ell^3}{24EJ}(8Y + 3q\ell) + \frac{Y}{k} = 0\]

So:

\[Y = -\frac{3kq\ell^4}{8k\ell^3 + 24EJ} = -1875\, \text{N}\]

And:

\[w = -\frac{Y}{k} = \frac{3q\ell^4}{8k\ell^3 + 24EJ}\]

Final Answer: \(w = 1.25\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 5

Determine the strain energy stored in the spring of torsional stiffness \(K_t\).

Data:

\(\ell = 1000\, \text{mm}\)    \(EJ = 10^{12}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)    \(q = 1\, \text{N/mm}\)

\(K_t = 10^{9}\, \text{N·mm}\)

Solution

Real System

Equilibrium equations:

\[\begin{cases} V + W = q\ell & \Rightarrow V = q\ell - W \\ X = \dfrac{q\ell^2}{2} - W\ell & \Rightarrow W = \dfrac{q\ell}{2} - \dfrac{X}{\ell} \end{cases}\]

So:

\[V = q\ell - \frac{q\ell}{2} + \frac{X}{\ell} = \frac{q\ell}{2} + \frac{X}{\ell}\]

Bending moment:

\[M = -X + Vx - \frac{qx^2}{2}\]
Dummy System

Apply unit moment at torsional spring:

\[\delta M = -1 + \frac{1}{\ell}x\]

By application of the PCVW:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M \cdot M}{EJ}\, dx + \frac{X}{K_t} = 0\]

From which:

\[X = K_t \cdot \frac{q\ell^3}{24EJ + 8K_t\ell} = 31250\, \text{N·mm}\]

And so, the strain energy:

\[U = \frac{1}{2} \frac{X^2}{K_t}\]

Final Answer: \(U = 0.4883\, \text{N·mm}\)

Exercise 9

Determine the vertical displacement at the point A using the simplest polynomial approximation and the Ritz method.

Data:

\(\ell_1 = 1300\, \text{mm}\)    \(EJ = 10^{12}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)

\(\ell_2 = 500\, \text{mm}\)     \(GJ_0 = 2 \cdot 10^{11}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)

\(\ell_3 = 700\, \text{mm}\)     \(GJ_1 = 5 \cdot 10^{11}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)

\(k = 500\left(1 + \dfrac{M}{10}\right)\, \text{N/mm}\)

\(P = 7000\, \text{N}\)

Solution

Essential Boundary Conditions:
\(\theta(0) = 0\)
\(w(0) = 0\)
\(w'_x(0) = 0\)

Variable torsional stiffness:

\[GJ(x) = GJ_0 + GJ_1 \frac{x}{\ell_1}\]
Trial Functions

The simplest polynomial trial functions satisfying the essential boundary conditions are found as:

\[\theta(x) = C_1 \left(\frac{x}{\ell_1}\right) \quad \Rightarrow \quad \theta' = C_1 \frac{1}{\ell_1}\] \[w(x) = d_1 \left(\frac{x}{\ell_1}\right)^2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad w'' = d_1 \frac{2}{\ell_1^2}\]
Principle of Virtual Works

The Principle of Virtual Works reads:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^{\ell_1} \left(\delta w'' \cdot EJ \cdot w'' + \delta\theta' \cdot GJ(x) \cdot \theta'\right) dx + \delta\left(w(\ell_1) - \theta(\ell_1)\ell_2\right) k \left(w(\ell_1) - \theta(\ell_1)\ell_2\right)\] \[\delta W_e = \delta\left(w(\ell_1) + \theta(\ell_1)\ell_3\right) P\]

From which, the stiffness matrix:

\[\underline{\underline{K}} = \begin{bmatrix} k\ell_2^2 + \dfrac{2GJ_0 + GJ_1}{2\ell_1} & -k\ell_2 \\[10pt] -k\ell_2 & k + \dfrac{4EJ}{\ell_1^3} \end{bmatrix}\]

And the force vector:

\[\underline{F} = \begin{Bmatrix} \ell_3 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} P\]

So:

\[\underline{\underline{K}} \begin{Bmatrix} C_1 \\ d_1 \end{Bmatrix} = \underline{F}\]

Solving:

\[C_1 = 0.0127\, \text{rad}\] \[d_1 = 4.3875\, \text{mm}\]

The vertical displacement at \(x = \ell_1\) reads:

Final Answer: \(w(\ell_1) = d_1 = 4.3875\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 14

Determine the vertical displacement at the middle using a Ritz approximation with 1 trigonometric trial function.

Recall the integration rule:

\[\int_0^{a} \left(\sin\frac{\pi x}{a}\right)^3 dx = \frac{4a}{3\pi}\]
Data:

\(\ell = 1700\left(1 + \dfrac{C}{10}\right)\, \text{mm}\)    \(q = 15\, \text{N/mm}\)

\(EJ_0 = 10^{12}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)

Solution

Essential Conditions:
\(w(0) = 0\)
\(w(\ell) = 0\)

Natural Conditions:
\(w''(0) = 0\)
\(w''(\ell) = 0\)

A sine-type expansion satisfies both essential and natural conditions. Assuming a single-dof approximation:

\[w(x) = a \sin\frac{\pi x}{\ell}\]

And, by application of the PVW:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \delta w'' \cdot EJ(x) \cdot w''\, dx = \int_0^{\ell} \delta w \cdot q\, dx\]

From which:

\[ka = f\]

where:

\[k = \frac{4}{3} EJ_0 \left(\frac{\pi}{\ell}\right)^3\] \[f = q \frac{2\ell}{\pi}\]

So:

\[a = \frac{f}{k} = 1.9292\, \text{mm}\]

And so:

\[w_{mid} = a \sin\frac{\pi x}{\ell}\Big|_{x=\ell/2}\]

Final Answer: \(w_{mid} = 1.9292\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 22

Determine \(A\) such that the maximum displacement is \(u = 3\, \text{mm}\).

Data:

\(\ell = 3000\left(1 + \dfrac{A}{10}\right)\, \text{mm}\)

\(a = 200\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 150\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 1.2\, \text{mm}\)

\(p = 1.6 \cdot 10^{-3}\, \text{N/mm}^2\)

\(E = 72000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(G = 27000\, \text{MPa}\)

Solution

Distributed load per unit length:

\[q = pa\]
Real System
\[T = -qx\] \[M = -\frac{qx^2}{2}\]
Dummy System

Apply unit force at free end:

\[\delta T = -1\] \[\delta M = -x\]

By application of the PCVW:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \left(\frac{\delta M \cdot M}{EJ} + \frac{\delta T \cdot T}{GA^*}\right) dx = u\]

So:

\[u = \frac{q\ell^4}{8} \cdot \frac{1}{EJ} + \frac{q\ell^2}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{GA^*}\]
Section Properties

But:

\[EJ = EA\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2 \cdot 4 = EAb^2\] \[GA^* = G \frac{T^2}{\sum_i q_i^2 \ell_i t_i} = G \cdot 2bt\]

(Note: \(2bt\) is the total area of the vertical webs)

So:

\[u = \frac{q\ell^4}{8} \cdot \frac{1}{EJ} + \frac{q\ell^2}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{GA^*}\] \[\frac{q\ell^4}{8 \cdot EAb^2} = S - \frac{q\ell^2}{4Gbt}\]

Solving for \(A\):

\[A = \frac{q\ell^4}{8Eb^2} \cdot \frac{1}{\left(u - \dfrac{q\ell^2}{4Gbt}\right)}\]

Final Answer: \(A = 701.3\, \text{mm}^2\)
