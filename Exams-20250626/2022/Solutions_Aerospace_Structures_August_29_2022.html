Exercise 1

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by the two concentrated moments \(M_1\) and \(M_2\). Compute the rotation \(\theta\) in the middle of the beam.

(Unit for result: rad)

Data:
\(l = 400\, \text{mm}\)
\(M_1 = 50000\, \text{Nmm}\)
\(M_2 = 20000\, \text{Nmm}\)
\(EJ = 6.0 \times 10^6\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(EA = 7.2 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\)

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate reaction force \(F_2\)

From equilibrium:

\[F_2 = \frac{M_1 + M_2}{l}\]

Step 2: Express bending moment \(M_x\)

\[M_x = -M_1 + F_2 \cdot z\]

Step 3: Define virtual moment distribution

For \(0 < z < \frac{l}{2}\):

\[M'_{x_1} = \frac{1}{l} z\]

For \(\frac{l}{2} < z < l\):

\[M'_{x_2} = -1 + \frac{1}{l} z\]

Step 4: Apply virtual work principle

\[\theta = \int_0^{l/2} \frac{M'_{x_1} \cdot M_x}{EJ}\, dz + \int_{l/2}^{l} \frac{M'_{x_2} \cdot M_x}{EJ}\, dz\]

Apply the integral with given values to obtain \(\theta\) in radians.

Exercise 2

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by the concentrated force \(F\). Compute the beam displacement \(w\) in the \(z\) direction of point \(A\).

(Unit for result: mm)

Data:
\(l = 1400\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 2500\, \text{N}\)
\(EJ_{xx} = EJ_{yy} = 6.0 \times 10^{10}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(GJ = 8.0 \times 10^{10}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(EA = 1.0 \times 10^5\, \text{N}\)

Note: the beam cross-section stiffness properties are given in a local reference system.

Solution:

Step 1: Define internal moments in local coordinates

\[M_{z_1} = -F \cdot z_1\] \[M_{t_2} = F \cdot l\] \[M_{x_2} = -(F + R) \cdot z_2\]

Step 2: Define virtual moment

\[M'_{x_2} = -z_2\]

Step 3: Apply compatibility condition

\[\int_0^{2l} \frac{(F + R) \cdot z_2^2}{EJ}\, dz_2 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R = -F\]

Step 4: Calculate torsional rotation

With \(M_{x_1} = 0\) and \(M'_{t_2} = -1\):

\[\theta(z_2 = l) = \int_l^{2l} \frac{-F \cdot l}{GJ}\, dz_2 = -\frac{F \cdot l^2}{GJ}\]

Step 5: Calculate displacement at point A

\(\psi_z(z_1 = l) = 0\) because \(M_{z_1} = 0\)

\[\psi = \theta(z_2 = l) \cdot \frac{l}{2} = -\frac{F \cdot l^3}{GJ \cdot 2}\]

Final Answer: \(w = -\dfrac{F \cdot l^3}{2 \cdot GJ}\) mm

Exercise 3

The semi-monocoque beam model in the figure is loaded at the free extremity by a concentrated moment \(M\). It has five stringers, each with concentrated area \(A\). The upper face is made by two panels, that are connected through the stringer positioned in the middle at \(a/2\). Carefully check the detailed sketch on the right, and in case of doubt ask for a clarification!

These two panels are connected to the stringer by means of rivets, spaced by \(p\), and with a diameter \(d\).

Compute the shear stress of one rivet.

(Unit for result: MPa)

Data:
\(a = 600\, \text{mm}\), \(b = 100\, \text{mm}\), \(t_1 = 1\, \text{mm}\), \(t_2 = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_3 = 2.5\, \text{mm}\), \(t_4 = 3\, \text{mm}\), \(t_5 = 1\, \text{mm}\), \(A = 100\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(M = 1 \times 10^5\, \text{Nmm}\), \(p = 30\, \text{mm}\), \(d = 2\, \text{mm}\)

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate shear flow

\[q = \frac{M}{2ab}\]

Step 2: Calculate force on rivet

\[F = q \cdot p\]

Step 3: Calculate shear stress in rivet

\[\tau = \frac{F}{\pi \cdot d^2/4} = \frac{4F}{\pi d^2}\]

Final Answer: \(\tau = \dfrac{4 \cdot q \cdot p}{\pi d^2} = \dfrac{4M \cdot p}{2ab \cdot \pi d^2} = \dfrac{2Mp}{\pi a b d^2}\) MPa

Exercise 4

Consider the semi-monocoque cross section model sketched in the figure. Compute the axial stress of stringer number 2 at a distance of \(l/2\) from the clamp.

(Unit for result: MPa)

Data:
\(A = 400\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(a = 200\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(l = 6000\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 2000\, \text{N}\)

Solution:

Step 1: Locate centroid

\[y_{CG} = \frac{a}{3}\] \[y'_{CG} = \frac{a}{3}\]

Step 2: Transform to principal axes (45° rotation for symmetry)

\[F_{x'} = \frac{F}{\sqrt{2}}\] \[F_{y'} = \frac{F}{\sqrt{2}}\]

Step 3: Calculate second moments of area

\[J_{x'x'} = A\left(\frac{a}{3\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 + 2A\left(\frac{a}{\sqrt{2}} - \frac{a}{3\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{3}A \cdot a^2\] \[J_{y'y'} = 2A\left(\frac{a}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 = A \cdot a^2\]

Step 4: Calculate moments at \(z = l/2\)

\[M_{x'}\left(\frac{l}{2}\right) = -F_{y'} \cdot \frac{l}{2}\] \[M_{y'}\left(\frac{l}{2}\right) = F_{x'} \cdot \frac{l}{2}\]

Step 5: Position of stringer 2

\[x'(2) = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}}\] \[y'(2) = \frac{a}{3\sqrt{2}}\]

Step 6: Calculate axial stress

\[\sigma_{zz}\left(2, z = \frac{l}{2}\right) = \frac{M_{x'} \cdot y'}{J_{x'x'}} - \frac{M_{y'} \cdot x'}{J_{y'y'}}\]

Apply values to obtain \(\sigma_{zz}\) at stringer 2 in MPa.

Exercise 5

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by the concentrated moment \(M\) at \(z = 2l\) from the leftmost hinge and by the concentrated force \(F\) at \(z = 7l/2\).

By resorting to the displacement method, and using a trigonometric approximation with only one term, estimate the rotation \(\theta\) of point \(A\) (the point at \(z = l\) from the leftmost hinge).

(Unit for result: rad)

Data:
\(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EJ = 6 \times 10^8\, \text{Nmm}^2\)
\(M = 4 \times 10^5\, \text{Nmm}\)
\(F = 2000\, \text{N}\)

Solution:

Step 1: Assume displacement shape function

\[v = \ell \cdot \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\]

Step 2: Calculate derivatives

\[v' = \ell \cdot \frac{\pi}{l} \cos\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\] \[v'' = -\ell \cdot \frac{\pi^2}{l^2} \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\]

Step 3: Apply virtual work principle

\[\int_0^{4l} \delta\ell \cdot \sin^2\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right) \cdot \ell \cdot EJ \cdot \frac{\pi^4}{l^4}\, dz = -\delta\ell \cdot \frac{\pi}{l} \cos(2\pi) \cdot M + \delta\ell \cdot \sin\left(\frac{7\pi}{2}\right)\] \[= -\delta\ell \cdot \frac{\pi}{l} M - \delta\ell \cdot F\]

Step 4: Solve for amplitude \(\ell\)

\[\frac{2l \pi^4 EJ}{l^4} \cdot \ell = -M \frac{\pi}{l} - F\] \[\ell = \left(-\frac{M\pi}{l} - F\right) \bigg/ \left(\frac{2l\pi^4 EJ}{l^4}\right)\]

Step 5: Calculate rotation at point A

\[\theta = -v' = \ell \cdot \frac{\pi}{l}\]

Final Answer: \(\theta = \ell \cdot \dfrac{\pi}{l}\) rad, where \(\ell = \dfrac{-M\pi/l - F}{2\pi^4 EJ/l^3}\)

Exercise 6

The nine-nodes parabolic element sketched in the figure is loaded by a triangular force per unit of length, going from \(0\, \text{N/mm}\) to \(p\, \text{N/mm}\).

Compute the virtual work of the load for a unit virtual displacement in the direction of the load for the upper middle node (i.e. the node identified by an "X" in the sketch).

(Unit for result: Nmm)

Data:
\(a = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 3\, \text{mm}\)
\(p = 10\, \text{N/mm}\)

Solution:

Step 1: Define shape function for upper middle node

Using local coordinate \(x\) ranging from \(-1.5\) to \(1.5\) (with \(b = 3\) mm):

\[N_x = 1 - \frac{x^2}{2.25}\]

Step 2: Express triangular load distribution

\[\hat{p} = \frac{p}{b}\left(x + \frac{b}{2}\right)\]

Step 3: Calculate virtual work

\[\delta\phi_e = \int_{-1.5}^{1.5} \left(1 - \frac{x^2}{2.25}\right)\left(x + 1.5\right) \cdot \frac{p}{3}\, dx = ... = p\]

Final Answer: \(\delta\phi_e = p = 10\, \text{Nmm}\)
