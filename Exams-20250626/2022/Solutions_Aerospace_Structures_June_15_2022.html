Problem 1: Cantilever Beam Reaction Force

Compute the \(y\) component of the reaction force at \(B\).

Given Data:
\(\ell = 400\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 7.2 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\)
\(F = 5000\, \text{N}\)
Unit for result: N

Solution:

Step 1: Define stiffness coefficients for each segment \[k_1 = \frac{EA}{\ell}\] \[k_2 = \frac{EA}{2\ell}\]
Step 2: Apply equilibrium equation \[(k_1 + k_2)u = -F\]
Step 3: Solve for displacement \[u = \frac{-F}{k_1 + k_2}\]
Step 4: Calculate reaction force at B \[Y = -k_2 u = \frac{k_2 F}{k_1 + k_2}\]

Final Answer: \(Y = 1666.7\, \text{N}\)

Problem 2: Beam Rotation Calculation

Compute the rotation \(\varphi_A\) of point \(A\).

Given Data:
\(\ell = 1400\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 2500\, \text{N}\)
\(EI_{yy} = 6 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)
Unit for result: deg

Solution:

Step 1: Utilize symmetric structure and load

For \(0 < z < \ell\): \(M_y = M\)

For \(\ell < z < 2\ell\): \(M_y = M + F(-z + \ell)\)

Virtual moment: \(\delta M_y = 1\)

Step 2: Apply the Principle of Virtual Work \[\int_0^{\ell} \delta M_y \frac{M_y}{EI_{yy}} dz + \int_{\ell}^{2\ell} \frac{\delta M_y M_y}{EI_{yy}} dz = \varphi\]
Step 3: Solve the compatibility equation \[\frac{2M\ell}{EI_{yy}} \left[1 + \frac{3}{2}\frac{F\ell^2}{EI_{yy}} - \frac{F\ell^2}{EI_{yy}}\right] = 0\] \[M = +\frac{1}{4}F\ell\]
Step 4: Change coordinates (Cambio coordinate)

For \(0 < z < \ell\): \(\delta M_y = 1\)

For \(-\ell < \bar{z} < 0\): \(\delta M_y = 0\)

For \(0 < z < \ell\): \(M_y = M - Fz\)

Step 5: Calculate the rotation \[\frac{1}{EI_{yy}} \int_0^{\ell} \left(+\frac{1}{4}F\ell - Fz\right) dz = -\frac{1}{4}\frac{F\ell^2}{EI_{yy}}\]
Step 6: Convert to degrees \[\varphi \text{ in deg} = -\frac{1}{4}\frac{F\ell^2}{EI_{yy}} \cdot \frac{180}{\pi}\]

Final Answer: \(\varphi_A = -1.1698°\)

Problem 3: Semi-Monocoque Axial Stress

The semi-monocoque structure in the figure is loaded at point (4) by the concentrated forces \(F_x\) and \(F_y\). Compute the axial stress \(\sigma_{zz}\) of stringer (1) at a distance of \(\ell/2\) from the clamp.

Given Data:
\(\ell = 4000\, \text{mm}\)
\(F_x = 5000\, \text{N}\)
\(F_y = 1000\, \text{N}\)
\(A = 2000\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(a = 200\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 72000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
Unit for result: MPa

Solution:

Step 1: Transform forces to principal axes (rotated 45°) \[F_{x'} = -\frac{F_x}{\sqrt{2}} + \frac{F_y}{\sqrt{2}}\] \[F_{y'} = \frac{F_x + F_y}{\sqrt{2}}\]
Step 2: Calculate the distance to the neutral axis \[d = \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}}\]
Step 3: Calculate the second moment of area \[\bar{I}_{y'y'} = 2A \cdot d^2 = A \cdot a^2\]
Step 4: Calculate the bending moment at \(z = \ell/2\) \[M_{y'} = -F_{x'} \cdot \frac{\ell}{2}\]
Step 5: Apply the flexure formula \[\tilde{\sigma}_{zz} = \frac{M_{y'}}{\bar{I}_{y'y'}}(-d) = \frac{(-F_x + F_y)}{4A} \cdot \frac{\ell}{a}\]

Final Answer: \(\sigma_{zz} = -20\, \text{MPa}\)

Problem 4: Three-Cell Semi-Monocoque Shear Flux

The symmetric three cells semi-monocoque cross section in the figure is loaded by the force \(F\) in the plane of symmetry. Compute the flux \(q\).

Given Data:
\(A = 2000\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(a = 200\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(h = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 5000\, \text{N}\)
\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)

Solution:

Step 1: Identify cell structure and exploit symmetry

For 3 cells with shear flows \(q_1°\), \(q_2°\), \(q_3°\):

\[q_1° = 0 \quad \text{(for symmetry)}\] \[q_2° = q_3°\]
Step 2: Calculate open section shear flows \[q_1' = 0\] \[q_2' = q_4' = \frac{F}{2h}\]
Step 3: Apply twist compatibility

\(\dot{\theta}_1 = 0\) for symmetry

Cell area: \(\Omega_2 = \frac{ah}{2}\)

\[\dot{\theta}_2 = \frac{1}{ah}\left(-\frac{\ell_4 q_4'}{t} + \frac{(\ell_5 + \ell_4)q_2°}{t}\right)\]
Step 4: Calculate geometric lengths \[\ell_4 = \sqrt{\left(\frac{a-b}{2}\right)^2 + h^2}\] \[\ell_5 = \sqrt{\left[a - \left(\frac{a-b}{2}\right)\right]^2 + h^2}\]
Step 5: Solve for closed section shear flow \[q_2° = \frac{\ell_4 q_4'}{\ell_5 + \ell_4}\]

Final Answer: \(q_2° = 9.5636\, \text{N/mm}\)

Problem 5: Beam Displacement with Linearly Varying Load

The beam in the figure is loaded by a linearly-varying force per unit of length. Resort to a displacement-based approach and using the simplest possible polynomial approximation in order to estimate the vertical displacement in the middle of the beam.

Given Data:
\(\ell = 3000\, \text{mm}\)
\(p = 4\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(E\bar{I}_{xx} = 6 \times 10^{11}\, \text{N·mm}^2\)
Unit for result: mm

Solution:

Step 1: Assume displacement function satisfying boundary conditions \[v = c \cdot z(z - \ell)\] \[\delta v = \delta c \cdot z(z - \ell)\]
Step 2: Define the distributed load \[\bar{p} = \frac{p}{\ell} \cdot z\]
Step 3: Calculate second derivatives (curvature) \[v'' = 2c\] \[\delta v'' = 2\delta c\]
Step 4: Apply the Principle of Virtual Work \[\int_0^{\ell} \delta c \cdot 4E\bar{I} \cdot c \, dz = \int_0^{\ell} \delta c \left(z^3 - z^2 \ell\right) \frac{p}{\ell} \, dz\]
Step 5: Solve for the coefficient \(c\) \[4E\bar{I} \cdot \ell \cdot c = -\frac{1}{12}\ell^3 p\] \[c = -\frac{1}{48E\bar{I}} \ell^2 p\]
Step 6: Evaluate displacement at midspan \[v\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right) = \frac{1}{192 E\bar{I}} \ell^4 p\]

Final Answer: \(v\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right) = 2.8125\, \text{mm}\)
