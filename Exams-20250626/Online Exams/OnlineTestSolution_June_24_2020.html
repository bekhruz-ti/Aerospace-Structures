Problem 1: Cantilever Beam Displacement

Find the displacement at \(x = L/3\) for a cantilever beam with triangular distributed load.

Given Data:

  • \(L = 2000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA = 1.8 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 1.8 \cdot 10^8 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(h = 2(1 + F/10) \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution \((F = 0)\)

Step 1: Evaluate the unknown reaction force X

Using the virtual work method with a unit dummy load:

For the real system:

\[N = X + \frac{hx^2}{2L}\]

For the dummy system:

\[\delta N = 1\]

Step 2: Apply compatibility condition

\[\int_0^L \frac{N \delta N}{EA} dx = 0\]

Solving for X:

\[X = -\frac{hL}{6}\]

Step 3: Evaluate the displacement

For the region \(x \geq \frac{2}{3}L\):

\[\delta N = +1\]

Applying the virtual work integral:

\[\int_{2/3 L}^{L} \frac{N \delta N}{EA} dx = S\]

Step 4: Calculate final displacement

\[S = \frac{54X + 19Lh}{162 \, EA} \cdot L\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{S = 0.274 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 2: Torsion of Hollow Section

Determine the shear stress \(\tau\) in the panel of thickness \(t_2\).

Given Data:

  • \(A = 500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(h = 200(1 + E/10) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 150 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_1 = 1.3 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_2 = 1.6 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(M_t = 10^7 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\)

Solution \((E = 0)\)

Step 1: Apply Bredt's formula for shear flow

The shear flow in the panels is obtained from:

\[2q\Omega = M_t\]

where the enclosed area is:

\[\Omega = bh + \frac{\pi h^2}{4}\]

Step 2: Calculate shear stress

The shear stress in the panel of thickness \(t_2\):

\[\tau = \frac{q}{t_2}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\tau = 50.88 \text{ MPa}}\]

Problem 3: Shear Flow in Frame Cross-Section

Calculate the shear flow \(q_2\) in the left vertical panel.

Given Data:

  • \(A = 800(1 + A/10) \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(T = 7600 \text{ N}\)
  • \(a = 250 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 600 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_1 = 1 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_2 = 2(1 + C/10) \text{ mm}\)

Solution \((A = C = 0)\)

Step 1: Calculate section properties

Second moment of area and first moments:

\[J_{xx} = \frac{3}{2} A a^2\] \[S'_{x_3} = \frac{Aa}{2}\] \[S'_{x_4} = \frac{3}{2} Aa\] \[S'_{x_1} = S'_{x_3}\]

Step 2: Apply the shear flow equation

\[q'_3 = -T \frac{S'_{x_3}}{J_{xx}} = -\frac{T}{3a}\] \[q'_1 = q'_3\] \[q'_4 = -T \frac{S'_{x_4}}{J_{xx}} = -\frac{T}{a}\]

Step 3: Apply torsional equivalence condition

\[2\Omega_c q^* + 2q'_3 \Omega_3 + 2q'_4 \Omega_4 = 0\]

where:

\[\Omega_c = ab \quad ; \quad \Omega_3 = \Omega_4 = \frac{ab}{2}\]

Therefore:

\[2q^* + q'_3 + q'_4 = 0\]

Step 4: Solve for closed shear flow

\[q^* = \frac{2}{3} \frac{T}{a} = 20.27 \text{ N/mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{q_2 = q^* = 20.27 \text{ N/mm}}\]

Problem 4: Beam with Spring Support - Ritz Method

Using the Ritz method and a 1-DOF polynomial approximation, determine the vertical displacement in correspondence of the spring.

Given Data:

  • \(L = 750 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 1.25 \cdot 10^8 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(K = 50(1 + A/10) \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(q = 2\)

Solution \((A = 0)\)

Step 1: Assume displacement shape function

Choose a polynomial that satisfies boundary conditions at the fixed end:

\[W = (x^3 - Lx^2)q_1\]

Step 2: Apply the principle of virtual work

\[\int_0^L \delta W_{,xx} \, EJ \, W_{,xx} \, dx + \delta w(L/2) \, k \, w(L/2) = \int_0^L \delta W \cdot q \frac{x}{L} \, dx\]

Step 3: Solve the equilibrium equation

\[\left(4EJL^3 + \frac{K}{64} L^6\right) q_1 = -\frac{1}{20} q L^4\]

Step 4: Calculate displacement at spring location

\[w(L/2) = -\frac{L^3}{8} q_1\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{w(L/2) = 4.768 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 5: L-Frame Analysis - Ritz Method

Use the Ritz method and the simplest polynomial approximation for evaluating the displacement \(S\) at the junction.

Given Data:

  • \(L = 730 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA_1 = 1.15 \cdot 10^6 (1 + F/10) \text{ N}\)
  • \(EA_2 = 3 \cdot 10^5 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 1.8 \cdot 10^7 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(P = 2500(1 + A/10) \text{ N}\)

Solution \((A = F = 0)\)

Step 1: Identify uncoupled behavior

Due to uncoupling between axial and bending, we can consider only the axial response of beam 1 and the bending one of beam 2.

Step 2: Assume displacement functions

For axial displacement of beam 1:

\[u_1 = q_1 x\]

For transverse displacement of beam 2:

\[W_2 = q_2 x^2\]

Step 3: Apply compatibility at junction

The compatibility condition requires:

\[u_1(L) = W_2(L) \Rightarrow q_1 = q_2 L\]

Therefore:

\[u_1 = q_2 x_1 L\] \[W_2 = q_2 x_2^2\]

Step 4: Apply virtual work principle

\[\int_0^L \delta u_{1,x} \, EA_1 \, u_{1,x} \, dx_1 + \int_0^L \delta W_{2,xx} \, EJ \, W_{2,xx} \, dx_2 = \delta u_1(L) \, P_x\]

This leads to:

\[(EA_1 L^3 + 4 EJL) q_2 = P_x L^2\]

Step 5: Calculate displacement

\[S = W_2(L) = q_2 L^2\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{S = 1.12 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 6: Ribbed Panel Structure

Model Rib #2 as a beam, and evaluate the bending moment at \(x = a/2\) as \(M/M_{ref}\).

Given Data:

  • \(a = 700 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(c = 100 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(L = 2000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 0.6 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 700 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(P = 2000 \text{ N}\)
  • \(q = 2(1 + F/10) \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(M_{ref} = 9.8 \cdot 10^5 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\)

Solution \((F = 0)\)

Step 1: Determine equilibrating shear flows on Rib #2

The equilibrating shear flows are obtained as:

  • Top edge: \(qa\) (upward)
  • Left and right edges: \(\frac{qa}{2b}\) (downward)

Step 2: Model as simply supported beam

The rib is modeled as a beam with:

  • Uniformly distributed load \(q\) (upward)
  • Support reactions \(\frac{qa}{2}\) at each end (downward)

Step 3: Calculate bending moment distribution

\[M(x) = \frac{qa}{2} \cdot x - q \cdot \frac{x^2}{2}\]

Step 4: Evaluate moment at midspan

At \(x = a/2\):

\[\frac{M(a/2)}{M_{ref}}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\frac{M(a/2)}{M_{ref}} = 0.125}\]
