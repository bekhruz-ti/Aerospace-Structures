Problem 1: Beam with Spring Support and Distributed Load

Consider the structure in the figure. It is loaded with a distributed load, which is introduced continuously. The section is square with dimension a; all the panels have thickness t, while the lumped area of the stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is A. The Young and shear moduli of the material are E and G, respectively. Determine the vertical displacement v in correspondence of the spring by taking shear deformability effects into account. For this purpose, refer to a semi-monocoque model of the section. (Unit for result: mm)

Given Data (solution for B = 0):

  • \(\ell = 200\) mm
  • \(a = 40\) mm
  • \(t = 0.2\) mm
  • \(A = 400\) mm²
  • \(G = 28000\) MPa
  • \(E = 72000\) MPa
  • \(k = 2000 \times (1 + B/10)\) N/mm
  • \(q = 100\) N/mm
  • \(W = 4.0 \times 10^6\) N·mm

Solution

Step 1: Beam Section Properties

The bending stiffness is:

\[EJ = E \cdot 2A \left(\frac{a}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 = EA \cdot a^2\]

For the shear stiffness \(GA^*\): due to the symmetry of the section, the shear flows due to the shear force \(T\) (applied at the shear center) are:

From equilibrium:

\[q \cdot \frac{a}{\sqrt{2}} \cdot 4 = T\]

So:

\[q = \frac{T}{4a}\sqrt{2}\]

From which the shear stiffness is:

\[GA^* = G \frac{T^2}{\sum_i q_i^2 l_i / t_i} = G \frac{T^2}{4q^2 a/t} = G \cdot 2at\]

Step 2: Real System Analysis

The internal forces in the real system are:

\[T = -\left(X + \frac{q\ell}{2}\right)\] \[M = -W - \frac{q\ell^2}{6} - \left(X + \frac{q\ell}{2}\right)x\]

Step 3: Dummy System

For the dummy system with unit load at the spring location:

\[\delta T = -1\] \[\delta M = -x\]

Step 4: Application of PCVW (Principle of Complementary Virtual Work)

\[\int_0^{2\ell} \left(\delta M \frac{M}{EJ} + \delta T \frac{T}{GA^*}\right) dx + \frac{X}{k} = 0\]

By integrating:

\[\frac{\ell^2}{3EJ}\left(5q\ell^2 + 8X\ell + 6W\right) + \frac{\ell}{GA^*}\left(q\ell + 2X\right) + \frac{X}{k} = 0\]

Solving for X:

\[\left(\frac{\ell^3}{3EJ} \cdot 8 + \frac{\ell}{GA^*} \cdot 2 + \frac{1}{k}\right)X = -\frac{\ell^2}{3EJ}\left(5q\ell^2 + 6W\right) - \frac{\ell}{GA^*}q\ell\]

Therefore:

\[X = -12615 \text{ N}\]

Step 5: Calculate Displacement

\[S = \frac{X}{k} = -5.84 \text{ mm}\]

(S positive in the downward direction)

Final Answer: \[\boxed{v = 5.84 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 2: Statically Indeterminate Rectangular Frame

The structure in the figure is composed of three beams with same length L, bending stiffness EJ and axial stiffness EA. Shear deformability is negligible. Determine the reaction force H. (Unit for result: N)

Given Data (solution for D = 0):

  • \(\ell = 1000 \times (1 + D/10)\) mm
  • \(EJ = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\) N·mm²
  • \(EA = 1.0 \times 10^{6}\) N
  • \(P = 500\) N

Solution

Step 1: Real System Analysis

Beam 1 (Right vertical member):

\[N = -V\] \[M = -Xx_1\]

Beam 2 (Horizontal member):

\[N = X\] \[M = -X\ell - Vx_2\]

Step 2: Beam 3 Analysis

Beam 3 (Left vertical member):

\[N = V\] \[M = (P+X)x_3 - (V+X)\ell\]

Step 3: Dummy System

Apply unit horizontal force at right support with \(V = 0\):

Beam 1: \(\delta N = 0\), \(\delta M = -x_1\)

Beam 2: \(\delta N = 1\), \(\delta M = -\ell\)

Beam 3: \(\delta N = 0\), \(\delta M = -\ell + x_3\)

Step 4: Application of PCVW

\[\int_0^\ell \delta N \frac{N}{EA} dx_2 + \sum_{i=1}^{3} \int_0^\ell \delta M \frac{M}{EJ} dx_i = 0\]

Which leads to:

\[\frac{X\ell}{EA} + \frac{5}{3EJ}\ell^3 X + \frac{5}{6EJ}\ell^3 P = 0\]

Rearranging:

\[X\left(\frac{\ell}{EA} + \frac{5\ell^3}{3EJ}\right) = -\frac{5}{6EJ}\ell^3 P\]

Solving:

\[X = -5 \frac{EA\ell^2}{6EJ + 10EA\ell^2} P\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{X = H = -156.25 \text{ N}}\]

Problem 3: Two Beams Hinged Through Inextensible Joint (Ritz Method)

Two beams are hinged through an inextensible joint (see figure). Determine the vertical displacement w in correspondence of the applied force F using the Euler-Bernoulli beam model in conjunction with the Ritz method. For this purpose, adopt a polynomial expansion of the displacement field where: the displacement is expanded up to the third order for the first beam; the displacement is expanded up to the second order for the second beam. (Unit for result: mm)

Given Data (solution for E = 0):

  • \(\ell = 500\) mm
  • \(EJ = 1.0 \times 10^{10}\) N·mm²
  • \(k = 80 \times (1 + E/10)\) N/mm
  • \(k_t = 2.0 \times 10^{7}\) N·mm
  • \(F = 2500\) N

Solution

Step 1: Displacement Field Expansion

The displacement field is expanded as:

\[w_1 = C_1 \left(\frac{x_1}{\ell}\right)^2 + C_2 \left(\frac{x_1}{\ell}\right)^3\] \[w_2 = C_3 \left(\frac{x_2}{\ell}\right)^2\]

The expansions exclude constant and linear terms to satisfy essential boundary conditions:

\[\begin{cases} w_1(0) = 0 \\ w_2(0) = 0 \end{cases}\]

The additional essential condition from the joint is:

\[w_1(\ell) = w_2(\ell) = 0\]

Therefore:

\[C_1 + C_2 = C_3\]

Substituting:

\[w_1 = C_1 \left(\frac{x_1}{\ell}\right)^2 + C_2 \left(\frac{x_1}{\ell}\right)^3\] \[w_2 = (C_1 + C_2) \left(\frac{x_2}{\ell}\right)^2\]

Step 2: Derivatives

\[w_1' = C_1 \frac{2x_1}{\ell^2} + C_2 \frac{3x_1^2}{\ell^3}\] \[w_2' = (C_1 + C_2) \frac{2x_2}{\ell^2}\] \[w_1'' = C_1 \frac{2}{\ell^2} + C_2 \frac{6x_1}{\ell^3}\] \[w_2'' = (C_1 + C_2) \frac{2}{\ell^2}\]

Step 3: Principle of Virtual Work

The PVW reads:

\[\int_0^\ell \delta w_1'' \, EJ \, w_1'' \, dx_1 + \int_0^\ell \delta w_2'' \, 2EJ \, w_2'' \, dx_2 + \delta w_2(\ell) \, k \, w_2(\ell) + \delta w_1'(\ell) \, k_t \, w_1'(\ell) = \delta w_2(\ell) \, F\]

Defining non-dimensional parameters:

\[\hat{k} = \frac{k\ell^3}{EJ} \quad ; \quad \hat{k}_t = \frac{k_t \ell}{EJ}\]

The linear system to be solved is:

\[\begin{bmatrix} \hat{k} + 12 + 4\hat{k}_t & \hat{k} + 14 + 4\hat{k}_t \\ \hat{k} + 14 + 4\hat{k}_t & \hat{k} + 20 + 4\hat{k}_t \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} C_1 \\ C_2 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} \frac{F\ell^3}{EJ}\]

Step 4: Numerical Solution

Substituting values:

\[\begin{bmatrix} 17.00 & 19.00 \\ 19.00 & 25.00 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} C_1 \\ C_2 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 31.25 \\ 31.25 \end{Bmatrix}\]

From which:

\[C_1 = 2.93 \text{ mm}\] \[C_2 = -0.98 \text{ mm}\]

The displacement at \(x_2 = \ell\) is:

\[w_2 = (C_1 + C_2) \left(\frac{\ell}{\ell}\right)^2 = C_1 + C_2\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{w = 1.95 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 4: Torsional Stiffness of Three-Cell Section

Determine the torsional stiffness GJ of the three-cell section in the figure. All the panels have thickness t, while the lumped area of the stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is A. The shear modulus of the material is G. To solve the exercise, use a semi-monocoque approximation of the section. Report the result as GJ / GJref, where GJref is reference value available in the data. (Unit for result: adim)

Given Data (solution for B = 0):

  • \(a = 100 \times (1 + B/10)\) mm
  • \(t = 1.5\) mm
  • \(A = 400\) mm²
  • \(G = 27000\) MPa
  • \(GJ_{ref} = 1.0 \times 10^{10}\) N·mm²

Solution

Step 1: Shear Flow Analysis

Moment equivalence:

\[2\Omega (q_1^* + q_2^* + q_3^*) = M_t \quad \text{with } \Omega = a^2\]

Step 2: Compatibility Equations

\[2G\Omega t \theta_1' = 4a q_1^* - a q_2^*\] \[2G\Omega t \theta_2' = 4a q_2^* - a q_1^* - a q_3^*\] \[2G\Omega t \theta_3' = 4a q_3^* - a q_2^*\]

From equal twist rates:

\(\theta_1' = \theta_2'\) leads to: \(5q_1^* - 5q_2^* + q_3^* = 0\)

\(\theta_1' = \theta_3'\) leads to: \(q_1^* = q_3^*\) (symmetry)

Step 3: Solve for Shear Flows

From the system of equations:

\[\begin{cases} 6q_1^* - 5q_2^* = 0 \\ 2q_1^* + q_2^* = \frac{M_t}{2\Omega} \end{cases}\]

Solving:

\[q_1^* = \frac{5}{16} \frac{M_t}{2\Omega}\] \[q_2^* = \frac{3}{8} \frac{M_t}{2\Omega}\]

Step 4: Calculate Torsional Stiffness

Recalling that \(M_t = GJ\theta'\) and \(\theta' = \theta_1'\):

\[2G\Omega t \theta_1' = 4a q_1^* - a q_2^*\] \[\theta_1' = \frac{1}{2G\Omega t} \frac{M_t a}{2\Omega} \left(\frac{5}{4} - \frac{3}{8}\right) = \frac{7}{32} \frac{M_t}{Ga^3 t}\]

Therefore:

\[GJ = \frac{M_t}{\theta_1'} = G \frac{32}{7} a^3 t = 1.85 \times 10^{11} \text{ N·mm}^2\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{GJ/GJ_{ref} = 18.51}\]

Problem 5: Shear Stress in Thin-Walled Beam with Pressure Load

The thin-walled beam in the figure is loaded with a uniform pressure p. The section is modeled with a semi-monocoque approximation, where the lumped stringers (including the contribution of the panels) have area A, and the thickness of the panels is t. Assuming that the load is introduced in correspondence of the ribs, determine the shear stress in the panel indicated in the figure at the section A. (Unit for result: MPa)

Given Data (solution for D = 0):

  • \(\ell = 2000 \times (1 + D/10)\) mm
  • \(a = 70\) mm
  • \(b = 100\) mm
  • \(c = 30\) mm
  • \(t = 1.2\) mm
  • \(A = 400\) mm²
  • \(p = 1.0 \times 10^{-2}\) N/mm

Solution

Step 1: Convert Distributed Load to Discrete Forces

The equivalent discrete force at each rib:

\[F = pa \cdot \frac{\ell}{6} = 233.33 \text{ N}\]

Step 2: Calculate Shear Force at Section A

The shear force at section A is:

\[T = \frac{5}{2} F = 583.33 \text{ N}\]

Due to the symmetry of the section, the torsional moment with respect to the shear center is null.

Step 3: Calculate Shear Flow

The shear flows are readily available by exploiting the symmetry of the section:

\[q = \frac{T}{2c} = 9.72 \text{ N/mm}\]

Step 4: Calculate Shear Stress

\[\tau = \frac{q}{t}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\tau = 8.10 \text{ MPa}}\]

Problem 6: Rotation Angle of Cantilever Thin-Walled Beam

The thin-walled beam in the figure is loaded with a force per unit length q. The lumped stringers, including the contribution of the panels, have area A, and the thickness of the panels is t. The shear modulus of the material is G. Assume that the load is introduced in correspondence of the ribs. By using a semi-monocoque approximation, determine the rotation of the section at x = l / 3. Report the absolute value of the rotation angle expressed in degrees. (Unit for result: deg - absolute value)

Given Data (solution for E = 0):

  • \(\ell = 2500\) mm
  • \(a = 150 \times (1 + E/10)\) mm
  • \(A = 500\) mm²
  • \(t = 1.2\) mm
  • \(G = 27000\) MPa
  • \(q = 100\) N/mm

Solution

Step 1: Equivalent Rib Forces

This is a 1-cell section, so Bredt's formula can be applied.

Torsional constant:

\[J = \frac{4\Omega^2}{\oint \frac{1}{t} d\Gamma} = \frac{4a^4 t}{4a} = a^3 t\]

Step 2: Torsion Analysis

The torsion \(\theta'\) is evaluated by referring to the section's shear center, which is readily available due to the section's symmetry.

The shear force at the top edge creates a torsional moment about the shear center:

\[M_t = T\frac{a}{2}\]

So:

\[\theta' = \frac{M_t}{GJ} = \frac{Ta}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{Ga^3 t} = \frac{T}{2Ga^2 t}\]

Step 3: Calculate Twist Rates for Each Bay

The discrete force is:

\[F = \frac{q\ell}{6}\]

For the first bay (closest to x = ℓ/3):

\[T_1 = \left(5 + \frac{1}{2}\right)F\]

For the second bay:

\[T_2 = \left(4 + \frac{1}{2}\right)F\]

Twist rates:

\[\theta_1' = \frac{T_1}{2Ga^2 t}\] \[\theta_2' = \frac{T_2}{2Ga^2 t}\]

Step 4: Total Rotation

The rotation is accumulated over two bays:

\[\theta = \theta_1' \cdot \frac{\ell}{6} + \theta_2' \cdot \frac{\ell}{6}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta = 6.02°}\]
