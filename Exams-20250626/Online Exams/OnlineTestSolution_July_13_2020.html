Problem 1: Cantilever Beam Displacement Optimization

Find the minimum value \(EJ/EJ_0\) such that the vertical displacement at \(x = L/2\) is smaller than \(W_{max}\).

Given Data:

  • \(L = 1200 \left(1 + B/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ_0 = 6 \cdot 10^7 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(q = 1.0 \cdot \left(1 + C/10\right) \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(W_{max} = 5.0 \text{ mm}\)

Solution \((B = C = 0)\)

Step 1: Find the unknown reaction force X

Using the virtual work method with real and dummy load systems:

Step 2: Express bending moments

Real system bending moment:

\[M_x = -Xz - \frac{qz^2}{2}\]

Dummy system bending moment:

\[\delta M_x = -z\]

Step 3: Apply compatibility condition

\[\int_0^L \frac{M_x \delta M_x}{EJ} \, dz = 0\]

Solving for X:

\[X = -\frac{3}{8} qL\]

Step 4: Evaluate the displacement at x = L/2

Dummy moment for displacement calculation:

\[\delta M_x = -z + \frac{L}{2}\]

Applying the displacement integral:

\[\int_{L/2}^{L} \frac{\delta M_x M_x}{EJ} \, dz = S = W_{max}\]

Step 5: Solve for required flexural rigidity

From the displacement condition:

\[EJ = \frac{1}{W_{max}} \int_{L/2}^{L} \delta M_x M_x \, dz\]
Final Expression for Stiffness Ratio: \[\boxed{\frac{EJ}{EJ_0} = \frac{1}{EJ_0} \cdot \frac{L^3 (40X + 17qL)}{384 \, W_{max}}}\]

Problem 2: Torsional Stiffness Ratio of Rectangular Frame Sections

Calculate the ratio between the torsional stiffness of the two sections \(GJ_2 / GJ_1\).

Given Data:

  • \(a = 200 \left(1 + F/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 100 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 500 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(t = 1 \text{ mm}\)

Solution

Analysis

Both sections have the same enclosed area and wall thickness. The torsional stiffness for thin-walled closed sections depends on the enclosed area and perimeter, which remain equivalent when dimensions are swapped.

Final Answer: \[\boxed{\frac{GJ_2}{GJ_1} = 1.0}\]

Problem 3: Truss Frame with Spring Support

Determine the vertical displacement in correspondence of the spring. (Refer to the x axis in the figure)

Given Data:

  • \(A = 250 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(a = 150 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 0.6 \left(1 + B/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(L = 1200 \left(1 + F/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(k = 100 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(E = 72000 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(P = 1700 \text{ N}\)

Solution \((B = F = 0)\)

Step 1: Evaluate section properties using symmetry

Shear:

\[A^* = 2at\] \[GA^* = 2atG\]

Torsional:

\[GJ = Ga^3t\]

Bending:

\[EJ = EAa^2\]

Step 2: Set up real and dummy load systems

Evaluate the unknown reaction force \(X\) at the spring location.

Real System

  • \(T = P + X\)
  • \(M = (P + X)z\)
  • \(M_t = (P - X)\frac{a}{2}\)

Dummy System

  • \(\delta T = 1\)
  • \(\delta M = z\)
  • \(\delta M_t = -\frac{a}{2}\)

Step 3: Apply virtual work equation (compatibility)

\[\int_0^L \left( \frac{\delta T \cdot T}{GA^*} + \frac{\delta M \cdot M}{EJ} + \frac{\delta M_t \cdot M_t}{GJ} \right) dz + \frac{X}{k} = 0\]

Step 4: Solve for reaction force X

\[X = P \cdot \frac{\dfrac{a^2}{4GJ} - \dfrac{L^2}{3EJ} - \dfrac{1}{6A^*}}{\dfrac{1}{GA^*} + \dfrac{L^2}{3EJ} + \dfrac{a^2}{4GJ} + \dfrac{1}{kL}} = -222.55 \text{ N}\]
Final Answer - Vertical Displacement: \[\boxed{S = \frac{X}{k} = -2.23 \text{ mm}}\]

(Negative sign indicates upward movement)

Problem 4: Strain Energy Using Ritz Method (2-DOF)

Using the Ritz method and a 2-DOF polynomial approximation, determine the strain energy.

Given Data:

  • \(L = 2700 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA_0 = 2.8 \cdot 10^5 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EA_1 = 6.5 \cdot 10^5 \text{ N}\)
  • \(k = 300 \left(1 + B/10\right) \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(h = 2\left(1 + F/10\right) \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution \((B = F = 0)\)

Step 1: Assume displacement field

\[u(x) = q_1 x + q_2 x^2\]

Step 2: Write virtual work equation

\[\int_0^L \delta u_{,x} \, EA(x) \, u_{,x} \, dx + \delta u(L) \, k \, u(L) = \int_0^L \delta u \, h \, dx\]

With axial stiffness variation:

\[EA(x) = \alpha + \beta x\]

where:

\[\alpha = EA_0 \quad ; \quad \beta = \frac{EA_1 - EA_0}{L}\]

Step 3: Compute stiffness matrix coefficients

\[k_{11} = \alpha L + \frac{\beta L^2}{2} + kL^2\] \[k_{12} = k_{21} = \alpha L^2 + \frac{2}{3} \beta L^3 + kL^3\] \[k_{22} = \frac{4}{3} \alpha L^3 + \beta L^4 + kL^4\]

Step 4: Compute force vector coefficients

\[f_1 = \frac{L^2}{2} h\] \[f_2 = \frac{L^3}{3} h\]

Step 5: Solve the system \(\underline{K} \, \underline{q} = \underline{f}\)

\[q_1 = 9.23 \cdot 10^{-3}\] \[q_2 = -2.51 \cdot 10^{-6}\]
Final Answer - Strain Energy: \[\boxed{U = \frac{1}{2} \underline{q}^T \underline{K} \, \underline{q} = 17151 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}}\]

Problem 5: Strain Energy Using Ritz Method (1-DOF Torsion)

Using the Ritz method and a 1-DOF polynomial approximation, determine the strain energy. (\(GJ\) varies linearly from \(GJ_0\) at \(x = 0\) to \(GJ_1\) at \(x = L_2\))

Given Data:

  • \(L_1 = 180 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(L_2 = 1800 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(GJ_0 = 1.1 \cdot 10^8 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ_1 = 3.3 \cdot 10^8 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(P = 50 \left(1 + D/10\right) \text{ N}\)

Solution \((D = 0)\)

Step 1: Assume rotation field

\[\theta = q_1 x\]

Step 2: Define torsional stiffness variation

\[GJ(x) = \alpha + \beta x\]

where:

\[\alpha = GJ_0 \quad ; \quad \beta = \frac{GJ_1 - GJ_0}{L_2}\]

Step 3: Write virtual work equation

\[\int_0^{L_2} \delta\theta' \, GJ(x) \, \theta' \, dx = \delta\theta(L_2) \, P L_1\]

Step 4: Substitute and simplify

\[\left(\alpha L_2 + \frac{\beta L_2^2}{2}\right) q_1 = P L_1 L_2\]

Step 5: Solve for generalized coordinate

\[q_1 = 4.091 \cdot 10^{-5}\]
Final Answer - Strain Energy: \[\boxed{U = \frac{1}{2} \left(\alpha L_2 + \frac{\beta L_2^2}{2}\right) q_1^2 = 331.36 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}}\]

Problem 6: Axial Stress in Stringer

Evaluate the axial stress in stringer #1 at \(x = 0\).

Given Data:

  • \(a = 180 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 350 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(L = 1600 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(P = 1300 \left(1 + F/10\right) \text{ N}\)

Solution \((A = F = 0)\)

Step 1: Calculate second moment of area

\[J_{xx} = Aa^2\]

Step 2: Determine bending moment at x = 0

The bending moment at \(x = 0\) is:

\[M = PL\]

Step 3: Apply bending stress formula

The distance from neutral axis to stringer #1 includes the geometric factor:

\[\sigma = \frac{M}{J_{xx}} \cdot \frac{a\sqrt{2}}{2}\]
Final Answer - Axial Stress: \[\boxed{\sigma = 23.35 \text{ MPa}}\]
