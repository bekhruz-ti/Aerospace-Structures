Exercise 1: Two-Segment Cantilever Beam

Determine the value of the concentrated force \(X\) such that the vertical displacement at the corresponding point of application is zero.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell_1 = 1000 \left(1 + \frac{8}{10}\right) \text{ mm} = 1800 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\ell_2 = 1500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(q = 1 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(P = 1000 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EA_1 = 1 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EA_2 = 1 \cdot 10^7 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ_1 = 1 \cdot 10^{12} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(EJ_2 = 1 \cdot 10^{12} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)

Solution

Step 1: Real System Analysis

Beam 1 (Right segment):

\[N_1 = P\] \[M_1 = -\frac{qx_1^2}{2}\]

Beam 2 (Left segment):

\[N_2 = P\] \[M_2 = -\frac{q\ell_1^2}{2} - (X + q\ell_1)x_2\]

Step 2: Dummy System Analysis

Beam 2:

\[\delta M = -x_2\]

Step 3: Application of PCVW

By application of the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^{\ell_2} \frac{M \cdot \delta M}{EJ_2} dx_2 = 0\]

Substituting the moment expressions and solving for \(X\):

\[X = -\left(q\ell_1 + \frac{3}{4} q \frac{\ell_1^2}{\ell_2}\right)\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{X = -1500 \text{ N}}\]

(The negative sign indicates the force acts downward, opposite to the assumed direction)

Exercise 4: Cantilever Beam with Spring Support

Determine the vertical displacement \(w\) in correspondence of the spring.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 10^{12} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(k = 1500 \left(1 + \frac{8}{10}\right) \text{ N/mm} = 2700 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(q = 15 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Real System Analysis

With coordinate \(x\) pointing left from the spring location:

\[M = -Yx - \frac{qx^2}{2}\]

Step 2: Dummy System Analysis

For the dummy system with unit load at spring location:

\[\delta M = -x\]

Step 3: Application of PCVW

By application of the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M \cdot M}{EJ} dx + \frac{Y}{k} = 0\]

Evaluating the integral:

\[\frac{\ell^3}{24EJ}\left(8Y + 3q\ell\right) + \frac{Y}{k} = 0\]

Step 4: Solve for Spring Force Y

\[Y = -\frac{3kq\ell^4}{8k\ell^3 + 24EJ} = -1875 \text{ N}\]

Step 5: Calculate Displacement

\[w = -\frac{Y}{k} = \frac{3q\ell^4}{8k\ell^3 + 24EJ}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{Y = -1875 \text{ N}}\] \[\boxed{w = 1.25 \text{ mm}}\]

Exercise 5: Simply Supported Beam with Torsional Spring

Determine the strain energy stored in the spring of torsional stiffness \(K_t\).

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 10^{12} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(K_t = 10^9 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\)
  • \(q = 1 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Real System Analysis

Equilibrium equations:

\[\begin{cases} V + W = q\ell & \Rightarrow & V = q\ell - W \\ X = \frac{q\ell^2}{2} - W\ell & \Rightarrow & W = \frac{q\ell}{2} - \frac{X}{\ell} \end{cases}\]

Therefore:

\[V = q\ell - \frac{q\ell}{2} + \frac{X}{\ell} = \frac{q\ell}{2} + \frac{X}{\ell}\]

Bending moment in the beam:

\[M = -X + Vx - \frac{qx^2}{2}\]

Step 2: Dummy System Analysis

For the dummy system with unit moment at the left support:

\[\delta M = -1 + \frac{x}{\ell}\]

Step 3: Application of PCVW

By application of the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M \cdot M}{EJ} dx + \frac{X}{K_t} = 0\]

Step 4: Solve for Spring Moment X

\[X = K_t \frac{q\ell^3}{24EJ + 8K_t\ell} = 31250 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\]

Step 5: Calculate Strain Energy

\[U = \frac{1}{2} \frac{X^2}{K_t}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{X = 31250 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}}\] \[\boxed{U = 0.4883 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}}\]

Exercise 9: Multi-Segment System with Rigid Links

Determine the vertical displacement at point A using the simplest polynomial approximation and the Ritz method.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell_1 = 1300 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\ell_2 = 500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\ell_3 = 700 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 10^{12} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ_0 = 2 \cdot 10^{11} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ_1 = 5 \cdot 10^{11} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(k = 500 \left(1 + \frac{4}{10}\right) \text{ N/mm} = 700 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(P = 7000 \text{ N}\)

Variable torsional rigidity: \(GJ(x) = GJ_0 + GJ_1 \frac{x}{\ell_1}\)

Solution

Step 1: Identify Essential Boundary Conditions

Essential boundary conditions at \(x = 0\) (fixed end):

\[\theta(0) = 0, \quad w(0) = 0, \quad w'_x(0) = 0\]

Step 2: Select Trial Functions

The simplest polynomial trial functions satisfying the essential boundary conditions are:

\[\theta(x) = c_1 \left(\frac{x}{\ell_1}\right) \quad \Rightarrow \quad \theta' = \frac{c_1}{\ell_1}\] \[w(x) = d_1 \left(\frac{x}{\ell_1}\right)^2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad w'' = \frac{2d_1}{\ell_1^2}\]

Step 3: Principle of Virtual Works

The internal virtual work:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^{\ell_1} \left( \delta w'' \cdot EJ \cdot w'' + \delta \theta' \cdot GJ(x) \cdot \theta' \right) dx + \delta \left( w(\ell_1) - \theta(\ell_1) \ell_2 \right) k \left( w(\ell_1) - \theta(\ell_1) \ell_2 \right)\]

The external virtual work:

\[\delta W_e = \delta \left( w(\ell_1) + \theta(\ell_1) \ell_3 \right) P\]

Step 4: Assemble Stiffness Matrix and Force Vector

Stiffness matrix:

\[\underline{\underline{K}} = \begin{bmatrix} k\ell_2^2 + \frac{2GJ_0 + GJ_1}{2\ell_1} & -k\ell_2 \\ -k\ell_2 & k + \frac{4EJ}{\ell_1^3} \end{bmatrix}\]

Force vector:

\[\underline{F} = \begin{Bmatrix} \ell_3 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} P\]

System of equations:

\[\underline{\underline{K}} \begin{Bmatrix} c_1 \\ d_1 \end{Bmatrix} = \underline{F}\]

Step 5: Solve the System

Solving the linear system yields:

\[c_1 = 0.0127 \text{ rad}\] \[d_1 = 4.3875 \text{ mm}\]

Step 6: Calculate Displacement at Point A

The vertical displacement at \(x = \ell_1\) (point A):

\[w(\ell_1) = d_1 \left(\frac{\ell_1}{\ell_1}\right)^2 = d_1\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{c_1 = 0.0127 \text{ rad}}\] \[\boxed{d_1 = 4.3875 \text{ mm}}\] \[\boxed{w(\ell_1) = 4.3875 \text{ mm}}\]

Exercise 14: Simply Supported Beam - Ritz Approximation

Determine the vertical displacement at the middle using a Ritz approximation with 1 trigonometric trial function.

Recall the integration rule:

\[\int_0^{a} \left( \sin \frac{\pi x}{a} \right)^3 dx = \frac{4a}{3\pi}\]

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1700 \left( 1 + \frac{C}{10} \right) \text{ mm}\) (where C is a student parameter)
  • \(EJ_0 = 10^{12} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(q = 15 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Identify Boundary Conditions

Essential boundary conditions:

\[w(0) = 0, \quad w(\ell) = 0\]

Natural boundary conditions:

\[w''(0) = 0, \quad w''(\ell) = 0\]

Step 2: Select Trial Function

A sine-type expansion satisfies both essential and natural conditions. Assuming a single-DOF approximation:

\[w(x) = a \sin \frac{\pi x}{\ell}\]

Step 3: Application of PVW

By application of the Principle of Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \delta w'' \cdot EJ(x) \cdot w'' dx = \int_0^{\ell} \delta w \cdot q \, dx\]

This leads to:

\[ka = f\]

Step 4: Calculate Stiffness and Force

Generalized stiffness:

\[k = \frac{4}{3} EJ_0 \left( \frac{\pi}{\ell} \right)^3\]

Generalized force:

\[f = q \frac{2\ell}{\pi}\]

Step 5: Solve for Amplitude

\[a = \frac{f}{k} = 1.9292 \text{ mm}\]

Step 6: Calculate Midspan Displacement

\[w_{mid} = a \sin \frac{\pi x}{\ell} \bigg|_{x = \ell/2} = a \sin \frac{\pi}{2} = a\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{a = 1.9292 \text{ mm}}\] \[\boxed{w_{mid} = 1.9292 \text{ mm}}\]

Exercise 22: Cantilever Beam with Hollow Rectangular Cross-Section

Determine dimension \(A\) such that the maximum displacement is \(u = 3 \text{ mm}\).

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 3000 \left(1 + \frac{A}{10}\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(a = 200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 150 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 1.2 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(p = 1.6 \cdot 10^{-3} \text{ N/mm}^2\)
  • \(E = 72000 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(G = 27000 \text{ MPa}\)

Solution

Step 1: Convert Distributed Pressure to Line Load

\[q = pa\]

Step 2: Real System Internal Forces

With coordinate \(x\) pointing left from the free end:

\[T = -qx\] \[M = -\frac{qx^2}{2}\]

Step 3: Dummy System (Unit Load at Free End)

\[\delta T = -1\] \[\delta M = -x\]

Step 4: Application of PCVW

By application of the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \left( \frac{\delta M \cdot M}{EJ} + \frac{\delta T \cdot T}{GA^*} \right) dx = u\]

This yields the displacement expression:

\[u = \frac{q\ell^4}{8} \frac{1}{EJ} + \frac{q\ell^2}{2} \frac{1}{GA^*}\]

Step 5: Calculate Section Properties

Flexural rigidity:

\[EJ = EA \left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2 \cdot 4 = EAb^2\]

Shear rigidity:

\[GA^* = G \frac{T^2}{\sum_i q_i^2 \ell_i / t_i} = G \cdot 2bt\]

(Note: \(2bt\) is the total area of the vertical webs)

Step 6: Solve for Cross-Sectional Area A

Substituting into the displacement equation:

\[u = \frac{q\ell^4}{8} \frac{1}{EAb^2} + \frac{q\ell^2}{2} \frac{1}{G \cdot 2bt}\]

Rearranging to solve for \(A\):

\[\frac{q\ell^4}{8} \frac{1}{EAb^2} = u - \frac{q\ell^2}{4Gbt}\] \[A = \frac{q\ell^4}{8Eb^2} \cdot \frac{1}{\left(u - \frac{q\ell^2}{4Gbt}\right)}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{A = 701.3 \text{ mm}^2}\]
