Problem 1: L-Shaped Frame Analysis

Determine the axial force at \(x = L/2\)

Given Data:

  • \(L = 1200 \left(1 + D/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA_1 = 2.0 \cdot 10^4 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EA_2 = 1.0 \cdot 10^4 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EA_3 = 2.9 \cdot 10^4 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 1.5 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(P = 1200 \left(1 + F/10\right) \text{ N}\)

Solution (D = F = 0)

Step 1: Define coordinate systems

Two systems are established:

  • Real system: L-shaped frame with force \(P\) applied upward at junction. Local coordinates \((z_1, y_1)\) for vertical member and \((z_2, y_2)\) for horizontal member.
  • Dummy system: Same geometry with unit load applied upward in the direction of desired displacement.

Step 2: Define internal forces for beam 1 (vertical member)

\[N = -X \qquad \delta N = -1 \qquad \text{(beam 1)}\]

Step 3: Define internal moments for beam 2 (horizontal member)

\[M_x = -(P + X) z_2 \qquad \delta M_x = -z_2 \qquad \text{(beam 2)}\]

Step 4: Apply virtual work equation (compatibility)

\[\int_0^L \frac{N \cdot \delta N}{EA_3} \, dz_1 + \int_0^L \frac{M_x \cdot \delta M_x}{EJ} \, dz_2 = 0\]

Step 5: Solve for redundant force X

\[X = -\frac{PL^2/3}{\dfrac{EJ}{EA_3} + \dfrac{L^2}{3}} = -577.59 \text{ N}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{X = -577.59 \text{ N}}\]

(Negative sign indicates compression in the vertical member)

Problem 2: Three-Rib Arch Panel Structure

Evaluate the shear flow in the panel with thickness \(t_2\) at \(x = 0\).

Given Data:

  • \(A = 700 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(b = 600 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(h = 150 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_1 = 1 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_2 = 1.5 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(L = 1800 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(p = 2.5 \cdot 10^{-2} \left(1 + B/10\right) \text{ N/mm}^2\)

Solution (A = B = 0)

Step 1: Calculate shear force at x = 0

The shear at \(x = 0\) is:

\[T = p \cdot L \cdot (b - 2h)\]

Step 2: Calculate shear flow from symmetry

From the symmetry of the section:

\[q = \frac{T}{2h} = 45 \text{ N/mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{q = 45 \text{ N/mm}}\]

Problem 3: Cantilever Beam with Rigid Link - Ritz Method

Using the Ritz method and a 1-DOF polynomial approximation for any displacement component, determine the strain energy.

Given Data:

  • \(L = 1200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(d = 45 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA_0 = 1.15 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EA_1 = 6.5 \cdot 10^5 \left(1 + B/10\right) \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ_0 = 1.5 \cdot 10^9 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(EJ_1 = 7.5 \cdot 10^9 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(P = 600 \text{ N}\)

Note: EA and EJ vary linearly with x from \(EA_0/EJ_0\) to \(EA_1/EJ_1\).

Solution (B = 0)

Step 1: Define displacement approximations

\[u = q_1 x \qquad w = q_2 x^2\]

Step 2: Define stiffness variations

\[EJ(x) = \alpha + \beta x\] \[EA(x) = \delta + \gamma x\]

Step 3: Define coefficients

\[\alpha = EJ_0 \qquad \beta = \frac{EJ_1 - EJ_0}{L}\] \[\delta = EA_0 \qquad \gamma = \frac{EA_1 - EA_0}{L}\]

Step 4: Write virtual work equation

\[\int_0^L \left( \delta u_{,x} \cdot EA(x) \cdot u_{,x} + \delta w_{,xx} \cdot EJ(x) \cdot w_{,xx} \right) dx = - \delta u(L) \cdot P - \delta w_{,x}(L) \cdot P \cdot d\]

Step 5: Substitute approximations and form matrix equation

\[\begin{bmatrix} \delta L + \gamma L^2/2 & 0 \\ 0 & 4(\alpha L + \beta L^2/2) \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} q_1 \\ q_2 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -PL \\ -2LPd \end{Bmatrix}\]

This can be written as: \(\underline{K} \, \underline{q} = \underline{f}\)

Step 6: Solve for q and evaluate strain energy U

Solving for \(\underline{q}\) and evaluating \(U\):

\[U = \frac{1}{2} \underline{q}^T \underline{K} \, \underline{q} = 599.47 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{U = 599.47 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}}\]

Problem 4: Rectangular Frame with Two Ribs

Given Data:

  • \(a = 500 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 250 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 0.6 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(L = 2000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(P = 1000 \text{ N}\)

Solution (A = 0)

Step 1: Determine equilibrating shear flows

The equilibrating shear flows are found as:

  • Top edge: \(\frac{1}{4} \frac{P}{b}\) (horizontal)
  • Left edge: \(\frac{3}{4} \frac{P}{b}\) (vertical, downward)
  • Bottom edge: \(\frac{1}{4} \frac{P}{b}\) (horizontal)
  • Right edge: \(\frac{1}{4} \frac{P}{b}\) (vertical, downward)

Step 2: Convert to equivalent beam loading

The shear flow distribution on Rib #2 is converted to equivalent beam loads:

  • Vertical force \(\frac{1}{4}P\) upward at left end
  • Vertical force \(\frac{1}{4}P\) downward at right end
  • Distributed moment along the span with \(M = 0\) in center region

The bending moment at \(x = a/3\) is evaluated using the equivalent beam model derived from the shear flow analysis.

Problem 5: Two-Segment Frame - Torsional Stiffness Ratio

Find the value of \(GJ_2/GJ_1\) such that \(S = S_{max}\).

Given Data:

  • \(L_1 = 300 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(L_2 = 750 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ_1 = 2 \cdot 10^9 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(EJ_2 = 3 \cdot 10^9 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ_1 = 9 \cdot 10^7 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(P = 1200 \text{ N}\)
  • \(S_{max} = 15 \left(1 + F/10\right)\)

Solution (F = 0)

Step 1: Define coordinate systems

Real system: Member 1 (horizontal) with local axes \((z_1, y_1)\), Member 2 (inclined) with local axes \((z_2, y_2)\). Force \(P\) upward at left, force \(P\) downward at joint.

Dummy system: Same geometry with unit load applied upward at left end (location of \(S\)).

Step 2: Define bending moments for beam 1

\[M_1 = -Pz_1 \qquad \delta M_1 = -z_1 \qquad \text{(beam 1)}\]

Step 3: Define moments for beam 2

\[M_2 = -2PL_1 \qquad \delta M_2 = -L_1 \qquad \text{(beam 2)}\]

Step 4: Apply virtual work equation

\[\int_0^{L_1} \frac{\delta M_1 \cdot M_1}{EJ_1} \, dz_1 + \int_0^{L_2} \frac{\delta M_2 \cdot M_2}{GJ_2} \, dz_2 = S = S_{max}\]

Step 5: Solve for GJ₂

From which:

\[GJ_2 = \frac{2PL_1^2 L_2}{S_{max} - \dfrac{PL_1^3}{3EJ_1}}\]

Step 6: Calculate ratio

\[\frac{GJ_2}{GJ_1} = 187.5\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\frac{GJ_2}{GJ_1} = 187.5}\]

Problem 6: Cylindrical Tube - Shear Deformability

Evaluate the contribution of shear deformability to the displacement \(S\).

Given Data:

  • \(L = 2300 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(R = 150 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 0.8 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(P = 1700 \left(1 + B/10\right) \text{ N}\)
  • \(G = 27000 \text{ MPa}\)

Solution (A = B = 0)

Step 1: Calculate effective shear area

For a thin-walled circular tube:

\[A^* = \frac{8 \, t \, R}{\pi}\]

Step 2: Calculate shear contribution to displacement

\[S_{shear} = \frac{PL}{GA^*} = 0.4739 \text{ mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{S_{shear} = 0.4739 \text{ mm}}\]
