Problem 1: L-Frame Vertical Displacement

The beams composing the structure in the figure have stiffnesses EJ and GJ. Shear deformability is negligible. Determine the vertical displacement v as shown in the figure.

Unit for result: mm

Given Data (solution for A = 0):

  • \(\ell = 1000 \times (1 + A/10)\) mm
  • \(EJ = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\) N·mm²
  • \(GJ = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\) N·mm²
  • \(q_1 = 1.0\) N/mm

Solution

Step 1: Real System Analysis

Beam 1 (Horizontal member):

\[M_y = -X x_1 - \frac{2 q_1 x_1^3}{\ell}\]

Beam 2 (Vertical member):

\[M_y = -2 q_1 \ell^2 - (X + 8q_1 \ell) x_2\] \[M_t = X\ell\]

Step 2: Dummy System #1

Beam 1: \(^1\delta M_y = -x_1\)

Beam 2: \(^1\delta M_y = -x_2\), \(^1\delta M_t = \ell\)

By application of the PCVW:

\[\int_0^{\ell} {^1\delta M_y} \frac{M_y}{EJ} dx_1 + \int_0^{\ell} {^1\delta M_y} \frac{M_y}{EJ} dx_2 + \int_0^{\ell} {^1\delta M_t} \frac{M_t}{GJ} dx_2 = 0\]

From which:

\[\frac{\ell^3}{15EJ} \left(5X + 6\ell q\right) + \frac{\ell^3}{3EJ} \left(X + 11 \ell q\right) + \frac{\ell^3}{GJ} X = 0\]

And so:

\[X = -\frac{61 \cdot GJ \cdot \ell \cdot q}{15EJ + 10 \cdot GJ} = -2440 \text{ N}\]

Step 3: Dummy System #2 for Displacement

A second dummy system is introduced for evaluating the displacement.

Beam #2: \(^2\delta M_y = -x_2\)

By application of the PCVW:

\[\int_0^{\ell} M_y \frac{^2\delta M_y}{EJ} dx_2 = s\]

From which:

\[s = \frac{\ell^3}{3EJ} \left(X + 11 \ell q\right)\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{s = 2.85 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 2: Rectangular Frame with Spring Support

The structure in the figure is composed of three beams with same length l and bending stiffness EJ. Shear deformability is negligible and so is the contribution due to the axial stiffness. Determine the strain energy stored in the spring.

Unit for result: N·mm

Given Data (solution for C = 0):

  • \(\ell = 1000 \times (1 + C/10)\) mm
  • \(EJ = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\) N·mm²
  • \(k = 1000\) N/mm
  • \(P = 1000\) N

Solution

Step 1: Real System Analysis

Equilibrium equation:

\[-V\ell + X\ell - P\ell = 0\] \[V = X - P\]

Beam 1 (Left vertical): \(N = +V\)

Beam 2 (Top horizontal):

\[N = -X\] \[M = +V x_2\]

Beam 3 (Right vertical):

\[N = -V\] \[M = +V\ell + (P - X) x_3\]

Step 2: Dummy System

Beam 1: \(\delta N = 1\)

Beam 2:

\[\delta N = -1\] \[\delta M = x_2\]

Beam 3:

\[\delta N = -1\] \[\delta M = \ell - x_3\]

Step 3: Application of PCVW

The PCVW reads:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \delta N \frac{N}{EA} dx_1 + \int_0^{\ell} \delta N \frac{N}{EA} dx_2 + \int_0^{\ell} \delta N \frac{N}{EA} dx_3 + \int_0^{\ell} \delta M \frac{M}{EJ} dx_2 + \int_0^{\ell} \delta M \frac{M}{EJ} dx_3 + \frac{X}{k} = 0\] \[\frac{\ell}{EA} \left(2V + X\right) + \frac{\ell^3}{3EJ} V + \frac{\ell^3}{6EJ} \left(P + 3V - X\right) + \frac{X}{k} = 0\]

Substituting \(V = X - P\):

\[\frac{\ell}{EA} \left(3X - 2P\right) + \frac{\ell^3}{3EJ} \cdot 2(X - P) + \frac{X}{k} = 0\]

From which:

\[X = \frac{2EA \cdot k\ell^3 + 6EJ \cdot k\ell}{2EA \cdot k\ell^3 + 9EJ \cdot k\ell + 3EA \cdot EJ} P\]

Step 4: Simplified Solution (Neglecting Axial Stiffness)

If the energy contribution of the axial stiffness is neglected:

\[X = \frac{2k\ell^3}{2k\ell^3 + 3EJ} P = 400 \text{ N}\]

And the strain energy reads:

\[U_{spring} = \frac{1}{2} \frac{X^2}{k}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{U_{spring} = 80 \text{ N·mm}}\]

Problem 3: Two-Span Continuous Beam Stiffness Ratio

The structure in the figure is composed of two beams with bending stiffnesses equal to EJ1 and EJ2. Determine the bending stiffness EJ2 such that the vertical displacement at the free end is equal to w when the structure is loaded with a uniformly distributed force per unit length. Report the result as EJ2 / EJ1.

Unit for result: dimensionless

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1000\) mm
  • \(EJ_1 = 1.0 \times 10^{12}\) N·mm²
  • \(q = 1.0\) N/mm
  • \(w = 0.5 \times (1 + A/10)\) mm

Solution

Step 1: Equilibrium Analysis

\[q\ell \cdot \frac{3}{2}\ell + V\ell = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad V = -\frac{3}{2}q\ell\]

Step 2: Real System Moments

Right span (cantilever):

\[M = -\frac{q x_1^2}{2}\]

Left span:

\[M = -\frac{q\ell^2}{2} - (V + q\ell) x_2\]

Step 3: Dummy System

Unit downward force applied at free end.

\[\delta M = -x_1 \qquad (0 \leq x_1 \leq \ell)\] \[\delta M = +x_2 - \ell \qquad (0 \leq x_2 \leq \ell)\]

Step 4: Application of PCVW

\[\int_0^{\ell} \delta M \frac{M}{EJ_2} dx_1 + \int_0^{\ell} \delta M \frac{M}{EJ_1} dx_2 = w\]

From which:

\[\frac{\ell^3}{12 EJ_1} \left(2V + 5q\ell\right) + \frac{q\ell^4}{8EJ_2} = w\]

Upon substitution of V:

\[\frac{q\ell^4}{8EJ_2} + \frac{q\ell^4}{6EJ_1} = w\]

From which:

\[EJ_2 = \frac{q\ell^4}{8w - \frac{4q\ell^4}{3EJ_1}}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\frac{EJ_2}{EJ_1} = 0.375}\]

Problem 4: Thin-Walled Racetrack Cross-Section Shear Stress

The thin-walled beam in the figure is subjected to an internal shear force equal to P. The force is referred to the shear center of the section. The area of the lumped stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is equal to A; the panels have thickness equal to t. By using a semi-monocoque approximation of the section, determine the shear stress in the panel indicated in the figure.

Unit for result: MPa

Given Data (solution for A = 0):

  • \(a = 200\) mm
  • \(R = 45\) mm
  • \(t = 1.2 \times (1 + A/10)\) mm
  • \(P = 9000\) N

Solution

Step 1: Shear Flow from Symmetry

Due to the symmetry of the structure, the shear flows are readily found as shown in the figure, where:

\[q \cdot 2R \cdot 2 = P\]

So:

\[q = \frac{P}{4R} = \frac{9000}{4 \times 45} = 50 \text{ N/mm}\]

Step 2: Shear Stress Calculation

The shear stress in the panel is then:

\[\tau = \frac{q}{t_z}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\tau = 41.67 \text{ MPa}}\]

Problem 5: Two-Cell Section Torsional Stiffness

Consider the two-cell section in the figure. The area of the lumped stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is equal to A; the panels have thickness equal to t. The shear modulus of the material is G. Determine the torsional stiffness GJ. For this purpose, adopt a semi-monocoque approximation of the section. Report the result as GJ / GJref, where GJref is reference value available in the data.

Unit for result: dimensionless

Given Data (solution for C = 0):

  • \(a = 100 \times (1 + C/10)\) mm
  • \(t = 1.5\) mm
  • \(A = 400\) mm²
  • \(G = 27000\) MPa
  • \(GJ_{ref} = 1.0 \times 10^{10}\) N·mm²

Solution

Step 1: Equivalent Moment Equation

\[2\Omega_1 q_1^* + 2\Omega_2 q_2^* = M_t\]

with \(\Omega_1 = a^2\) and \(\Omega_2 = 2\Omega_1\)

So:

\[q_1^* + 2 q_2^* = \frac{M_t}{2\Omega_1}\]

Step 2: Compatibility Equations

\[2G\Omega_1 t \theta_1' = q_1^* \cdot 4a - q_2^* \cdot a\] \[2G\Omega_2 t \theta_2' = q_2^* \cdot 6a - q_1^* \cdot a\]

Imposing \(\theta_1' = \theta_2'\):

\[8 q_1^* - 2 q_2^* = 6 q_2^* - q_1^*\]

From which:

\[9 q_1^* - 8 q_2^* = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad q_1^* = \frac{8}{9} q_2^*\]

Step 3: Solve for Shear Flows

Substituting into moment equation:

\[\frac{8}{9} q_2^* + 2 q_2^* = \frac{M_t}{2\Omega_1}\] \[q_2^* = \frac{9}{26} \frac{M_t}{2\Omega_1} \quad ; \quad q_1^* = \frac{4}{13} \frac{M_t}{2\Omega_1}\]

Step 4: Calculate Twist Rate and Torsional Stiffness

\[\theta_1' = \frac{1}{2G\Omega_1 t} \left(4 q_1^* - q_2^*\right) a\] \[= M_t \frac{23}{104} \frac{1}{G a^3 t}\] \[GJ = \frac{M_t}{\theta_1'} = G \cdot \frac{104}{23} a^3 t = 1.83 \times 10^{11} \text{ N·mm}^2\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\frac{GJ}{GJ_{ref}} = 18.31}\]

Problem 6: Thin-Walled Circular Beam Rotation

The thin-walled beam in the figure is loaded with two concentrated forces F1 and F2. The lumped area of the stringers, including the contribution of the panels, is equal to A. The thickness of panels is t. The shear modulus of the material is G. By using a semi-monocoque approximation, determine the rotation of the section at a distance equal to 1/3 l from the free end. Report the absolute value of the rotation angle expressed in degrees.

Unit for result: degrees (absolute value)

Given Data (solution for E = 0):

  • \(\ell = 2500\) mm
  • \(R = 25\) mm
  • \(t = 0.6\) mm
  • \(A = 500\) mm²
  • \(G = 27000\) MPa
  • \(F_1 = 2000\) N
  • \(F_2 = 5000 \times (1 + E/10)\) N

Solution

Step 1: Shear Center and Torsional Moment

The position of the shear center is readily available due to the section symmetry. The torsional moment evaluated with respect to the shear center is:

  • \(F_1 R\) over length \(\ell/6\)
  • \((F_1 + F_2) R\) over remaining length

Step 2: Torsional Constant (Bredt's Formula)

\[J = \frac{4\Omega^2}{\oint_p \frac{1}{t} dn}\]

where \(\Omega = \pi R^2\), so:

\[J = \frac{4\pi^2 R^4 t}{2\pi R} = 2\pi R^3 t\]

Step 3: Twist Rate

Recalling that the torsion is \(\theta' = M_t / GJ\), it follows that:

\[\theta' = \frac{(F_1 + F_2) R}{GJ}\]

Step 4: Rotation at \(x = \frac{2}{3}\ell\)

The rotation of the section at \(x = \frac{2}{3}\) is:

\[\theta = \theta' \cdot \frac{2}{3}\ell\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta = 10.51°}\]
