Structural Mechanics Exam

Problem 1: L-Shaped Frame with Spring Support

Find the rotation \(\theta\) (in degrees) at \(x = L/2\)

Given Data:

  • \(L = 400 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA = 7.2 \cdot 10^4 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 1.25 \cdot 10^6 \left(1 + C/10\right) \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(K = 60 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(P = 5000 \text{ N}\)

Solution (A = C = 0)

Step 1: Evaluate the reaction force at the spring

Using the unit load method with "Real" and "Dummy" load cases:

Real system: Force \(P + X\) applied at spring location

Dummy system: Unit force 1 applied at spring location

Bending moment expressions:

\[M_y = -(P + X)z \qquad \delta M_y = -z\]

Step 2: Apply compatibility condition

The displacement at the spring must equal the spring deformation:

\[\int_0^L \frac{M_y \cdot \delta M_y}{EJ} \, dz + \frac{X}{K} = 0\]

From which:

\[X = -\frac{P}{\left(1 + \frac{3EJ}{KL^3}\right)}\]

Step 3: Evaluate the rotation

Apply a new dummy load case with unit moment at the point of interest:

Real system: Frame with forces X and P

Dummy system: Unit moment at top (where rotation is sought)

For \(z \geq L/2\):

\[\delta M_y = -1\]

Apply the virtual work equation:

\[\int_{L/2}^{L} \frac{M_y \cdot \delta M_y}{EJ} \, dz = \theta\]

Step 4: Calculate final rotation

From the integration:

\[\theta = \frac{3L^2(P + X)}{8EJ} = 12.23°\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta = 12.23°}\]

Problem 2: Triangular Truss - Shear Center Location

Find the vertical position of the shear center (measured from \(x = 0\)).

Given Data:

  • \(A = 500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(h = 150 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_1 = 1 \cdot (1 + F/10)\)
  • \(t_3 = 2 \cdot (1 + A/10)\)

Solution (A = F = 0)

Step 1: Calculate section properties

Second moment of area about y-axis:

\[J_{yy} = 2A \left(\frac{h}{2}\right)^2 \cdot 2 = Ah^2\]

First moments of area:

\[S_{y_1}' = 2A \cdot \frac{h}{2} = Ah\] \[S_{y_2}' = Ah\]

Step 2: Apply shear flow equation

Apply force \(T = 1000 \text{ N}\) at arbitrary distance \(d\) from reference position.

Applying the shear flow equation two times:

\[q_1' = -T \frac{S_{y_1}'}{J_{yy}} = -\frac{T}{h} = -6.67 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_2' = -T \frac{S_{y_2}'}{J_{yy}} = -\frac{T}{h} = -6.67 \text{ N/mm}\]

Step 3: Moment equivalence about point 1

\[2q_* \Omega + 2q_2' \Omega = -Td\]

Step 4: Impose zero twist condition

Impose \(\theta' = 0\):

\[\theta' = \frac{1}{2G\Omega} \sum_i \frac{q_i \ell_i}{t_i} = 0\] \[\frac{q_1 h}{t_1} + \frac{q_2 h}{t_1} + \frac{q_3 h}{t_3} = 0\]

Substituting the shear flow components:

\[(q_1' + q^*) + (q_2' + q^*) + \frac{t_1}{t_3} q^* = 0\] \[\Rightarrow q^* = \frac{2T}{h} \cdot \frac{1}{2 + t_1/t_3} = 5.33 \text{ N/mm}\]

Step 5: Calculate shear center position

\[d = -\frac{2\Omega (q_* + q_1')}{T} = 25.98 \text{ mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{d = 25.98 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 3: Rectangular Section with Diagonal Brace - Torsional Stiffness

Find the torsional stiffness of the section \(GJ/GJ_{ref}\)

Given Data:

  • \(A = 600 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(a = 400 \cdot (1 + B/10) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 1 \cdot (1 + A/10) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(G = 2.7 \cdot 10^4 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(GJ_{ref} = 1 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)

Solution (A = B = 0)

Step 1: Identify symmetry

From the symmetries of the section, it is immediate to note that the position of the shear center is in the middle of the section. Therefore, the shear flow along the oblique panel does not provide any torsional resistance.

Owing to the symmetry of the section, the flows on the four remaining panels are equal.

Step 2: Apply Bredt's formula

Torsional moment relationship:

\[M_t = 2\Omega_c \cdot q \qquad \text{with } \Omega_c = a^2\]

Therefore:

\[q = \frac{M_t}{2a^2}\]

Step 3: Calculate torsional stiffness

From the shear flow analysis:

\[GJ = G a^3 t\]

And so:

\[\frac{GJ}{GJ_{ref}} = \frac{6a^3 t}{GJ_{ref}} = 172.80\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\frac{GJ}{GJ_{ref}} = 172.80}\]

Problem 4: Circular Thin-Walled Shaft - Torsional Rotation

Evaluate the rotation \(\theta\) (in degrees) at the free end.

Given Data:

  • \(A = 500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(R = 80 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 1.2 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(L = 2500 \left(1 + F/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(G = 27000 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(P = 7500 \text{ N}\)

Solution (A = F = 0)

Step 1: Apply Bredt's formula for torsional stiffness

For a thin-walled circular section:

\[GJ = G \cdot 2\pi R^3 t\]

Step 2: Calculate rate of twist

The torsional moment is \(M_t = 2PR\) (from the force couple).

\[\theta' = \frac{M_t}{G \cdot 2\pi R^3 t} = \frac{2PR}{G \cdot 2\pi R^3 t} = \frac{P}{G\pi R^2 t}\]

Step 3: Calculate total rotation

The rotation at the free end is:

\[\theta = \theta' \cdot L\] \[\theta = 1.65°\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta = 1.65°}\]

Problem 5: Cantilever Beam with Distributed Torsion - Ritz Method

Use the Ritz method and a 1-DOF polynomial approximation to determine the torsional moment \(M_t / M_{t_{ref}}\) at \(x = L/2\)

Given Data:

  • \(L = 3700 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(GJ = 2.2 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(k = 1 \cdot 10^4 \left(1 + D/10\right) \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\)
  • \(m_t = 520 \left(1 + A/10\right) \text{ N}\)
  • \(M_{t_{ref}} = 3 \cdot 10^4 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\)

Solution (A = D = 0)

Step 1: Define trial function

Assume a linear polynomial approximation for the twist angle:

\[\theta = x \, q_1 \qquad ; \qquad \theta' = q_1\]

Step 2: Apply virtual work principle

The weak form equation including the spring at the support:

\[\int_0^L \delta\theta' \, GJ \, \theta' \, dx + \delta\theta(L) \, k \, \theta(L) = \int_0^L \delta\theta \, m_t \, dx\]

Step 3: Substitute trial function and solve

Substituting the trial function:

\[\delta q_1 \left(GJL + kL^2\right) q_1 = \delta q_1 \frac{L^2}{2} m_t\]

Step 4: Calculate torsional moment

The torsional moment is:

\[M_t = GJ\theta' = GJ q_1 = \frac{GJ \cdot L^2 \, m_t}{2(GJL + kL^2)}\] \[\frac{M_t}{M_{t_{ref}}} = 1.8\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\frac{M_t}{M_{t_{ref}}} = 1.8}\]

Problem 6: Two-Span Continuous Beam - Ritz Method

Use the Ritz method and a 1-DOF trigonometric approximation to determine the vertical displacement at \(x = L/2\)

Given Data:

  • \(L = 300 \left(1 + B/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 1.25 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(q = 0.2 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution (B = 0)

Step 1: Define trial functions for each span

Using local coordinates \(x_1\) for the left span and \(x_2\) for the right span:

\[w_1 = \sin \frac{\pi x_1}{L} \, q_1\] \[w_2 = \sin \frac{\pi x_2}{2L} \, q_2\]

Step 2: Apply boundary conditions

Continuity of displacement at middle support:

\[w_1(L) = w_2(0) \quad \rightarrow \quad \text{identically satisfied}\]

Continuity of slope at middle support (to be imposed):

\[w_{1/x}(L) = w_{2/x}(0)\]

This leads to:

\[w_1 = \sin \frac{\pi x_1}{L} \, q_1\] \[w_2 = -2 \sin \frac{\pi x_2}{2L} \, q_1\]

Step 3: Apply virtual work equation

\[\int_0^L \delta w_{1/xx} \, EJ \, w_{1/xx} \, dx_1 + \int_0^{2L} \delta w_{2/xx} \, EJ \, w_{2/xx} \, dx_2 = \int_0^{2L} \delta w_2 \, q \, dx_2\]

Step 4: Solve for the generalized coordinate

From the virtual work equation:

\[q_1 = -\frac{8L}{\pi} \, q \, \frac{1}{\left(\frac{\pi}{L}\right)^4 EJ \, \frac{3}{4} \, L}\]

Step 5: Calculate displacement at x = L/2

The displacement at \(x = L/2\) is:

\[w_1\left(L/2\right) = \sin \frac{\pi}{2} \, q_1 = q_1 = -0.452 \text{ mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{w_1(L/2) = -0.452 \text{ mm}}\]

(Negative sign indicates downward displacement)
