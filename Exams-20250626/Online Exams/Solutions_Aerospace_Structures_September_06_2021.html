Structural Mechanics Exam Solutions

Exercise 6

Determine the reaction force in the figure. The structure is loaded with a prescribed displacement \(\bar{u}\).

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1500\left(1 + E/10\right) \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\bar{u} = 10 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 10^{11}\)

Solution

Step 1: Real System Analysis

Setting up the equilibrium equations for the beam with reaction forces \(X\) and \(Y\):

\[\begin{cases} X + Y = F \\ Y\ell - 2F\ell = 0 \end{cases}\]

Solving for the reactions:

\[\Rightarrow \begin{cases} X = F - Y = -F \\ Y = 2F \end{cases}\]

Step 2: Bending Moment Expressions

For segment 1 (from right):

\[M_1 = Fx_1\]

For segment 2 (from left):

\[M_2 = F\ell - Fx_2\]

Step 3: Dummy System

Virtual bending moments for the dummy system with unit load:

\[\delta M_1 = x_1\] \[\delta M_2 = \ell - x_2\]

Step 4: Application of PCVW

By application of the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M_1 M_1}{EJ} \, dx_1 + \int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M_2 M_2}{EJ} \, dx_2 = \bar{u}\]

From which:

\[F = \frac{3EJ\bar{u}}{2\ell^3} = 444.44 \text{ N}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{V = 2F = 888.88 \text{ N}}\]

Exercise 7

Determine the vertical displacement (positive in the downward direction) due to a torsional moment \(\hat{M}_t\).

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\hat{M}_t = 10^5\left(1 + F/10\right) \text{ N·mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 10^{10} \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ = 10^{8} \text{ N·mm}^2\)

Solution

Step 1: Real System Internal Forces

Torsional moment in the horizontal member:

\[M_{t_1} = \hat{M}_t\]

Bending moment in the inclined member:

\[M_2 = \hat{M}_t\]

Step 2: Dummy System

Applying unit vertical force at the point of interest, the virtual bending moment in the inclined member:

\[\delta M_2 = x_2\]

Step 3: Application of PCVW

By application of the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M_2 \cdot M_2}{EJ} \, dx_2 = V\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{V = \frac{M_t}{EJ} \cdot \frac{\ell^2}{2} = 7.20 \text{ mm}}\]

Exercise 8

Determine the vertical displacement \(v\).

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 10^{12} \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(k = 750\left(1 + G/10\right) \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(q = 12 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Real System Bending Moments

For segment 1 (from right):

\[M_1 = -q\ell x_1 + q\frac{x_1^2}{2}\]

For segment 2 (from left):

\[M_2 = -q\ell x_2 + q\frac{x_2^2}{2}\]

Step 2: Dummy System

Virtual bending moments with unit load at midspan:

\[\delta M_1 = -\frac{1}{2}x_1\] \[\delta M_2 = -\frac{1}{2}x_2\]

Step 3: Application of PCVW

By application of the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M_1 M_1}{EJ} dx_1 + \int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M_2 M_2}{EJ} dx_2 + \delta F_s \frac{F_s}{k} = V\]

where:

\[\delta F_s = 1/2\] \[F_s = q\ell\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{V = \frac{5}{24} \frac{q\ell^4}{EJ} + \frac{q\ell}{2k} = 14.78 \text{ mm}}\]

Exercise 11

Determine the vertical displacement \(V\) in correspondence of the tip of the rigid link in the figure. The rigid body is loaded at \(x = \ell/2\), i.e. in the middle of the beam. Use Ritz with polynomial expansion and the lowest possible number of DOFs.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(L = 200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 10^{11} \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ_0 = 10^{10} \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ_1 = 2 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(\hat{\theta} = 10 \text{ deg}\)
  • \(F = 5000\left(1 + b/10\right)\)

Solution

Step 1: Essential Conditions

The essential boundary conditions are:

\[\theta(0) = 0 \quad \quad w(0) = 0\] \[\theta(\ell) = \hat{\theta} \quad \quad w'(0) = 0\]

Step 2: Trial Function for \(w\)

Starting with:

\[w = a_0 + a_1\left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right) + a_2\left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)^2\]

Imposing \(w(0) = 0\) and \(w'(0) = 0\), one obtains:

\[w = a_2\left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)^2 \quad \text{So:} \quad w'' = a_2 \frac{2}{\ell^2}\]

Step 3: Trial Function for \(\theta\)

The expansion can be represented as:

\[\theta = \theta_H + \theta_p\]

where \(\theta_H\) restores homogeneous conditions and \(\theta_p\) is the particular solution.

For the homogeneous part with \(\theta_H(0) = 0\) and \(\theta_H(\ell) = 0\):

\[\theta_H = b_0 + b_1\left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right) + b_2\left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)^2\]

Imposing conditions: \(b_0 = 0\) and \(b_1 + b_2 = 0 \Rightarrow b_1 = -b_2\)

\[\theta_H(\ell) = \left[\left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)^2 - \left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)\right]b_2\]

For the particular part:

\[\theta_p(x) = \frac{x}{\ell}\hat{\theta}\]

Therefore:

\[\theta(x) = \left[\left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)^2 - \left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)\right]b_2 + \left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right)\hat{\theta}\] \[\theta' = \frac{1}{\ell}\left(\frac{2x}{\ell} - 1\right)b_2 + \frac{1}{\ell}\hat{\theta}\]

Step 4: Application of PVW

By application of the Principle of Virtual Work:

\[\int_0^{\ell} \delta w'' \, EJ \, w'' \, dx + \int_0^{\ell} \delta \theta' \, GJ \, \theta' \, dx = -\delta w(\ell) F\]

From which: \(\underline{\underline{K}} \, \underline{u} = \underline{F}\), where:

\[\underline{\underline{K}} = \begin{bmatrix} 4EJ/\ell^3 & 0 \\ 0 & (2GJ_0 + GJ_1)/6\ell \end{bmatrix}\] \[\underline{F} = \begin{Bmatrix} -F \\ -\frac{GJ\hat{\theta}}{6\ell} \end{Bmatrix} \quad \quad \underline{u} = \begin{Bmatrix} a_2 \\ b_2 \end{Bmatrix}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{V = -w(\ell/2) + \theta(\ell/2) L = 27.22 \text{ mm}}\]

Exercise 12

Evaluate the rotation \(\theta\) at \(x = \ell/2\) solving the problem exactly (circular bar). Report the result in degrees.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(GJ = 10^9 \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(m_t = 200\left(1 + B/10\right) \text{ N}\)

Solution

Step 1: Governing Equations

The governing equations are:

\[\begin{cases} GJ\theta'' + m_t = 0 \\ \theta(0) = 0 \\ GJ\theta'(\ell) = 0 \end{cases}\]

Step 2: Solution Structure

The solution of the system is found as:

\[\theta = \theta_H + \theta_p\]

a) Homogeneous solution:

\[GJ\theta_H'' = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \theta_H' = a_0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \theta_H = a_0 x + a_1\]

b) Particular solution:

\[GJ\theta_p'' = -m_t \quad \Rightarrow \quad \theta_p' = -\frac{m_t}{GJ}x \quad \Rightarrow \quad \theta_p = -\frac{1}{2}\frac{m_t}{GJ}x^2\]

Step 3: Apply Boundary Conditions

General solution:

\[\theta = a_1 + a_0 x - \frac{1}{2}\frac{m_t}{GJ}x^2\]

Imposing boundary conditions:

\[\theta(0) = 0 \Rightarrow a_1 = 0\] \[\theta'(\ell) = 0 \Rightarrow a_0 = \frac{m_t \ell}{GJ}\]

Step 4: Final Solution

Therefore:

\[\theta = \frac{m_t}{GJ}\left(\ell x - \frac{1}{2}x^2\right)\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta\left(\ell/2\right) = \frac{m_t}{GJ} \cdot \frac{3\ell^2}{8} = 9.67 \text{ deg}}\]

Exercise 24

Model the rib as a beam and determine the bending moment at \(x = \ell_1/2\).

Given Data:

  • \(\ell_1 = 500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\ell_2 = 1500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\ell_3 = 150 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 1.0 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(F = 6000 \text{ N}\)

Solution

Step 1: Load Decomposition

The applied load is decomposed into two loading cases:

  1. Symmetric shear loading with force \(F\)
  2. Torsional loading with moment \(F\ell_1/2\)

Step 2: Shear Flow Analysis

The shear flows are readily determined. For symmetric shear:

\[\text{Shear flow} = \frac{F}{2\ell_3}\]

For torsional shear flow:

\[\frac{F\ell_1}{2} = 2q\Omega_c \quad \Rightarrow \quad q = \frac{F\ell_1}{4\ell_1\ell_3} = \frac{F}{4\ell_3}\]

Step 3: Superposition of Effects

By superposition of effects, the combined shear flows on the cross-section are:

  • Left side: \(\frac{3F}{4\ell_3}\)
  • Right side: \(\frac{F}{4\ell_3}\)
  • Top and bottom: \(\frac{F}{4\ell_3}\)

The equilibrating flows produce the beam model of the rib.

Step 4: Beam Model

The beam model has:

  • Upward force \(F\) at left end
  • Distributed load with resultant \(F/4\)
  • Downward force \(\frac{3}{4}F\) at left

From which the bending moment is:

\[M(x) = \frac{F}{4}x - \frac{F}{4}x = 0\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{M(x) = 0}\]
