Problem 1: 3D Beam Frame — Displacement Calculation

The hinges at points A and B constrain the three components of displacement, \(u\), \(v\) and \(w\). The constraint at point B constrains only the displacement in the \(y\) direction, \(v\). Compute the displacement in the \(z\) direction of point D.

Given Data:

  • \(EA = 7.2 \times 10^7 \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(E\bar{I}_{xx} = E\bar{I}_{yy} = 6 \times 10^{11} \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ = 2E\bar{I}_{xx}\)
  • \(\ell = 1200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(F = 2500 \text{ N}\)
  • Beam cross-section stiffness in a local reference system

Unit for result: mm

Solution

Step 1: Internal forces and moments for each member

Member (1):

\[M_x = Fz\]

Member (2):

\[M_z = F\ell\] \[M_x = -Fz\]

Member (3):

\[N_z = R_x + F\] \[M_x = +F\ell - \frac{F}{2}z_3\] \[M_y = -F\ell + \frac{F}{2}z_3\]

Step 2: Virtual axial force for member 3

\[3: \quad \delta N_z = 1\]

Step 3: Apply virtual work equation for axial deformation

\[\int_0^{2\ell} \frac{\delta N_z (R_x + F)}{EA} \, dz_3 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_x = -F\]

Step 4: Virtual moment for member 3

For \(0 < z_3 < \ell\):

\[\delta M_y = -\frac{1}{2}z_3\]

Extended expression:

\[= -\frac{1}{2}z_3 + z_3 - \ell = \frac{1}{2}z_3 - \ell\]

Step 5: Virtual work integration for bending

\[\int_0^{\ell} \frac{\delta M_y \cdot M_y}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \, dz_3 + \int_{\ell}^{2\ell} \frac{\delta M_y \cdot M_y}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \, dz_3\] \[= \frac{1}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} F\ell^3 \left(\frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{12}\right) = \frac{F\ell^3}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \cdot \frac{1}{4}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{w_D = 1.8 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 2: Cantilever Beam — Rotation Calculation

Compute the rotation \(\phi_y\) of the beam at point A.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 4000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(E\bar{I}_{yy} = 6 \times 10^{11} \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(F = 8000 \text{ N}\)

Unit for result: rad

Solution

Step 1: Bending moment expressions

For \(0 < z < \ell\):

\[M_y = -Fz\]

For \(\ell < z < 2\ell\):

\[M_y = -Fz + R_x(z - \ell)\]

Step 2: Virtual moment

\[\delta M_y = \begin{cases} 0 & 0 < z < \ell \\ (z - \ell) & \ell < z < 2\ell \end{cases}\]

Step 3: Set up virtual work integral

\[\frac{1}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \int_{\ell}^{2\ell} \left[-Fz^2 + Fz\ell + R_x\left(z^2 + \ell^2 - 2\ell z\right)\right] dz\]

Step 4: Evaluate integral

\[= \frac{1}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \left(-\frac{1}{3}Fz^3 + \frac{1}{2}F\ell z^2 + \frac{1}{3}R_x z^3 + R_x \ell^2 z - R_x \ell z^2\right)\Big|_{\ell}^{2\ell}\] \[= \frac{1}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \left[F\ell^3\left(-\frac{8}{3} + 2 + \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{2}\right) + R_x \ell^3\left(\frac{8}{3} + 2 - 4 - \frac{1}{3} - 1 + 1\right)\right] = 0\]

Step 5: Solve for reaction

\[-\frac{5}{6}F + \frac{1}{3}R_x = 0\] \[R_x = \frac{5}{2}F\]

Step 6: Updated moment expression

For \(0 < z < \ell\):

\[M_y = -F(z + \ell) + \frac{5}{2}Fz\]

Virtual moment:

\[\delta M_y = 1\]

Step 7: Compute rotation

\[\int_0^{\ell} \frac{-F(z + \ell) + \frac{5}{2}Fz}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} \, dz = (-1) \cdot \phi_y\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\phi_y = \frac{1}{4} \frac{F\ell^2}{E\bar{I}_{yy}} = 0.053 \text{ rad}}\]

Problem 3: Semi-Monocoque Beam — Shear Stress Calculation

The semi-monocoque beam model in the figure is loaded by a pressure distribution that is constant along the beam span and varies linearly from 0 to \(p\) along the upper side with dimension \(a\). Compute the shear stress in panel (1) at a distance \(2\ell - \varepsilon\), with \(0 < \varepsilon < \ell\), accounting for the fact that the external load is introduced by the ribs.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 2000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(E = 72000 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(p = 0.2 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(a = 800 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(h = 150 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(t = 4 \text{ mm}\)

Unit for result: MPa

Solution

Step 1: Calculate resultant force

\[R = \frac{pa}{2} \cdot \ell \cdot \frac{5}{2}\]

Step 2: Calculate moment (torsion)

\[M = \frac{pa}{2} \cdot \frac{5}{2}\ell \cdot \left(\frac{a}{2} - \frac{a}{3}\right) = \frac{5}{24} p a^2 \ell\]

Step 3: Calculate shear flows from shear force

\[q_1' = q_2' = \frac{R}{2h}\]

Step 4: Calculate shear flow from torsion

\[q^{\alpha} = \frac{M}{2ah}\]

Step 5: Calculate net shear flow in panel 1

\[q_1 = q_1' - q^{\alpha}\]

Step 6: Calculate shear stress

\[\tau_1 = \frac{q_1}{t_1}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\tau_1 = 277.78 \text{ MPa}}\]

Problem 4: Two-Cell Semi-Monocoque — Torsional Stiffness

Consider the two-cells semi-monocoque cross section model sketched in the figure. Compute its torsional stiffness.

Given Data:

  • \(a = 200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 100 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 1 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 1000 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(E = 72000 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)

Unit for result: N·mm²/rad

Solution

Step 1: Symmetry condition

By symmetry of the two-cell section:

\[q_1^0 = q_2^0\]

Step 2: Shear flow expression

\[q_1^* = \frac{M_z}{4ab}\]

Step 3: Twist rate equation

\[\dot{\theta}_1 = \frac{1}{2abG} \cdot \frac{(2a+b)}{t} q_1^0 = \left[\frac{1}{2ab} \cdot \frac{(2a+b)}{t} \cdot \frac{1}{4ab}\right] \cdot M_z\]

Step 4: Calculate torsional stiffness

\[K_{\theta} = \frac{G}{\left[\frac{1}{2ab} \cdot \frac{(2a+b)}{t} \cdot \frac{1}{4ab}\right]}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{K_{\theta} = \frac{1.77 \times 10^{11}}{6.48 \times 10^{6}} \text{ N·mm/rad}}\]

Problem 5: Semi-Monocoque Beam — Rivet Force Calculation

The semi-monocoque beam model in the figure is loaded by a pressure distribution that is constant along the beam span and varies linearly from 0 to \(p\) along the side with dimension \(a\). The rib at \(2\ell\) from the clamp is connected to the upper panel by means of a row of rivets, with pitch \(\rho\) and diameter \(d\). Compute the force transmitted by one of these rivets.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 700 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 800 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(E = 72000 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(a = 400 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(b = 200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 2 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(p = 0.3 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(\rho = 30 \text{ mm}\) (rivet pitch)
  • \(d = 3 \text{ mm}\) (rivet diameter)

Unit for result: N

Solution

Step 1: Calculate resultant shear force

\[R = \frac{pa\ell}{2}\]

Step 2: Calculate moment (torsion)

\[M = \frac{pa\ell}{2}\left(\frac{a}{2} - \frac{a}{3}\right)\]

Step 3: Calculate shear flows from shear force

\[q_1' = q_2' = \frac{R}{2h}\]

Step 4: Calculate shear flow from torsion

\[q^{\alpha} = \frac{M}{2ah}\]

Step 5: Calculate shear flow in panel 3

\[q_3 = q^{\alpha}\]

Step 6: Calculate rivet force

\[P_c = q_3 \cdot \rho\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{P_c = 525 \text{ N}}\]

Problem 6: Three-Node Parabolic Element — Virtual Work

The three-node parabolic element sketched in the figure is loaded by a constant force per unit of length. Compute the virtual work of the load for a unit virtual displacement in the \(y\) direction of node (1) (the node at \(x = -2\)).

Given Data:

  • \(\mu = 0.56 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • Node positions: \(x_1 = -2\), \(x_3 = 0\), \(x_2 = 2\)

Unit for result: N·mm

Solution

Step 1: Shape function for node 1

\[N_1 = \frac{x \cdot (x-2)}{8}\]

Step 2: Set up virtual work integral

\[\int_{-2}^{2} N_1 \cdot \mu \, dx = \int_{-2}^{2} \left(\frac{x^2}{8} - \frac{x}{4}\right) \mu \, dx\]

Step 3: Evaluate integral

\[= \left(\frac{x^3}{24} - \frac{x^2}{8}\right)\Big|_{-2}^{2} \cdot \mu\]

Computing the limits:

\[= \left[\left(\frac{8}{24} - \frac{4}{8}\right) - \left(\frac{-8}{24} - \frac{4}{8}\right)\right] \cdot \mu\] \[= \frac{2}{3}\mu\]

Step 4: Substitute numerical value

\[W_{virtual} = \frac{2}{3} \times 0.56 = 0.373 \text{ N·mm}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{W_{virtual} = 0.373 \text{ N·mm}}\]
