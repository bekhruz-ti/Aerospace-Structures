Exercise 1

The beam sketched in the figure, with bending stiffness \(EI\), is loaded by the concentrated force \(F\). Compute the bending moment at point A. (Unit for result: N mm)

Data \(a = 400\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 800\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 1.0 \times 10^5\, \text{N}\)
\(EI = 6.0 \times 10^{10}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)

\(K = 100\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(F = 5000\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:

Define the horizontal reaction force at the spring as \(X\). Using three beam segments with local coordinates \(z_1\), \(z_2\), \(z_3\):

Bending moments:
\(M_{z_1} = (F - X) z_1\)
\(M_{z_2} = (F - X) a\)
\(M_{z_3} = (F - X) z_3\)

Virtual moments (for unit virtual force at spring):
\(M'_{z_1} = -z_1\), \(M'_{z_2} = -a\), \(M'_{z_3} = -z_3\)

Applying PVW with spring compatibility: \[ \int_0^b \frac{(-F+X)}{EA} dx_1 + \int_0^a \frac{(-F+X)z_1^2}{EI} dz_1 + \int_0^b \frac{(X-F)a^2 b}{EI} + \int_0^a \frac{(-F+X)z_3^2}{EI} dz_3 + \frac{X}{K} = 0 \] Simplifying: \[ \left(\frac{EI \cdot b}{EA} + \frac{2}{3}a^3 + a^2 b + \frac{EI}{K}\right) X = \left(\frac{2}{3}a^3 + a^2 b + \frac{b \cdot EI}{EA}\right) F \] Solving for X and computing moment: \[ M = (F - X) \cdot a \] Final Answer: \(M_B(A) = 9.59 \times 10^5\, \text{N mm}\)

Exercise 2

The FE mesh sketched in the figure has two bilinear planar element and eight nodes. It is loaded by the distributed load sketched in the figure, with a maximum load per unit of length equal to \(f\). Compute the virtual external work for the unitary virtual vertical displacement of node 5. (Unit for result: N mm)

Data \(a = 4\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 6\, \text{mm}\)
\(f = 20\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer

Solution:

For node 5 on the right element, the shape functions are:
\(N_1 = 1 - \frac{x}{a}\) and \(N_2 = \frac{x}{a}\)

Virtual displacement interpolation: \(\delta u_y = N_1 \delta u_1 + N_2 \delta u_2\)

The triangular distributed load varies as: \(f(x) = \frac{f}{a} \cdot x\)

For unitary virtual displacement at node 5 (\(\delta u_2 = 1\)):
\[ \int_0^a \delta u_2 \cdot N_2(x) \cdot f(x) \, dx = \int_0^a 1 \cdot \frac{x}{a} \cdot \frac{f}{a} \cdot x \, dx = \frac{f}{a^2} \int_0^a x^2 \, dx = \frac{f}{a^2} \cdot \frac{a^3}{3} = \frac{1}{3} a f \] Since node 5 receives contributions from both elements:
\[ \delta L_e(\text{node } 5) = \frac{2}{3} a f \] Substituting values: \(\frac{2}{3} \times 4 \times 20 = \frac{160}{3}\)

Final Answer: \(\delta L_e(\text{node } 5) = 53.33\, \text{N mm}\)

Exercise 3

The semi-monocoque beam cross section model in the figure is loaded by a concentrated moment \(M_z\). It has six stringers, each with concentrated area \(A\). Compute the derivative of the cross section rotations angle with respect to the beam axis. (Unit for result: rad/mm)

Data \(a = 600\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 300\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)

\(t_1 = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_2 = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(M_z = 5 \times 10^6\, \text{Nmm}\)

Answer

Solution:

For a two-cell cross section with shear flows \(q_1\) and \(q_2\):

Rate of twist equations: \[ \dot{\theta}_1 = \frac{1}{4abG}\left(\frac{4a+b}{t_1} + \frac{b}{t_2}\right)q_1 + \frac{1}{4abG}\left(\frac{2a+2b}{t_1}\right)q_2 \] \[ \dot{\theta}_2 = \frac{1}{2abG}\left(\frac{2a+b}{t_1}\right)q_1 + \frac{1}{2abG}\left(\frac{2a+2b}{t_1}\right)q_2 \] Compatibility and equilibrium equations: \[ \begin{cases} \dot{\theta}_1 - \dot{\theta}_2 = 0 \\ 4ab \cdot q_1 + 2ab \cdot q_2 = M_z \end{cases} \] Solving the system:
\(q_1 = 7.25\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(q_2 = -0.6\, \text{N/mm}\)

Final Answer: \(\dot{\theta} = 1 \times 10^{-6}\, \text{rad/mm}\)

Exercise 4

Consider the semi-monocoque cross section model sketched in the figure. Compute the x positions of the shear center. (Unit for result: mm)

Data \(a = 400\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 800\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_1 = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_2 = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(t_3 = 3\, \text{mm}\)
\(A_1 = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)

\(A_2 = 1000\, \text{mm}^2\)

\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)

Answer

Solution:

Step 1: Locate the centroid
\(x_{CG} = \frac{a}{2}\)

For \(y_{CG}\), from moment equilibrium about bottom:
\[ \frac{A_2(b - y_{CG}) - A_1(y_{CG})}{A_1 + A_2} = 0 \] \[ y_{CG} = \frac{b \cdot A_2}{A_1 + A_2} \] Step 2: Calculate moment of inertia \[ I_{xx} = 2A_2(b - y_{CG})^2 + 2A_1 y_{CG}^2 \] Step 3: Calculate shear flows for unit shear \(T_y\)
\(q_2 = \frac{T_y}{b}\)

\(q_1 = \frac{-T_y S'_x}{I_{xx}}\) where \(S'_x = -A_1 y_{CG}\)

\(q_3 = q_1\)

Step 4: Moment equilibrium about shear center \[ -q_3 \cdot b \cdot a = T_y \cdot x_{sc} \] \[ x_{sc} = \frac{-q_3 \cdot b}{T_y} \] Final Answer: \(x_{sc} = -200\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 5

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by the concentrated force \(F\) at \(x = l\) and by the distributed force for unit of length \(q\). By resorting to the displacement method, and using a trigonometric approximation with only one term, estimate the vertical displacement in the y direction of point A (the point at \(x = l/2\) from the hinge). Account for the axial pre-stress. (Unit for result: mm)

Data \(q = 1.2\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(F_x = 10000\, \text{N}\)
\(EA = 1 \times 10^8\, \text{N}\)
\(EI = 1 \times 10^{11}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)

\(l = 4000\, \text{mm}\)

Answer

Solution:

Step 1: Assume trigonometric displacement approximation
\[ w = a \sin\left(\frac{\pi x}{l}\right) \] Derivatives:
\(w' = \frac{\pi}{l} a \cos\left(\frac{\pi x}{l}\right)\)
\(w'' = -\frac{\pi^2}{l^2} w\)

Step 2: Apply PVW with axial pre-stress \[ \int_0^l \left(\delta w'' EI \, w'' + \delta w' w' F_x\right) dx = \int_0^l -\delta w \cdot q \, dx \] Step 3: Evaluate integrals \[ \delta a \left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^4 EI \frac{l}{2} a + \delta a \left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^2 F_x \frac{l}{2} a = -\delta a \frac{l}{\pi} \cdot 2q \] Step 4: Solve for coefficient \(a\) \[ \left[\left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^4 EI \frac{l}{2} + \left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^2 F_x \frac{l}{2}\right] a = -2q\frac{l}{\pi} \] \[ a = \frac{-2q\frac{l}{\pi}}{\left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^4 EI \frac{l}{2} + \left(\frac{\pi}{l}\right)^2 F_x \frac{l}{2}} \] Step 5: Displacement at \(x = l/2\)
At \(x = l/2\): \(\sin(\pi/2) = 1\), so \(w(l/2) = a\)

Final Answer: \(w(l/2) = -34.55\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 6

The beam sketched in the figure is loaded by a triangular force per unit of length, going from \(0\, \text{N/mm}\) to \(f\, \text{N/mm}\). By resorting to the displacement method, and using a polynomial approximation with only one term, compute the vertical displacement in the y direction of point A. (Unit for result: mm)

Data \(f = 14\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(l = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EI = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{Nmm}^2\)

\(K = 100\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer

Solution:

Step 1: Assume polynomial displacement approximation
For a cantilever with spring at free end:
\(v = a z^2\) (satisfies \(v(0) = 0\), \(v'(0) = 0\))
\(v'' = 2a\)

Step 2: Triangular load distribution
\(q(z) = \frac{f}{l} z\)

Step 3: Apply PVW \[ \delta a \int_0^l 4 EI \, dz \cdot a + \delta a \cdot l^4 K \cdot a = \delta a \int_0^l -\frac{f}{l} z \cdot z^2 \, dz \] Step 4: Evaluate integrals \[ \delta a \left(4EI \cdot l + K \cdot l^4\right) a = \delta a \left(-\frac{1}{4} \frac{f}{l} l^4\right) \] \[ \delta a \left(4EI \cdot l + K \cdot l^4\right) a = \delta a \left(-\frac{1}{4} f l^3\right) \] Step 5: Solve for \(a\) \[ a = \frac{-\frac{1}{4} f l^3}{4EI \cdot l + K \cdot l^4} \] Step 6: Displacement at \(z = l\) \[ v = a \cdot l^2 \] Final Answer: \(v(z = l) = -66.67\, \text{mm}\)

True/False Questions (Put a T (true) or F (false) at the end of the sentence)

  1. a beam is loaded with a shear force; the torsional moment is equal to the value of the force multiplied by the distance between the force line of action and the barycenter: F (False - torsional moment depends on distance to shear center, not barycenter)
  2. the bending moment of over-constrained beam structures cannot be computed by resorting to the PVW: F (False - PVW can be applied to over-constrained structures)
  3. the axial stress of a beam transmitting a given bending moment \(M_x\) is function of the beam material elasic modulus \(E\): F (False - axial stress \(\sigma = My/I\) does not depend on \(E\))
