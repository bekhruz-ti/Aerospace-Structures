Exercise 1

The beam structure sketched in the figure is loaded at point C by the force F, applied the x direction. Compute the displacement, in the x direction, of point C. Neglect shear deformation. The coordinates of the points are given, with respect to the sketched reference system, in the data. (Unit for result: mm)

Data
A : (0; 1000; 0) mm
B : (1000; 1000; 0) mm
C : (1000; 0; 0) mm
D : (1000; 0; 1000) mm
\(F = 1000.0\, \text{N}\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = GJ = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(EA = 1 \times 10^4\, \text{N}\)

Answer \(u = 29.167\, \text{mm}\)

Solution:
Using the principle of virtual work with \(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\):
Internal forces in each member:
• Member 1 (A-B): \(N_1 = X\), \(M_{yy,1} = M_{zz,1} = M_{xx,1} = 0\)
• Member 2 (B-C): \(N_2 = 0\), \(M_{xx,2} = X \cdot z_2\), \(M_{yy,2} = 0\)
• Member 3 (C-D): \(N_3 = 0\), \(M_{xx,3} = (X-F)z_3\), \(M_{zz,3} = X \cdot l\)

Solving for reaction \(X\): \[X = \frac{\frac{1}{3}\frac{F l^3}{EI_{xx}}}{\frac{l}{EA} + \frac{2}{3}\frac{l^3}{EI_{xx}} + \frac{l^3}{GJ}}\] Displacement using virtual work with \(\tilde{M}_{xx,3} = -z_3\): \[u = \frac{1}{3}\frac{(F-X)l^3}{EI_{xx}} = 29.167\, \text{mm}\]

Exercise 2

Compute the critical buckling load F of the beam structure sketched in the figure. Note that the hinge and the two sliders prevent the out of plane displacement both in the x and y directions. (Unit for result: N)

Data
\(l = 4\, \text{m}\)
\(EI_{xx} = 3 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(EI_{yy} = 2 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)

Answer \(F_{cr} = 49348\, \text{N}\)

Solution:
The boundary conditions (hinge at top, slider at bottom) give an effective length factor of 0.5:
\[\hat{l} = \frac{l}{2} = 2\, \text{m} = 2000\, \text{mm}\] Buckling occurs about the weaker axis (y-y direction) where \(EI_{yy} = 2 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\).
Critical buckling load (Euler formula): \[F_{cr} = \frac{\pi^2 EI_{yy}}{\hat{l}^2} = \frac{\pi^2 \times 2 \times 10^{10}}{(2000)^2} = 49348\, \text{N}\]

Exercise 3

Consider the semi-monocoque cross-section sketched in the figure. All the paneles have thickness equal to t. Compute, with respect to the sketched reference system, the x coordinate of the shear center. (Unit for result: mm)

Data
\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(A_1 = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(A_2 = 250\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(l_1 = 200\, \text{mm}\)
\(l_2 = 300\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)

Answer \(x_{SC} = 140\, \text{mm}\)

Solution:
Centroid location: \(x_{CG} = \frac{2 A_2 l_2}{2(A_1 + A_2)}\)

Second moment of area about centroid: \(I_{xx,G} = 2(A_1 + A_2)\left(\frac{l_1}{2}\right)^2\)

Shear flows due to unit shear force \(F\):
\[\tilde{q}_1 = \frac{A_1 \cdot \frac{l_1}{2}}{I_{xx,G}} F, \quad \tilde{q}_2 = \frac{A_2 \cdot \frac{l_2}{2}}{I_{xx,G}} F, \quad \tilde{q}_3 = 0\] Equilibrium and zero twist condition for shear center: \[F \cdot x = \tilde{q}_2 \cdot l_1 \cdot l_2 + 2q^* \cdot l_1 \cdot l_2\] Setting twist rate \(\theta' = 0\) for the closed section and solving: \[x = \left[\tilde{q}_1 l_1 - \tilde{q}_2 l_1 + \tilde{q}_2(l_1 + l_2)\right] \frac{l_1 l_2}{(l_1 + l_2)F} = 140\, \text{mm}\]

Exercise 4

Consider the 3-D beam semi-monocoque beam model sketched in the figure, loaded at its extremity by the vertical force F, that is aligned to the panel going from stringer #1 to stringer #3. Compute the axial force in the concentrated area #1 (area equal to A) at a distance equal to s/2 from the clamp. (Unit for result: N)

Data
\(A = 100\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(l = 0.3\, \text{m}\)
\(s = 10\, \text{m}\)
\(F = 1000\, \text{N}\)
\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\);

Answer \(N_1 = 16667\, \text{N}\)

Solution:
For the triangular cross-section with side \(l\):
Centroid location: \(x_{CG} = \frac{1}{3}l\), \(y_{CG} = \frac{1}{3}l\)

Distance from centroid to stringer #1: \(d = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}l\)

Second moment of area about the neutral axis \(\hat{u}\): \[I_{\hat{u}\hat{u}} = \frac{A l^2}{3}\] Force decomposition (force aligned with panel 1-3): \[F_{\hat{y}} = \frac{F}{\sqrt{2}}\] Bending moment at \(z = s/2\): \[M_{\hat{x}} = F_{\hat{y}} \cdot \frac{s}{2}\] Axial force in stringer #1: \[N_1 = \frac{M_{\hat{x}}}{I_{\hat{u}\hat{u}}} \cdot A \cdot d = 16667\, \text{N}\]

Exercise 5

Consider the three-dimensional beam sketched in the figure, and loaded by the vertical force F. By re- sorting to the Ritz method, and adopting the simplest possible polynomial approximations for the vertical displacement and the torsional rotation, accounting for the torsional strain energy and for the bending strain energy of both beams, compute the approximated vertical displacement of the point of application of the force. Neglect shear deformability. (Unit for result: mm)

Data
\(l = 1\, \text{m}\)
\(F = 1000\, \text{N}\)
\(GJ = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)

Answer \(v_2 = -450\, \text{mm}\)

Solution:
Using Ritz method with simplest polynomial approximations:

Beam 1 (horizontal, torsion + bending):
\[\theta_1 = a \cdot z_1, \quad v_1 = b \cdot z_1^2\] Beam 2 (inclined, bending):
\[v_2 = c + d \cdot z_2 + e \cdot z_2^2\] Continuity conditions:
• \(v_2(0) = v_1(l)\) → \(c = b l^2\)
• \(v_2'(0) = \theta_1(l)\) → \(d = a l\)

Thus: \(v_2 = b l^2 + a l z_2 + e z_2^2\)

Curvatures: \(\theta_1' = a\), \(v_1'' = 2b\), \(v_2'' = 2e\)

Virtual work principle: \[\int_0^l \delta\theta_1' GJ \theta_1' + \delta v_1'' EI v_1'' \, dz_1 + \int_0^l \delta v_2'' EI v_2'' \, dz_2 = -\delta v_2(l) \cdot F\] Solving the system: \[a = \frac{-l F}{GJ}, \quad b = \frac{-l F}{4EI}, \quad e = \frac{-l F}{4EI}\] Displacement at force application point: \[v_2(l) = b l^2 + a l^2 + e l^2 = -450\, \text{mm}\]

Exercise 6

The two-node finite element sketched in the figure is loaded by the distributed force f(x). Compute the virtual work of the force for the virtual displacement of node #2. The overall lenght of the finite element is equal to 1 mm (Unit for result: N mm)

Data
\(f(x) = -4x^2 + 4x\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer \(W = \frac{1}{3}\, \text{N mm}\)

Solution:
For a two-node linear element, the shape functions are:
\[N_1(x) = 1 - x, \quad N_2(x) = x\] The virtual work for virtual displacement of node #2: \[\delta W = \int_0^1 \delta u_2 \cdot N_2(x) \cdot f(x) \, dx\] \[= \delta u_2 \int_0^1 x \cdot (-4x^2 + 4x) \, dx\] \[= \delta u_2 \int_0^1 (-4x^3 + 4x^2) \, dx\] \[= \delta u_2 \left[-x^4 + \frac{4x^3}{3}\right]_0^1\] \[= \delta u_2 \left(-1 + \frac{4}{3}\right) = \delta u_2 \cdot \frac{1}{3}\] Virtual work: \(W = \frac{1}{3}\, \text{N mm}\)

True/False Questions (Put a T (true) or F (false) at the end of the sentence)

  1. whenever a slender structure works in plane strain the axial deformation is null: T
  2. the critical buckling force for a simply supported compressed beam is inversely proportional to its slenderness (\(\propto 1/\lambda\)): F
    (The critical buckling force is proportional to \(1/\lambda^2\), not \(1/\lambda\))
  3. the relation \(\sigma_{zz} = E\varepsilon_{zz}\), where E is the elastic modulus, is correct only for a state of plane stress: F
    (This relation is also valid for uniaxial stress states, not only plane stress)
