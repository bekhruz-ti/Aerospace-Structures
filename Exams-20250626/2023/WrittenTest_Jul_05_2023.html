Exercise 1

The beam sketched in the figure is clamped at one end. A displacement \(v\) is prescribed at the other end, still leaving the beam extremity free to rotate. This is (of course) accomplished by applying a force \(F\) at the extremity whose actual value of the force is not given (only the prescribed displacement \(v\) is known). Compute the beam bending moment \(M_{xx}\) at \(z = l/3\) (from the clamp). Neglect shear deformation. (Unit for result: N mm)

Data \(l = 3000\, \text{mm}\)
\(v = 5\, \text{mm}\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 1 \times 10^{12}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(EA = 1 \times 10^4\, \text{N}\)
Answer Solution:
Bending moment: \(M_{x'x'} = -F \cdot z\)
Unit load moment: \(M'_{x'x'} = -z\)
Virtual work: \(\displaystyle \int_0^l \frac{F \cdot z^2}{EI} \, dz = v\)
\(\displaystyle \frac{F \cdot l^3}{3EI} = v \quad \Rightarrow \quad F = \frac{3 \cdot v \cdot EI}{l^3} = \frac{3 \times 5 \times 10^{12}}{(3000)^3} = 555.6\, \text{N}\)
At \(z = \frac{l}{3}\) from clamp (i.e., \(z = \frac{2l}{3}\) from free end):
\(\displaystyle M_{xx} = -F \cdot \frac{2}{3}l = -555.6 \times 2000 = \boxed{-1.11 \times 10^6\, \text{N mm}}\)

Exercise 2

The beam structure sketched in the figure has a prescribed rotation \(\theta_z\), around the z axis, at point C (a moment is applied there, leaving free the three displacement components and the other two rotation components). Compute the reaction force in the y direction transmitted by the constraint of point B. Neglect shear deformation. The coordinates of the points are given, with respect to the sketched reference system, in the data. The bending and torsional stinness of the beams are given with respect to local reference systems, with the z axis aligned with the beam axis. Be careful with the measurement units. (Unit for result: N)

Data \(A : (0; 1000; 0)\, \text{mm}\)
\(B : (1000; 1000; 0)\, \text{mm}\)
\(C : (1000; 1000; 1000)\, \text{mm}\)
\(\theta_z = 5^\circ\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(GJ = 2 \times 10^{11}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
Answer Solution:
Convert angle: \(\displaystyle \theta_z = 5° = \frac{5}{180}\pi \approx 0.08727\, \text{rad}\)
At point C: \(\theta_{z_2}^{(0)} = -\theta_z = -0.08727\, \text{rad}\)
Moments: \(M_{z_2} = -M\), \(M_{x_1} = -M - F \cdot z_1\) (where \(M\) and \(F\) are unknowns)
Beam length: \(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
Compatibility equations:
\(\displaystyle \int_0^l \frac{M}{GJ}\, dz_2 + \int_0^l \frac{M + F \cdot z_1}{EI_{xx}}\, dz_1 = -0.08727\)
\(\displaystyle \int_0^l \frac{(M + F \cdot z_1) \cdot z_1}{EI_{xx}}\, dz_1 = 0\)
Simplify:
\(\displaystyle \frac{M \cdot l}{GJ} + \frac{M \cdot l}{EI_{xx}} + \frac{F \cdot l^2}{2EI_{xx}} = -0.08727\)
\(\displaystyle \frac{M \cdot l^2}{2EI_{xx}} + \frac{F \cdot l^3}{3EI_{xx}} = 0\)
Solving the system:
\(M = -2.909 \times 10^6\, \text{N mm}\), \(\boxed{F = 4364\, \text{N}}\)

Exercise 3

Approximate the critical buckling load \(F\) of the structure sketched in the figure by resorting to a polynomial approximation of the beam transverse displacement truncated to one unknown coefficient. The stiffness of the linear spring attached at the beam extremity is equal to \(k\). Neglect shear deformation. (Unit for result: N)

Data \(EA = 1 \times 10^8\, \text{N}\)
\(l = 3\, \text{m}\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 1 \times 10^{12}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(k = 500\, \text{N/mm}\)
Answer Solution:
Assume displacement: \(v = a \cdot z^2\) (satisfies \(v(0) = 0\), \(v'(0) = 0\) at clamp)
Derivatives: \(v'' = 2a\), \(v' = 2az\), \(v(l) = al^2\)
Virtual work equation:
\(\displaystyle \int_0^l \left(\delta v'' \cdot EI \cdot v'' - F \cdot \delta v' \cdot v'\right) dz + \delta v(l) \cdot k \cdot v(l) = 0\)
Substitute and integrate:
\(\displaystyle \int_0^l \left(4\delta a \cdot EI \cdot a - 4F \cdot \delta a \cdot z^2 \cdot a\right) dz + \delta a \cdot k \cdot l^4 \cdot a = 0\)
\(\displaystyle \delta a \left(4EI \cdot l + k \cdot l^4 - \frac{4}{3}l^3 \cdot F\right) a = 0\)
Buckling condition: \(\displaystyle \frac{4}{3}l^3 \cdot F = 4EI \cdot l + k \cdot l^4\)
With \(l = 3000\, \text{mm}\):
\(\displaystyle F = \frac{4EI \cdot l + k \cdot l^4}{\frac{4}{3}l^3} = \frac{4 \times 10^{12} \times 3000 + 500 \times (3000)^4}{\frac{4}{3} \times (3000)^3} = \boxed{1.458 \times 10^6\, \text{N}}\)

Exercise 4

Consider the 3-D semi-monocoque beam model sketched in the figure, loaded at its extremity by the torsional moment \(M_z\). All the panels have thickness equal to \(t\). Compute torsional rotation angle \(\theta_z\) of the cross-section at \(z = \frac{2}{3}l\) from the free extremity. (Unit for result: rad)

Data \(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(a = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 500\, \text{mm}\)
\(l = 6000\, \text{mm}\)
\(A_1 = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(A_2 = 1000\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(M_z = 1 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}\)
Answer Solution:
Shear modulus: \(\displaystyle G = \frac{E}{2(1+\nu)} = \frac{70000}{2 \times 1.3} = 26923\, \text{MPa}\)
Torsion equilibrium: \(4ab \cdot q = M_z\)
Shear flow: \(\displaystyle q = \frac{M_z}{4ab} = \frac{10^9}{4 \times 1000 \times 500} = 500\, \text{N/mm}\)
By symmetry considerations: \(q_1^A = q_2^A\), so \(q_2 = 0\)
Twist rate: \(\displaystyle \dot{\theta} = \frac{1}{2abG}\left(\frac{2qa}{t} + \frac{qb}{t}\right) = 4.6429 \times 10^{-5}\, \text{rad/mm}\)
Position: \(z = \frac{2l}{3}\) from free end \(= \frac{l}{3}\) from clamp
\(\displaystyle \theta\left(\frac{l}{3}\text{ from clamp}\right) = \dot{\theta} \cdot \frac{l}{3} = 4.6429 \times 10^{-5} \times 2000 = \boxed{0.09286\, \text{rad}}\)

Exercise 5

Consider the three-dimensional beam sketched in the figure, and loaded by the vertical force \(F\), applied along one of the two axis of symmetry of the cross-section. The two horizontal plates have a cross-section area equal to \(A(\text{El#1})\) and \(A(\text{El#2})\), respectively and are placed at a distance equal to \(b\). The vertical plate has a cross-section area equal to \(A(\text{El#3})\). The overall barycenter moment of inertia of the beam cross section with respect to the horizontal axis is equal to \(I_{xx}\). The horizontal plates are connected to the vertical one by means of four L-shaped stringers (with negligible cross-section area), placed at the two sides of the vertical plate, as sketched in the figure. One solid rivet for each stringer connects the stringer to the horizontal plates. The vertical plate is connected to the two stringers in the upper position by a single rivet; a single rivet is used as well in order to connect the vertical plate to the two stringers in the lower position. All the rivet connections are repeated along the beam axis with a pitch equal to \(p\). The rivets shank diameter is equal to \(\phi\). Compute the average shear stress \(\tau\) in the shank of rivet #1 (riv#1 in the figure). (Unit for result: MPa)

Data \(I_{xx}(\text{barycentric}) = 1.5 \times 10^8\, \text{mm}^4\)
\(A(\text{El#1}) = 5000\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(A(\text{El#2}) = 5000\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(A(\text{El#3}) = 3500\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(a = 500\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 240\, \text{mm}\)
\(p = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(\phi = 7\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 10000\, \text{N}\)
Answer Solution:
Shear flow: \(\displaystyle |q| = \frac{A(\text{El\#2}) \cdot \frac{b}{2} \cdot F}{I_{xx}} = \frac{5000 \times 120 \times 10000}{1.5 \times 10^8} = 40\, \text{N/mm}\)
Rivet force (by symmetry, load shared by 2 rivets):
\(\displaystyle F_{\text{rivet}} = \frac{q}{2} \cdot p = \frac{40}{2} \times 100 = 2000\, \text{N}\)
Rivet shear stress:
\(\displaystyle \tau = \frac{F_{\text{rivet}}}{\frac{\phi^2 \pi}{4}} = \frac{2000}{\frac{49\pi}{4}} = \frac{2000}{38.485} = \boxed{51.97\, \text{MPa}}\)

Exercise 6

The clamped beam in the figure, with lenght \(l\), is loaded with a constant distributed axial force per unit of lenght \(f\). The beam response is approximated by means of a finite element model with two linear elements and three nodes, as sketched at the bottom of the figure. Compute the horizontal displacement of node #3 at the free extremity of the beam. Be careful with the measurement units. (Unit for result: mm)

3

Data \(l = 3\, \text{m}\)
\(EA = 7 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\)
\(f(x) = 10\, \text{N/mm}\)
Answer Solution:
Element length: \(\displaystyle \frac{l}{2} = 1500\, \text{mm}\)
Shape functions: \(\displaystyle N_1(z) = \frac{2}{l}\left(-z + \frac{l}{2}\right)\), \(\displaystyle N_2(z) = \frac{2z}{l}\)
Displacement: \(u = u_1 N_1(z) + u_2 N_2(z)\)
Derivative: \(\displaystyle u' = u_1\left(-\frac{2}{l}\right) + u_2\left(\frac{2}{l}\right)\)
Element stiffness: \(\displaystyle K_e = EA \begin{bmatrix} \frac{2}{l} & -\frac{2}{l} \\ -\frac{2}{l} & \frac{2}{l} \end{bmatrix}\)
Load vector per element: \(\displaystyle \int N^T f\, dz = f \begin{Bmatrix} \frac{l}{4} \\ \frac{l}{4} \end{Bmatrix}\)
After assembly and applying BC (\(u_1 = 0\)):
\(\displaystyle \begin{bmatrix} \frac{4}{l} & -\frac{2}{l} \\ -\frac{2}{l} & \frac{2}{l} \end{bmatrix} EA \begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix} = f \begin{Bmatrix} \frac{l}{2} \\ \frac{l}{4} \end{Bmatrix}\)
Solving the system:
\(u_2 = 0.4821\, \text{mm}\), \(\boxed{u_3 = 0.6429\, \text{mm}}\)
