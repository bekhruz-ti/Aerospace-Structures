Exercise 1

The beam structure sketched in the figure is made with a thin-walled square cross section, and is designed to whitstand a maximum load equal to \(F\). Compute the maximum value of the beam compressive stress at the clamp. Be careful with the measurement units.

(Unit for result: MPa)

Data \(e = 0.3\, \text{m}\)
\(e_1 = 0.29\, \text{m}\)
\(a = 3.0\, \text{m}\)
\(d = 0.4\, \text{m}\)
\(b = 1.0\, \text{m}\)
\(c = 0.8\, \text{m}\)
\(F = 2300\, \text{N}\)

Answer Solution:
The stress formula for combined axial load and bending: \[\sigma_{zz} = \frac{F_z}{A} + \frac{M_x}{I_{xx}} \cdot y\] Moment of inertia for hollow square section: \[I_{xx} = \frac{1}{12}\left(e^4 - e_1^4\right)\] Maximum distance from neutral axis: \[y_{max} = \frac{e}{2}\] Final Answer: \(\sigma_{zz} = -4.4202\, \text{MPa}\)

Exercise 2

The structure sketched in the figure is composed by two beams; the first one has a L shape, and is clamped at one extremity; the second one is hinged at both extremities, i.e. to the ground and to the corner of the first one. Compute the vertical displacement \(v\) at the point of application of the force \(F\). Neglect shear deformation. The coordinates of the points are given, with respect to the sketched reference system, in the data. The bending and torsional stiffness of the beams are given with respect to local reference systems, with the \(z\) axis aligned with the beam axis.

(Unit for result: mm)

Data \(A : (0; 1000; 0)\, \text{mm}\)
\(B : (1000; 1000; 0)\, \text{mm}\)
\(C : (1000; 1000; -1000)\, \text{mm}\)
\(D : (1000; 1500; 0000)\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 1 \times 10^5\, \text{N}\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(GJ = 2 \times 10^{11}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(F = 1000\, \text{N}\)

Answer Solution:
Using the Principle of Virtual Work with dummy load method.

Beam segment lengths: \(l_1 = 1000\, \text{mm}\), \(l_2 = 1000\, \text{mm}\), \(l_3 = 500\, \text{mm}\)

Segment 1 (A-B):
\(T_{z_1} = 0\), \(T_{y_1} = -F\), \(M_{x_1} = -Fz_1\)
Dummy: \(T'_{z_1} = 0\), \(T'_{y_1} = -1\), \(M'_{x_1} = -z_1\)

Segment 2 (B-C):
\(T_{y_2} = H_B - F\), \(M_{x_2} = (H_B - F)z_2\), \(M_{z_2} = F \cdot l\)
Dummy: \(T'_{y_2} = -1\), \(M'_{x_2} = -z_2\), \(M''_{x_2} = +z_2\)

Segment 3 (rod B-D):
\(w = \int_0^{l/2} \frac{F}{EA} \cdot \frac{2EA}{l} \, dz_3 = \frac{F \cdot l}{2EA}\)

Apply PVW: \(\delta W_{e_1} = 1 \cdot v\)
\[\delta W_{i_1} = \int_0^l M'_{x_1} \cdot \frac{M_{x_1}}{EI_{xx}} dz_1 + \int_0^l M'_{x_2} \cdot \frac{M_{x_2}}{EI_{xx}} dz_2 + \int_0^l M'_{z_2} \cdot \frac{M_{z_2}}{GJ} dz_2\] Solve system of equations for \(v\) and \(H_B\).

Final Answer: \(v = 42.6812\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 3

The semi-monocoque structure sketched in the figure is loaded by the force \(F\). All panels have thickness equal to \(t\). Compute the flux \(q_1\).

(Unit for result: N/mm)

Data \(a = 3000\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 300\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(t = 3\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 30000\, \text{N}\)

Answer Solution:
By symmetry of the structure:
\(q_1 = q_2\), \(q_3 = q_4\), \(q_6 = q_7\), \(q_5 = 0\)

Vertical equilibrium: \[b(2q'_1 + 2q'_6) = F\] Diagonal member length: \[l_6 = \sqrt{\frac{a^2}{4} + b^2}\] From compatibility and equilibrium, calculate the open section flow \(q^0_1\): \[q^0_1 = \frac{-q'_6 \cdot l_6 + q'_1 \cdot b}{b + \frac{a}{2} + l_6}\] Final shear flux: \[q_1 = q'_1 - q^0_1\] Final Answer: \(q_1 = 37.987\, \text{N/mm}\)

Exercise 4

Consider the semi-monocoque beam open cross section sketched in the figure; all panels have thickness equal to \(t\); all the concentrated areas are equal to \(A\). Compute the \(x\) position of the shear center.

(Unit for result: mm)

Data \(a = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 500\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 300\, \text{mm}^2\)

Answer Solution:
Second moment of area about x-axis: \[J_{xx} = 4A\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 + 4A\left(\frac{3a}{2}\right)^2\] Static moments of areas: \[S_{x_{12}} = -A\frac{3}{2}a, \quad S_{x_{78}} = A\frac{3}{2}a\] \[S_{x_{34}} = -A\frac{1}{2}a, \quad S_{x_{56}} = A\frac{1}{2}a\] Shear flows from open section analysis: \[q_1 = -F \cdot \frac{S_{x_1}}{J_{xx}} = +q_7\] \[q_2 = q_1 - F\frac{S_{x_2}}{J_{xx}} = q_6\] \[q_3 = q_2 - F\frac{S_{x_3}}{J_{xx}} = q_5\] \[q_4 = q_3 - F\frac{S_{x_4}}{J_{xx}}\] Rotational equilibrium about centroid: \[F \cdot x_{SC} = -q_3 \cdot 2\left(\frac{ab}{2}\right) + q_4 \cdot 2\left(\frac{ab}{2}\right) + q_5 \cdot 2\left(\frac{b \cdot 2a}{2}\right) - q_7 \cdot 2\left(\frac{b \cdot 3a}{2}\right)\] Final Answer: \(x_{SC} = 150\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 5

The two beams sketched in the figure are constrained by means of two clamps and one hinge connecting them together. A force \(F\) is applied to the hinge in the middle. By resorting to a parabolic approximation for each beam (two different parabolic functions, at least in principle), compute the approximated value of the critical buckling load \(F\).

(Unit for result: N)

Data \(EA_1 = 3.5 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\)
\(EA_2 = 1.4 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\)
\(EI_{xx1} = 1.5 \times 10^{11}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(EI_{xx2} = 1.5 \times 10^{11}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(l = 3500\, \text{mm}\)

Answer Solution:
Axial displacement compatibility at hinge: \[\mu = \frac{F \cdot l}{EA_1 + EA_2}\] Axial forces in each segment: \[N_1 = -\mu \cdot EA_1 = \frac{EA_1 \cdot l}{EA_1 + EA_2} \cdot F = d_1 \cdot F\] \[N_2 = \mu \cdot EA_2 = \frac{EA_2 \cdot l}{EA_1 + EA_2} \cdot F = d_2 \cdot F\] Parabolic approximation for lateral displacement: \[v_1 = a_1 z_1^2, \quad v_2 = a_2 z_2^2\] Matching condition at hinge: \(v_1(l) = v_2(l)\) \[\Rightarrow a_1 = a_2 = a\] Principle of Virtual Work: \[\int_0^l \left(\delta v_1'' \cdot EI_1 \cdot v_1'' + \delta v_1' \cdot N_1 \cdot v_1'\right) dz_1 + \int_0^l \left(\delta v_2'' \cdot EI_2 \cdot v_2'' + \delta v_2' \cdot N_2 \cdot v_2'\right) dz_2 = 0\] Critical buckling load: \[F = \frac{-8l \cdot EI_{11}}{(d_1 + d_2) \cdot \frac{4}{3} l^3}\] Final Answer: \(F = 1.7143 \times 10^5\, \text{N}\)

Exercise 6

The beam sketched in the figure is hinged at both extremities, and has a continuous elastic support, with stiffness \(k\), underneath (you see different springs in the figure, but they are meant to represent a contiunuous support, not a set of discrete springs). The beam is loaded by the vertical force per unit of length \(f\). By resorting to a trigonometric approximation of the transverse displacement, truncated to a single term, compute the approximated vertical displacement \(v\) in the middle of the beam.

(Unit for result: mm)

Data \(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EI = 1 \times 10^{12}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(k = 8\, \text{MPa}\)
\(f = 100\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer Solution:
Trigonometric approximation (single term): \[v = a \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\] Derivatives: \[v' = \frac{\pi}{l} a \cos\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\] \[v'' = -\frac{\pi^2}{l^2} a \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\] Principle of Virtual Work: \[\int_0^l \left(\delta v'' \cdot EI \cdot v'' + \delta v \cdot k \cdot v\right) dz = \int_0^l \delta v \cdot (-f) \, dz\] Integrate and solve for coefficient \(a\): \[a = \frac{-2fl}{\pi} \cdot \frac{1}{\frac{1}{2}\frac{\pi^4}{l^3} \cdot EI + \frac{1}{2} k l}\] At midpoint \(z = l/2\): \(\sin(\pi/2) = 1\), so \(v = a\) Final Answer: \(v = -1.1079\, \text{mm}\)
