Exercise 1

A thin-walled beam is fixed at one end and loaded at the free end with a distributed force per unit length \(q\). The total length is \(l\), while the geometry of the section is depicted in the figure. The lumped stringers have area \(A\), while the thickness of the panels is denoted with \(t\).

By considering a semi-monocoque approximation, determine the shear and the axial stresses acting at the mid-span section.

Given Data:

  • \(l = 6000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(a = 200 \text{ mm}\); \(b = 200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(c = 400 \text{ mm}\); \(d = 50 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 1.5 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 1000 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(q = 150 \left( \dfrac{x}{400} \right)^2 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(x \in [0, 400] \text{ mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Resultant Force

Integrate the distributed load to find the resultant force:

\[F_0 = \int_0^{400} 150 \left( \frac{\xi}{400} \right)^2 d\xi = 20 \text{ kN}\]

Step 2: Calculate Resultant Moment

Integrate to find the resultant moment about the reference point:

\[M_0 = \int_0^{400} 150 \left( \frac{\xi}{400} \right)^2 \xi \, d\xi = 6 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N·mm}\]

Step 3: Shear Flows and Stresses

The shear flows and stresses are obtained as:

Step 4: Axial Stress in the Stringers

Note: The vertical position of the principal axis origin is at 350 mm from the bottom. The horizontal position is available from the symmetry of the section.

The bending moment expression:

\[M_x = -F_0 \cdot z\]

At the mid-span section \(z = 3000 \text{ mm}\):

\[M_x = -6.00 \cdot 10^7 \text{ N·mm}\]

Step 5: Calculate Stringer Stresses

The stresses on the stringers are:

Stringer 1 (top):

\[\sigma_{xx_1} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}} (600 - 350) = -78.95 \text{ MPa}\]

Stringers 2 and 3 (left and right):

\[\sigma_{xx_2} = \sigma_{xx_3} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}} (400 - 350) = -15.79 \text{ MPa}\]

Stringer 4 (bottom):

\[\sigma_{xx_4} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}} (-350) = 110.53 \text{ MPa}\]
Final Answers:

Shear stresses: \(\tau_{max} = 17.83 \text{ MPa}\) (bottom left member)

Axial stresses: \(\boxed{\sigma_{xx_1} = -78.95 \text{ MPa}, \quad \sigma_{xx_2} = \sigma_{xx_3} = -15.79 \text{ MPa}, \quad \sigma_{xx_4} = 110.53 \text{ MPa}}\)

Exercise 2

A structure is composed of two beams. The geometric and elastic properties, calculated with respect to the principal axes, are denoted as \(EA_i\), \(EJ_i\) and \(GJ_i\), with \(i = 1, 2\), as illustrated in the figure. Note, \(EJ_i = EJ_{xx} = EJ_{yy}\). The structure is fixed at one end, and elastically constrained by means of two linear springs of stiffness \(k_1\) and \(k_2\).

Considering a loading condition with an axial force \(P\) applied at the free end and a linearly varying distributed load \(q = q(x)\), determine the horizontal and vertical displacement in correspondence of the two springs.

Given Data:

  • \(l_1 = 1000 \text{ mm}\); \(l_2 = 800 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA_1 = 5.7 \times 10^7 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ_1 = 9.0 \times 10^8 \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ_1 = 4.0 \times 10^8 \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(EA_2 = 2.8 \times 10^7 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ_2 = 7.0 \times 10^8 \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ_2 = 2.0 \times 10^8 \text{ N·mm}^2\)
  • \(k_1 = 2.8 \text{ N/mm}\); \(k_2 = 3.5 \times 10^4 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(q = 6 \times 10^{-3} \dfrac{x}{800} \text{ N/mm}\); \(P = 40 \text{ kN}\)

Solution

Step 1: Define Reference Systems

Define local coordinate systems for each beam:

  • Beam 1: coordinates \((x_1, y_1, z_1)\)
  • Beam 2: coordinates \((x_2, y_2, z_2)\)

Express the distributed load as:

\[q(z_2) = a \cdot z_2 \quad \text{with} \quad a = 7.5 \cdot 10^{-6} \text{ N/mm}^2\]

Step 2: Internal Moment Equations

Beam 2 (horizontal):

\[M_x^{(2)} = -a \frac{z_2^3}{6}\]

Beam 1 (inclined):

\[M_x^{(1)} = -\left( \frac{a \ell_2^2}{2} + Y \right) z_1\] \[M_y^{(1)} = -(F + X) z_1\] \[M_z^{(1)} = \frac{a \ell_2^3}{6}\]

where \(X\) and \(Y\) are the spring reaction forces.

Step 3: Virtual Moment Fields (Dummy Load Method)

Dummy #1 (Unit vertical load)

\[^1\delta M_x^{(2)} = 0\] \[^1\delta M_x^{(1)} = -z_1\] \[^1\delta M_y^{(1)} = 0\] \[^1\delta M_z^{(1)} = 0\]

Dummy #2 (Unit horizontal load)

\[^2\delta M_x^{(2)} = 0\] \[^2\delta M_x^{(1)} = 0\] \[^2\delta M_y^{(1)} = -z\] \[^2\delta M_z^{(1)} = 0\]

Step 4: Apply Principle of Complementary Virtual Work (PCVW)

First equation (for vertical reaction Y):

\[\left( \frac{\ell_1^3}{3} + \frac{EJ_1}{k_1} \right) Y = -\frac{R \ell_1^3}{3} \quad \text{where} \quad R = \frac{a \ell_2^2}{2}\]

Second equation (for horizontal reaction X):

\[\left( \frac{\ell_1^3}{3} + \frac{EJ_2}{k_2} \right) X = -\frac{F \ell_1^3}{3}\]

Step 5: Solve for Reactions and Displacements

Solving the system of equations:

\[X = -40 \text{ kN}\] \[Y = -1.22 \text{ N}\]

The spring displacements are:

\[S_{\text{vertical}} = -\frac{Y}{k_1} = 0.44 \text{ mm}\] \[S_{\text{horizontal}} = -\frac{X}{k_2} = 1.14 \text{ mm}\]
Final Answers:

\[\boxed{\text{Vertical displacement} = 0.44 \text{ mm}}\] \[\boxed{\text{Horizontal displacement} = 1.14 \text{ mm}}\]
