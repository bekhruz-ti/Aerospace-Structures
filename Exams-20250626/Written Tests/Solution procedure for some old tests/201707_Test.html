Written Test, July 10th, 2017

Exercise 1

Consider the structure in the figure. The three beams are characterized by axial stiffness \(EA\), bending stiffness \(EJ\) and torsional stiffness \(GJ\). The two ends are fixed, while two forces of intensity \(P\) are applied as illustrated in the sketch. Determine the reaction forces at the two fixed ends and plot the internal actions.

Given Data:

  • \(EA = 1.8 \times 10^6 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ_{xx} = EJ_{yy} = EJ = 3.75 \times 10^6 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ = 3.00 \times 10^6 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(l = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(P = 1.5 \text{ kN}\)

Solution

Step 1: Exploit Anti-Symmetry

Anti-symmetry conditions can be used for simplifying the solution procedure. The structure can be analyzed using half of the system with appropriate boundary conditions.

The unknowns at the symmetry plane are:

  • \(X\) - horizontal force
  • \(Y\) - vertical force
  • \(W\) - moment

Step 2: Internal Actions for Real System

Beam 1:
\[M_x^{(1)} = Yz_1\] \[M_y^{(1)} = -Xz_1\] \[M_z^{(1)} = W\]
Beam 2:
\[M_x^{(2)} = W + (Y - P)z\] \[M_y^{(2)} = -X \frac{l}{2}\] \[M_z^{(2)} = -Y \frac{l}{2}\] \[T_z^{(2)} = -X\]

Step 3: Virtual Systems (Dummy Loads)

Dummy #1 (Unit force in Y direction):
\[{}^1\delta M_y^{(1)} = -z_1\] \[{}^1\delta M_y^{(2)} = -l/2\] \[{}^1\delta T_z^{(2)} = -1\]
Dummy #2 (Unit force in Z direction):
\[{}^2\delta M_x^{(1)} = z_1\] \[{}^2\delta M_x^{(2)} = z_2\] \[{}^2\delta M_z^{(2)} = -l/2\]
Dummy #3 (Unit moment):
\[{}^3\delta M_z^{(1)} = 1\] \[{}^3\delta M_x^{(2)} = 1\]

Step 4: Apply PCVW (Principle of Complementary Virtual Work)

The compatibility equations are:

\[X = 0\] \[\left( \frac{65l^3}{24EJ} + \frac{l^3}{2GJ} \right) Y + \frac{2l^2}{EJ} W = \frac{8}{3} \frac{Pl^3}{EJ}\] \[\frac{2l^2}{EJ} Y + \left( \frac{l}{2GJ} + \frac{2l}{EJ} \right) W = \frac{2Pl^2}{EJ}\]

Step 5: Solve the System

From the equations above:

\[Y = 947.36 \text{ N}\] \[W = 421052 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\]

Step 6: Calculate Reaction Forces

The reaction forces are obtained by evaluating the internal actions of beam #2 at \(z_2 = 2l\):

\[M_x^{(2)}(2l) = W + (Y - P) \cdot 2l = -684948 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\] \[M_y^{(2)}(2l) = -X \frac{l}{2} = 0 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\] \[M_z^{(2)}(2l) = -Y \frac{l}{2} = -473500 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\] \[T_y^{(2)}(2) = Y - P = -553 \text{ N}\]

Note: Forces are in N, Moments are in NÂ·mm

Step 7: Plot of Internal Actions

Shear Force \(T_y\):
\[T_y^{(1)} = 947 \text{ N}\] \[T_y^{(2)} = -553 \text{ N}\]
Bending Moment \(M_x\):
\[M_x^{(1)} = 947 \, z_1 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\] \[M_x^{(2)} = 421052 - 553 \, z_2 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\]
Bending Moment \(M_z\):
\[M_z^{(1)} = 421052 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\] \[M_z^{(2)} = -473500 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\]
Final Answers:
\[Y = 947.36 \text{ N}, \quad W = 421052 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\] \[M_x^{(2)}(2l) = -684948 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\] \[M_z^{(2)}(2l) = -473500 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\] \[T_y^{(2)} = -553 \text{ N}\]

Exercise 2

A thin-walled beam section is modeled according to the semi-monocoque scheme, as reported in the figure. All the panels have thickness equal to 1 mm, while the lumped area of the stringers, viz. including the contribution of the panels, is equal to \(A\); the dimensions \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\) are reported below. An internal shear force \(T\) is considered as reported in the figure.

Determine: 1. the state of internal shear stress; 2. the position of the shear center of the section.

Given Data:

  • \(A = 200 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(a = 300 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(c = 100 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(T = 3000 \text{ N}\)

Solution

Solution Procedure

  1. Evaluate \(x_{sc}\) (shear center x-coordinate)
  2. Determine the shear flows by letting \(x_{sc} = 0\)

Step 1: Section Properties

\[I_{xx} = Ab^2\]

First moments of area:

\[S_{x_1} = -\frac{Ab}{2} \qquad S_{x_3} = -Ab\] \[S_{x_2} = -Ab \qquad S_{x_4} = -\frac{Ab}{2}\]

Step 2: Initial Shear Flows (Open Section)

Shear flows for open section (shear center contribution):

\[q_1^I = -\frac{F}{2b}\] \[q_2^I = -\frac{F}{b}\] \[q_3^I = -\frac{F}{b}\] \[q_4^I = -\frac{F}{2b}\]

Step 3: Shear Center Position

Equivalence with respect to x:
\[2ab \, q_1^* + bc \, q_2^* - Fl = F \frac{a}{2} + Fc\]

where \(\quad l = a - x_{sc}\)

Compatibility (\(\theta_1^I = \theta_2^I\)):
\[\left[ 2(a+b) + \bar{\Omega}b \right] q_1^* - \left[ b + \bar{\Omega}(2d+b) \right] q_2^* = F \left( \frac{a}{b} - 2\bar{\Omega} \frac{d}{b} \right)\]

where \(\quad \bar{\Omega} = \frac{\Omega_{cell_1}}{\Omega_{cell_2}}\)

Compatibility (\(\theta_1^I = 0\)):
\[2 q_1^* (a+b) - q_2^* b = F \frac{a}{b}\]

Step 4: Solve System of Equations

The solving equations are (dividing by \(1 \times 10^5\)):

\[\begin{cases} 1.2 \, q_1^* + 0.2 \, q_2^* - 0.03 \, l = 75 \\ 0.022 \, q_1^* - 0.031 \, q_2^* = -0.2086 \\ 0.01 \, q_1^* - 0.002 \, q_2^* = 0.045 \end{cases}\]

From which:

\[l = 100.32 \text{ mm}\]

Step 5: Shear Center Position

\[\boxed{x_{sc} = 199.68 \text{ mm}}\]

From symmetry: \(\quad y_{sc} = 0\)

Step 6: Calculate Shear Flows

The solving equations for shear flows are:

\[\begin{cases} 2ab \, q_1^* + bc \, q_2^* = \frac{3}{2} Fa + Fc \\ \left[ 2(a+b) + \bar{\Omega}b \right] q_1^* - \left[ b + \bar{\Omega}(2d+b) \right] q_2^* = F \left( \frac{a}{b} - 2\bar{\Omega} \frac{d}{b} \right) \end{cases}\]

From which:

\[q_1^* = 11.29 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_2^* = 14.78 \text{ N/mm}\]

Step 7: Total Shear Flows

Combining the open section flows with the redundant flows:

\[q_1 = q_1^I + q_1^* = 3.79 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_2 = q_2^I + q_2^* = -0.22 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_3 = q_3^I + q_2^* = -0.22 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_4 = q_4^I + q_1^* = 3.79 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_5 = q_1^* = 11.29 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_6 = q_1^* - q_2^* = -3.49 \text{ N/mm}\]
Final Answers:

Shear Center Position: \[\boxed{x_{sc} = 199.68 \text{ mm}, \quad y_{sc} = 0}\]
