Exercise 1

The thin-walled beam in the figure is characterized by a semi-circular section with five webs of thickness \(t\) and four stringers of area \(A\) (note: \(A\) is the equivalent stringer area, inclusive of the contribution due to the panels). The length of the beam is denoted with \(l\). The material is homogeneous and isotropic with Young modulus \(E\) and Poisson's ratio 0.3. The beam is grounded with two linear elastic springs of stiffness \(k\), as reported in the sketch, and is loaded with a concentrated force \(P\) applied at one of the two ends. Determine the reaction forces in correspondence of the springs by accounting for the beam shear deformability, and plot the internal actions in the beam.

Given Data:

  • \(E = 70 \text{ GPa}\); \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(A = 200 \text{ mm}^2\); \(t = 1 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(R = 200 \text{ mm}\); \(l = 2000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(k = 10^6 \text{ N/mm}\); \(P = 1 \text{ kN}\)

Solution

Step 1: Evaluation of Section Properties — \(GA^*\)

By imposing:

  • Shear flow equations
  • Equivalence to moment
  • Compatibility \((\theta'_1 = \theta'_2)\)
  • Compatibility \((\theta'_1 = 0)\)

It is obtained:

\[x_{sc} = 122.20 \text{ mm}\]

The corresponding shear flows are:

\[q_1 = q_4 = 1.5275 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_2 = q_3 = -0.9725 \text{ N/mm}\]

From which:

\[A^* = \frac{T_y^2}{\displaystyle\sum_i \frac{q_i^2 \ell_i}{t_i}} = 654.65 \text{ mm}^2\] \[GA^* = 1.76 \cdot 10^7 \text{ N}\]

Step 2: Evaluation of Section Properties — \(EJ\)

\[EJ = E(2AR^2) = 1.12 \cdot 10^{12} \text{ N mm}^2\]

Step 3: Evaluation of Section Properties — \(GJ\)

A torsional moment \(M_t = 1 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N mm}\) is considered:

The resulting shear flows are:

\[q_1 = q_2 = q_3 = q_4 = 7.96 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_5 = 0\]

The torsional constant is:

\[J_t = \frac{M_t}{\dfrac{1}{2\Omega_{cell_k}} \displaystyle\sum \frac{q_k \ell_k}{t_k}} = 1.54 \cdot 10^7 \text{ mm}^4\]

Note: The summation is over panels belonging to cell #k. Can consider here cell #1 or #2.

And so:

\[GJ = G \cdot J_t = 4.13 \cdot 10^{11} \text{ N mm}^2\]

Step 4: Reaction Forces Setup

The beam axis runs along the shear center portions. Define \(d = x_{sc}\).

Real System — Region ①

\[T_y^{①} = -(P + X)\] \[M_x^{①} = -(P + X)z_1\] \[M_z^{①} = -(P + X)d\]

Real System — Region ②

\[T_y^{②} = -(P + X + Y)\] \[M_x^{②} = -(P + X)\ell_2 - (P + X + Y)z_2\] \[M_z^{②} = -(P + X + Y)d\]

Step 5: Dummy Load Cases

Dummy #1 — Region ①

\[{}^1\delta T_y^{①} = -1\] \[{}^1\delta M_x^{①} = -z_1\] \[{}^1\delta M_z^{①} = -d\]

Dummy #1 — Region ②

\[{}^1\delta T_y^{②} = -1\] \[{}^1\delta M_x^{②} = -\ell_2 - z_2\] \[{}^1\delta M_z^{②} = -d\]

Dummy #2 — Region ①

\[{}^2\delta T_y^{①} = 0\] \[{}^2\delta M_x^{①} = 0\] \[{}^2\delta M_z^{①} = 0\]

Dummy #2 — Region ②

\[{}^2\delta T_y^{②} = -1\] \[{}^2\delta M_x^{②} = -z\] \[{}^2\delta M_z^{②} = -d\]

Step 6: Principle of Virtual Work (PEVW) Equations

The equations are:

\[\left(\frac{\ell^3}{3EJ} + \frac{\ell}{GA^*} + \frac{d^2\ell}{GJ} + \frac{1}{k}\right)X + \left(\frac{\ell}{2GA^*} + \frac{5}{48}\frac{\ell^3}{EJ} + \frac{d^2\ell}{2GJ}\right)Y + \left(\frac{\ell^3}{3EJ} + \frac{\ell}{GA} + \frac{d^2\ell}{GJ}\right)P = 0\] \[\left(\frac{\ell}{2GA^*} + \frac{5\ell^3}{48EJ} + \frac{d^2\ell}{2GJ}\right)X + \left(\frac{\ell}{2GA^*} + \frac{\ell^3}{24EJ} + \frac{d^2\ell}{2GJ} + \frac{1}{k}\right)Y + \left(\frac{5\ell^3}{48EJ} + \frac{d^2\ell}{2GJ} + \frac{\ell}{2GA^*}\right)P = 0\]
Final Answer — Reaction Forces: \[\boxed{X = -998.71 \text{ N}}\] \[\boxed{Y = -2.74 \text{ N}}\]

Exercise 2

The bar in the figure is made of isotropic material of Young modulus \(E\) and Poisson's ratio \(\nu\). The section varies from \(A_0\) to \(A_1\) with a linear variation along the bar axis. The bar is loaded with a uniformly distributed load per unit length \(\hat{n}\), and a concentrated force \(P\) is applied at the tip.

Determine stress and displacement at the midspan by using a Ritz solution with one degree of freedom. Compare the predicted stress with the exact solution and discuss how the Ritz solution is expected to behave if the number of trial functions is increased.

Given Data:

  • \(E = 70 \text{ GPa}\); \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(A_0 = 5 \text{ mm}^2\); \(A_1 = 15 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(l = 400 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(P = 1 \text{ kN}\); \(\hat{n} = 2 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Define Trial Function

The trial function is taken as:

\[u = c\left(\frac{x}{\ell}\right) = c\phi \quad \text{with} \quad \phi = \frac{x}{\ell}\]

Step 2: Stiffness Matrix (1×1)

Internal virtual work:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta u_{,x} \, EA \, u_{,x} \, dx = \delta c \, k \, c\]

where:

\[k = \int_0^\ell \phi_{,x} \, EA \, \phi_{,x} \, dx\] \[= \int_0^\ell \frac{1}{\ell^2} \left(EA_0 + E\frac{A_1 - A_0}{\ell}x\right) dx\] \[= \frac{EA_0}{\ell} + E\frac{A_1 - A_0}{2\ell}\]

Step 3: External Forces

External virtual work:

\[\delta W_e = \int_0^\ell \delta u \, \hat{n} \, dx + \delta u(\ell) \, P = \delta c \, f\]

where:

\[f = \int_0^\ell \phi \, \hat{n} \, dx + \phi(\ell) P\] \[= \frac{\hat{n}\ell}{2} + P\]

Step 4: Solve for Displacement Coefficient

\[c = \frac{f}{k} = 0.80 \text{ mm}\]

Step 5: Displacement and Stress at \(x = \ell/2\)

Displacement:

\[u = \phi(\ell/2) \cdot c = \frac{1}{2} \, c = 0.40 \text{ mm}\]

Stress:

\[\sigma = E \, u_{,x} = E \, \phi_{,x}(\ell/2) \, c\] \[= E \, \frac{1}{\ell} \cdot c = 140 \text{ MPa}\]

Step 6: Comparison with Exact Solution

Exact stress value:

\[\sigma = \frac{P + \hat{n}\ell/2}{A(\ell/2)} = 140 \text{ MPa}\]
Final Answer:

At midspan (\(x = \ell/2\)):

\[\boxed{u = 0.40 \text{ mm}}\] \[\boxed{\sigma_{Ritz} = 140 \text{ MPa}}\] \[\boxed{\sigma_{exact} = 140 \text{ MPa}}\]

The Ritz solution with one DOF gives the exact stress at midspan.
