Written Test, February 11th, 2019

Exercise 1

The two-cell beam in the figure is composed of five panels of thickness \(t\), and four stringers of areas \(A\) and \(2A\) (note: the area is the lumped one). The section is square and has dimension \(h\).

The material is a light aluminum alloy, whose elastic properties are provided in terms of Young's modulus and Poisson's coefficient, denoted with \(E\) and \(\nu\), respectively. The beam is fixed at one end and free at the other one, and is loaded with a concentrated force of magnitude \(P\).

Determine the internal stresses at a distance \(l/2\) from the free end.

Given Data:

  • \(h = 100 \text{ mm}\); \(l = 1500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 1.5 \text{ mm}\); \(A = 150 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(E = 72000 \text{ MPa}\); \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(P = 15 \text{ kN}\)

Solution

Step 1: Section Properties

The principal axes are available from the symmetry of the section.

Calculate the diagonal dimension:

\[a = h\sqrt{2} = 141.42 \text{ mm}\]

Second moments of area:

\[J_{xx} = 2A \left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 = 1.5 \times 10^6 \text{ mm}^4\] \[J_{yy} = 4A \left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 = 3.0 \times 10^6 \text{ mm}^4\]

Static moments at each node:

Node \(S_x\) \(S_y\)
1 \(S_{x_1} = 0\) \(S_{y_1} = -Aa\)
2 \(S_{x_2} = A \cdot a/2\) \(S_{y_2} = -Aa\)
3 \(S_{x_3} = A \cdot a/2\) \(S_{y_3} = 0\)
4 \(S_{x_4} = 0\) \(S_{y_4} = 0\)

Step 2: Shear Flows

Open section shear flows:

\[q_1^I = +\frac{P_x S_{y_1}}{J_{yy}} + \frac{P_y S_{x_1}}{J_{xx}} = -\frac{P_x}{a} = -75 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_2^I = 0 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_3^I = \frac{P_y}{a} = 75 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_4^I = q_5^I = 0\]

Step 3: Equivalence Condition (with respect to point 1)

\[2\Omega_2 q_2^I + 2\Omega_3 q_3^I + 2\Omega_{C_1} q_1^* + 2\Omega_{C_2} q_2^* = 0\]

Where the cell areas are:

\[\Omega_2 = \Omega_3 = \Omega_{C_1} = \Omega_{C_2} = \frac{a^2}{2}\]

Therefore:

\[q_2^I + q_3^I + q_1^* + q_2^* = 0\]
\[q_1^* + q_2^* = -75\]

Step 4: Compatibility Condition

\[q_1^* - q_2^* = \frac{(q_3^I - q_1^I) h}{2(h + a)}\]
\[q_1^* - q_2^* = 31.07\]

Step 5: Solve for Redundant Shear Flows

System of equations:

\[\begin{cases} q_1^* + q_2^* = -75 \\ q_1^* - q_2^* = 31.07 \end{cases}\]

Solution:

\[q_1^* = -21.97 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_2^* = -53.04 \text{ N/mm}\]

Step 6: Total Shear Flows and Shear Stresses

Panel Shear Flow \(q\) (N/mm) Shear Stress \(\tau\) (MPa)
1 \(q_1 = q_1^I + q_1^* = -96.97\) \(\tau_1 = 64.65\)
2 \(q_2 = q_2^* = -53.04\) \(\tau_2 = 35.36\)
3 \(q_3 = q_3^I + q_2^* = 21.96\) \(\tau_3 = 14.64\)
4 \(q_4 = q_1^* = -21.97\) \(\tau_4 = 14.65\)
5 (diagonal) \(q_5 = q_2^* - q_1^* = -31.08\) \(\tau_5 = 20.72\)

Step 7: Axial Stresses \(\sigma_{zz}\)

Referring to the principal axes:

\[M_x = P_y \cdot \frac{l}{2}\] \[M_y = P_x \cdot \frac{l}{2}\]

The stresses are:

\[\sigma_{zz} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}} y - \frac{M_y}{J_{yy}} x\]

Stringers 2, 4: \(x = 0\), \(y = \pm a/2\)

\[\sigma_{zz} = \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}} \left(\pm \frac{a}{2}\right) = \pm 375 \text{ MPa}\]

Stringers 1, 3: \(x = \pm a/2\), \(y = 0\)

\[\sigma_{zz} = -\frac{M_y}{J_{yy}} \left(\pm \frac{a}{2}\right) = \mp 187.5 \text{ MPa}\]
Final Results:

Shear Stresses: \(\tau_1 = 64.65\) MPa, \(\tau_2 = 35.36\) MPa, \(\tau_3 = 14.64\) MPa, \(\tau_4 = 14.65\) MPa, \(\tau_5 = 20.72\) MPa

Axial Stresses: Stringers 2,4: \(\boxed{\sigma_{zz} = \pm 375 \text{ MPa}}\)  |  Stringers 1,3: \(\boxed{\sigma_{zz} = \mp 187.5 \text{ MPa}}\)

Exercise 2

The slender beam in the figure is fixed at one end, and is elastically supported by two springs of stiffness \(k_x\) and \(k_y\) at the other end. The beam axial stiffness is \(EA\), while the bending stiffnesses are \(EJ_{xx}\) and \(EJ_{yy}\) (note: \(EJ_{xy} = 0\)). A concentrated force \(P\), whose components are \(P_x\), \(P_y\) and \(P_z\), is applied at the middle.

Determine the unknown reaction forces and the strain energy stored in the structure (including the springs).

Given Data:

  • \(l = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA = 7 \times 10^7 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ_{xx} = 5 \times 10^9 \text{ Nmm}^2\)
  • \(EJ_{yy} = 8 \times 10^9 \text{ Nmm}^2\)
  • \(k_x = 5 \text{ N/mm}\); \(k_y = 2 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(P_x = 150 \text{ N}\); \(P_y = 100 \text{ N}\); \(P_z = 10000 \text{ N}\)

Solution

Problem Setup

The structure is 2 times statically indeterminate.

Step 1: Analysis in xz-Plane

Real system bending moments:

\[M_y^1 = X z_1\] \[M_y^2 = X l + (X + P_x) z_2\]

Dummy system bending moments:

\[\delta M_y^1 = z_1\] \[\delta M_y^2 = z_2 + l\]

Principle of Complementary Virtual Work (PCVW):

\[\int_0^l \frac{M_y^1 \cdot \delta M_y^1}{EJ_{yy}} dz_1 + \int_0^l \frac{M_y^2 \cdot \delta M_y^2}{EJ_{yy}} dz_2 + \frac{X}{k_x} = 0\]

This yields:

\[X \left(\frac{8}{3} l^3 + \frac{EJ_{yy}}{k_x}\right) + \frac{5}{6} P_x l^3 = 0\]

Step 2: Analysis in yz-Plane

By analogy with the xz-plane, it is immediate to obtain:

\[Y \left(\frac{8}{3} l^3 + \frac{EJ_{xx}}{k_y}\right) + \frac{5}{6} P_y l^3 = 0\]

Step 3: Reaction Forces

Solving the compatibility equations:

\[X = -29.27 \text{ N}\]
\[Y = -16.13 \text{ N}\]

Step 4: Internal Strain Energy

The total strain energy consists of:

\[U = U_{bxz} + U_{byz} + U_{ax} + U_{springs}\]

Where:

  • \(U_{bxz}\) = bending energy in xz-plane
  • \(U_{byz}\) = bending energy in yz-plane
  • \(U_{ax}\) = axial strain energy
  • \(U_{springs}\) = energy stored in springs

Bending energy in xz-plane:

\[U_{bxz} = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^l \frac{M_y^1 M_y^1}{EJ_{yy}} dz_1\] \[= \frac{1}{EJ_{yy}} \left(\frac{2}{3} X^2 l^3 + \frac{1}{6} (X + P_x)^2 l^3 + \frac{1}{2} X (X + P_x) l^3\right)\] \[= 0.154 \text{ J}\]

Bending energy in yz-plane:

\[U_{byz} = \frac{1}{EJ_{xx}} \left(\frac{2}{3} Y^2 l^3 + \frac{1}{6} (Y + P_y)^2 l^3 + \frac{1}{2} Y (Y + P_y) l^3\right)\] \[= 0.133 \text{ J}\]

Axial strain energy:

\[U_{ax} = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^l \frac{P_z P_z}{EA} dz_2 = \frac{1}{2} \frac{P_z^2}{EA} l = 0.714 \text{ J}\]

Spring energy:

\[U_{springs} = \frac{1}{2} \frac{X^2}{k_x} + \frac{1}{2} \frac{Y^2}{k_y} = 0.150 \text{ J}\]
Final Results:

Reaction Forces: \(\boxed{X = -29.27 \text{ N}}\)  |  \(\boxed{Y = -16.13 \text{ N}}\)

Strain Energy Components:
\(U_{bxz} = 0.154 \text{ J}\), \(U_{byz} = 0.133 \text{ J}\), \(U_{ax} = 0.714 \text{ J}\), \(U_{springs} = 0.150 \text{ J}\)

Total Strain Energy: \(\boxed{U_{total} = 1.151 \text{ J}}\)
