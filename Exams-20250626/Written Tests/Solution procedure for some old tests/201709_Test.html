Exercise 1

The structure in the figure is composed of three beams of length \(l\), which are characterized by the same elastic properties. In particular, the bending and torsional stiffnesses are denoted with \(EJ\) and \(GJ\), respectively. The structure is elastically supported by a torsional spring of stiffness \(k_t\) at one end. A linearly varying force per unit length is applied along one of the beams, as illustrated in the sketch. The force per unit length is equal to zero at one end, and is equal to \(\bar{q}\) at the other end. Determine the reaction forces and plot the internal actions.

Given Data:

  • \(EJ = 72 \times 10^6 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(GJ = EJ/2\)
  • \(k_t = 72 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\)
  • \(l = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\bar{q} = 100 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Define Reference Systems

Define local coordinate systems for each beam:

  • Beam 1: axes \(x_1\), \(y_1\), \(z_1\)
  • Beam 2: axes \(x_2\), \(y_2\), \(z_2\)
  • Beam 3: axes \(x_3\), \(y_3\), \(z_3\)

Step 2: Real System Analysis

Note: \(X\) is a torsional moment. The distributed load varies as: \[q(z_1) = \frac{\bar{q}}{l} z_1\]

Internal Moments:

Beam 1:
\[M_x^{(1)} = -\frac{\bar{q}}{6l} z_1^3\] \[M_z^{(1)} = X\]
Beam 2:
\[M_x^{(2)} = -Y z_2\]
Beam 3:
\[M_x^{(3)} = X - \left(\frac{\bar{q}l}{2} + Y\right) z_3\] \[M_z^{(3)} = \frac{\bar{q}l^2}{6} - Yl\]

Step 3: Dummy Load Analysis

Dummy #1 (Unit Torsional Moment):

\[^1\delta M_z^{(1)} = 1\] \[^1\delta M_x^{(3)} = 1\]

Dummy #2 (Unit Force):

\[^2\delta M_x^{(2)} = -z_2\] \[^2\delta M_x^{(3)} = -z_3\] \[^2\delta M_z^{(3)} = -l\]

Step 4: Principle of Complementary Virtual Work (PCVW)

Equation 1:

\[\left(\frac{l}{GJ} + \frac{l}{EJ} + \frac{1}{k_t}\right) X - \frac{l^2}{2EJ} Y = \frac{\bar{q}l^3}{4EJ}\]

Equation 2:

\[\frac{l^2}{2EJ} X - \left(\frac{2l^3}{3EJ} + \frac{l^3}{GJ}\right) Y = \frac{l^3}{3EJ} \frac{\bar{q}l}{2} - \frac{l^4 \bar{q}}{6GJ}\]
Final Answers:
  • \[\boxed{X = 28043.5 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}}\]
  • \[\boxed{Y = 6255.3 \text{ N}}\]

Exercise 2

The beam in the figure is made of an isotropic material with modulus \(E\) and Poisson's coefficient \(\nu\). It is characterized by a square cross section of dimension \(a\), where \(a(x) = \gamma \sqrt[4]{a_0 + (a_1 - a_0)\frac{x}{l}}\), and \(x \in [0, l]\).

The first end of the beam is fixed; the second one is free to translate along the transverse direction, while the rotation is prevented. A concentrated load of magnitude \(P\) is applied at \(x = l\).

Assume a Euler-Bernoulli beam model, and determine: the transverse displacement at \(x = l\); the maximum stress at \(x = l/4\). To this aim, apply the method of Ritz and, using polynomial functions, solve the problem by considering one single shape function.

Discuss how the problem can be formulated if two shape functions are adopted (report the relevant equations; no calculations are required).

Given Data:

  • \(E = 72000 \text{ N/mm}^2\)
  • \(l = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\gamma = 10 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(a_0 = 1\)
  • \(a_1 = 16\)

Solution

Step 1: Cross-Section Properties

Cross-section dimension function:

\[a(x) = \gamma \sqrt[4]{a_0 + (a_1 - a_0) \frac{x}{l}}\]

Bending stiffness:

\[EJ = \frac{1}{12} a^4 E = \frac{1}{12} E \left(\gamma^4 a_0 + (a_1 - a_0) \frac{x}{l}\right)\]

This can be written as:

\[EJ = EJ_0 + EJ_1 \left(\frac{x}{l}\right)\]

where:

\[EJ_0 = \frac{1}{12} E \gamma^4 a_0\] \[EJ_1 = \frac{1}{12} E \gamma^4 (a_1 - a_0)\]

Step 2: Trial Functions

General polynomial form:

\[w = C_0 + C_1 x + C_2 x^2 + C_3 x^3\]

The essential boundary conditions are:

\[\begin{cases} w(0) = 0 \\ w_{,x}(0) = 0 \\ w_{,x}(l) = 0 \end{cases}\]

Applying these conditions:

\[w = C_3 \left(x^3 - \frac{3}{2} l x^2\right)\]

Step 3: Non-dimensional Form

The expression is re-organized to obtain a non-dimensional trial function (not strictly necessary!).
\[w = C \left(\left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^3 - \frac{3}{2}\left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^2\right)\]

So \(w = C_* \phi\) with shape function:

\[\phi = \left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^3 - \frac{3}{2}\left(\frac{x}{l}\right)\]

Step 4: Stiffness Matrix

Internal virtual work:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^l \delta w_{,xx} \, EJ \, w_{,xx} \, dx\] \[= \delta C \, k \, C\]

where the stiffness coefficient:

\[k = \int_0^l \left[EJ_0 + EJ_1 \left(\frac{x}{l}\right)\right] \phi_{,xx}^2 \, dx\] \[= \frac{1}{l^3} \left(3EJ_0 + \frac{3}{2} EJ_1\right)\]

Step 5: External Loads

External virtual work:

\[\delta W_e = \delta w(l) \, P\] \[= \delta C \, f\]

where:

\[f = -\frac{1}{2} P\]

Step 6: Solution

Solving for the coefficient \(C\):

\[C = \frac{f}{k} = -\frac{l^3}{2} \frac{P}{3EJ_0 + \frac{3}{2}EJ_1} = -32.68 \text{ mm}\]

Step 7: Displacement at \(x = l\)

Evaluating the shape function at \(x = l\):

\[\phi(l) = 1 - \frac{3}{2} = -\frac{1}{2}\]

Therefore:

\[w(l) = C \cdot \phi(l) = (-32.68) \times \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) = 16.34 \text{ mm}\]

Step 8: Stress at \(x = l/4\)

The stress is given by:

\[\sigma = E \varepsilon = E(-y \, w_{,xx})\] \[= E(-y \, \phi_{,xx}) C\]

At \(x = l/4\):

\[\sigma(l/4) = -E \, y \, \phi_{,xx}\left(\frac{l}{4}\right) C\]

The maximum stress is obtained at \(y = a/2\), so:

\[\sigma(l/4) = -E \frac{a}{2} \phi_{,xx}\left(\frac{l}{4}\right) C\]

where \(a = 14.76 \text{ mm}\) at \(x = l/4\).

Final Answers:
  • Coefficient: \(\boxed{C = -32.68 \text{ mm}}\)
  • Displacement at \(x = l\): \(\boxed{w(l) = 16.34 \text{ mm}}\)
  • Maximum stress at \(x = l/4\): \(\boxed{\sigma = 26.05 \text{ MPa}}\)
  • Cross-section dimension at \(x = l/4\): \(a = 14.76 \text{ mm}\)
