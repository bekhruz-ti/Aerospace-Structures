Exercise 1

Consider the thin-walled beam reported in the figure. The beam has a total length equal to \(l\), while the ribs are equally spaced with pitch \(d\). The load is a pressure distribution \(p = p_0 \frac{\eta}{b+c}\), uniform along the beam axis and linear along the chord.

The cross section of the beam is sketched here below.

By making use of the semi-monocoque scheme, determine: the shear flows and the shear stresses acting on the panels, and the axial force carried by the stringers for the section at \(\xi = 900\) mm.

Given Data:

  • \(d = 400\) mm
  • \(l = 2000\) mm
  • \(p_0 = 2\text{e-}2\) N/mm²
  • \(a = 200\) mm
  • \(b = 200\) mm
  • \(c = 300\) mm
  • \(t = 4\) mm
  • \(h = 20\) mm
  • All panels have thickness 0.8 mm

Solution to Exercise 1

Step 1: Stringer Area Calculation

The stringer area is calculated from the L-shaped profile:

\[A_{st} = th + t(h - t) = 144\, \text{mm}^2\]

Step 2: Lumped Areas

The cross-section has 6 stringers numbered 1-6 (top: 1, 2, 3; bottom: 6, 5, 4). The lumped areas include contributions from stringers and adjacent panel sections:

\[A_1 = A_6 = A_{st} + \frac{1}{2}(b + a)t = 304\, \text{mm}^2\] \[A_3 = A_4 = A_{st} + \frac{1}{2}(a + c)t = 344\, \text{mm}^2\] \[A_2 = A_5 = 2A_{st} + \frac{1}{2}(a + b + c)t = 568\, \text{mm}^2\]

Step 3: Section Properties

The neutral axis \(x\) passes through the center due to symmetry of the section.

First moments of area for each stringer:

\[S_{x_1} = -S_{x_6} = A_1 \frac{a}{2} = 30400\, \text{mm}^3\] \[S_{x_2} = -S_{x_5} = A_2 \frac{a}{2} = 56800\, \text{mm}^3\] \[S_{x_3} = -S_{x_4} = A_3 \frac{a}{2} = 34400\, \text{mm}^3\]

Second moment of area:

\[J_{xx} = 2(A_1 + A_2 + A_3)\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 = 24{,}320{,}000\, \text{mm}^4\]

Step 4: External Loads

The triangular pressure distribution is converted to an equivalent line load:

\[q = p \frac{b + c}{2} = 5\, \text{N/mm}\]

The resultant load acts at \(\frac{2}{3}(b+c)\) from one edge and \(\frac{1}{3}(b+c)\) from the other edge (centroid of triangular distribution).

Step 5: Internal Actions

Assuming that loads are introduced by ribs, discrete loads of \(qd\) act at each rib location, with \(\frac{qd}{2}\) at the end.

Internal actions at \(\xi = 900\) mm:

Shear force:

\[T = -q \cdot d\left(2 + \frac{1}{2}\right) = -\frac{5}{2}\, qd = -5000\, \text{N}\]

Bending moment:

\[M = -q \cdot d \cdot (1050) = -2{,}100{,}000\, \text{Nmm}\]

Step 6: Evaluation of Shear Stresses

A preliminary remark: once the internal actions at \(\xi = 900\) mm are available, one can arbitrarily consider the section at the left or right side of the "cut". We consider here the left side (but considering the right one would be correct as well).

Step 7: Shear Flow Results

The shear flow distribution (all units in N/mm):

2.75 → 1.00 →
↓ 9.00 ↓ 9.93 ↓ 6.07
← 2.75 ← 1.00

The shear stresses are obtained as \(\tau_i = q_i / t_i\)

Shear stress distribution (all units in MPa):

3.44 → 1.26 →
↓ 11.26 ↓ 12.41 ↓ 7.58
← 3.44 ← 1.26

Step 8: Axial Forces in the Stringers

Using the bending formula:

\[F_1 = A_1 \frac{M}{J_{xx}} \left(\frac{a}{2}\right) = -2625\, \text{N}\] \[F_2 = A_2 \frac{M}{J_{xx}} \left(\frac{a}{2}\right) = -4904\, \text{N}\] \[F_3 = A_3 \frac{M}{J_{xx}} \left(\frac{a}{2}\right) = -2970\, \text{N}\]

\(F_i < 0\) indicates compression.

By symmetry:

\[F_6 = -F_1 \qquad F_4 = -F_3\] \[F_5 = -F_2\]
Final Answers:

Shear Flows: 2.75, 9.00, 9.93, 6.07, 1.00 N/mm
Shear Stresses: 3.44, 11.26, 12.41, 7.58, 1.26 MPa
Axial Forces:
\(F_1 = -2625\) N, \(F_2 = -4904\) N, \(F_3 = -2970\) N
\(F_4 = 2970\) N, \(F_5 = 4904\) N, \(F_6 = 2625\) N

Exercise 2

Consider the beam reported in the figure. It is fixed at one end, and hinged at the other end. The bending stiffness of the beam varies linearly from \(EJ_0\) at \(\xi = 0\) to \(EJ_1\) at \(\xi = 2a + b\). Two linear springs of stiffness \(k\) restrain the transverse displacement at \(\xi = a\) and \(\xi = a + b\). A uniform force per unit length of magnitude \(q\) is applied in the interval \(a + b \leq \xi \leq 2a + b\).

Assume a Euler-Bernoulli beam model and apply the method of Ritz to obtain an approximation of the displacement field. Consider a single-degree-of-freedom description for the bending displacement.

Given Data:

  • \(a = 300\) mm
  • \(b = 400\) mm
  • \(l = 2a + b = 1000\) mm
  • \(q = 20\) N/mm
  • \(EJ_0 = 1\text{e}9\) Nmm²
  • \(EJ_1 = 2\, EJ_0\)
  • \(k = 100 \frac{EJ_0}{l^3} = 100\) N/mm

Solution to Exercise 2

Step 1: Trial Function Selection

A polynomial function is considered. The beam has:

  • 2 essential boundary conditions at the fixed end (\(W(0) = 0\), \(W_{,x}(0) = 0\))
  • 1 essential boundary condition at the hinged end (\(W(l) = 0\))

3 essential conditions + 4 DOF approximation = 4 terms in the polynomial expansion:

\[W = a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + a_3 x^3\]

Step 2: Imposing Boundary Conditions

Applying the essential boundary conditions:

\[\begin{cases} W(0) = 0 \\ W_{,x}(0) = 0 \\ W(l) = 0 \end{cases} \Rightarrow W = a_3 \left(x^3 - l x^2\right)\]

Step 3: Non-dimensional Trial Function

The expression can be re-arranged (this is not strictly necessary!) to have a non-dimensional trial function:

\[W = C \frac{1}{l^3} \left(x^3 - l x^2\right)\]

where \(C\) has dimensions of length [l] and the remaining term is non-dimensional.

This gives:

\[W = C \phi \qquad \text{where} \qquad \phi = \left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^3 - \left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^2\]

Step 4: Stiffness Matrix

The virtual work of internal forces (with \(l = 2a + b\)):

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^l \delta w_{,xx}\, EJ\, w_{,xx}\, dx + \delta w(a)\, k\, w(a) + \delta w(b)\, k\, w(b)\] \[= \delta C\, K\, C\]

where the generalized stiffness is:

\[K = \int_0^l \phi_{,xx}\, EJ\, \phi_{,xx}\, dx + \phi(a)\, k\, \phi(a) + \phi(b)\, k\, \phi(b)\] \[= 9.56\, \text{N/mm}\]

Step 5: External Load

The virtual work of external forces:

\[\delta W_e = \int_{a+b}^{2a+b} \delta w\, q\, dx\] \[= \delta C\, f\]

where the generalized force is:

\[f = \int_{a+b}^{2a+b} \phi\, q\, dx = -580.50\, \text{N}\]

Step 6: Solution

From the equilibrium equation \(KC = f\):

\[C = \frac{f}{K} = -60.74\, \text{mm}\]
Final Answer:

The displacement field is approximated as: \[\boxed{W = -60.74 \left[\left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^3 - \left(\frac{x}{l}\right)^2\right] \text{ mm}}\]
