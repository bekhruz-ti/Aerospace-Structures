Exercise 1

The slender L-shaped beam structure in the figure is clamped at one extremity and has a torsional spring with torsional stiffness \(K_\theta\) (such that \(M = K_\theta\theta\)) at a distance \(l\) from the clamp. Compute the sketched vertical displacement \(v\) due to the sketched force \(F\). Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: mm)

Data \(l = 1\, \text{m}\)
\(K_\theta = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}\)
\(EI = 1 \times 10^{15}\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(GJ = 7 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(F = 150\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:

In the real system, substitute the torsional spring with reactions: \(M_{K_\theta} = -K_\theta \cdot \theta\), from which \(\theta = -\frac{M_{K_\theta}}{K_\theta}\)

Segment Analysis (3 segments):
① \(M_{x_1} = F \cdot z_1\), \(M'_{x_1} = -z_1\)
② \(M_{x_2} = F \cdot z_2\), \(M_{z_2} = -F \cdot \ell\), \(M'_{x_2} = -z_2\), \(M'_{z_2} = 1 \cdot \ell\)
③ \(M_{x_3} = F(\ell + z_3)\), \(M_{z_3} = -F \cdot \ell - M_{K_\theta}\), \(M'_{x_3} = -(\ell + z_3)\), \(M'_{z_3} = (\ell + z_3)\)

PVW (independent equations):
\[\delta W_e = -1 \cdot \theta = -1 \cdot \frac{M_{K_\theta}}{K_\theta}\] \[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \frac{M''_{z_3} \cdot M_{z_3}}{GJ}\, dz_3 = \frac{F\ell^2}{GJ} + \frac{M_{K_\theta} \cdot \ell}{GJ}\] Solving for \(M_{K_\theta}\): \[M_{K_\theta} = -\frac{F\ell^2}{GJ} \left(\frac{1}{K_\theta} + \frac{\ell}{GJ}\right)^{-1}\] Virtual work for displacement:
\[\delta W_e = 1 \cdot v\] Final displacement formula: \[v = -F\ell^3 \left(\frac{3}{EI} + \frac{5}{2GJ}\right) - M_{K_\theta} \ell^2 \cdot \frac{3}{2GJ}\]
Final Answer: \(v \approx -0.27\, \text{mm}\) (magnitude \(\approx 0.27\, \text{mm}\))

Exercise 2

Consider the two-cells semi-monocoque beam model sketched in the figure. All the six stringers have area equal to \(A\), and all the seven panes have thickness equal to \(t\). The beam is clamped at one extremity, and loaded by the horizontal force \(F\) at the other. Compute the shear flux \(q\) sketched in the figure.

(Unit for result: N mm\(^{-1}\))

Data \(A = 100\, \text{mm}^2\)

\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(a = 250\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(l = 1\, \text{m}\)
\(E = 77000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.33\)
\(F = 50000\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:

Note: The beam behavior does not enter the cross-section shear flow calculation.

With reference system: \(T_x = -F\)

Static moments (DEN 6/7):
\[S_{y_1} = S_{y_3} = S_{y_5} = -S_{y_2} = -S_{y_4} = -S_{y_6} = -\frac{1}{2}Aa\] Second moment of area:
\[J_{yy} = 6 \cdot A \left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{3}{2}Aa^2\] Open section shear flows:
\[q'_2 = q'_4 = q'_1 = -\frac{T_x \cdot S_{y_1}}{J_{yy}} = +\frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{T_x}{a} = q'\] \[q'_3 = q'_5 = 0 \quad \text{(since } S_{y_1} + S_{y_2} = 0\text{)}\] Horizontal equilibrium w.r.t. \(s\):
\[0 = 2ab \cdot q^*_1 + 2ab \cdot q^*_2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad q^*_1 = -q^*_2 = q^*\] Compatibility (twist rate equality):
\[\theta'_1 = \frac{1}{2Gtab}\left(q^*_1 \cdot 2(a+b) + (1-1)q' \cdot a - q^*_2 \cdot a\right)\] \[\theta'_2 = \frac{1}{2Gtab}\left(q^*_2 \cdot 2(a+b) + (1-1)q' \cdot a - q^*_1 \cdot a\right)\] From \(\theta'_1 = \theta'_2\): \(q^*_1(3a + 2b) = q^*_2(3a + 2b)\)
Combined with \(q^*_1 = -q^*_2\): \(q^* = q^*_1 = q^*_2 = 0\)

Final shear flux: \[q_1 = q'_1 = +\frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{T_x}{a} = -\frac{F}{3a}\]
Final Answer: \(q = -\frac{50000}{3 \times 250} = -66.67\, \text{N/mm}\)

Exercise 3

Consider the vertical slender beam model with length \(l\) sketched in the figure. It is constrained by an hinge at one extremity and a slider at the other. Two horizontal springs, with stiffness \(K_1\) and \(K_2\), are attached at a distance \(l_1\) from the hinge. By approximating the transverse displacement with a polyno- mial function with only one unknown estimate the critical buckling compressive force \(F\) of the structure. Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: N)

Data \(l = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
\(l_1 = 750\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 6 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N}\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 12 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(GJ = 7 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(K_1 = 75 \times 10^2\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(K_1 = 25 \times 10^2\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer

Solution:

PVW with Euler-Bernoulli beam and nonlinear Green-Lagrange strain tensor for infinitesimal displacements.

Combined spring stiffness: \(k = K_1 + K_2\)

From PVW equilibrium: \(N = -F\) (axial force)

Displacement assumption:
Take \(v = Az^2 + Bz + C\) with boundary conditions:
- \(v(0) = 0\) (hinge)
- \(v(\ell) = 0\) (slider)

This gives: \(v = A(z^2 - z\ell)\)
\[v'_z = A(2z - \ell), \quad \delta v'_z = \delta A(2z - \ell)\] \[v''_{zz} = 2A, \quad \delta v''_{zz} = 2\delta A\] PVW equation:
\[\delta A \int_0^\ell 2EI \cdot 2A - (2z-\ell)F \cdot A(2z-\ell)\, dz = -\delta A(\ell_1^2 - \ell_1\ell)k \cdot A(\ell_1^2 - \ell_1\ell)\] Taking \(A \neq 0\):
\[4EI\ell - \int_0^\ell (2z-\ell)^2 F\, dz = (\ell_1^2 - \ell_1\ell)^2 k\] \[4EI\ell - \frac{4}{3}F\ell^3 - F\ell^3 + \frac{4}{2}F\ell^3 = k(\ell_1^2 - \ell_1\ell)^2\] Critical buckling force: \[F = \frac{k(\ell_1^2 - \ell_1\ell)^2 + 4EI\ell}{2\ell^3 - \ell^3 - \frac{4}{3}\ell^3}\] With \(k = 7500 + 2500 = 10000\, \text{N/mm}\), \(\ell_1^2 - \ell_1\ell = 750^2 - 750 \times 2000 = -937500\, \text{mm}^2\)


Final Answer: \(F \approx 1.37 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\)

Exercise 4

Consider the slender cantilever beam sketched in the figure, with length \(l\). It has a torsional spring, at a distance equal to \(l/2\) from the clamp, that works for the bending rotation \(\theta\) of the beam (i.e. the derivative of the transverse displacement). The beam is loaded by the concentrated vertical force \(F\), as sketched. By assuming a quadratic transverse displacement approximation estimate the bending rotation \(\theta\) at \(l/2\). Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: rad)

Data \(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(K_\theta = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{Nmm}\)
\(EI = 12 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(F = 150\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:

Displacement assumption:
Assume \(v(z) = Az^2\) due to boundary conditions \(v(0) = 0\), \(v'(0) = 0\)
\[v'_z = 2Az, \quad v''_{zz} = 2A\] PVW formulation:
\[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta v''_{zz} \cdot EI \cdot v''_{zz}\, dz\] \[\delta W_e = -\delta v(\ell) \cdot F - \delta v'_z\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right) \cdot K_\theta \cdot v'_z\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right)\] Substituting the displacement assumption:
\[\delta A \cdot 4EI\ell \cdot A = -\delta A\left(F\ell^2 + 4 \cdot \frac{\ell}{2} \cdot K_\theta \cdot \frac{\ell}{2} \cdot A\right)\] Solving for \(A\):
\[A = -\frac{F\ell^2}{K_\theta\ell^2 + 4EI\ell} = -\frac{F\ell}{K_\theta\ell + 4EI}\] Bending rotation at \(\ell/2\):
\[\theta = v'_z\left(z = \frac{\ell}{2}\right) = 2A \cdot \frac{\ell}{2} = A\ell = \frac{F\ell^2}{K_\theta\ell + 4EI}\] Numerical calculation:
\[\theta = \frac{150 \times 1000^2}{1 \times 10^{10} \times 1000 + 4 \times 12 \times 10^9}\] \[\theta = \frac{1.5 \times 10^8}{1 \times 10^{13} + 4.8 \times 10^{10}} = \frac{1.5 \times 10^8}{1.0048 \times 10^{13}}\]
Final Answer: \(\theta \approx 1.49 \times 10^{-5}\, \text{rad}\)

Exercise 5

The same two-cells semi-monocoque beam model of Exercise 2 is loaded at its free end by the forces \(F_1\) and \(F_2\), as sketched. Compute the axial strain (\(\varepsilon_{zz}\)) of stringer B at the clamp (cfr. the figure).

(Unit for result: -)

Data \(A = 100\, \text{mm}^2\)

\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(a = 250\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(l = 1\, \text{m}\)
\(E = 77000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.33\)
\(F_1 = 50000\, \text{N}\)
\(F_2 = 2500\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:

Internal moments at the clamp:
\[M_x = -F_2 \cdot \ell = -2500 \times 1000 = -2.5 \times 10^6\, \text{N·mm}\] \[M_y = +F_1 \cdot \ell = +50000 \times 1000 = 5 \times 10^7\, \text{N·mm}\] Second moments of area:
\[J_{xx} = 4Ab^2 = 4 \times 100 \times 100^2 = 4 \times 10^6\, \text{mm}^4\] \[J_{yy} = \frac{3}{2}Aa^2 = \frac{3}{2} \times 100 \times 250^2 = 9.375 \times 10^6\, \text{mm}^4\] Axial strain formula:
\[\varepsilon_{zz} = \frac{1}{E}\sigma_{zz} = \frac{1}{E}\left(\frac{M_x}{J_{xx}} \cdot b - \frac{M_y}{J_{yy}} \cdot \frac{a}{2}\right)\] Numerical calculation:
\[\varepsilon_{zz} = \frac{1}{77000}\left(\frac{-2.5 \times 10^6 \times 100}{4 \times 10^6} - \frac{5 \times 10^7 \times 125}{9.375 \times 10^6}\right)\] \[\varepsilon_{zz} = \frac{1}{77000}\left(-62.5 - 666.67\right)\] \[\varepsilon_{zz} = \frac{-729.17}{77000}\]
Final Answer: \(\varepsilon_{zz} \approx -9.47 \times 10^{-3}\)

Exercise 6

The clamped slender beam sketched in the figure has a circular cross-section. It is loaded by the vertical force \(F\) applied at the free end on the lateral side of the cross-section, cfr. the sketch. Compute the torsional rotation at the free end. Neglect shear deformability..

(Unit for result: rad)

Data \(l = 200\, \text{mm}\)
\(r = 10\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 2.5 \times 10^7\, \text{N}\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 6 \times 10^8\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(GJ = 4 \times 10^8\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(F = 2000\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:

The force \(F\) applied at the lateral side of the cross-section creates a torsional moment.

Torsional moment:
\[M = -F \cdot r\] Torsional rotation assumption:
\[\theta(x) = Ax, \quad \theta'(x) = A\] PVW formulation:
\[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta\theta' \cdot GJ \cdot \theta'\, dx\] \[\delta W_e = \delta\theta(\ell) \cdot M\] Applying PVW:
\[\int_0^\ell GJA\, dx = \ell \cdot (-F \cdot r)\] \[GJA\ell = -Fr\ell\] \[A = -\frac{Fr}{GJ}\] (This can also be obtained from \(M = GJ\theta'\))

Torsional rotation at free end:
\[\theta(\ell) = A\ell = -\frac{Fr\ell}{GJ}\] Numerical calculation:
\[\theta(\ell) = -\frac{2000 \times 10 \times 200}{4 \times 10^8} = -\frac{4 \times 10^6}{4 \times 10^8}\]
Final Answer: \(\theta = -0.01\, \text{rad}\) (or \(|\theta| = 0.01\, \text{rad}\))
