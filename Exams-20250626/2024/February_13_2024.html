Exercise 1

The semi-monocoque wing box model represented in the figure has five uniformly spaced ribs (including the rib located at the clamp) and ten stringers (five uniformly spaced on the upper side, five uniformly spaced on the lower side). Each stringer has a concentrated area \(A\). The panels thickness is equal to \(t\). The wing box is loaded by a pressure load that is constant along the wing span and has a triangular distribution along the chord, with the maximum value equal to \(p\). Compute the upper right stringer jump of axial stress \(\Delta\sigma_{zz}\) across the mid-span rib.

(Unit for result: MPa)

Data \(4l = 3\, \text{m}\)
\(a = 1\, \text{m}\)
\(b = 10\, \text{cm}\)
\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 10\, \text{cm}^2\)

\(p = 1000\, \text{Pa}\)

Answer

Solution:

The pressure load has a triangular distribution along the chord and is constant along the span. At each rib location, the resultant force is: \[F = \frac{1}{2} p \cdot a \cdot a\] Since the loading is constant along the span (uniform distribution), the bending moment \(M_x\) does not change across the mid-span rib: \[\Delta M_x = 0\] Therefore, the jump in axial stress in the stringer across the mid-span rib is: \[\boxed{\Delta\sigma_{zz} = 0\, \text{MPa}}\]

Exercise 2

The semi-monocoque beam model sketched in the figure has a triangular cross-section with three con- centrated areas \(A\), two panels of thickness \(t\) and one panel of thickness \(2t\). The beam has length \(l\), is clamped at one extremity and loaded by the force \(F\), aligned with the vertical panel, at the free extremity. Compute the overall torsional rotation of the loaded extremity section.

(Unit for result: rad)

Data \(a = 0.5\, \text{m}\)
\(A = 4\, \text{cm}^2\)

\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(l = 4\, \text{m}\)
\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(F = 10000\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:

Convert to consistent units: \(a = 500\, \text{mm}\), \(A = 400\, \text{mm}^2\), \(l = 4000\, \text{mm}\)

Shear modulus: \(G = \frac{E}{2(1+\nu)} = \frac{70000}{2(1.3)} = 26923\, \text{MPa}\)

For the triangular cross-section:
  • \(T_y = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}F = -T_x\)
  • \(b = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}a\), \(c = 2b\)
  • \(J_{xx} = 2Ab^2\), \(J_{yy} = \frac{2}{3}Ab^2\)
Static moments and shear flows calculated:
\(S_{x_1} = -S_{x_3} = -Ab\), \(S_{x_2} = 0\)
\(S_{y_1} = S_{y_3} = -\frac{Ab}{3}\), \(S_{y_2} = \frac{2Ab}{3}\)

Using the compatibility equation: \(0 = 2\Omega_{cell} q^*\) where \(\Omega_{cell} = \frac{1}{2}cb\)

Twist rate: \[\theta' = \frac{1}{2\Omega_{cell} G}\left(\frac{q^* \cdot c}{2t} + \frac{q_1' \cdot a}{t} + \frac{q_2' \cdot a}{t}\right)\] Total rotation: \[\boxed{\theta = -(\theta' \cdot l) = 0.059\, \text{rad}}\]

Exercise 3

Consider the thin beam model sketched in the figure, and loaded by the force \(F\) applied at point \(A\). All the beams have the same cross-section. Compute the rotation \(\theta\) of the point where the beam of length \(b\) is joined to the two beams of length \(a\). Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: rad)

Data \(a = 1\, \text{m}\)
\(b = 2\, \text{m}\)
\(c = 3\, \text{m}\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 12 \times 10^{14}\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(EA = 6 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N}\)
\(GJ = 7 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(K = 1\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(F = 10000\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:

Convert to consistent units: \(a = 1000\, \text{mm}\), \(b = 2000\, \text{mm}\), \(c = 3000\, \text{mm}\)

Using the Principle of Virtual Work with three beam segments:

Segment 1 (length a): Axial member
\(T_{z_1} = -F - H_{Y_A}\), \(T'_{z_1} = -1\)

Segment 2 (length b): Bending
\(M_x = (-F - H_{Y_A})z_2\), \(M'_x = (-1) \cdot z_2\)

Segment 3 (length c): Combined bending and torsion
\(M_{z_3} = (F + H_{Y_A})b\), \(M'_{z_3} = (1)b\)
\(M_{x_3} = (-F - H_{Y_A}) \cdot z_3\), \(M'_{x_3} = (-1)z_3\)

Virtual work equations: \[\delta W_e' = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \cdot \frac{H_{Y_A}}{k} \\ 1 \cdot \theta \end{bmatrix}\] \[\delta W_i' = \int_0^a T'_{z_1} \frac{T_{z_1}}{EA}dz_1 + \int_0^b M'_{x_2} \frac{M_{x_2}}{EI}dz_2 + \int_0^c M'_{z_3} \frac{M_{z_3}}{GJ}dz_3 + \int_0^c M'_{x_3} \frac{M_{x_3}}{EI}dz_3\] Solving \(\delta W_e' = \delta W_i'\): \[\boxed{\theta = 1.3816 \times 10^{-5}\, \text{rad}}\]

Exercise 4

Consider the simply supported thin beam model sketched in the figure, with overall length \(l\), and loaded by the piece-wise constant force per unit of length \(a\). Compute the rotation \(\theta(l/2)\) in the middle of the beam. Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: rad)

Data \(a = 1000\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(l = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EI = 12 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)

Answer

Solution:

The beam has three equal spans of length \(\ell/3\) each with alternating distributed loads.

Global Equilibrium:
\[H_{Y_A} = -H_{Y_B} \quad \Rightarrow \quad H_{Y_A} = -\frac{3}{4}a\ell\] From moment equilibrium: \[8H_{Y_B} = \frac{2a \cdot (\ell/2) \cdot (\ell/4)}{(\ell/6)} = \frac{3}{4}a\ell\] Virtual loads: \(H'_{Y_A} = -\frac{3}{\ell}\), \(H'_{Y_B} = \frac{3}{\ell}\)

Segments 1 and 3: Dummy (unloaded in virtual system)

Segment 2 (and symmetric segment 4):
\[M_{x_2} = -\left[a \cdot \left(\frac{\ell}{3} + z_2\right)\right] \cdot \left(\frac{\ell}{3} + z_2\right) \cdot \frac{1}{2} + \frac{3}{4}a\ell \cdot z_2\] \[M'_{x_2} = \frac{3}{\ell} \cdot z_2\] Principle of Virtual Work: \[\theta = 2\int_0^{\ell/6} M'_{x_2} \cdot \frac{M_{x_2}}{EI}dz_2\] \[\boxed{\theta\left(\frac{l}{2}\right) = 0.09\, \text{rad}}\]

Exercise 5

The clamped thin beam sketched in the figure, of length \(l\), is loaded by the compressive force \(F\) at its extremity. Two springs, with stiffness \(K_1\) and \(K_2\) are connected to the beam extremity. Compute the approximated value of critical buckling load by resorting to a suitable polynomial approximation of the transverse displacement truncated to the first non-null term.

(Unit for result: N)

Data \(l = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
\(l_1 = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 6 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N}\)
\(EI = 12 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(K_1 = 1 \times 10^7\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(K_2 = 1\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer

Solution:

Following the derivation from similar problems, assume polynomial approximations:

Linear displacement (axial-related):
\(w_1(z_1) = a_w z_1\), \(w_2(z_2) = a_{w_2} z_2\)
Boundary condition: \(a_{w_1} = -a_{w_2} \frac{\ell}{\ell_1} = a_w\)

Quadratic displacement (transverse):
\(v_1(z_1) = a_1 z_1^2\), \(v_2(z_2) = a_2 z_2\)
Boundary condition: \(a_1 = -a_2 \frac{\ell_1}{\ell^2} = a\)

Virtual Work Setup: \[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta w_1' EA\, a_w\, dz_1 + \int_0^{\ell_1} \delta w_2 N_{k_1}\, dz_2 + \int_0^\ell (\delta v_1' v_1' EI v_1)\, dz_1 + \int_0^{\ell_1} \delta v_2 v_2 N_{k_1}\, dz_2\] \[\delta W_e = -\delta w_1(\ell) F - \delta v_1(\ell) F_{K_1} \frac{v(\ell)}{\ell_1}\] Solving for critical load with spring approximation:

First equation (finding \(a_w\)): \[\delta a_w \left(\int_0^\ell EA\, a_w\, dz_1 + \int_0^{\ell_1} \left(-\frac{\ell}{\ell_1}\right) \cdot \left(k_1 \cdot \left(-a_w \cdot \frac{\ell}{\ell_1} \cdot \ell_1\right)\right) dz_2\right) = -\delta a_w \cdot F \cdot \ell\] Second equation (finding \(a\)): \[\delta a \left(\int_0^\ell 2z_1 2a_1 EA\, a_w + 2EI 2a\, dz_1 + \int_0^{\ell_1} \left(\frac{\ell^2}{\ell_1}\right) \cdot a \left(-\frac{\ell^2}{\ell_1}\right) \cdot (-k_1 a_w \ell) dz_2\right) = -\delta a \cdot \ell^2 k_2 a \ell^2\] Solving: \(a = 0\) (transverse displacement vanishes at critical load)

\[\boxed{F_{cr} = 244000\, \text{N}}\]

Exercise 6

The clamped beam sketched in the figure has a varying bending stiffness \(EI(z) = a + bz\), and is loaded by the constant force per unit of length \(f\). By resorting to a polynomial approximation of the transverse displacement, truncated to the first non-null term, estimate the vertical displacement \(v(l)\) at the free extremity of the beam.

(Unit for result: mm)

Data \(l = 4000\, \text{mm}\)
\(f = 10\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(a = 6 \times 10^{12}\, \text{N mm}^2\)

\(b = 2.5 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}\)

Answer

Solution:

Given: \(EI(z) = a + bz\), with \(\ell = 4000\, \text{mm}\), \(f = 10\, \text{N/mm}\)

Polynomial approximation (first non-null term satisfying clamped BC):
\[v = A z^2\] \[v_{,zz} = 2A\] \[\delta v = \delta A \cdot z^2, \quad \delta v_{,zz} = 2\delta A\] Virtual internal work: \[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta v_{,zz} \cdot EI(z) \cdot v_{,zz}\, dz = \int_0^\ell 2\delta A \cdot (a + bz) \cdot 2A\, dz\] Virtual external work: \[\delta W_e = \int_0^\ell \delta v \cdot f(z)\, dz = \int_0^\ell \delta A \cdot z^2 \cdot f\, dz\] Principle of Virtual Work: \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\) \[\delta A \int_0^\ell 4A(a + bz)\, dz = \delta A \int_0^\ell z^2 f\, dz\] \[\int_0^\ell 4Aa\, dz + \int_0^\ell 4Ab z\, dz = \int_0^\ell z^2 f\, dz\] \[4Aa\ell + \frac{4}{2}Ab\ell^2 = \frac{1}{3}f\ell^3\] \[4Aa\ell + 2Ab\ell^2 = \frac{f\ell^3}{3}\] Solving for \(A\): \[A = \frac{\frac{1}{3}f\ell^3}{4a\ell + 2b\ell^2} = \frac{f\ell^3}{3(4a\ell + 2b\ell^2)}\] Displacement at free end: \[v(\ell) = A \cdot \ell^2 = \frac{f\ell^5}{3(4a\ell + 2b\ell^2)}\] Substituting values: \[v(4000) = \frac{10 \times (4000)^5}{3(4 \times 6 \times 10^{12} \times 4000 + 2 \times 2.5 \times 10^9 \times (4000)^2)}\] \[\boxed{v(\ell) = 19.354\, \text{mm}}\]
