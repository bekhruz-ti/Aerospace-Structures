Exercise 1

The semi-monocoque structure in the figure has a three stringers with concentrated area equal to \(A\) each and three panels with thickness \(t\). The structure is clamped at one extremity and loaded by the vertical force \(F\), applied to the rightmost stringer, at the other end. Compute the value of the concentrated area \(A\) required so that the maximum absolute value of the axial stress is equal to the prescribed value \(\sigma_{zz}\).

(Unit for result: \(\text{mm}^2\))

Data \(l_1 = 200\, \text{mm}\)
\(l_2 = 150\, \text{mm}\)
\(l = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 0.3\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 72000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(F = 10000\, \text{N}\)
\(\sigma_{zz} = 400\, \text{MPa}\)

Answer

Solution:
For the semi-monocoque structure, the maximum axial stress in the stringers is related to the bending moment by:

\[\frac{F \cdot l}{l_2 \cdot A} = \sigma_{zz}\]
Solving for the required area \(A\):
\[A = \frac{F \cdot l}{l_2 \cdot \sigma_{zz}}\]
Substituting the given values:
\[A = \frac{10000 \times 2000}{150 \times 400} = \frac{20{,}000{,}000}{60{,}000}\]
Final Answer: \(A = 333.33\, \text{mm}^2\)

Exercise 2

The sketched square is infinitely rigid. It is hinged at the bottom-right corner, and is connected to two horizontal and two vertical springs, each with an unknown stiffness \(K\), at the bottom-left and top left corners, as skethced. It is also loaded by the horizontal force \(F\) at the top-right corner. Compute the spring stiffness \(K\) so that the horizontal displacement \(v\) at the the point of application of the force \(F\) is equal to the prescribed value. Assume infinitesimal displacements and rotations.

(Unit for result: \(\text{N/mm}\))

Data \(l = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(F = 1500\, \text{N}\)
\(v = 0.1\, \text{mm}\)

Answer

Solution:
Since the square is rigid and hinged at the bottom-right corner, it rotates by angle \(\theta\) under the applied force.

Kinematic relationships for small rotation \(\theta = v/l\):
\[u_3 = -\theta l, \quad u_1 = -\theta l, \quad v_1 = -\theta l, \quad u_2 = 0, \quad v_2 = -\theta l = -v\]
Applying virtual work principle:
\[\delta v_1 \cdot K \cdot v_1 + \delta u_1 \cdot K \cdot u_1 + \delta u_2 \cdot K \cdot u_2 + \delta v_2 \cdot K \cdot v_2 = -\delta u_3 \cdot F\]
This simplifies to:
\[\delta\theta \cdot l \cdot K(\bar{v} + \bar{v} + \bar{v}) = \delta\theta \cdot l \cdot F\]
\[3\bar{v} \cdot l \cdot K = l \cdot F\]
Solving for \(K\):
\[K = \frac{F}{3v} = \frac{1500}{3 \times 0.1} = \frac{1500}{0.3}\]
Final Answer: \(K = 5000\, \text{N/mm}\)

Exercise 3

Consider the three-hinged beam structure sketched in the figure, where each beam is hinged at its extremity, loaded by the concentrated force \(F\). Compute the displacement \(v\) at the point of application of the force \(F\). Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: \(\text{mm}\))

Data \(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 1 \times 10^8\, \text{N}\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 1 \times 10^{13}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(GJ = 7 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(F = 1000\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:
For the triangular truss structure with hinged beams at angle \(\pi/3\):

From equilibrium, the force in inclined members:
\[2F_1 \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right) = F \quad \Rightarrow \quad F_1 = \frac{F}{2\sin(\pi/3)}\]
Using the principle of virtual work to find displacement \(v\):
\[v = \frac{F}{4\sin(\pi/3)} \cdot l \cdot \frac{2}{EA} + \frac{F\cos^2(\pi/3)}{4\sin(\pi/3)} \cdot \frac{l}{EA}\]
With \(\sin(\pi/3) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) and \(\cos(\pi/3) = \frac{1}{2}\):
\[v = \frac{Fl}{EA} \cdot \left[\frac{1}{2\sin(\pi/3)} + \frac{\cos^2(\pi/3)}{4\sin(\pi/3)}\right]\]
\[v = \frac{Fl}{EA} \cdot \frac{1}{\sin(\pi/3)}\left[\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{16}\right] = \frac{Fl}{EA} \cdot \frac{9}{8\sqrt{3}}\]
Substituting values: \(F = 1000\,\text{N}\), \(l = 1000\,\text{mm}\), \(EA = 10^8\,\text{N}\):
\[v = \frac{1000 \times 1000}{10^8} \times \frac{9}{8 \times 1.732} = 0.01 \times 0.6495\]
Final Answer: \(v \approx 0.0065\, \text{mm}\)

Exercise 4

Consider the clamped beam sketched in the figure, with length \(l\), and connected to the two springs with stiffness \(K_1\) and \(K_2\). Estimate, by using a polynomial approximation with one term for the beam transverse displacement, the critical buckling load \(F\) of the structure.

(Unit for result: \(\text{N}\))

Data \(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(GA^* = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N}\)
\(EI = 12 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(EA = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N}\)
\(K_1 = 1.5 \times 10^7\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(K_2 = 2500\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer

Solution:
Using Rayleigh-Ritz method with polynomial approximation \(v = az^2\):

First, define the equivalent axial stiffness of the beam:
\[K_3 = \frac{EA}{l} = \frac{10^{10}}{1000} = 10^7\, \text{N/mm}\]
The axial forces in spring \(K_1\) and beam segment:
\[N_{11} = \frac{F \cdot K_1}{K_1 + K_3}, \quad N_3 = \frac{F \cdot K_3}{K_1 + K_3}\]
With \(K_1 = 1.5 \times 10^7\) and \(K_3 = 10^7\):
\[N_3 = \frac{F \cdot 10^7}{2.5 \times 10^7} = 0.4F\]
The energy functional with \(v = az^2\), \(v' = 2az\), \(v'' = 2a\):
\[\int_0^l \left[\delta a \cdot 4EI \cdot a - N_3 \cdot \delta a \cdot 4z^2 \cdot a\right]dz + \delta a \cdot \frac{l^4}{16} \cdot K_2 = 0\]
Solving for critical load \(F_{cr}\):
\[4EI \cdot l - \frac{4N_3 l^3}{3} + \frac{l^4 K_2}{16} = 0\]
Substituting and solving:
Final Answer: \(F_{cr} \approx 1.22 \times 10^5\, \text{N}\)

Exercise 5

Consider the slender beam structure sketched in the figure, loaded by the concentrated force \(F\). Compute the displacement \(u\) of the extremity of the spring. Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: \(\text{mm}\))

Data \(a = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 1500\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N}\)
\(EI_{xx} = EI_{yy} = 1 \times 10^{13}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(GJ = 7 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(K = 1 \times 10^3\, \text{N/mm}\)
\(F = 1000\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:
The structure is statically indeterminate. Let \(H_x\) be the horizontal reaction (hyperstatic unknown).

Equilibrium equations:
\[X: \quad H_x - F + R_{xA} = 0\] \[Y: \quad R_{yA} + R_{yB} = 0\] \[M_B^{(3)}: \quad +F \cdot a - H_x \cdot a - R_y \cdot b = 0\]
Internal forces for each segment computed, then applying the Principle of Virtual Work (PVW):
\[\int_0^a \left(\frac{N_1 N_1'}{EA} + \frac{M_1 M_1'}{EI}\right)dz_1 + \int_0^b \left(\frac{N_2 N_2'}{EA} + \frac{M_2 M_2'}{EI}\right)dz_2 + \int_0^a \frac{N_3 N_3'}{EA}dz_3 + \frac{X}{K} = 0\]
After integration and solving for \(H_x\):
\[-\frac{H_x}{K} = \frac{H_x a^3}{EAb^2} - \frac{Fa^3}{EAb^2} + \frac{H_x}{EA} \cdot b + \frac{1}{3}\frac{H_x a^2 b^2}{EI \cdot b^2} + \ldots\]
Solving for \(H_x\) and computing \(u = -H_x/K\):
Final Answer: \(u = -7.699 \times 10^{-2}\, \text{mm}\) (or \(|u| \approx 0.077\, \text{mm}\))

Exercise 6

The single bilinear finite element sketched in the figure has the displacement of the top left, bottom left and bottom right nodes completely constrained. The element has unit thickness, and the material works in a state of plane stress, so that

\[ \begin{pmatrix} \sigma_{xx} \\ \sigma_{yy} \\ \tau_{xy} \end{pmatrix} = \frac{E}{1 - \nu^2} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & \nu & 0 \\ \nu & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & (1 - \nu)/2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \varepsilon_{xx} \\ \varepsilon_{yy} \\ \gamma_{xy} \end{pmatrix} \tag{1} \]

The top right node is loaded by the vertical force \(F\), as sketched. Compute the horizontal displacement \(u\) of the top right node.

(Unit for result: \(\text{mm}\))

Data \(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(a = 4\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 3\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 72000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(F = 100\, \text{N}\)

Answer

Solution:
With only the top-right node (node 3) free, the bilinear shape functions reduce to:
\[u(x,y) = \frac{xy}{ab} \cdot u_3, \quad v(x,y) = \frac{xy}{ab} \cdot v_3\]
Computing strains:
\[\varepsilon_{xx} = \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{y}{ab} \cdot u_3\] \[\varepsilon_{yy} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} = \frac{x}{ab} \cdot v_3\] \[\gamma_{xy} = \frac{x}{ab} \cdot u_3 + \frac{y}{ab} \cdot v_3\]
The strain-displacement matrix \(B\) and elasticity matrix \(E_\varepsilon\) are formed.
The stiffness matrix is computed via:
\[K = \int_0^a \int_0^b B^T \cdot E_\varepsilon \cdot B \, dy\, dx\]
The system of equations:
\[K \begin{pmatrix} u_3 \\ v_3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -F \end{pmatrix}\]
Solving: \(\begin{pmatrix} u_3 \\ v_3 \end{pmatrix} = K^{-1} \begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ -F \end{pmatrix}\)

With the given material properties and geometry, solving the 2×2 system:
Final Answer: \(u_3 \approx 1.74 \times 10^{-5}\, \text{mm}\)

True/False Questions (Put a T (true) or F (false) at the end of the sentence)

  1. The torsional stiffness of a single-cell thin-walled cross section is proportional to the second power of the wall thickness \(t\). F (False) - Torsional stiffness is proportional to \(t\) (first power), not \(t^2\).
  2. According to the de Saint-Venant solutions, it is possible to compute the torsional rotation in a beam only if the shear force \(F\) is applied through the shear center. F (False) - Torsional rotation can be computed regardless of where the shear force is applied.
  3. The axial stress \(\sigma_{zz}\) in a clamped beam loaded at the free extremity by a shear force \(F\) varies linearly with respect to the axial coordinate \(z\). T (True) - The bending moment \(M = F \cdot z\) varies linearly with \(z\), and so does \(\sigma_{zz}\).
