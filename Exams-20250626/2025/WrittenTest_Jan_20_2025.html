Exercise 1

The simply supported beam in the figure cross section is made with two different materials, as sketched in Sez A-A. The beam is loaded with a distributed force per unit of length \(q\). Compute the maximum value, over the whole structure, of the axial stress \(\sigma_{zz}\). Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: MPa)

Data \(l = 1500\, \text{mm}\)
\(a = 30\, \text{mm}\)
\(E_1 = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(E_2 = 210000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(q = 1\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer Solution:
Moment distribution: \(M_x(z) = \frac{ql}{2}z - \frac{q z^2}{2}\)
Maximum moment at \(z = \frac{l}{2}\): \(M_{x,\max} = \frac{ql^2}{8}\)
For composite section: \(\overline{E} = \frac{E_1 + E_2}{2}\), \(J_{xx} = \frac{a^4}{6}\)
Strain at extreme fiber: \(\varepsilon_{zz,\max} = \frac{M_{x,\max} \cdot \frac{a}{2}}{\overline{E} \cdot J_{xx}} = \frac{3ql^2}{8\overline{E}a^3}\)
Maximum stress (in \(E_2\) material): \(\sigma_{zz,\max} = E_2 \cdot \varepsilon_{zz,\max} = \frac{3ql^2 E_2}{8\overline{E}a^3}\)
\[= \frac{3 \times 1 \times 1500^2 \times 210000}{8 \times 140000 \times 30^3} = \boxed{46.875\, \text{MPa}}\]

Exercise 2

The beam in the figure is clamped at both ends, and is loaded with an distributed axial force per unit of length \(f\). Compute the displacement of the point at \(z = l/2\).

(Unit for result: mm)

Data \(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EA = 6 \times 10^9\, \text{N}\)
\(f = 300\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer Solution:
From symmetry, reaction: \(R_z = -\frac{fl}{2}\)
Axial force: \(N = \frac{fl}{2} - fz\)
Axial strain: \(\varepsilon_{zz} = \frac{N}{EA}\)
Displacement at \(z = \frac{l}{2}\):
\[w\left(\frac{l}{2}\right) = \int_0^{l/2} \varepsilon_{zz}\, dz = \frac{1}{EA}\int_0^{l/2}\left(\frac{fl}{2} - fz\right)dz\] \[= \frac{1}{EA}\left[\frac{fl}{2}z - \frac{fz^2}{2}\right]_0^{l/2} = \frac{1}{EA}\left(\frac{fl^2}{4} - \frac{fl^2}{8}\right) = \frac{fl^2}{8EA}\] \[= \frac{300 \times 1000^2}{8 \times 6 \times 10^9} = \boxed{0.00625\, \text{mm}}\]

Exercise 3

Consider the semi-monocoque cross section sketched in the figure, with four concentrated areas, each with area \(A\), five panels of thickness \(t\) and two cells. Compute the torsional stiffness \(GJ\) of the cross section.

(Unit for result: N mm2)

Data \(a = 500\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 2\, \text{mm}\)
\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(A = 200\, \text{mm}^2\)

Answer Solution:
Shear modulus: \(G = \frac{E}{2(1+\nu)} = \frac{70000}{2(1.3)} = 26923\, \text{MPa}\)
For two-cell section with open-cell fluxes = 0:
Moment equilibrium: \(1 = 2q_1^* \cdot \frac{1}{2}a^2 + 2q_2^* \cdot \frac{1}{2}a^2\)
Rotation compatibility for cells with diagonal member:
\(\theta_1' = \frac{1}{2 \cdot \frac{1}{2}a^2 \cdot Gt}\left(2q_1^* a + q_1^* \sqrt{2}a - q_2^* \sqrt{2}a\right)\)
From compatibility \(\theta_1' = \theta_2'\): \(q_1^* = q_2^*\)
Rotation rate: \(\theta' = \frac{M_t}{a^3 G t}\)
Torsional stiffness: \(GJ = \frac{M_t}{\theta'} = a^3 G t\)
\[GJ = 500^3 \times 26923 \times 2 = \boxed{6.73 \times 10^{12}\, \text{N mm}^2}\]

Exercise 4

The semi-monocoque beam sketched in the figure, with overall length \(3l\), has four panels, each with thickness \(t\), and four concentrated areas, each with area \(A\). It is loaded on the upper panel, by force per unit of surface \(q\). The load is introduced into the structure by four different ribs, with pitch equal to \(l\) (the first rib is at the clamp, the last one at the beam free extremity). Compute the axial stress at the point \(B\), as sketched in the figure, located at \(z = 3/2l\) from the clamp.

(Unit for result: MPa)

Data \(l = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
\(a = 700\, \text{mm}\)
\(b = 100\, \text{mm}\)
\(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
\(A = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
\(E = 70000\, \text{MPa}\)
\(\nu = 0.3\)
\(q = 0.01\, \text{N/mm}^2\)

Answer Solution:
Section property: \(J_{xx} = 4A\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2 = Ab^2 = 500 \times 100^2 = 5 \times 10^6\, \text{mm}^4\)
Rib forces: \(F_1 = \frac{qal}{2}\), \(F_2 = qal\)
Moment at \(z_B = \frac{3l}{2}\):
\[M_x(z_B) = -qal \cdot \frac{l}{2} - qa \cdot \frac{l}{2} \cdot \frac{3l}{2}\] \[= -0.01 \times 700 \times 1000 \times 500 - 0.01 \times 700 \times 500 \times 1500\] \[= -3.5 \times 10^6 - 5.25 \times 10^6 = -8.75 \times 10^6\, \text{N mm}\] Axial stress at B (top, \(y = \frac{b}{2}\)):
\[\sigma_{zz} = -\frac{M_x(z_B)}{J_{xx}} \cdot \frac{b}{2} = -\frac{-8.75 \times 10^6}{5 \times 10^6} \times 50 = \boxed{-87.5\, \text{MPa}}\]

Exercise 5

Consider the slender beam structure sketched in the figure, loaded at the free extremity by the concentrated moment \(M_x\). The bending stiffness varies linearly along the beam, \(EI_{xx} = a + bz\) Estimate the transverse displacement \(v\) at the extremity by assuming a polynomial approximation of the transverse displacement truncated to the first non null term. Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: mm)

Data \(l = 3000\, \text{mm}\)
\(a = 1 \times 10^{13}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(b = 3 \times 10^9\, \text{N mm}\)
\(M_x = 1 \times 10^5\, \text{N mm}\)

Answer Solution:
Assumed displacement (first non-null term satisfying BCs): \(v(z) = cz^2\)
Derivatives: \(v' = 2cz\), \(v'' = 2c\), \(\delta v'' = 2\delta c\), \(\delta v' = 2\delta c \cdot z\)
PVW internal work: \(\delta W_i = \int_0^l \delta v'' \cdot EI_{xx} \cdot v''\, dz = \delta c \int_0^l 4(a+bz)c\, dz\)
PVW external work: \(\delta W_e = -\delta v'(l) \cdot M_x = -\delta c \cdot 2l \cdot M_x\)
Equilibrium: \(\delta c(4al + 2bl^2)c = -\delta c \cdot 2l \cdot M_x\)
Solving: \(c = \frac{-2lM_x}{4al + 2bl^2} = \frac{-M_x}{2a + bl}\)
Displacement at tip: \(v(l) = cl^2 = \frac{-M_x l^2}{2a + bl}\)
\[= \frac{-1 \times 10^5 \times 3000^2}{2 \times 10^{13} + 3 \times 10^9 \times 3000} = \frac{-9 \times 10^{11}}{2.9 \times 10^{13}} = \boxed{-0.031\, \text{mm}}\]

Exercise 6

The single simply supported beam sketched in the figure, with bending stiffness \(EI_{xx}\), has a supporting spring in the middle with stiffness \(K\). The beam is loaded by the compressive force \(F\). Estimate the critical buckling value of \(F\) by assuming a trigonometric expansion of the transverse displacement, truncated to one term. Neglect shear deformability.

(Unit for result: N)

Data \(l = 3000\, \text{mm}\)
\(EI_{xx} = 1 \times 10^{10}\, \text{N mm}^2\)
\(K = 100\, \text{N/mm}\)

Answer Solution:
From axial equilibrium: \(N = -F\)
Assumed displacement: \(v(z) = A\sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\)
Derivatives: \(v' = A\frac{\pi}{l}\cos\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\), \(v'' = -A\frac{\pi^2}{l^2}\sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{l}\right)\)
PVW with bending and axial contributions:
\[\delta A\left[\frac{\pi^4}{l^4}EI_{xx}\frac{l}{2} - \frac{\pi^2}{l^2}\frac{l}{2}F\right]A = 0\] Spring contribution: \(\delta W_e = -\delta v\left(\frac{l}{2}\right) \cdot K \cdot v\left(\frac{l}{2}\right) = -\delta A \cdot K \cdot A\)
Combined equilibrium: \(\delta A\left(\frac{\pi^4 EI_{xx}}{2l^3} + K - \frac{\pi^2}{2l}F\right)A = 0\)
Critical load (for \(A \neq 0\)):
\[F = \frac{\pi^2 EI_{xx}}{l^2} + \frac{2Kl}{\pi^2}\] \[= \frac{9.8696 \times 10^{10}}{9 \times 10^6} + \frac{2 \times 100 \times 3000}{9.8696} = 10966 + 60793 = \boxed{71759\, \text{N}}\]
