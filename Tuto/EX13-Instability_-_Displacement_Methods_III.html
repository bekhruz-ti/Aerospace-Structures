Exercise Session 13

Structural Mechanics - Critical Load and Virtual Work Problems

Exercise 1 (Exam 05/07/2023)

Find the critical load using polynomial approximation for a cantilever beam with spring support.

Given Data:

  • \(EA = 4 \cdot 10^8 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 1 \cdot 10^{12} \text{ Nmm}^2\)
  • \(\ell = 3000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(k = 500 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Define Strain Assumptions

Infinitesimal displacement in x-direction, small displacement in y-direction.

Green-Lagrange strain tensor:

\[\varepsilon_{ik} = \frac{1}{2}\left(u_{i/k} + u_{k/i} + u_{/i} \cdot u_{/k}\right)\]

With notation: \(u_x = u\), \(u_y = v\), \(u_z = w\)

\[\varepsilon_{xx} = \frac{1}{2}\left(u_{/x} + u_{/x} + u_{/x}^2 + v_{/x}^2 + w_{/x}^2\right)\] \[\varepsilon_{xx} = u_{/x} + \frac{1}{2}v_{/x}^2\]

Step 2: Euler-Bernoulli Beam Kinematics

For EB beam:

\[u = u_o - y \cdot v_{o/x}\] \[v = v_o\]

where \(u_o = u_o(x)\) and \(v_o = v_o(x)\)

Therefore:

\[\varepsilon_{xx} = u_{o/x} - y \cdot v_{o/xx} + \frac{1}{2}v_{o/x}^2\]

From now on, subscript "o" will be dropped.

Step 3: Internal Virtual Work

\[\delta W_i = \int_V \delta\varepsilon_{xx} \cdot \sigma_{xx} \, dV\]

where:

\[\delta\varepsilon_{xx} = \delta u_{/x} + \delta v_{/x} \cdot v_{/x} - y\delta v_{/xx}\]

Using: \(\delta(v_{/x} \cdot v_{/x}) = \delta v_{/x} \cdot v_{/x} + v_{/x} \delta v_{/x}\)

\[\delta W_i = \int_V \left[\left(\delta u_{/x} + \delta v_{/x} \cdot v_{/x}\right)\sigma_{xx} - \delta v_{/xx} \cdot y \cdot \sigma_{xx}\right] dV\]

Known relations:

\[\int_A \sigma_{xx} \, dA = N \qquad \int_A y \sigma_{xx} \, dA = M = -EJ v_{/xx}\]

So:

\[\delta W_i = \int_\ell \left(\delta u_{/x} + \delta v_{/x} \cdot v_{/x}\right) N + \delta v_{/xx} EJ v_{/xx} \, dx\]

Step 4: External Virtual Work

\[\delta W_e = -\delta v(\ell) \cdot k \cdot v(\ell) - \delta u(\ell) \cdot F\]

Known:

\[\int_0^\ell \delta u_{/x} \cdot F \, dx = \delta u(\ell) F - \delta u(0)F = \delta u(\ell) F\]

Step 5: Principle of Virtual Work

PVW: \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\)

\[\int_0^\ell \delta u_{/x} (N + F) + \int_0^\ell \delta v_{/x} N v_{/x} + \delta v_{/xx} EJ v_{/xx} \, dx + \delta v(\ell) k \, v(\ell) = 0\]

Stating the axial equilibrium: \(N = -F\)

Step 6: Polynomial Approximation

Assume:

\[v(x) = Cx^2 \qquad \delta v(x) = \delta C \, x^2\] \[v_{/x}(x) = 2Cx \qquad \delta v_{/x}(x) = \delta C \cdot 2x\] \[v_{/xx}(x) = 2C \qquad \delta v_{/xx}(x) = \delta C \cdot 2\]

Step 7: Substitution and Solution

\[\delta C \left[\int_0^\ell \left(2EJ \cdot 2C - 2x \cdot F \cdot 2xC\right) dx + \ell^2 k C \ell^2\right] = 0\] \[\left[4EJ\ell - \frac{4}{3}F\ell^3 + k\ell^4\right] C = 0\]

For non-trivial solution (\(C \neq 0\)):

\[F = \frac{(4EJ + k\ell^3) \cdot 3}{4\ell^2}\]
Critical Load: \[\boxed{F_{crit} = 1.46 \cdot 10^6 \text{ N}}\]

Exercise 2 (Exam 13/02/2024)

Find the critical load using polynomial approximation for a beam with two spring supports.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 2000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA = 6 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 12 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ Nmm}^2\)
  • \(k_2 = 1 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(k_1 = 1 \cdot 10^3 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(\ell_1 = 1000 \text{ mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Internal Work Formulation

Internal work for beam with nonlinear GL strain (infinitesimal z, small y):

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta w_{/z} N \, dz + \int_0^\ell \delta v_{/zz} EJ v_{/zz} + \delta v_{/z} N v_{/z} \, dz\]

Step 2: Polynomial Approximations

Take approximations:

  • LINEAR: \(w_1 = a_{w_1} \cdot z_1\)
  • LINEAR: \(w_2 = a_{w_2} \cdot z_2\)
  • QUADRATIC: \(v_1 = a_1 z_1^2\)
  • LINEAR: \(v_2 = a_2 z_2\)

Boundary conditions:

\[a_{w_1} = -a_{w_2} \cdot \frac{\ell_1}{\ell} = a_w \quad \Rightarrow \quad a_{w_2} = -a_w \frac{\ell}{\ell_1}\] \[a_1 = -a_2 \frac{\ell_1}{\ell^2} = a \quad \Rightarrow \quad a_2 = -a\frac{\ell^2}{\ell_1}\]

Step 3: Virtual Work Expressions

Internal virtual work on beam:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta w_{1/z_1} N \, dz_1 + \int_0^\ell \delta v_{1/z_1} N v_{/z_1} + \delta v_{1/z_1 z_1} EJ v_{1/z_1 z_1} \, dz_1\]

On spring \(k_1\):

\[+ \int_0^{\ell_1} \delta w_{2/z_2} N_{k_1} dz_2 + \int_0^{\ell_1} \delta v_{2/z_2} N_{k_1} v_{2/z_2} \, dz_2\]

External virtual work:

\[\delta W_e = -\delta w_1(\ell) F - \delta v_1(\ell) k v_1(\ell)\]

Step 4: Known Relations

\[N_{k_1} = k_1 \cdot w_2(\ell_1) = -k_1 \cdot a_w \cdot \ell\] \[N = EA \, w_{1/z_1} = EA \, a_w\]

Step 5: Finding \(a_w\)

From equilibrium:

\[F + \frac{EA}{\ell} \cdot w_1(\ell) + k_1 w_1(\ell) = 0\] \[w_1(\ell) = -\frac{F}{EA + k_1 \ell} \cdot \ell = a_w \cdot \ell\]

Step 6: Solve for Critical Load

Substituting and collecting terms for \(\delta a\):

\[\delta a \left(\int_0^\ell 2z_1 \cdot 2z_1 a \cdot EA \cdot \left(-\frac{F}{EA + k_1 \ell}\right) + 4EJ a \, dz_1 + \ldots \right)\]

where \(N = EA \, a_w = EA \, w_{/z_1}\)

\[\left( + \int_0^\ell \left(-\frac{\ell^2}{\ell_1}\right) \cdot a \left(-\frac{\ell^2}{\ell_1}\right) \cdot \left(-k_1 \cdot a_w \cdot \ell\right) dz_2 \right) = -\delta a \cdot \ell^2 k_2 \cdot a \cdot \ell^2\]

Solve for \(a = 0\) (trivial) or \(a \neq 0\) (critical load):

Critical Load: \[\boxed{F_{crit} = 244{,}000 \text{ N}}\]

ans = 
F == 244000 | a == 0

Exercise 3 (Exam 09/09/2024)

Find the critical load using polynomial approximation for an L-shaped frame structure with spring supports.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(GA^* = 1 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 12 \cdot 10^9 \text{ Nmm}^2\)
  • \(EA = 1 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ N}\)
  • \(k_1 = 1.5 \cdot 10^7 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(k_2 = 2500 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Polynomial Approximations

  • LINEAR: \(w_1(z_1) = a_{w_1} \cdot z_1\)
  • LINEAR: \(w(z) = a_w \cdot z\)
  • QUADRATIC: \(v(z) = a \, z^2\)
  • CONSTANT: \(v_1(z) = a_1\)

Step 2: Boundary Conditions

\[w_1\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right) = -w(\ell) \quad \Rightarrow \quad a_{w_1} = -2a_w\] \[v_1\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right) = -v(\ell) \quad \Rightarrow \quad a_1 = -a \cdot \ell^2\]

Step 3: Virtual Work Expressions

Internal virtual work:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta w_{/z} N \, dz + \int_0^{\ell/2} \delta w_{1/z_1} N_{k_1} \, dz_1 + \int_0^\ell \left(\delta v_{/zz} EJ \, v_{/zz} + \delta v_{/z} N \, v_{/z}\right) dz\]

External virtual work:

\[\delta W_e = -\delta w(\ell) \cdot F - \delta v\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right) k_2 \, v\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right)\]

Step 4: Known Relations

\[N = EA \cdot a_w\] \[N_{k_1} = k_1 \cdot w_1\left(\frac{\ell}{2}\right) = -k_1 a_w \cdot \ell\]

Same as Exercise 2:

\[a_w = -\frac{F}{EA + k_1 \ell}\]

Step 5: Equation for \(\delta a\)

\[\delta a \cdot \int_0^\ell \left(4EJ a + 4a z^2 \cdot N\right) dz = -\delta a \frac{\ell^2}{4} k_2 a \frac{\ell^2}{4}\]

where \(N = EA \cdot \left(-\frac{F}{EA + k_1 \ell}\right)\)

Solve for \(a \neq 0\):

Critical Load: \[\boxed{F_{crit} = 3.830 \cdot 10^5 \text{ N}}\]

Exercise 4 (Exam 29/08/2022)

Find the virtual work of the force for a unitary virtual displacement of point A in the vertical direction.

Given Data:

  • \(a = 2 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 3 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(p = 10 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Parabolic Element Approximation

Deformed shape for unitary displacement \(v_A\):

\[v(\eta) = \left(-\frac{4\eta^2}{b^2} + 1\right) v_A\]

Step 2: Load Distribution

The triangular distributed load:

\[f(\eta) = \frac{p}{b} \cdot \eta + \frac{p}{2}\]

Step 3: External Virtual Work

\[\delta W_e = \int_{-\frac{b}{2}}^{\frac{b}{2}} \delta v \cdot f \, d\eta\] \[= \int_{-\frac{b}{2}}^{\frac{b}{2}} \delta v_A \cdot \left(-\frac{4\eta^2}{b^2} + 1\right) \cdot \left(\frac{p}{b}\eta + \frac{p}{2}\right) d\eta\]
Virtual Work of Force: \[\boxed{\delta W_e = \delta v_A \cdot \frac{1}{3}bp}\]

Exercise 5 (Exam 23/08/2022)

Find the rotation \(\theta\) using trigonometric approximation with one term.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 6 \cdot 10^8 \text{ Nmm}^2\)
  • \(M = 4 \cdot 10^5 \text{ Nmm}\)
  • \(F = 2000 \text{ N}\)

Solution

Step 1: Trigonometric Approximation

Assume displacement field:

\[v(z) = C \cdot \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{\ell}\right)\]

Step 2: Internal Virtual Work

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^{4\ell} \delta v_{/zz} \, EJ \, v_{/zz} \, dz\] \[= \delta C \, EJ \frac{\pi^4}{\ell^4} \cdot C \int_0^{4\ell} \sin^2\left(\frac{\pi z}{\ell}\right) dz\] \[= \delta C \cdot EJ \cdot C \cdot \frac{2\pi^4}{\ell^3}\]

Step 3: External Virtual Work

\[\delta W_e = -\delta v_{/z}(2\ell) \cdot M + \delta v\left(\frac{7}{2}\ell\right) \cdot F\] \[= \delta C \left(-\frac{\pi}{\ell} \cdot M - F\right)\]

Step 4: Apply PVW

From \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\):

\[C = -\frac{F + \frac{\pi}{\ell} \cdot M}{\frac{2\pi^4}{\ell^3} EJ}\]

Step 5: Calculate Rotation

\[\theta = -v_{/z}(\ell) = + C \cdot \frac{\pi}{\ell}\]
Rotation at Support: \[\boxed{\theta = -\frac{F + \frac{\pi}{\ell} M}{\frac{2\pi^4}{\ell^3} EJ} \cdot \frac{\pi}{\ell}}\]
