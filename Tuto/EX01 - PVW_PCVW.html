1. Introduction to the Principle of Virtual Work

Static Equilibrium Condition

The Principle of Virtual Work states:

\[\delta W_i = \delta W_e\]

for any arbitrary virtual compatible displacement \(\delta u\) and \(\delta \varepsilon\).

Virtual Work Expression

\[\delta W = F \cdot \delta \eta\]

We are looking for the only equilibrated solution among the infinite compatible ones.

Linear Elastic Constitutive Law

\[\sigma_{zz} = \frac{F}{A} = E \cdot \varepsilon_{zz}\] \[\varepsilon_{zz} = \frac{u_z}{\ell} = \frac{\Delta \ell}{\ell}\]
Note: This relation holds for infinitesimal displacements.

TRUSS: It can carry only axial load.

Virtual Internal Work Derivation

Starting Expression:
\[\delta W_i = \int_V \delta \varepsilon_{zz} \cdot \sigma_{zz} \, dV = \int_0^\ell \int_A \delta \varepsilon_{zz} \cdot \sigma_{zz} \, dA \cdot dz\]
Separating Integrals:
\[= \int_0^\ell \delta \varepsilon_{zz} \cdot \int_A \sigma_{zz} \, dA \cdot dz = \int_0^\ell \delta \varepsilon_{zz} \cdot F \, dz = \delta \Delta \ell \cdot F\]
Final Form:
\[= \delta(u_1 - u_2) \cdot F\]
Important: It's not the position, it's the displacements.

2. Spring Element Formulation

We can describe our truss as a spring, which kinematics is described by \(u_1\) and \(u_2\).

Spring Force-Displacement Relation

\[F = K(u_1 - u_2) = K \cdot [1 \quad -1] \cdot \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}\]

Note: Curly brackets denote column vectors.

PVW: \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\)

REAL

\(W_i\) and \(W_e\):

\[(u_1 - u_2) \cdot F = \Delta \ell \cdot F\]

VIRTUAL

\(\delta W_i\) and \(\delta W_e\):

\[\delta(u_1 - u_2) \cdot F = \delta \Delta \ell \cdot F\]
Elongation:
\[\Delta \ell = u_1 - u_2\] \[\Delta \ell = [1 \quad -1] \cdot \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}\] \[\delta \Delta \ell = [1 \quad -1] \cdot \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}\]
Virtual Internal Work:
\[\delta W_i = \int_V \delta \underline{\varepsilon} \cdot \underline{\underline{\sigma}} \, dV = \delta \Delta \ell \cdot F\] \[= \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \cdot K [1 \quad -1] \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}\]

Outer Scalar Product

\[\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \otimes [1 \quad -1] = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\]
Stiffness Matrix of the Spring: \[\delta W_i = \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{bmatrix} K & -K \\ -K & K \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}\]
Virtual External Work:
\[\delta W_e = \delta \Delta \ell \cdot F = \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \cdot F = \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{bmatrix} F \\ -F \end{bmatrix}\]

3. Spring Constitutive Equation

PVW: \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\)

\[\delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{bmatrix} K & -K \\ -K & K \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} = \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{Bmatrix} F \\ -F \end{Bmatrix}\]
TBN (To Be Noted): You cannot eliminate \(\delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T\) from both sides of the equation:
  • It's mathematically WRONG
  • It's conceptually misleading

What We Actually Do:

\[\delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \left( \begin{bmatrix} K & -K \\ -K & K \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - \begin{Bmatrix} F \\ -F \end{Bmatrix} \right) = \emptyset\]

This must be \(\emptyset\) for any value of \(\delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T\).

Spring Constitutive Equation: \[\begin{bmatrix} K & -K \\ -K & K \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - \begin{Bmatrix} F \\ -F \end{Bmatrix} = 0\]

4. Multi-Spring System (Series Configuration)

Let's try with a more complex system.

Individual Spring Stiffness Matrices

For the first spring:

\[\begin{Bmatrix} f_1 \\ f_2 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 \\ -K_1 & K_1 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}\]

For the second spring:

\[\begin{Bmatrix} f_2 \\ f_3 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} K_2 & -K_2 \\ -K_2 & K_2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}\]

Virtual Work Formulation

Internal Virtual Work:
\[\delta W_i = \delta W_{i_1} + \delta W_{i_2} = \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 \\ -K_1 & K_1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} + \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} K_2 & -K_2 \\ -K_2 & K_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}\]
External Virtual Work:
\[\delta W_e = \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{Bmatrix} f_1 \\ f_2 \\ f_3 \end{Bmatrix}\]

Applying Boundary Conditions

Let's add the BC: \(u_3 = \emptyset\) and \(\delta u_3 = \emptyset\)

\[\delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 \\ -K_1 & K_1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} + \delta u_2 \cdot K_2 \cdot u_2 = \delta u_1 \cdot f_1\]
Expanding:
\[\delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 \\ -K_1 & K_1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} + \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} \emptyset & \emptyset \\ \emptyset & K_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{Bmatrix} f_1 \\ \emptyset \end{Bmatrix} = 0\]
Factoring:
\[\delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \left( \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 \\ -K_1 & K_1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \emptyset & \emptyset \\ \emptyset & K_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - \begin{Bmatrix} f_1 \\ \emptyset \end{Bmatrix} \right) = \emptyset\]
Assembled System: \[\begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 \\ -K_1 & K_1 + K_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - \begin{Bmatrix} f_1 \\ \emptyset \end{Bmatrix} = 0\]

5. Alternative Assembly Method

Let's do it in a different way.

Global Assembly

\[\begin{pmatrix} f_1 \\ f_2 \\ f_3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 & \emptyset \\ -K_1 & K_1+K_2 & -K_2 \\ \emptyset & -K_2 & K_2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{pmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{pmatrix}\]

PVW with Full Assembly

\[\delta \begin{pmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{pmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 & \emptyset \\ -K_1 & K_1+K_2 & -K_2 \\ \emptyset & -K_2 & K_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{pmatrix} = \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{Bmatrix} f_1 \\ f_2 \\ f_3 \end{Bmatrix}\]
After applying B.C.:
\[\delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 \\ -K_2 & K_1+K_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{Bmatrix} F \\ \emptyset \end{Bmatrix} = \emptyset\]
Final form:
\[\begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 \\ -K_1 & K_1+K_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - \begin{Bmatrix} F \\ \emptyset \end{Bmatrix} = \emptyset\]
Solution for Series Springs: \[u_1 = F \left( \frac{1}{K_1} + \frac{1}{K_2} \right)\]

7. Series Spring Analysis using PCVW

Equilibrium along z-axis

\[F + R = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R = -F\]
SERIES Configuration: The internal force is constant along the structure.
  • Is this statically determined? Is this ISOSTATIC? YES

If our structure is isostatic, we can solve our elastic problem simply IMPOSING THE EQUILIBRIUM.

PCVW Analysis

Constitutive relation: \(F = K \cdot \delta \ell\)

Internal Virtual Work:
\[\delta W_i = \delta W_{i_1} + \delta W_{i_2} = \delta F_1 \cdot \Delta \ell_1 + \delta F_2 \cdot \Delta \ell_2\] \[= \delta F_1 \cdot \frac{F_1}{K_1} + \delta F_2 \cdot \frac{F_2}{K_2}\]
For Series: \[\delta F = \delta F_1 = \delta F_2\] \[F = F_1 = F\]
Simplifying:
\[\delta W_i = \delta F \left( \frac{F}{K_1} + \frac{F}{K_2} \right)\]
External Virtual Work:
\[\delta W_e = \delta F \cdot u_1 + \delta F \cdot \cancel{u_3}\] \[= \delta F \cdot u_1\]

Note: The reaction forces don't produce work.

PCVW: \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\)

\[\delta F \cdot F \left( \frac{1}{K_1} + \frac{1}{K_2} \right) - u_1 \cdot \delta F = 0\] \[\delta F \left( F \left( \frac{1}{K_1} + \frac{1}{K_2} \right) - u_1 \right) = 0\]
Solution: \[u_1 = F \left( \frac{1}{K_1} + \frac{1}{K_2} \right)\]

8. Parallel Spring System (Hyperstatic)

TBN: We have only axial behavior.

PARALLEL: \(\Delta \ell = \Delta \ell_1 = \Delta \ell_2\)

Problem Statement

Given \(F\), let's find \(u\).

  • Statically determined? 3 rigid DOF
  • NO! The system is hyperstatic.
Impose equilibrium along z:
\[F - R_1 - R_2 = \emptyset\]

Two unknowns in one equation!

First Method: PCVW

Virtual system with \(\delta F = 1\):

\[\delta F = \delta F_1 + \delta F_2\]
Internal Virtual Work:
\[\delta W_i = \delta W_{i_1} + \delta W_{i_2} = \cancel{\delta F_1} \cdot \frac{F_1}{K_1} + \delta F_2 \cdot \frac{F_2}{K_2}\]
External Virtual Work:
\[\delta W_e = \delta F \cdot u = u \quad [N \cdot mm]\]

With \(\delta F_2 = \delta F\):

\[\delta F_2 \cdot \frac{F_2}{K_2} = u \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{F_2}{K_2} = u\]

Similarly, \(\frac{F_1}{K_1} = u\) and \(F_1 = F - F_2\)

\[u = \frac{F_1}{K_1} = \frac{F - F_2}{K_1} = \frac{F_2}{K_2}\]

Solving for F₂

\[K_2 F - K_2 F_2 - F_2 K_1 = \emptyset\] \[F_2 = F \cdot \frac{K_2}{K_1 + K_2}\]
Displacement: \[u = \frac{F_2}{K_2} = \frac{F}{K_1 + K_2}\]

Smarter System of Equilibrated Forces

We can choose a smarter system of EQUILIBRATED FORCES.

With \(F_1 = F - F_2\):

Virtual Internal Work:
\[\delta W_i = \delta F_2 \cdot \frac{F_2}{K_2} + \delta F_1 \cdot \frac{F_1}{K_1}\] \[= -1 \cdot \frac{F_2}{K_2} + 1 \cdot \frac{F - F_2}{K_1}\]
External Virtual Work:
\[\delta W_e = u \cdot \emptyset = \emptyset\]

Leading to:

\[-K_1 F_2 + K_2 F - K_2 F_2 = \emptyset\] \[-F_2(K_1 + K_2) = -K_2 F\] \[F_2 = F \cdot \frac{K_2}{K_1 + K_2}\]
Second Method: PVW
\[\delta u \cdot K_1 u + \delta u \cdot K_2 u = \delta u \cdot F\] \[F = (K_1 + K_2) u\]
\[u = \frac{F}{K_1 + K_2}\]

9. Vertical Parallel Spring System

TBN: This is NOT a SERIES configuration!

If \(K_1 \neq K_2\), then \(F_1 \neq F_2\)

\(F = F_1 + F_2\)

I) PVW Method
\[\delta \begin{Bmatrix} \emptyset \\ u \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} K_1 & -K_1 \\ -K_1 & K_1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} \emptyset \\ u \end{Bmatrix} + \delta \begin{Bmatrix} u \\ \emptyset \end{Bmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} K_2 & -K_2 \\ -K_2 & K_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u \\ \emptyset \end{Bmatrix} = \delta u \cdot F\] \[\delta u \cdot K_1 \cdot u + \delta u \cdot K_2 \cdot u - \delta u \cdot F = \emptyset\]
\[(K_1 + K_2) \cdot u = F\]
II) PCVW Method

VIRTUAL 1: \(\delta F = 1\)

\[\delta F \cdot \frac{F_1}{K_1} = \delta F \cdot u \quad \Rightarrow \quad u = \frac{F_1}{K_1}\]

VIRTUAL 2: \(\delta F = 2\) (both negative)

\[\delta F \cdot \frac{F_2}{K_2} = \delta F \cdot u \quad \Rightarrow \quad u = \frac{F_2}{K_2}\]

Combining:

\[u = \frac{F_1}{K_1} = \frac{F_2}{K_2} = \frac{F - F_2}{K_1}\] \[\frac{F_2}{K_2} = \frac{F - F_2}{K_1} \quad \Rightarrow \quad K_1 F_2 = (F - F_2) K_2\] \[F = \frac{(K_1 + K_2)}{K_2} F_2 = (K_1 + K_2) \cdot u\]
\[u(K_1 + K_2) = F\]

10. Four-Spring Network System

Problem Statement

Given \(F\), find \(u\).

  • Is it isostatic? No, thus it's not statically determined.
Reaction Forces:
\[F_2 = F\] \[F_3 = F_4 = F - F_1\]

Using PCVW Two Times

  • One to find the reaction forces
  • One to find \(u\)
VIRTUAL 1: Finding Reaction Forces

As we did before, let's choose a smart system of equilibrated forces.

\[1 \cdot \frac{F_1}{K_1} - 1 \cdot \frac{(F - F_1)}{K_3} - 1 \cdot \frac{(F - F_1)}{K_4} = 1 \cdot \emptyset\]
  • \(\frac{F_1}{K_1}\): positive: TENSED
  • \(\frac{(F-F_1)}{K_3}\): negative: COMPRESSED
  • \(\frac{(F-F_1)}{K_4}\): negative: COMPRESSED
Expanding:
\[K_3 K_4 \cdot F_1 - K_1 K_4 (F - F_1) - K_1 K_3 (F - F_1) = \emptyset\]
\[F_1 = \frac{K_1 K_4 + K_1 K_3}{K_3 K_4 + K_1 K_4 + K_1 K_3} \cdot F\]

Then \(F_1, F_2, F_3, F_4\) are known.

VIRTUAL 2: Finding Displacement u

If we want to find \(u\), let's put \(\delta F_1 = 1\) there.

\[1 \cdot \frac{F_1}{K_1} + 1 \cdot \frac{F}{K_2} = 1 \cdot u\]
Final Answer: \[u = \frac{F_1}{K_1} + \frac{F}{K_2}\]

where \(F_1 = \frac{K_1 K_4 + K_1 K_3}{K_3 K_4 + K_1 K_4 + K_1 K_3} \cdot F\)
