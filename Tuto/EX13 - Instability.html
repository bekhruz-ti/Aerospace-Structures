EX 13 - Displacement Methods and Instability

Critical Load Analysis using Polynomial Approximation and Principle of Virtual Work

Problem 1 — EXAM 05/07/2023

Find the critical load for a 2D cantilever beam with a horizontal spring and vertical point load using a polynomial approximation and the Principle of Virtual Work (PVW) for an Euler-Bernoulli beam.

Given Data:

  • \(EA = 1 \cdot 10^4 \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 1 \cdot 10^{12} \text{ Nmm}^2\)
  • \(\ell = 3000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(K = 500 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Euler-Bernoulli Beam Kinematics

For an Euler-Bernoulli beam, define the displacement fields:

  • \(U(x)\) → \(x\)-displacement
  • \(V(x)\) → \(y\)-displacement
\[ \begin{cases} U(x) = U_o(x) - y \cdot V_{o,x}(x) \\ V(x) = V_o(x) \end{cases} \]

Step 2: Green-Lagrange Strain Tensor (Finite Displacement)

In the assumption of FINITE displacement, the Green-Lagrange strain tensor is:

\[ \mathcal{E}_{ik} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \underbrace{U_{i/k} + U_{k/i}}_{\text{linear strain}} + U^j_{/k} \cdot U^j_{/i} \right) \]

For the \(xx\)-component in a 3D case:

\[ \mathcal{E}_{xx} = \frac{1}{2} \left( U_{/x} + U_{/x} + \left( U_{/x} \right)^2 + \left( V_{/x} \right)^2 + \left( W_{/x} \right)^2 \right) \]

Assuming infinitesimal displacement in \(x\) and finite displacement in \(y\):

\[ \mathcal{E}_{xx} = U_{/x} + \frac{1}{2} \left( V_{/x} \right)^2 \]

Substituting the Euler-Bernoulli kinematics:

\[ \mathcal{E}_{xx} = U_{o/x} - y \cdot V_{o/xx} + \frac{1}{2} \left( V_{o/x} \right)^2 \]

Step 3: Virtual Internal Work

Using the variation identity: \(\delta(V_{/x} \cdot V_{/x}) = \delta V_{/x} \cdot V_{/x} + V_{/x} \cdot \delta V_{/x}\)

\[ \delta W_i = \int_V \delta \mathcal{E}_{xx} \cdot \sigma_{xx} \, dV \]

The variation of strain:

\[ \delta \mathcal{E}_{xx} = \delta U_{/x} - y \cdot \delta V_{/xx} + \delta V_{/x} \cdot V_{/x} \]

Expanding the virtual internal work:

\[ \delta W_i = \int_V \left[ \left( \delta U_{/x} + \delta V_{/x} V_{/x} \right) \cdot \sigma_{xx} - \delta V_{/xx} \cdot y \cdot \sigma_{xx} \right] dV \]

Using the stress resultant definitions:

\[ \int_A \sigma_{xx} \, dA = N \quad \text{and} \quad \int_A y \cdot \sigma_{xx} \, dA = M = -EJ \cdot V_{/xx} \]

The virtual internal work becomes:

\[ \delta W_i = \int_\ell \left[ \underbrace{\left( \delta U_{/x} + \delta V_{/x} \cdot V_{/x} \right) \cdot N + \delta V_{/xx} \, EJ \, V_{/xx}}_{\text{BEAM}} \right] dx + \underbrace{\delta V(\ell) \cdot K \cdot V(\ell)}_{\text{SPRING}} \]

Step 4: Virtual External Work

\[ \delta W_e = - \int_0^\ell \delta U_x \cdot F \, dx = - \delta U(\ell) \cdot F \]

Step 5: Principle of Virtual Work (PVW)

Setting \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\):

\[ \int_0^\ell \underbrace{\delta U_{/x} (N + F) \, dx}_{\text{AXIAL}} + \int_0^\ell \underbrace{\left( \delta V_{/x} \, N \, V_{/x} + \delta V_{/xx} \, EJ \, V_{/xx} \right)}_{\text{BENDING}} dx + \delta V(\ell) \cdot K \cdot V(\ell) = 0 \]

From axial equilibrium: \(N = -F\)

Step 6: Polynomial Approximation with Boundary Conditions

Assume a polynomial form satisfying boundary conditions:

\[ V(x) = C \cdot x^2 \qquad \delta V(x) = \delta C \cdot x^2 \]

Computing derivatives:

\[ V_{/x} = 2Cx \qquad \delta V_{/x} = 2 \delta C \cdot x \] \[ V_{/xx} = 2C \qquad \delta V_{/xx} = 2 \delta C \]

Step 7: Substitution and Integration

Substituting into the PVW equation:

\[ \delta C \cdot \left[ \int_0^\ell \left( 2 \, EJ \cdot 2C - 2x \cdot F \cdot 2x \, C \right) dx + \ell^2 \cdot K \cdot C \, \ell^2 \right] = 0 \]

After integration:

\[ \left[ 4 \, EJ\ell - \frac{4}{3} F\ell^3 + K\ell^4 \right] \cdot C = 0 \]

Step 8: Critical Load Determination

For non-trivial solution (\(C \neq 0\)), we require:

\[ 4 \, EJ\ell - \frac{4}{3} F\ell^3 + K\ell^4 = 0 \]

Solving for \(F\):

\[ F = \frac{(4EJ + K\ell^3) \cdot 3}{4\ell^2} \]

Substituting values: \(EJ = 1 \times 10^{12}\), \(K = 500\), \(\ell = 3000\):

\[ F = \frac{(4 \times 10^{12} + 500 \times 27 \times 10^9) \cdot 3}{4 \times 9 \times 10^6} \]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{F_{cr} = 1.46 \times 10^6 \text{ N}}\]

The case \(C = 0\) corresponds to no transversal displacement (trivial solution).

Problem 2 — EXAM 13/02/2024

Find the critical load \(F\) for a cantilever beam system with two springs using a polynomial approximation with external boundary conditions.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 2000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(EA = 6 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ N}\)
  • \(EJ = 12 \cdot 10^{10} \text{ Nmm}^2\)
  • \(K_1 = 1 \cdot 10^7 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(K_2 = 1 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(\ell_1 = 1000 \text{ mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Polynomial Approximation with External Boundary Conditions

Define polynomial approximations for the displacements:

\[ W_1 = \partial_{w1} \cdot z_1 \qquad W_2 = \partial_{w2} \cdot z_2 \] \[ V_1 = \partial_1 \cdot z_1^2 \qquad V_2 = \partial_2 \cdot z_2^2 \]

Internal BC — Vertical displacement continuity:

\[ \partial_{w1} \cdot \ell = - \partial_{w2} \cdot \ell_1 \]

This gives:

\[ \partial_{w1} = - \partial_{w2} \cdot \frac{\ell_1}{\ell} = \partial_w \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad \partial_{w2} = - \partial_w \frac{\ell}{\ell_1} \]

Transversal displacement continuity:

\[ \partial_1 \cdot \ell^2 = - \partial_2 \cdot \ell_1 \]

This gives:

\[ \partial_1 = - \partial_2 \cdot \frac{\ell_1}{\ell^2} = \partial \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad \partial_2 = -\partial \frac{\ell^2}{\ell_1} \]

Step 2: Virtual Internal Work

The virtual internal work includes contributions from the beam, axial action, and both springs:

BEAM

\[ \delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta W_{1/z_1} \cdot N \, dz_1 + \int_0^\ell \left( \delta V_{1/z_1 z_1} \, EJ \cdot V_{1/z_1 z_1} + \delta V_{1/z_1} \, N \, V_{1/z_1} \right) dz_1 \]

SPRING 1 (axial internal action in spring):

\[ + \int_0^{\ell_1} \delta W_{2/z_2} \, N_{K1} \, dz_2 + \int_0^{\ell_1} \delta V_{2/z_2} \, N_{K1} \, V_{2/z_2} \, dz_2 \]

SPRING 2:

\[ + \delta V_1(\ell) \cdot K_2 \cdot V_1(\ell) \]

Constitutive relations:

\[ N = EA \, w_{1/z_1} = EA \cdot \partial_w \] \[ N_{K1} = K_1 \cdot w_2(\ell_1) = - K_1 \cdot \partial_w \cdot \ell \]

Step 3: Virtual External Work

\[ \delta W_e = - \delta W_1(\ell) \cdot F \]

Note: The parameters \(\partial_w\) and \(\partial\) are dependent through the compatibility conditions.

Step 4: Axial System Equilibrium

AXIAL EQUILIBRIUM

\[ F + \frac{EA}{c} \cdot w_1(\ell) + K_1 \cdot w_1(\ell) = 0 \]

Solving for the displacement at the spring location:

\[ w_1(\ell) = \frac{-F}{EA + K_1 \ell} \cdot \ell = \partial_w \cdot \ell \]

Step 5: Combined Virtual Work Equation

Combining axial and bending contributions:

\[ \delta \partial_w \left( \int_0^\ell EA \cdot \partial_w \, dz_1 + \int_0^{\ell_1} \left( -\frac{\ell}{\ell_1} \right) \cdot \left( K_1 \cdot \left( - \partial_w \cdot \frac{\ell}{\ell_1} \right) \cdot \ell_1 \right) dz_2 \right) = - \delta \partial_w \cdot F \]

BEAM contribution:

\[ \delta \partial \cdot \left( \int_0^\ell \left( 2z_1 \cdot 2z_1 \cdot \partial \cdot EA \cdot \left( -\frac{F}{EA + K_1 \ell} \right) + 4 \, EJ \cdot \partial \right) dz_1 \right) \]

where \(N = EA \cdot \partial_w = EA \, w_{1/z_1}\)

Step 6: Final Integration

Including the spring 1 bending contribution:

\[ + \int_0^{\ell_1} \left( \left( -\frac{\ell^2}{\ell_1} \right) \cdot \partial \cdot \left( -\frac{\ell^2}{\ell_1} \right) \cdot \left( -K_1 \cdot \partial_w \cdot \ell \right) \right) \cdot dz_1 = - \delta \partial \cdot \ell^2 K_2 \, \partial \, \ell^2 \]

where the terms under the brackets are \(\partial_2\) and \(N_{K1}\) respectively.

Step 7: Critical Load Determination

Solving the characteristic equation yields two cases:

  • \(\partial = 0\): No transversal displacement (trivial solution)
  • \(\partial \neq 0\): Non-trivial solution gives the critical load
Final Answer: \[\boxed{F_{cr} = 244{,}000 \text{ N}}\]

The case \(\partial = 0\) corresponds to no transversal displacement (trivial solution).
