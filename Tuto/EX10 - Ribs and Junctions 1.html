Problem 1: Riveted Connection Between Thin Plates

EXAM 05/07/2023 — Find the shear stress in rivet #1 for a riveted connection between three thin rectangular plates.

Given Data:

  • \(J_{xx} = 1.5 \times 10^4 \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\)
  • \(A_1 = A_2 = A = 5000 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(A_3 = 3500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(P = 100 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\phi = 7 \text{ mm}\) (rivet diameter)
  • \(a = 500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 240 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(F = 1000 \text{ N}\)

Solution

Step 1: Shear Fluxes in the Panels

The shear flux in the panel is given by:

\[q = -F \cdot \frac{S_{x1}}{J_{xx}} = -P \cdot \left( -\frac{A \cdot \frac{b}{2}}{J_{xx}} \right)\]

Note: Here there is not a stringer (i.e., axial load), thus \(q\) is constant along all the panel side.

Step 2: Analysis of Plate 3

The force transmitted through the rivet in plate 3:

\[F_3 = q \cdot P\]

The shear stress in the rivet for plate 3:

\[\tau_3 = \frac{F_3}{A_{rivet}} = \frac{q \cdot P}{\pi \left( \frac{\phi}{2} \right)^2}\]

This represents the area of influence \(\int q \cdot P\).

Step 3: Analysis of Plate 2

The shear flux from plate 3 is distributed to plate 2. The force in the rivet:

\[F_2 = \frac{F_3}{2} = \frac{q \cdot P}{2}\]

The shear stress in rivet #1:

\[\tau_1 = \frac{F_2}{A_{rivet}} = \frac{\frac{1}{2} q \cdot P}{\pi \left( \frac{\phi}{2} \right)^2}\]
Shear Stress in Rivet #1: \[\boxed{\tau_1 = \frac{q \cdot P}{2\pi \left( \frac{\phi}{2} \right)^2}}\]

Problem 2: Clamped Beam Assembly with Distributed Loads

For a rectangular box beam with clamped support, find the shear flux \(q_i\) and normal stress \(\sigma_{zz}\) at \(\ell/2\) from the clamp, and plot the internal actions in the rib.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(a = 400 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 250 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_1 = 1 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_2 = 2 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t_3 = 1.5 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A_1 = 2000 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(A_2 = 1000 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(F = 10 \text{ kN}\)
  • \(p_1 = 90 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(p_2 = 300 \text{ N/mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Section Properties

Centroid location:

\[\bar{y}_C = \frac{2 A_2 b}{2A_1 + 2A_2} = \frac{1}{3} b\]

Second moment of area:

\[J_{xx} = 2A_2 \left( \frac{2}{3} b \right)^2 + 2A_1 \left( -\frac{1}{3} b \right)^2 = \frac{4}{3} A_2 b^2\]

First moments of area:

\[S_{x1} = S_{x2} = -A_1 \cdot \frac{b}{3} = -\frac{2}{3} A_2 b\] \[S_{x3} = S_{x4} = \frac{2}{3} A_2\]

Step 2: Open Cell Fluxes

Computing fluxes for a generic \(T_y\) and \(M_z\):

\[q_1' = -T_y \frac{S_{x1}}{J_{xx}} = \frac{T_y}{2b} = -q_3'\] \[q_2' = 0\]

Step 3: Moment Equilibrium (wrt point 2)

Left-hand side:

\[LHS = M_z - T_y \cdot \frac{a}{2}\]

Right-hand side:

\[RHS = 2 q^* \Omega_{cell} - 2 q_1' \Omega_1\]

Where \(\Omega_{cell} = a \cdot b\) and \(\Omega_1 = a \cdot \frac{b}{2}\)

Equilibrium equation:

\[M_z - T_y \frac{a}{2} = 2q^* ab - 2 \frac{T_y}{2b} \cdot \frac{ab}{2}\]

Solving for closed cell flux:

\[q^* = \frac{M_z}{2ab}\]

Step 4: Lumped Forces from External Loads

From point load \(F\):

\[T_{yF} = F\] \[M_{zF} = -F \cdot \frac{a}{2}\]

From distributed load \(p_1\):

\[T_{yP1} = p_1 \cdot a\] \[M_{zP1} = 0\]

From triangular distributed load \(p_2\):

\[T_{yP2} = \frac{1}{2} P_2 \cdot a\] \[M_{zP2} = -\left( \frac{1}{2} P_2 \cdot a \right) \cdot \frac{1}{6} a\]

Step 5: Internal Actions in the Beam

Section ①:

\[\begin{cases} T_{y1} = -T_{yF} - T_{yP1} \\ M_{x1} = -(T_{yF} + T_{yP1}) \cdot z_z \\ M_{z1} = M_{zF} + M_{zP1} \end{cases}\]

Section ②:

\[\begin{cases} T_{y2} = -T_{yP2} - T_{yP1} - T_{yF} \\ M_{x2} = -T_{yP2} \cdot z_2 - (T_{yP1} + T_{yF}) \cdot (\ell + z_2) \\ M_{z2} = M_{zP2} + M_{zP1} + M_{zF} \end{cases}\]

Step 6: Shear Fluxes at \(z_2 = \frac{\ell}{2}\)

\[q_1 = q_1' - q^* = \frac{T_{y2}}{2b} - \frac{M_{z2}}{2ab} = -262 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_2 = q_2' - q^* = -30 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_3 = q_3' - q^* = 182 \text{ N/mm}\] \[q_4 = -q^* = -30 \text{ N/mm}\]

Step 7: Axial Stress in Stringers at \(z_2 = \ell/2\)

\[\sigma_{zz1} = -\frac{M_{x2}}{J_{xx}} \cdot \frac{1}{3} b = 168.5 \text{ MPa} = \sigma_{zz2}\] \[\sigma_{zz3} = \sigma_{zz4} = \frac{M_{x2}}{J_{xx}} \cdot \frac{2}{3} b = -297 \text{ MPa}\]

Step 8: Internal Actions in the Rib

Shear flux continuity and jump relations:

\[q_{z2} = q_{z1}\] \[q_{zR} = q_{z2} - q_{z1}\]

The rib equilibrates: (1) the external force \(p_2\), and (2) the shear fluxes jump in the panel. The rib must be in equilibrium.

Step 9: Beam Model of the Rib

This model allows an estimation of the internal actions in the rib, along one of its sides.

Note: \(q_{2R} = q_{3R} = q^*\)

Distributed bending moment given by \(q_{2R}\) and \(q_{4R}\):

\[m = 2 q_{2R} \cdot \Omega + 2 q_{4R} \cdot \Omega = 2(q_{2R} + q_{4R}) \cdot \left( \frac{1}{2} \frac{b}{2} d\eta \right)\]

For \(d\eta = 1\): \(m\) = bending moment per unit length given by \(q_{2R}\) and \(q_{4R}\)

Internal shear in rib:

\[T = -q_{3R} \cdot b - \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\eta}{a} P_2 \right) \cdot \eta\]

Internal moment in rib:

\[M = -2 q_{3R} \left( \frac{1}{2} b \eta \right) - \left( \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{\eta}{a} \cdot P_2 \right) \eta \right) \cdot \left( \frac{1}{3} \eta \right) - \int_0^{\eta} m \, d\eta\]
Results at \(z_2 = \ell/2\):
\[\boxed{q_1 = -262 \text{ N/mm}, \quad q_2 = -30 \text{ N/mm}, \quad q_3 = 182 \text{ N/mm}, \quad q_4 = -30 \text{ N/mm}}\] \[\boxed{\sigma_{zz1} = \sigma_{zz2} = 168.5 \text{ MPa}, \quad \sigma_{zz3} = \sigma_{zz4} = -297 \text{ MPa}}\]

Problem 3: Square Hollow Section Beam with Riveted Connections

For a square hollow section beam with clamped support, find the normal stress \(\sigma_{zz}\) at \(\ell/2\) and the shear stress in the rivets.

Given Data:

  • \(A_1 = 300 \text{ mm}^2 = 2A\)
  • \(A_2 = 150 \text{ mm}^2 = A\)
  • \(t = 1.5 \text{ mm}\) (all panels)
  • \(w = 100 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(E = 72000 \text{ MPa}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(\ell = 1500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(d_1 = 2 \text{ mm}\), \(p_1 = 10 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(d_2 = 3 \text{ mm}\), \(p_2 = 12 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(F = 15 \text{ kN}\)

Solution

Step 1: Section Properties

If we rotate the section 45°, it becomes symmetric with respect to \(x\) and \(y\) axes, so \(J_{xy} = 0\).

Second moments of area:

\[J_{xx} = 2 A_1 \left( \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} w \right)^2 = A_1 w^2 = 2Aw^2\] \[J_{yy} = 2 A_2 \left( \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} w \right)^2 = A_2 w^2 = Aw^2\]

First moments of area:

\[S_{x1} = -S_{x3} = -A_1 \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} w = -\sqrt{2} Aw\] \[S_{x2} = S_{x4} = 0\] \[S_{y1} = S_{y3} = 0\] \[S_{y2} = -S_{y4} = A_2 \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} w = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} Aw\]

Step 2: Open Cell Fluxes

\[q_1' = -F_y \frac{S_{x1}}{J_{xx}} - F_x \frac{S_{y1}}{J_{yy}} = 0\] \[q_2' = -F_y \frac{S_{x1} + S_{x2}}{J_{xx}} - F_y \frac{S_{y1} + S_{y2}}{J_{yy}} = 0\] \[q_3' = -F_y \frac{S_{x4}}{J_{xx}} - F_x \frac{S_{y4}}{J_{yy}} = 0\]

Step 3: Moment Equivalence (wrt point 2)

\[LHS = 0\] \[RHS = 2q^* w^2 - 2q_3' \frac{w^2}{2}\]

Solving for closed cell flux:

\[q^* = \frac{q_3'}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \left( F_x \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} w \right) = \frac{\sqrt{2} F_x}{4}\]

Step 4: Closed Cell Total Fluxes

\[q_1 = q_1' + q^*\] \[q_2 = q_2' + q^*\] \[q_3 = q_3' - q^*\] \[q_4 = -q^*\]

Step 5: Reaction Forces and Internal Actions

Support reactions:

\[R_x = F_x \qquad RM_x = F_y \cdot \ell\] \[R_y = -F_y \qquad RM_y = F_x \cdot \ell\]

We don't care about \(RM_z\) because it doesn't act on stringers.

Internal actions at section \(z\):

\[T_x = -F_x\] \[T_y = F_y\] \[M_x = F_y (z - \ell)\] \[M_y = F_x (z - \ell)\]

Step 6: Stress in Stringers at \(z = \frac{\ell}{2}\)

\[\sigma_{zz1} = + \frac{M_x(\frac{\ell}{2})}{J_{xx}} \cdot y = + \frac{F_y \cdot \frac{\ell}{2}}{J_{xx}} \left( + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} w \right) = 187.5 \text{ MPa} = -\sigma_{zz3}\] \[\sigma_{zz2} = - \frac{M_y}{J_{yy}} \cdot x = + \frac{(+F_x \cdot z_1)}{J_{yy}} \cdot \left( \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} w \right) = 375.0 \text{ MPa} = -\sigma_{zz4}\]

Step 7: Shear Stress in Rivets

Rivet forces from shear flux:

\[F_1 = q_3 \cdot p_1\] \[F_2 = q_2 \cdot p_2\]

Shear stresses in rivets:

\[\tau_1 = \frac{F_1}{\pi \left( \frac{d_1}{2} \right)^2} = \frac{q_3 \cdot p_1}{\pi \left( \frac{d_1}{2} \right)^2} = 119.37 \text{ MPa}\] \[\tau_2 = \frac{F_2}{\pi \left( \frac{d_2}{2} \right)^2} = \frac{q_2 \cdot p_2}{\pi \left( \frac{d_2}{2} \right)^2} = 190.99 \text{ MPa}\]
Results:
\[\boxed{\sigma_{zz1} = 187.5 \text{ MPa}, \quad \sigma_{zz2} = 375.0 \text{ MPa}}\] \[\boxed{\sigma_{zz3} = -187.5 \text{ MPa}, \quad \sigma_{zz4} = -375.0 \text{ MPa}}\] \[\boxed{\tau_1 = 119.37 \text{ MPa}, \quad \tau_2 = 190.99 \text{ MPa}}\]

Problem 4: Moment in Rib of Rectangular Box Beam

For a rectangular box beam with a rib, find the moment in the rib at \(\eta = \frac{a}{3}\).

Given Data:

  • \(a = 500 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(b = 250 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(t = 0.6 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(A = 500 \text{ mm}^2\)
  • \(\ell = 2000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(P = 1000 \text{ N}\)

Solution

Step 1: Open Cell Fluxes

\[q_1' = q_2' = \frac{T_y}{2b}\] \[q_3' = 0\]

Step 2: Moment Equivalence (wrt point 4)

\[LHS = 0\] \[RHS = 2q^* ab + 2 \frac{T_y}{2b} \cdot \frac{ab}{2}\]

Solving for closed cell flux:

\[q^* = -\frac{T_y}{4b}\]

Step 3: Total Fluxes

\[q_1 = q_1' - q^* = \frac{T_y}{2b} + \frac{T_y}{4b} = \frac{3}{4} \frac{T_y}{b}\] \[q_2 = q_2' + q^* = \frac{T_y}{4b}\] \[q_3^* = -q^* = \frac{T_y}{4b}\] \[q_4 = -q^* = \frac{T_y}{4b}\]

Step 4: Internal Action in the Rib

Shear flux jumps in the rib (with \(T_y = P\)):

\[q_{1R} = \frac{3}{4} \frac{P}{b} \qquad q_{3R} = \frac{P}{4b}\] \[q_{2R} = \frac{P}{4b} \qquad q_{4R} = \frac{P}{4b}\]

Step 5: Moment Distribution in Rib

The moment at position \(\eta\) along the rib:

\[M(\eta) = 2 \cdot q_{1R} \left( \frac{b\eta}{2} \right) + 2 q_{3R} \left( \frac{b\eta}{4} \right) + 2 \cdot q_{4R} \left( \frac{b\eta}{4} \right) - P \cdot \eta\]
Moment at \(\eta = \frac{a}{3}\): \[\boxed{M\left( \frac{a}{3} \right) = 0}\]
