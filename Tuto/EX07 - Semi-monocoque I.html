LAB 7 - Semi-Monocoque Approximation

Shear Flux Analysis in Thin-Walled Structures

Semi-Monocoque Theory

Semi-Monocoque Assumptions

  • Panel thickness \(t\) is small → \(\tau\) is constant through the thickness
  • Panels carry shear loads only → shear flux is constant between any couple of directly connected stringers
  • Area of panels is concentrated in the stringers
  • Stringers must have an area which is smaller than the dimension of the section
  • Ribs (if present) have infinitely high stiffness in-plane

Centroid

Centroid = geometrical center

\[r_c = \frac{\int_A r \, dA}{\int_A dA}\]

Center of gravity = mass center

\[r_{CG} = \frac{\int_V \rho(r) \, dV}{\int_V dV}\]

If the density is uniform → centroid = center of gravity

For discrete areas:

\[\bar{x}_c = \frac{\sum_i A_i \bar{x}_i}{\sum_i A_i} \qquad \bar{y}_c = \frac{\sum_i A_i \bar{y}_i}{\sum_i A_i}\]

Static Moments

\[S_x = \int_A y \, dA \quad \rightarrow \quad S_x = \sum_i A_i \cdot y_i\] \[S_y = \int_A x \, dA \quad \rightarrow \quad S_y = \sum_i A_i \cdot x_i\]

Inertias (with respect to Centroid)

\[J_{xx} = \int_A y^2 \, dA \quad \rightarrow \quad J_{xx} = \sum_i A_i \cdot y_i^2\] \[J_{yy} = \int_A x^2 \, dA \quad \rightarrow \quad J_{yy} = \sum_i A_i \cdot x_i^2\] \[J_{xy} = \int_A x \cdot y \, dA \quad \rightarrow \quad J_{xy} = \sum_i A_i \cdot x_i \cdot y_i\]

Principal Inertial Axis

If \(J_{xy} \neq 0\), \(M_x\) and \(M_y\) are coupled:

\[\begin{bmatrix} M_x \\ M_y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} EJ_{xx} & -EJ_{xy} \\ -EJ_{xy} & J_{yy} \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} \varkappa_x \\ \varkappa_y \end{bmatrix}\]

Then we must rotate \(x\) and \(y\) by an angle \(\alpha\):

\[\alpha = \frac{1}{2} \arctan\left(\frac{2 \cdot J_{xy}}{J_{yy} - J_{xx}}\right)\]

Axial Stress in the Stringer

\[\sigma_{zz,i} = \frac{T_z}{\sum_i A_i} + \frac{M_x}{J_{xx}} \cdot y_i - \frac{M_y}{J_{yy}} \cdot x_i\]

Shear Fluxes in the Panel

Open Cell Fluxes

Assumption: An open-section beam has negligible torsional stiffness → we are isolating the shear fluxes given by the shear forces applied to the section.

For each region R of our section:

\[q'_R = -T_y \frac{\sum_i S_{x,i}}{J_{xx}} - T_x \frac{\sum_i S_{y,i}}{J_{yy}}\]

Sign convention:

  • OUTWARD → Positive
  • INWARD → Negative

Moment Equivalence

Torsional moment given by the shear fluxes (equivalent to \(T_y\)):

\[M_z = \sum_i q_i \cdot 2\Omega_i\]

where \(\Omega_i\) is the area formed by the chosen point (e.g., centroid) and the panel.

Total Shear Flux Decomposition

Define \(q^*\) as the shear flux given by the torsional moment, such that for each panel \(i\):

\[q_i = q'_i + q^*\]

where \(q'_i\) = shear flux from shear forces, \(q^*\) = constant shear flux from torsional moment

Moment of External Forces = Moment of the Shear Fluxes

\[T_y \cdot \bar{x} = \sum_i (q'_i + q^*) \cdot 2\Omega_i\]

Problem 1: Rectangular Section Shear Fluxes

Find the shear fluxes in a rectangular thin-walled section with four stringers at the corners under an applied shear force \(T_y\).

Given:

  • Rectangular section with width \(b\) and height \(a\)
  • Four stringers with area \(A\) at corners
  • Panel thickness \(t\)
  • Applied shear force \(T_y\)

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Centroid Location

\[\bar{x}_c = \frac{\sum_i A_i \bar{x}_i}{\sum_i A_i} = \frac{2Ab + 2A \cdot 0}{4A} = \frac{b}{2}\] \[\bar{y}_c = \frac{\sum_i A_i \bar{y}_i}{\sum_i A_i} = \frac{2Aa + 2a \cdot 0}{4A} = \frac{a}{2}\]

Step 2: Calculate Moments of Inertia

\[J_{xx} = \sum_i A_i \cdot y_i^2 = 2A \cdot \left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 + 2A \cdot \left(-\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 = Aa^2\] \[J_{yy} = \sum_i A_i \cdot x_i^2 = Ab^2\] \[J_{xy} = \sum_i A_i \cdot x_i \cdot y_i = 0 \quad \text{(x and y are principal axes)}\]

Step 3: Calculate Static Moment

\[S_{x1} = -A \cdot \frac{a}{2}\]

Step 4: Calculate Open Cell Fluxes

Region I:

\[q'_1 - 0 = -T_y \cdot \frac{S_{x1}}{J_{xx}} = -T_y \cdot \frac{-\frac{1}{2}Aa}{Aa^2} = \frac{1}{2}\frac{T_y}{a}\]

Region II:

\[q'_2 = -T_y \cdot \frac{S_{x2}}{J_{xx}} = \frac{1}{2}\frac{T_y}{a}\]

Region III:

\[q'_3 = -T_y \cdot \frac{(S_{x1} + S_{x4})}{J_{xx}} = -T_y \cdot \frac{-\frac{1}{2}Aa + \frac{1}{2}Aa}{Aa^2} = 0\]

Step 5: Moment Equivalence (with respect to Centroid)

\[T_y \cdot 0 = \sum_i (q'_i + q^*) \cdot 2\Omega_i\] \[0 = q^* \cdot 2\Omega_{cell} - q'_1 \cdot 2\Omega_1 + q'_2 \cdot 2\Omega_2\] \[0 = q^* \cdot 2ab - \frac{T_y}{2a} \cdot 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot a \cdot \frac{b}{2} + \frac{T_y}{2a} \cdot 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot a \cdot \frac{b}{2}\] \[q^* \cdot 2ab = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad q^* = 0\]
Final Answers: \[q_1 = q'_1 - q^* = \frac{T_y}{2a}\] \[q_2 = q'_2 + q^* = \frac{T_y}{2a}\] \[q_3 = q'_3 - q^* = 0\] \[q_4 = q'_4 - q^* = 0\]

Problem 2: Trapezoidal Section Analysis

Find the shear fluxes in a trapezoidal thin-walled section under combined shear force and bending moment.

Given:

  • \(A = 250\, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(a = 50\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(b = 600\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(T_y = 3000\, \text{N}\)
  • \(M_z = 650{,}000\, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Centroid and Inertias

\[\bar{x}_c = \frac{\sum_i A_i \bar{x}_i}{\sum_i A_i} = 0\] \[\bar{y}_c = \frac{3A \cdot 5a}{5A} = 3a\] \[J_{xx} = \sum_i A_i y_i^2 = 3A \cdot (2a)^2 + 2A \cdot (3a)^2 = 30Aa^2\]

Step 2: Static Moments

\[S_{x1} = S_{x2} = A \cdot (-3a) = -3Aa\] \[S_{x3} = S_{x4} = S_{x5} = A \cdot (2a) = 2Aa\]

Step 3: Open Cell Fluxes

Region I:

\[q'_1 = -T_y \cdot \frac{S_{x1}}{J_{xx}} = \frac{T_y}{10a}\]

Region II:

\[q'_2 = -T_y \cdot \frac{S_{x2}}{J_{xx}} = \frac{T_y}{10a}\]

Region III:

\[q'_3 = -T_y \cdot \frac{2Aa - 3Aa}{J_{xx}} = \frac{T_y}{10a}\]

By symmetry: \(q'_3 = q'_4\)

Step 4: Moment Equivalence (with respect to Centroid)

Moment of external forces = Moment of shear fluxes:

\[M_z + T_y \cdot 0 = q^* \cdot 2\Omega_{cell} - q'_1 \cdot 2\Omega_1 + q'_2 \cdot 2\Omega_2 + q'_3 \cdot 2\Omega_3 - q'_4 \cdot 2\Omega_4\]

Cell area:

\[\Omega_{cell} = \left(\frac{1}{2}b \cdot 5a\right) \cdot 3 = \frac{15ab}{2}\]

Torsional flux:

\[q^* = \frac{M_z}{2\Omega_{cell}} = 1\, \text{N/mm}\]
Final Answers: \[q_1 = q'_1 - q^* = 5\, \text{N/mm}\] \[q_2 = q'_2 + q^* = 7\, \text{N/mm}\] \[q_3 = q'_3 + q^* = 3\, \text{N/mm}\] \[q_4 = q'_4 - q^* = 1\, \text{N/mm}\] \[q_5 = q'_5 + q^* = 1\, \text{N/mm}\]

Problem 3: Cantilever Frame Structure (EXAM 16/06/2021)

Find the axial stress \(\sigma_{zz,4}\) at position \(z = \ell_3/2\) for an L-shaped cantilever frame.

Given:

  • \(\ell_1 = 600\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(\ell_2 = 250\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(\ell_3 = 2000\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(A = 800\, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(F = 8000\, \text{N}\)

Solution

Step 1: Stress Formula

\[\sigma_{zz,4}(z) = \frac{T_z(z)}{\sum_i A_i} + \frac{M_x(z)}{J_{xx}} \cdot y_4 - \frac{M_y(z)}{J_{yy}} \cdot x_4\]

Step 2: Centroid Location

\[x_c = \ell_1/2 \qquad y_c = \ell_2/2\]

Step 3: Moments of Inertia

\[J_{xx} = 4A \cdot \left(\frac{\ell_2}{2}\right)^2 = A\ell_2^2\] \[J_{yy} = 4A \cdot \left(\frac{\ell_1}{2}\right)^2 = A\ell_1^2\]

Step 4: Internal Actions

\[T_y = -F\] \[M_x = F \cdot (-z) = -Fz\]

Step 5: Calculate Stress at Node 4

\[\sigma_{zz,4}\left(\frac{\ell_3}{2}\right) = \frac{M_x(\ell_3/2)}{J_{xx}} \cdot y_4\] \[= \frac{-F \cdot \frac{\ell_3}{2}}{A\ell_2^2}\left(-\frac{\ell_2}{2}\right) = \frac{1}{4}\frac{F\ell_3}{A\ell_2}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\sigma_{zz,4}\left(\frac{\ell_3}{2}\right) = 32\, \text{MPa}}\]

Problem 4: Triangular Section Rotation (EXAM 13/02/2024)

Find \(\theta\), the rotation of the free extremity for a triangular thin-walled section.

Given:

  • \(a = 500\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(A = 400\, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(t = 1\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(\ell = 4000\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(E = 70\, \text{GPa}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(F = 10\, \text{kN}\)

Solution

Step 1: Centroid (Original Axes)

\[\bar{x}_c = \frac{-Aa}{3A} = \frac{a}{3} \qquad \bar{y}_c = \frac{Aa}{3A} = \frac{a}{3}\]

Step 2: Check for Principal Axes

\[J_{xy} = \sum_i A_i \cdot x_i \cdot y_i = A \cdot \frac{a}{3} \cdot \left(-\frac{a}{3}\right) + A\left(-\frac{2}{3}a\right) \cdot \left(-\frac{a}{3}\right) + A\left(\frac{a}{3}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{2}{3}a\right) = \frac{Aa^2}{3}\]

Since \(J_{xy} \neq 0\), we must rotate the section.

Step 3: Rotated Coordinate System

\[b = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}a \qquad c = 2b = \sqrt{2} \cdot a\]

After rotation:

\[J_{xy} = A\left(\frac{2}{3}b\right) \cdot 0 + A\left(-\frac{b}{3}\right)\left(\frac{c}{2}\right) + A\left(-\frac{b}{3}\right)\left(-\frac{c}{2}\right) = 0\] \[J_{xx} = 2A\left(\frac{c}{2}\right)^2 = 2Ab^2\] \[J_{yy} = 2A\left(\frac{b}{3}\right)^2 + A\left(\frac{2}{3}b\right)^2 = \frac{2}{3}Ab^2\]

Step 4: Static Moments

\[S_{x1} = -S_{x3} = -A \cdot \frac{c}{2} = -Ab \qquad S_{x2} = 0\] \[S_{y1} = S_{y3} = +\frac{Ab}{3} \qquad S_{y2} = -\frac{2}{3}Ab\]

Step 5: Load Components

\[T_y = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}F \qquad T_x = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}F\]

Step 6: Open Cell Fluxes

Region I:

\[q'_1 = -T_y \cdot \frac{S_{x1}}{J_{xx}} - T_x \cdot \frac{S_{y1}}{J_{yy}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}F \cdot \frac{Ab}{2Ab^2} - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}F \cdot \frac{Ab}{2Ab^2} = 0\]

Region II:

\[q'_2 = +T_y \cdot \frac{S_{x3}}{J_{xx}} + T_x \cdot \frac{S_{y3}}{J_{yy}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}F}{2} \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2b} + \frac{1}{2b}\right) = \frac{\sqrt{2}F}{2b}\]

Step 7: Moment Equivalence (with respect to point 2)

\[0 = q^* \cdot 2\Omega_{cell} \quad \Rightarrow \quad q^* = 0\] \[q_2 = q'_2 - q^* = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\frac{F}{b}\]

Step 8: Angle Computation

\[\theta' = \frac{d\theta}{dz} = \frac{1}{2\Omega_{cell} \cdot G} \cdot \sum_i \frac{q_i \cdot \ell_i}{t_i}\]

where \(G = \frac{E}{2(1+\nu)}\) and \(\Omega_{cell} = \frac{1}{2}a^2\)

\[\theta' = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2}{a^2} \cdot \frac{2(1+\nu)}{E} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}F}{2b} \cdot \frac{a}{t}\] \[\theta' = \frac{2(1+\nu)}{Eat} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}F}{2} \cdot \frac{2}{\sqrt{2}a} = \frac{2(1+\nu) \cdot F}{Ea^2t} = 1.49 \times 10^{-6}\, \text{rad/mm}\]

Step 9: Total Rotation at Free End

\[\theta(\ell) - \theta(0) = \int_0^\ell \theta' \, dz\]

Boundary condition: \(\theta(0) = 0\) (clamped end)

\[\theta(\ell) = \theta' \cdot \ell\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta(\ell) = 5.9 \times 10^{-3}\, \text{rad}}\]

Problem 5: Shear Center Location (EXAM 23/01/2024)

Find the shear center location for a right triangular thin-walled section.

Given:

  • \(a = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(A = 4000\, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(E = 10^4\, \text{MPa}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)

Solution

Step 1: Geometric Relationship

\[b = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}a\]

Step 2: Centroid Location

\[\bar{x}_c = \frac{2}{3}b \qquad \bar{y}_c = 0\]

By symmetry: \(y_{SC} = 0\)

Step 3: Inertias and Static Moments

\[J_{xx} = 2Ab^2\] \[S_{x1} = -Ab \qquad S_{x2} = Ab \qquad S_{x3} = 0\]

Step 4: Shear Center Definition

Define a shear force \(T_y = 1\) passing through the shear center. The rotation of the section due to \(T_y\) must be zero.

Step 5: Open Cell Fluxes

\[q'_1 = T_y \cdot \frac{S_{x2}}{J_{xx}} = \frac{Ab}{2Ab^2} = \frac{1}{2b}\] \[q'_2 = T_y \cdot \frac{S_{x1}}{J_{xx}} = \frac{-Ab}{2Ab^2} = -\frac{1}{2b}\]

Step 6: Moment Equivalence (with respect to point 2)

\[T_y \cdot \left(\frac{2}{3}b + x_{SC}\right) = 2\Omega_{cell} \cdot q^*\] \[q^* = \frac{\frac{2}{3}b + x_{SC}}{2b^2}\]

Step 7: Rotation Angle Condition

\[\theta' = \frac{d\theta}{dz} = \frac{1}{2\Omega_{cell} \cdot G} \cdot \left(\frac{q^* \cdot 2a}{t} + \frac{q^* \cdot 2b}{2t} - \frac{q'_1 \cdot a}{t} + \frac{q'_2 \cdot a}{t}\right)\]

Impose \(\theta' = 0\):

\[q^* = \frac{a}{b(2a+b)}\]

Step 8: Solve for Shear Center

Combining the expressions for \(q^*\):

\[\frac{\frac{2}{3}b + x_{SC}}{2b^2} = \frac{a}{b(2a+b)}\] \[x_{SC} = \frac{2ab^2}{b(2a+b)} - \frac{2}{3}b\]

Substituting \(b = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}a\):

\[x_{SC} = \frac{2a \cdot \frac{1}{2}a^2}{\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}a\left(2a + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}a\right)} - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}a\] \[= \frac{a^3}{a^2\left(\sqrt{2} + \frac{1}{2}\right)} - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}a\] \[= \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2} + \frac{1}{2}} - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}\right)a\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{x_{SC} = 51.00\, \text{mm}}\]

Notes on the Coordinate Systems

Centroid

For coordinate origin at lower left corner:

\[x = \frac{2Ab}{4A} = 2b \qquad y = \frac{2Aa}{4A} = 2a\]

For coordinate origin at upper right corner:

\[x = \frac{-2Ab}{4A} = -2b \qquad y = \frac{-2Aa}{4A} = -2a\]
Key Point: The position of the centroid does NOT depend on the coordinate system. It is a section property.

Inertia

For different coordinate system placements (all with respect to centroid):

\[J_{xx} = 2A \cdot \left(-\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 + 2A \cdot \left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 = Aa^2\]

Result is the same regardless of axis direction.

Key Point: Inertias depend on the position of the reference axes, but do NOT depend on their direction.

Static Moments

OUTWARD system:

\[S_{x1} = A \cdot \left(-\frac{a}{2}\right) = -\frac{Aa}{2}\] \[S_{y1} = A \cdot \left(-\frac{b}{2}\right) = -\frac{Ab}{2}\]

INWARD system:

\[S_{x1} = A\left(\frac{a}{2}\right) = \frac{Aa}{2}\] \[S_{y1} = A\left(\frac{b}{2}\right) = \frac{Ab}{2}\]
Key Point: For static moments and fluxes, we must select a surface, defined by its normal vector, and keep it!

Open Cell Fluxes

The fluxes orientation is ARBITRARY and does not depend on \(\vec{z}\), but we must keep the same normal.

OUTWARD: \(q'_1 > 0\), \(q'_2 > 0\)

INWARD: \(q'_1 < 0\), \(q'_2 < 0\)

Closed Cell Fluxes

The positive rotation is given by the normal axis \(z\):

  • OUTWARD → ANTI-CLOCKWISE
  • INWARD → CLOCKWISE

The direction of \(q^*\) is ARBITRARY.

Angle

The sign of \(\theta'\) depends on the normal axis \(z\).

GOOD PRACTICE: Choose a surface and always keep it throughout the analysis.
