LAB 8 - Semi-monocoque II - Multicell Sections

Problem 1: EXAM 25/01/2023

Analyze a two-cell rectangular cross-section under torsion. Find the rate of twist \(\theta'\).

Given Data:

  • \(a = 600\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(b = 300\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(A = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(t_1 = 1\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(t_2 = 2\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(E = 70\, \text{GPa}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(M_z = 5 \cdot 10^6\, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Open Cell Fluxes

Since there are no transverse shear forces:

\[T_x = T_y = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad q_i^1 = 0\]

Step 2: Closed Cell Fluxes

Define closed-cell shear flows \(q_1^*\) for the left cell and \(q_2^*\) for the right cell, both with counterclockwise positive direction.

The shear flow in the middle web is \((q_1^* - q_2^*)\) or \((q_2^* - q_1^*)\) depending on direction.

Step 3: Moment Equivalence with respect to point P

\[M_z = 2q_1^* \Omega_{cell1} + 2q_2^* \Omega_{cell2} = 2ab(q_1^* + q_2^*)\]

Step 4: Angle of Twist Expressions

For cell 1:

\[\theta_1' = \frac{d\theta_1}{dz} = \frac{1}{2\Omega_{cell1} \cdot G} \left( \frac{2q_1^* \cdot a}{t_2} + \frac{q_1^* \cdot b}{t_2} + \frac{(q_1^* - q_2^*)b}{t_1} \right)\]

For cell 2:

\[\theta_2' = \frac{1}{2\Omega_{cell2} \cdot G} \left( \frac{2q_2^* \cdot a}{t_2} + \frac{q_2^* \cdot b}{t_1} + \frac{(q_2^* - q_1^*)b}{t_1} \right)\]

Where the shear modulus is:

\[G = \frac{E}{2(1+\nu)}\]

Step 5: Compatibility Condition

For a rigid cross-section, both cells must rotate at the same rate:

\[\theta_1' = \theta_2'\]

Step 6: Solve the System

Solving the system of equations (Moment Equivalence + Compatibility) to find \(q_1^*\) and \(q_2^*\):

\[\begin{cases} \text{Moment Eq.} \\ \text{Compatibility} \end{cases} \longrightarrow \text{solve to find } q_1^*, q_2^*\]

Once we have them, we can use the angle expressions to find:

Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta' = 1.019 \cdot 10^{-6}\, \frac{\text{rad}}{\text{mm}}}\]

Problem 2: EXAM 05/07/2023

Analyze a cantilever beam with a two-cell rectangular cross-section under torsion. Find the rotation at \(z = \frac{\ell}{3}\).

Given Data:

  • \(t = 1\, \text{mm}\) for all the panels
  • \(\ell = 6000\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(a = 1000\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(b = 500\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(A_1 = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(A_2 = 1000\, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(E = 70\, \text{GPa}\)
  • \(\nu = 0.3\)
  • \(M_z = 10^9\, \text{N} \cdot \text{mm}\)

Solution

Step 1: Open Cell Fluxes

Since there are no transverse shear forces:

\[T_x = T_y = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad q_i^1 = 0\]

Step 2: Closed Cell Fluxes

The enclosed area of each cell:

\[\Omega_{cell} = a \cdot b\]

Step 3: Moment Equivalence with respect to point P

\[M_z = 2q_1^* \cdot \Omega_{cell1} + 2 \cdot q_2^* \cdot \Omega_{cell2}\]

Step 4: Angle of Twist Expressions

For cell 1:

\[\theta_1' = \frac{1}{2\Omega_{cell1} \cdot G} \left( \frac{q_1^*(2a + 2b)}{t} - \frac{q_2^* b}{t} \right)\]

For cell 2:

\[\theta_2' = \frac{1}{2\Omega_{cell2} \cdot G} \left( \frac{q_2^*(2a + 2b)}{t} - \frac{q_1^* \cdot b}{t} \right)\]

Step 5: Compatibility Condition

We impose \(\theta_1' = \theta_2'\):

\[\frac{1}{2\Omega_{cell1} G \cdot t} (q_1^*(2a+2b) - q_2^* b) = \frac{1}{2\Omega_{cell2} G \cdot t} (q_2^*(2a+2b) - q_1^* b)\]

Simplifying:

\[q_1^*(2a + 2b) + q_1^* b = q_2^*(2a + 2b) + q_2^* b\] \[q_1^*(2a + 3b) = q_2^*(2a + 3b)\]

Therefore:

\[q_1^* = q_2^* = q^*\]

Step 6: Solve for Closed-Cell Flux

\[q^* = \frac{M_z}{4\Omega_{cell}} = \frac{M_z}{4ab}\]

Step 7: Rate of Twist

\[\theta' = \frac{1}{2ab \cdot G \cdot t} \cdot \frac{M_z}{4ab} \cdot (2a + b)\]

Step 8: Compute the Rotation

At the clamped (fixed) end, \(\theta(0) = 0\). Integrating:

\[\theta\left(\frac{\ell}{3}\right) - \theta(0) = \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{3}} \theta' \, dz = \theta' \cdot \frac{\ell}{3}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{\theta\left(\frac{\ell}{3}\right) = 0.0929\, \text{rad}}\]

Problem 3: EXAM 05/09/2023

Analyze a triangular truss structure (semi-monocoque section) with a vertical load. Find the shear flow \(q_1\).

Given Data:

  • \(a = 3000\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(b = 300\, \text{mm}\)
  • \(A = 500\, \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(t = 3\, \text{mm}\) for all the panels
  • \(F = 30\, \text{kN}\)

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Inertias

Second moment of area about x-axis:

\[J_{xx} = 6A\left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{3}{2}Ab^2\]

First moments of area for each stringer:

\[S_{x1} = S_{x2} = S_{x3} = -\frac{Ab}{2}\] \[S_{x4} = S_{x5} = \frac{3}{4}Ab\]

Step 2: Open Cell Fluxes

Section I:

\[q_1^1 = +F \cdot \frac{S_{x4}}{J_{xx}} = F \cdot \frac{\frac{3}{4}Ab}{\frac{3}{2}Ab^2} = \frac{F}{2b}\]

Section II:

\[q_2^1 = q_1^1 = \frac{F}{2b}\]

Section III:

\[q_3^1 = q_1^1 + F \cdot \frac{S_{x1}}{J_{xx}} = \frac{F}{2b} - \frac{F}{3b} = \frac{F}{6b} = q_4^1\]

Step 3: Closed Cell Fluxes

Define closed-cell fluxes \(q_1^*\), \(q_2^*\), \(q_3^*\) for each cell with counterclockwise positive direction.

Step 4: Moment Equivalence with respect to node 2

\[0 = 2q_1^* \Omega_{cell1} + 2q_2^* \cdot \Omega_{cell2} + 2q_3^* \cdot \Omega_{cell3} + 2q_1^1 \Omega_{cell3} - 2q_1^1 \Omega_{cell2}\]

Where the cell areas are:

\[\Omega_{cell1} = \frac{ab}{2} \qquad \Omega_{cell2} = \Omega_{cell3} = \frac{ab}{4}\]

Simplifying:

\[0 = q_1^* + \frac{1}{2}q_2^* + \frac{1}{2}q_3^*\]

Step 5: Angle of Twist Expressions

The length of the diagonal member is:

\[\ell_d = \sqrt{\left(\frac{a}{2}\right)^2 + b^2}\]

For cell 1:

\[\theta_1' = \frac{1}{2 \cdot \frac{1}{2}ab \cdot G \cdot t} \left( a \cdot q_1^* + (2q_2^* - q_2^* - q_3^*) \ell_d \right)\]

For cell 2:

\[\theta_2' = \frac{1}{2 \cdot \frac{1}{4}ab \cdot G \cdot t} \left( b(q_2^* - q_1^1) + \frac{a}{2}(q_2^* - q_3^1) + (q_2^* - q_1^*) \cdot \ell_d \right)\]

For cell 3:

\[\theta_3' = \frac{1}{2 \cdot \frac{1}{4}ab \cdot G \cdot t} \left( b(q_3^* + q_2^1) + \frac{a}{2}(q_3^* + q_4^1) + (q_3^* - q_2^*) \cdot \ell_d \right)\]

Step 6: Solve the System

Apply compatibility conditions:

\[\text{solve} \begin{cases} \text{MOM. EQ.} \\ \theta_1' = \theta_2' \\ \theta_3' = \theta_2' \end{cases} \longrightarrow q_1^*, q_2^*, q_3^*\]

The shear flow in panel 1 is:

\[q_1 = q_1^1 - q_2^*\]

Alternative Method: Exploiting Symmetry

Due to symmetry, the following relationships hold:

\[q_1 = q_2\] \[q_3 = q_4\] \[q_6 = q_7\] \[q_5 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad q_5 = -q_3^* = 0\]

Also:

\[\theta_i' = 0\] \[q_1^* = -q_2^* = q^*\]

Open Cell Fluxes (from symmetry cut):

\[q_1^1 = -F \cdot \frac{-\frac{Ab}{2}}{\frac{3}{2}Ab^2} = \frac{F}{3b}\]

Force equilibrium:

\[F = b(2q_1^1 + 2q_6^1)\] \[q_6^1 = \frac{F}{2b} - q_1^1 = \frac{F}{6b}\]

From zero twist condition:

\[\theta_1' = 0 = \frac{1}{2\Omega_1 G t} \left( q^*(b + \ell_d + \frac{a}{2}) + q_6^1 \cdot \ell_d - q_1^1 b \right) = 0\]

Solving for \(q^*\):

\[q^* = \frac{-q_6^1 \ell_d + q_1^1 b}{b + \ell_d + \frac{a}{2}}\]

Finally:

\[q_1 = q_1^1 - q^*\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{q_1 = 37.9869\, \frac{\text{N}}{\text{mm}}}\]
