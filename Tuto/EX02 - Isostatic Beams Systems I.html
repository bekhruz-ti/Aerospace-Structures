Isostatic Beams Systems I

Definition: Isostatic System

Isostatic → n rigid DOF = n constraints

When this condition is satisfied, we can compute the reaction forces using the equilibrium equations.

Problem 1: 2D Cantilever Beam with Point Load

Analyze a cantilever beam system to find the reaction forces and internal actions.

Given:

  • Beam length: \(\ell\) (known)
  • Applied force: \(F\)
  • Fixed support at left end

Find:

  • Reaction Forces
  • Internal Actions

Isostatic Check (2D)

3 rigid DOF = 3 constraints → YES, the system is isostatic!

Therefore, we can compute the reaction forces using the equilibrium equations.

Solution

Step 1: Reaction Forces

Setting up equilibrium equations with reactions \(R_z\), \(R_y\), and \(RM_x\) at point A:

Translation in z:

\[R_z = 0\]

Translation in y:

\[R_y + F = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_y = -F\]

Rotation about x (with respect to A):

\[RM_x + F \cdot \ell = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad RM_x = -F\ell\]
Reaction Forces: \[R_z = 0, \quad R_y = -F, \quad RM_x = -F\ell\]

Step 2: Internal Actions

Let's virtually cut our beam and analyze the internal forces.

For the cut section at position \(z_2\) from the fixed end:

With respect to origin O:

\[T_{z2} = R_z = 0\] \[T_{y2} = -R_y = F\] \[M_{x2} = RM_x - R_y \cdot z_2 = -F\ell + F \cdot z_2\]

Step 3: Compute Internal Action at \(z = \frac{\ell}{2}\)

\[M_{x2} = -F\ell + F \cdot \frac{\ell}{2} = -F\frac{\ell}{2}\]
Key Principle: EVERYTHING IS IN EQUILIBRIUM:
  • Full beam
  • Beam parts
  • Interface between segments
Internal Actions: \[T_{z1} = 0, \quad T_{y1} = -F, \quad M_{x1} = -F \cdot z_1\]

Problem 2: 3D Angled Beam Structure

Analyze a 3D angled beam structure consisting of an inclined member connected to a horizontal segment.

Given:

  • Inclined segment length: \(\ell\)
  • Horizontal segment length: \(\frac{1}{2}\ell\)
  • Vertical upward force: \(F\) applied at junction
  • Fixed support at upper end

Find:

  • Reaction Forces
  • Internal Actions

Isostatic Check (3D)

6 rigid DOF = 6 constraints → YES, the system is isostatic!

Sign Convention:
  • ↑ Force (upward positive)
  • ↗ Moment (counterclockwise positive when viewed from positive axis direction)

Solution

Step 1: Reaction Forces

Force equilibrium:

\[X: \quad R_x = 0\] \[Y: \quad R_y + F = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_y = -F\] \[Z: \quad R_z = 0\]

Moment equilibrium:

\[\text{rot } X: \quad RM_x - F \cdot \ell = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad RM_x = F \cdot \ell\] \[\text{rot } Y: \quad RM_y = 0\] \[\text{rot } Z: \quad RM_z + F \cdot \frac{1}{2}\ell = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad RM_z = -\frac{1}{2}F \cdot \ell\]
Reaction Forces: \[R_x = 0, \quad R_y = -F, \quad R_z = 0\] \[RM_x = F\ell, \quad RM_y = 0, \quad RM_z = -\frac{1}{2}F\ell\]

Step 2: Internal Actions - Segment ①

For segment ① (horizontal segment), with respect to origin O:

\[T_{y1} = -F\] \[T_{z1} = 0\] \[M_{x1} = -F \cdot z_1\]

Step 3: Internal Actions - Segment ② (Piecewise Approach)

Method I: Piecewise Approach

To compute the internal actions of segment ②, we will use the internal actions of segment ① computed at its extremity (\(z_1 = \frac{1}{2}\ell\)).

We are imposing the equilibrium of the interface.

Internal actions for segment ② with respect to O:

\[T_{x2} = 0\] \[T_{y2} = -F\] \[T_{z2} = 0\] \[M_{x2} = -F \cdot z_2\] \[M_{y2} = 0\] \[M_{z2} = \frac{1}{2}F\ell\]

Step 4: Internal Actions - Segment ② (Global Approach)

Method II: Global Approach

Computing internal actions directly with respect to O:

\[T_{x2} = 0\] \[T_{y2} = -F\] \[T_{z2} = 0\] \[M_{x2} = -F \cdot z_2\] \[M_{y2} = 0\] \[M_{z2} = F \cdot \frac{1}{2}\ell\]

Important Note on Vector Products

When computing a moment, is the sign of the arm relevant?

Vector Product:

\[M = F \times b \quad \text{where} \quad |M| = |F| \cdot |b| \cdot \sin\theta\]

Sign properties:

\[a \times (-b) = -(a \times b)\] \[(-a) \times b = -(a \times b)\]

Examples:

\[M_x = F \cdot b = Fb \quad \text{(positive arm)}\] \[M_x = F \cdot (-b) = -Fb \quad \text{(negative arm)}\]

Problem 3: Cantilever Beam with Distributed Pressure

A pressure P is applied on the beam's top surface. Find the equivalent loads, reaction forces, and internal actions.

Given:

  • Beam length: \(\ell\) (with segments \(\ell_1\) and \(\ell_2\))
  • Distributed pressure: \(P\) [N/m²]
  • Point load: \(F\) (downward)
  • Beam width: \(c\)

Find:

  • Equivalent line load \(L_{eq}\) [N/m]
  • Equivalent concentrated load \(F_{eq}\) [N]
  • Reaction Forces (RF)
  • Internal Actions (IA)

Solution

Step 1: Find Equivalent Line Load \(L_{eq}\)

Let's find a line load [N/m] \(L_{eq}\) which is equivalent to P [N/m²].

\(L_{eq}\) must have:

  • The right MAGNITUDE to give an equivalent FORCE distribution
  • The right POINT OF APPLICATION to give an equivalent MOMENT distribution

Magnitude calculation:

\[L_{eq} = \int_0^c P(x,z) \, dx = \int_0^c P \, dx = \left[P \cdot x\right]_0^c = P \cdot c\]

Point of application (centroid):

\[x_{eq} = \frac{1}{L_{eq}} \int_0^c P(x,y) \cdot x \cdot dx = \frac{1}{Pc} \int_0^c P \cdot x \cdot dx = \frac{1}{Pc} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot Pc^2 = \frac{1}{2}c\]

Step 2: Find Equivalent Concentrated Load \(F_{eq}\)

Let's find a concentrated load \(F_{eq}\) [N] which is equivalent to \(L_{eq}\) [N/m].

Equivalent force:

\[F_{eq} = \int_0^\ell L_{eq} \cdot dz = \int_0^\ell pc \cdot dz = \left[pcz\right]_0^\ell = pc\ell\]

Location of equivalent force (centroid):

\[z_{eq} = \frac{1}{F_{eq}} \cdot \int_0^\ell L_{eq} \cdot z \cdot dz = \frac{1}{pc\ell} \int_0^\ell pc \cdot z \, dz\] \[= \frac{1}{pc\ell} \cdot \left[\frac{1}{2} pc \, z^2\right]_0^\ell = \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{pc\ell} \cdot pc\ell^2 = \frac{1}{2}\ell\]
Equivalent Loads: \[F_{eq} = pc\ell \quad \text{applied at} \quad z_{eq} = \frac{\ell}{2}\]

Step 3: Reaction Forces

Using equilibrium with \(F_{eq} = p \cdot c \cdot \ell\):

Force equilibrium (y-direction):

\[R_y - F + pc\ell = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_y = F - pc\ell\]

Force equilibrium (z-direction):

\[R_z = 0\]

Moment equilibrium (with respect to A):

\[RM_x - F \cdot \ell_1 + pc\ell \cdot \frac{\ell}{2} = 0\] \[RM_x = F \cdot \ell_1 - pc\frac{\ell^2}{2}\]

Step 4: Internal Actions - Segment ①

For segment ① (under distributed load):

\[T_{y1} = -p \cdot c \cdot z_1\] \[T_{z1} = 0\] \[M_{x1} = -pcz_1 \cdot \frac{z_1}{2} = -pc\frac{z_1^2}{2}\]

Note: The moment arm is \(\frac{z_1}{2}\) because the resultant of the distributed load over length \(z_1\) acts at the centroid.

Step 5: Internal Actions - Segment ②

For segment ② (under both distributed load and point load F):

\[T_{y2} = F - pc(\ell_2 + z_2)\] \[T_{z2} = 0\] \[M_{x2} = +Fz_2 - pc(\ell_2 + z_2) \cdot \frac{(\ell_2 + z_2)}{2}\]

Note: The moment arm for the distributed load is \(\frac{(\ell_2 + z_2)}{2}\) representing the centroid of the loaded length.

Problem 4: 3D Frame Structure with Three Supports

Analyze a 3D frame structure consisting of an inclined member and a horizontal beam with pinned supports.

Given:

  • Inclined member (segment 3): length \(\ell\), unloaded
  • Horizontal beam with supports at points A, B, and C
  • Support spacing: \(\frac{\ell}{4}\) between each support
  • Vertical upward force: \(F\) applied at junction

Find:

  • Reaction Forces (RF)
  • Internal Actions (IA)

System Analysis

The system is isostatic.

External loads don't generate translations in x or rotations in y.

Solution

Step 1: Reaction Forces - Initial Equilibrium

Reactions at supports: \(R_{xA}\), \(R_{yA}\), \(R_{zA}\) at A; \(R_{xB}\), \(R_{yB}\) at B; \(R_{yC}\) at C

Force equilibrium:

\[z: \quad R_{zA} = 0\] \[x: \quad R_{xA} = R_{xB} = 0\] \[y: \quad R_{yA} + R_{yB} + R_{yC} + F = 0 \quad (*)\]

Step 2: Reaction Forces - Moment Equations (with respect to B)

Rotation about x (wrt B):

\[R_{yA} \cdot \ell = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_{yA} = 0\]

Rotation about z (wrt B):

\[F \cdot \frac{\ell}{4} + R_{yC} \cdot \frac{\ell}{2} = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_{yC} = -\frac{1}{2}F\]

Substituting into equation (*):

\[R_{yB} + R_{yC} + F = 0\] \[R_{yB} - \frac{1}{2}F + F = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_{yB} = -\frac{1}{2}F\]
Reaction Forces: \[R_{xA} = R_{xB} = R_{zA} = R_{yA} = 0\] \[R_{yB} = -\frac{1}{2}F, \quad R_{yC} = -\frac{1}{2}F\]

Step 3: Internal Actions

The structure shows:

  • Segment ③: Inclined unloaded member from A to B
  • Segment ②: Between B and load application point
  • Segment ①: Between load point and C
  • Forces: \(\frac{1}{2}F\) downward at both B and C

Step 4: Internal Actions - Segment ①

For segment ① (between load point and support C):

\[T_{y1} = \frac{1}{2}F\] \[M_{x1} = \frac{1}{2}F \cdot z_1\]

Step 5: Internal Actions - Segment ②

For segment ② (between support B and load application point):

\[T_{y2} = \frac{1}{2}F + F = -\frac{1}{2}F\] \[M_{x2} = \frac{1}{2}F \cdot \left(\frac{\ell}{4} + z_2\right) - F \cdot z_2\]
Internal Actions Summary:

Segment ①:

\[T_{y1} = \frac{1}{2}F, \quad M_{x1} = \frac{1}{2}F \cdot z_1\]

Segment ②:

\[T_{y2} = -\frac{1}{2}F, \quad M_{x2} = \frac{1}{2}F \cdot \left(\frac{\ell}{4} + z_2\right) - F \cdot z_2\]
Note: Segment ③ (the inclined member) is unloaded, meaning the internal actions can be determined from equilibrium at the interface with the loaded segments.
