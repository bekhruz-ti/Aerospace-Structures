Problem 1: 2D Cantilever Beam with Triangular Distributed Load

Analyze a 2D isostatic cantilever beam system with a triangular distributed load. The beam has length \(\ell\) with a fixed support at the left end and a roller support at the right end. A linearly varying distributed load increases from zero at the left to maximum intensity \(p\) [N/mm] at the right end.

Objectives:

  • Find Reaction Forces
  • Find Internal Actions

Given:

  • Beam length: \(\ell\)
  • Maximum load intensity: \(p\) [N/mm]
  • Load function: \(f(z)\) with \(f(\ell) = p\)
  • System is Isostatic

Solution

Step 1: Determine the Load Function

The load function has the form:

\[f(z) = m \cdot z + q\]

where \(m = \frac{p}{\ell}\)

Since \(f(0) = 0\), we have \(q = 0\)

\[f(z) = \frac{p}{\ell} \cdot z\]

Step 2: Calculate the Equivalent Force

\[R = \int_0^\ell f(z) \, dz = \int_0^\ell \frac{p}{\ell} z \, dz = \left[\frac{1}{2} \frac{p}{\ell} z^2\right]_0^\ell = \frac{1}{2} p \cdot \ell\]

Step 3: Find the Equivalent Application Point

\[z_{eq} = \frac{1}{R} \int_0^\ell f(z) \cdot z \cdot dz = \frac{2}{3} \ell\]

Reaction Forces

Equilibrium in z-direction:

\[R_{zA} = 0\]

Equilibrium in y-direction:

\[R_{yA} + R_{yB} + \frac{1}{2}p\ell = 0\]

Moment equilibrium about point A:

\[-R_{yB} \cdot \ell - \frac{1}{2} p\ell \cdot \frac{2}{3}\ell = 0\]
\[R_{yB} = -\frac{1}{3} p\ell\] \[R_{yA} = -\frac{1}{6} p\ell\]

Internal Actions - From the Left

For a cut at position \(z_1\) from the left:

\[T_{z1} = 0\] \[T_{y1} = \frac{1}{6}p\ell - \frac{1}{2}\frac{p}{\ell} \cdot z_1 \cdot z_1\]

Note: The second term represents the area of the triangle (½ × height × base)

\[M_{x1} = \frac{1}{6}p\ell \cdot z_1 - \frac{1}{2}\frac{p}{\ell}z_1^2 \cdot \frac{1}{3}z_1\]

Note: The moment arm for the triangular load is \(\frac{1}{3}z_1\)

Internal Actions - From the Right

Load intensity relationships:

\[p - p\left(1-\frac{z_z}{\ell}\right) = \frac{p}{\ell}z_z\] \[\frac{p}{\ell}(\ell - z_z) = p\left(1 - \frac{z_z}{\ell}\right)\]

For a cut at position \(z_2\) from the right:

\[T_{z2} = 0\] \[T_{y2} = -\frac{1}{2}\frac{p}{\ell}z_2 \cdot z_2 - p\left(1-\frac{z_2}{\ell}\right) \cdot z_2 + \frac{1}{3}p\ell\] \[M_{x2} = \frac{1}{3}p\ell \cdot z_2 - \frac{1}{2}\frac{p}{\ell}z_2^2 \cdot \frac{2}{3}z_2 - p\left(1-\frac{z_2}{\ell}\right) \cdot z_2 \cdot \frac{1}{2}z_2\]

Problem 2: 3D Cantilever Beam with Uniform Distributed Load

Analyze a three-dimensional cantilever beam with a uniform distributed load \(p\) [N/mm²] applied over a partial length \(c\). The beam has total length \(\ell\) and is fixed at the left end.

Given:

  • Total beam length: \(\ell\)
  • Load applied over length: \(c\)
  • Load intensity: \(p\) [N/mm²]

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Resultant Force and Position

Resultant force:

\[R = \frac{1}{2}p \cdot c\]

Position of resultant from reference:

\[x_0 = \frac{1}{2}c - \frac{2}{3}c = -\frac{1}{6}c\]

Fixed-end moment:

\[M_z = R \cdot x_0 = -\frac{1}{2}pc \cdot \frac{1}{6}c = -\frac{1}{12}pc^2\]

Note: Units are Moment × unit length: \(\left[\frac{N}{mm^2} \cdot mm^2 = \frac{N \cdot mm}{mm}\right]\)

Reaction Forces

Equilibrium in z-direction:

\[R_z = 0\]

Equilibrium in y-direction:

\[R_y = -\frac{1}{2}pc\ell\]

Rotation about X-axis:

\[RM_x = -\frac{1}{2}pc\ell \cdot \frac{1}{2}\ell\]

Rotation about Z-axis (torsional moment):

\[RM_z = \frac{1}{12}pc^2 \cdot \ell\]
\[R_z = 0, \quad R_y = -\frac{1}{2}pc\ell\] \[RM_x = -\frac{1}{4}pc\ell^2, \quad RM_z = \frac{1}{12}pc^2\ell\]

Internal Actions

For a cut at position \(z_1\):

\[T_y = -\frac{1}{2}pc \cdot z_1\] \[M_x = -\frac{1}{2}pcz_1 \cdot \frac{1}{2}z_1 = -\frac{1}{4}pcz_1^2\] \[M_z = \frac{1}{12}pc^2 \cdot z_1\]

Problem 3: 3D Frame Structure with Inclined Member

Analyze a three-dimensional isostatic frame structure consisting of an inclined beam (member 3) connected to a horizontal beam with supports at points A and B. The inclined beam carries a uniform distributed load \(p \cdot c\) [N/mm] along its length \(\ell\). A concentrated force \(F\) is applied at the center of the horizontal beam.

Objectives:

  • Find Reaction Forces (RF)
  • Find Internal Actions (AI)

Given:

  • System is Isostatic
  • Distributed load: \(p \cdot c\) [N/mm]
  • Member length: \(\ell\)
  • Horizontal spans: \(\ell/4\), \(\ell/4\)
  • Note: The inclined beam is out-of-plane

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Resultant of Distributed Load

\[R = pc \cdot \ell\] \[z_0 = \frac{\ell}{2}\]

Reaction Forces

Equilibrium in z-direction:

\[R_{zC} = 0\]

Equilibrium in x-direction:

\[R_{xB} + R_{xC} = 0 \quad \rightarrow \quad R_{xB} = R_{xC} = 0\]

Equilibrium in y-direction:

\[R_{yA} + R_{yB} + R_{yC} - F + pc\ell = 0 \quad (*)\]

Moment Equilibrium about point C:

Rotation about X:

\[pc\ell \cdot \frac{\ell}{2} + R_{yB} \cdot \ell - F \cdot \ell + R_{yA} \cdot \ell = 0 \quad (**)\]

Rotation about Y:

\[R_{xB} \cdot \ell = 0\]

Rotation about Z:

\[F \cdot \frac{\ell}{4} - R_{yA} \cdot \frac{\ell}{2} = 0\]
\[R_{yA} = \frac{1}{2}F\] \[R_{yB} = \frac{1}{2}(F - pc\ell)\] \[R_{yC} = -\frac{1}{2}pc\ell\]

Internal Actions

Section ①:

\[T_{z1} = 0\] \[T_{y1} = -\frac{1}{2}F\] \[M_{x1} = -\frac{1}{2}F \cdot z_1\]

Section ②:

\[T_{y2} = F - \frac{1}{2}F = \frac{1}{2}F\] \[M_{x2} = F \cdot z_2 - \frac{1}{2}F \cdot \left(\frac{\ell}{4} + z_2\right)\]

Section ③ (Inclined member):

\[T_{y3} = \frac{1}{2}pc\ell - pc \cdot z_3\] \[M_{x3} = \frac{1}{2}pc\ell \cdot z_3 - pc \cdot z_3 \cdot \frac{z_3}{2}\]

Problem 4: 2D Frame with Trapezoidal Distributed Load

Analyze a 2D frame structure consisting of a horizontal beam connected to an inclined member. The horizontal beam has a trapezoidal distributed load varying from intensity \(p_1\) [N/mm] at the left end to \(p_1 + p_2\) at the right end. The structure has a roller support at C (left), a pin connection at A, and a pin support at B (bottom).

Objectives:

  • Find Reaction Forces
  • Find Internal Actions

Given:

  • Horizontal spans: \(\ell\), \(\ell\)
  • Vertical height: \(\ell\)
  • Load intensity varies from \(p_1\) to \(p_1 + p_2\)
  • Resultant forces: \(R_1 = 2p_1\ell\), \(R_2 = p_2\ell\)

Solution

Reaction Forces - Equilibrium Equations

Horizontal member equilibrium:

z-direction:

\[R_{zC} + R_{zA} = 0 \quad (1)\]

y-direction:

\[R_{yC} + R_{yA} + R_1 + R_2 = 0 \quad (2)\]

Moment about A:

\[R_{yC} \cdot \ell + R_1 \cdot \ell + R_2 \cdot \frac{2}{3}\ell = 0 \quad (3)\]

Equilibrium at interface:

z-direction:

\[R_{zB} - R_{zC} = 0 \quad (4)\]

y-direction:

\[R_{yB} - R_{yC} = 0 \quad (5)\]

Moment about B:

\[-R_{yC}\ell - R_{zC}\ell = 0 \quad (6)\]

Step 2: Solve for Reactions

From equation (3):

\[R_{yC} = -R_1 - R_2 = -\left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\]

From equation (6):

\[R_{zC} = -R_{yC} = \left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\]

From equation (1):

\[R_{zA} = -R_{zC} = -\left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\]

From equation (2):

\[R_{yA} = -R_{yC} - R_1 - R_2 = \left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell - 2p_1\ell - p_2\ell = -\frac{1}{3}p_2\ell\]

From equation (4):

\[R_{zB} = R_{zC} = \left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\]

From equation (5):

\[R_{yB} = R_{yC} = -\left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\]
Reaction Forces: \[R_{yC} = -\left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\] \[R_{zC} = \left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\] \[R_{zA} = -\left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\] \[R_{yA} = -\frac{1}{3}p_2\ell\] \[R_{zB} = \left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\] \[R_{yB} = -\left(2p_1 + \frac{2}{3}p_2\right)\ell\]

Internal Actions

Section ① (Left portion of horizontal beam):

\[T_{z1} = 0\] \[T_{y1} = -p_1 \cdot z_1 - \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{p_2}{2\ell} \cdot z_1\] \[M_{x1} = -\frac{1}{2}p_1 z_1^2 - \frac{1}{6}\frac{p_2}{2\ell}z_1^2\]

Section ② (Right portion of horizontal beam):

\[T_{z2} = 0\] \[T_{y2} = -p_1(\ell + z_2) - \frac{1}{2}\frac{p_2}{2\ell}(\ell + z_2) + 2p_1\ell + \frac{2}{3}p_2\ell\] \[T_{z2} = -2p_1\ell - \frac{2}{3}p_2\ell\] \[M_{x2} = -\frac{1}{2}p_1(\ell + z_2)^2 - \frac{1}{6}\frac{p_2}{2\ell}(\ell + z_2)^2 + \left(2p_1\ell + \frac{2}{3}p_2\ell\right) \cdot z_2\]

Section ③ (Inclined member):

\[T_{y3} = 0\] \[M_{x3} = 0\] \[T_{z3} = \sqrt{2} \cdot \left(2p_1\ell + \frac{2}{3}p_2\ell\right)\]
