EX 09 - Semi-monocoque III

Analysis of Semi-monocoque Structure with Spring Supports

Accounting for Beam Shear Deformability

Problem 1: Spring Reaction Forces in Semi-monocoque Beam

Determine the reaction forces at the base of the two springs for a semi-monocoque cylindrical beam with a semicircular cross-section, accounting for beam shear deformability.

Given Data:

  • \(E = 70 \text{ GPa}\) — Young's modulus
  • \(\nu = 0.3\) — Poisson's ratio
  • \(A = 200 \text{ mm}^2\) — Stringer cross-sectional area
  • \(t = 1 \text{ mm}\) — Panel thickness (for all panels)
  • \(R = 200 \text{ mm}\) — Radius
  • \(\ell = 2000 \text{ mm}\) — Length
  • \(K = 10^6 \text{ N/mm}\) — Spring stiffness
  • \(P = 1 \text{ kN}\) — Applied load

Solution

Step 1: Determine Shear Fluxes as Function of Generic Shear Load

Let's find the shear fluxes \(q_i\) as a function of a generic shear load \(T_y\):

\[q_i = q_i(T_y)\]
Centroid Location
\[\bar{y}_c = 0\] \[\bar{x}_c = \frac{RA}{4A} = \frac{R}{4}\]
Second Moments of Area (Inertias)
\[J_{xx} = 2AR^2\]

Static moments:

\[S_{x1} = -A \cdot R\] \[S_{x2} = S_{x4} = 0\] \[S_{x3} = AR\]

Step 2: Open Cell Fluxes

For node 3:

\[q_1^1 = T_y \cdot \frac{S_{x3}}{J} = T_y \cdot \frac{AR}{2AR^2} = \frac{T_y}{2R}\]

For node 5:

\[q_5^1 = 0\]

For node 1:

\[q_2^1 = -T_y \frac{S_{x1}}{J_{xx}} = \frac{T_y}{2R}\]

Therefore:

\[q_1^1 = q_2^1 = q^1\]

Step 3: Moment Equivalence with respect to G

\[LHS = 0\] \[RHS = 2 \cdot q_1^* \cdot \Omega_{cell1} + 2 \cdot q_2^* \cdot \Omega_{cell2}\]

where:

\[\Omega_{cell1} = \Omega_{cell2} = \frac{\pi R^2}{2} = \Omega\]

This gives:

\[q_1^* = -q_2^* = q^*\]

Step 4: Compatibility Equations

\[\theta_1' = \frac{1}{2\Omega Gt} \left( q_1^* \left(2R + \frac{2\pi R}{4}\right) - q_2^* R - q_2^1 \cdot R \right)\] \[\theta_2' = \frac{1}{2\Omega Gt} \left( q_2^* \left(2R + \frac{2\pi R}{4}\right) - q_1^* R - q_2^1 \cdot R \right)\]

Setting \(\theta_1' = \theta_2'\):

\[q^*(2R + \frac{\pi R}{2}) + q^* R - q^1 R = -q^*(2R + \frac{\pi R}{2}) - q^* R - q^1 R\]

Solving yields:

\[q^* = 0\]

Step 5: Total Fluxes

\[q_1 = q_1^1 - q_1^* = \frac{T_y}{2R}\] \[q_2 = q_2^1 - q_2^* = \frac{T_y}{2R}\] \[q_3 = q_3^* = 0\] \[q_4 = q_4^* = 0\] \[q_5 = q_5^1 + q_1^* - q_2^* = 0\]

HYPERSTATIC REACTION — 1st Method: Deformation Work of Panels

The internal work related to shear forces and torsional moments is computed directly inside panels.

Internal virtual work expression:

\[\delta W_i = \int_V \delta \sigma \cdot \varepsilon \, dV = \int_0^\ell T_z^1 \frac{T_z}{EA} \, dz + \int_0^\ell M_x^1 \frac{M_x}{EJ_{xx}} \, dz + \int_0^\ell M_y^1 \frac{M_y}{J_y} \, dz + \ldots\]

Plus contributions from panels and springs:

\[\int_{V_p} \delta \sigma : \varepsilon \, dV_p + F^1 \cdot \frac{F}{K}\]
Internal Actions — Section 1

REAL:

\[T_{y1} = -P - R_{y1}\] \[M_{x1} = -(P + R_{y1}) \cdot z_1\]

VIRTUAL 1:

\[T_{y1}^1 = -1\] \[M_{x1}^1 = -z_1\]
Internal Actions — Section 2

REAL:

\[T_y = -P - R_{y1} - R_{y2}\] \[M_x = -(P + R_{y1})\left(\frac{\ell}{2} + z_2\right) - R_{y2} \cdot z_2\]

VIRTUAL 1:

\[T_y^1 = -1\] \[M_x^1 = -\left(\frac{\ell}{2} + z_2\right)\]

VIRTUAL 2:

\[T_y^{''} = -1\] \[M_x^{''} = -z_2\]
Panels Internal Work

Starting from the general expression:

\[\delta W_{ip} = \int_{V_p} \delta \sigma : \varepsilon \, dV_p = \int_{V_p} \delta \tau : \gamma \, dV_p\]

Using constitutive relations \(\tau = G \cdot \gamma\) and \(\gamma = \frac{\tau}{G}\):

\[= \int_{V_p} \delta \tau \cdot \frac{\tau}{G} \, dV_p = \int_\ell \int_R \int_t \delta \tau \frac{\tau}{G} \, dx \cdot dy \cdot dz\]

We know that \(\tau = \frac{q}{t}\) and \(\delta \tau = \frac{\delta q}{t}\)

Also, \(q_i = f(T_y)\) and \(\delta q_i = f(T_y^1)\)

In our case, \(T_y\) depends only on \(z\), thus \(q\) does too:

\[= \int_\ell \frac{\delta q}{t} \cdot \frac{1}{Gt} \int_R \int_t dx \, dy \, dz = \frac{R \cdot t}{t^2 G} \int_\ell \delta q \cdot q \, dz\]
PCVW — Virtual 1

External virtual work:

\[\delta W_e = 0\]

Internal virtual work:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} M_{x1}^1 \cdot \frac{M_{x1}}{EJ_{xx}} \, dz_1 + \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} M_{x2}^1 \cdot \frac{M_{x2}}{EJ_{xx}} + \ldots \quad \text{(stringers)}\] \[+ \frac{R}{tG} \left( \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} \delta q_1(T_{y1}^1) \cdot q_1(T_{y1}) \, dz_1 + \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} \delta q_1(T_{y2}^1) \cdot q_1(T_{y2}) \, dz_2 \right) \quad \text{(panel 1)}\] \[+ \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} \delta q_2(T_{y1}^1) \cdot q_2(T_{y1}) \, dz_1 + \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} \delta q_2(T_{y2}^1) \cdot q_2(T_{y2}) \, dz_2 \quad \text{(panel 2)}\] \[+ 1 \cdot \frac{R_{y1}}{K}\]
PCVW — Virtual 2

External virtual work:

\[\delta W_{e2} = 0\]

Internal virtual work:

\[\delta W_{i2} = \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} M_{x2}^1 \cdot \frac{M_{x2}}{EJ_{xx}} \, dz_2 + \frac{R}{tG} \left( \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} \delta q_1(T_{y2}^{''}) \cdot q_1(T_{y2}) \, dz_2 + \ldots \right.\] \[\left. + \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} \delta q_2(T_{y2}^{''}) \cdot q_2(T_{y2}) \, dz_2 \right) + 1 \cdot \frac{R_{y2}}{K}\]
Solution System

Solve the system:

\[\begin{cases} \delta W_{i1} = \delta W_{e1} \\ \delta W_{i2} = \delta W_{e2} \end{cases} \quad \rightarrow \quad R_{y1}, \, R_{y2}\]

2nd Method: Section Properties

Shear fluxes are used to compute the section properties \(GA^*\), \(GJ\), and shear center. Knowing them, all the internal work can be computed without directly computing the internal work in the panels.

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell T_z^1 \frac{T_z}{EA} + \int_0^\ell M_x^1 \cdot \frac{M_x}{EJ_{xx}} \, dz + \int_0^\ell M_y^1 \cdot \frac{M_y}{EJ_{yy}} \, dz + \int_0^\ell T_y^1 \cdot \frac{T_y}{GA^*} \, dz\] \[+ \int_0^\ell T_x^1 \frac{T_x}{GA^*} \, dz + \int_0^\ell M_z^1 \cdot \frac{M_z}{GJ} \, dz\]
Finding \(GA^*\) and Shear Center

Apply unit shear force: \(T_y = 1\)

Moment Equivalence with respect to G:

\[LHS = T_y \cdot d\] \[RHS = 2q_1^* \Omega + 2q_2^* \Omega \quad \text{(as before)}\]

Solve the system with compatibility conditions \(\theta_1' = \theta_2'\):

\[\begin{cases} \theta_1' = \theta_2' \\ \theta_1' = \theta_2' = 0 \end{cases} \quad \rightarrow \quad d, \, q_1^*, \, q_2^*\]

Then compute shear stiffness (with \(T_y\) applied at the shear center):

\[GA^* = G \frac{T_y^2}{\sum q_i^2 \cdot \frac{L_i}{t_i}}\]

where the sum is over all panels and \(L_i\) is the panel length.

Finding \(GJ\)

Apply unit torsional moment at shear center: \(M_z = 1\), \(T_y = T_x = 0\)

Moment Equilibrium with respect to G:

\[LHS = M_z\] \[RHS = \text{as before}\]

Note: \(q_i^1 = 0\) (no shear from open cell fluxes)

Solve:

\[\begin{cases} LHS = RHS \\ \theta_1' = \theta_2' \end{cases} \quad \rightarrow \quad q_1^*, \, q_2^* \quad \rightarrow \quad \theta'\]

Then:

\[M_z = GJ \cdot \theta' \quad \Rightarrow \quad GJ = \frac{M_z}{\theta'}\]
Internal Actions with Torsion

We have already computed \(T_y\) and \(M_x\). The torsional moments are:

Section 1:

\[\begin{cases} M_{z1} = (P + R_{y1}) \cdot d \\ M_{z1}^1 = d \end{cases}\]

Section 2:

\[\begin{cases} M_{z2} = (P + R_{y1} + R_{2y}) \cdot d \\ M_{z2}^1 = d \\ M_{z2}^{''} = d \end{cases}\]
PCVW — Virtual 1
\[\delta W_{e1} = 0\] \[\delta W_{i1} = \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} \left( T_{y1}^1 \cdot \frac{T_{y1}}{GA^*} + M_{z1}^1 \frac{M_{z1}}{GJ} + M_{x1}^1 \frac{M_{x1}}{EJ_{xx}} \right) dz_1 + \ldots\] \[\ldots + \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} \left( T_{y2}^1 \cdot \frac{T_{y2}}{GA^*} + M_{z2}^1 \frac{M_{z2}}{GJ} + M_{x1}^1 \frac{M_{x1}}{EJ_{xx}} \right) dz_2 + 1 \cdot \frac{R_{y1}}{K}\]
PCVW — Virtual 2
\[\delta W_{e2} = 0\] \[\delta W_{i2} = \int_0^{\frac{\ell}{2}} \left( T_{y2}^{''} \cdot \frac{T_{y2}}{GA^*} + M_{z2}^{''} \frac{M_{z2}}{GJ} + M_{x2}^{''} \frac{M_{x1}}{EJ_{xx}} \right) dz_2 + 1 \cdot \frac{R_{y2}}{K}\]
Solution System

Solve the system:

\[\begin{cases} \delta W_{e1} = \delta W_{i1} \\ \delta W_{o2} = \delta W_{e2} \end{cases} \quad \rightarrow \quad R_{y1}, \, R_{y2}\]
Final Answer:

\[\boxed{R_{y1} = -998.7155 \text{ N}}\]
\[\boxed{R_{y2} = -2.746 \text{ N}}\]
Notes:
  • Two methods were presented: the 1st method uses deformation work of panels directly, while the 2nd method uses section properties (\(GA^*\), \(GJ\), shear center).
  • Both methods utilize the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work (PCVW).
  • The negative signs in the final answers indicate that both spring reactions are compressive (the springs are being compressed).
  • The first spring carries nearly the entire reaction (\(\approx 999\) N), while the second spring carries a much smaller load (\(\approx 2.7\) N).
