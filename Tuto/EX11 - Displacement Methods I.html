EX 11 - Displacement Methods I

Structural Analysis using Principle of Virtual Work

Problem 1: Cantilever Beam with Uniform Load - Polynomial Approximation

We assume an arbitrary COMPATIBLE displacement function, then we solve the problem using PVW (Principle of Virtual Work).

Given:

  • Beam length: \(\ell\)
  • Uniform distributed load: \(q(z) = p\)
  • Fixed support at point B (right end)
  • Free end at point A (left end)

Solution

Part I: Polynomial Approximation of the Displacement in y

General polynomial form for displacement:

\[V(z) = C_0 + C_1 \cdot z + C_2 z^2 + C_3 z^3 + \ldots\]

Applying Essential Boundary Conditions:

\[\begin{cases} V(0) = 0 & \rightarrow & C_0 = 0 \\ V(\ell) = 0 & \rightarrow & C_1 = -(C_2 \ell + C_3 \ell^2 + \ldots) \end{cases}\]

Substituting the boundary conditions:

\[V(z) = C_2(z^2 - \ell z) + C_3(z^3 - \ell^2 z) + C_4(z^4 - \ell^3 z) + \ldots\] \[= \sum_{i=2}^{\infty} C_i \cdot \phi_i \quad \text{where} \quad \phi_i = (z^i - \ell \cdot z^{(i-1)}) \quad \text{(shape function)}\]

In matrix form:

\[= \underline{\Phi} \cdot \underline{C} = [\phi_1, \phi_2, \phi_3, \ldots, \phi_\infty] \cdot \begin{bmatrix} C_1 \\ \vdots \\ C_\infty \end{bmatrix}\]

Virtual displacement:

\[\delta V(z) = \sum_{i=2}^{\infty} \delta C_i \cdot \phi_i\]

One Term Approximation

Using only the first shape function:

\[V(z) = C_2 \cdot \phi_2 = C_2(z^2 - \ell z)\]

PVW: Internal Virtual Work = External Virtual Work

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^\ell \delta V_{/zz} \cdot EJ \cdot V_{/zz} \, dz\]

Computing derivatives:

\[V_{/z} = 2C_2 \cdot z - C_2 \cdot \ell \qquad \qquad \delta V = \delta C_2 (z^2 - \ell z)\] \[V_{/zz} = 2C_2 \qquad \qquad \delta V_{/z} = 2 \cdot \delta C_2 \cdot z - \delta C_2 \cdot \ell\] \[\delta V_{/zz} = 2 \cdot \delta C_2\]

External virtual work:

\[\delta W_e = \int_0^\ell \delta V \cdot q(z) \, dz\]

Applying PVW:

\[\int_0^\ell 2 \cdot \delta C_2 \cdot EJ \cdot 2C_2 \, dz = \int_0^\ell \delta C_2 \cdot (z^2 - \ell z) \cdot p \, dz\] \[\cancel{\delta C_2} \cdot C_2 \int_0^\ell 4 EJ \, dz = \cancel{\delta C_2} \int_0^\ell (z^2 - \ell z) \cdot p \, dz\] \[\underbrace{\hspace{1cm}}_{\underline{C}} \underbrace{\hspace{1cm}}_{\underline{K}} = \underbrace{\hspace{2cm}}_{\underline{f}}\]
One Term Result: \[\boxed{C_2 = -\frac{p \cdot \ell}{24 EJ}}\]

(to be checked)

Two Terms Approximation

Displacement with two shape functions:

\[V(z) = \begin{bmatrix} z^2 - \ell z & z^3 - \ell^2 z \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} C_2 \\ C_3 \end{bmatrix} = \underline{\Phi} \cdot \underline{C}\]

Second derivative:

\[V_{/zz} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 6z \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} C_2 \\ C_3 \end{bmatrix} = \underline{\Phi_{/zz}} \cdot \underline{C}\]

PVW formulation:

\[\int_0^\ell \delta V_{/zz} \cdot EJ \cdot V_{/zz} \, dz = \int_0^\ell \delta V^T \cdot q(z) \, dz\] \[\delta \underline{C}^T \int_0^\ell \underline{\Phi}_{/zz}^T \cdot EJ \cdot \underline{\Phi}_{/zz} \, dz \cdot \underline{C} = \delta \underline{C}^T \int_0^\ell \underline{\Phi}^T \cdot p \, dz\]

Stiffness matrix assembly:

\[EJ \cdot \int_0^\ell \begin{bmatrix} \phi_{2/zz} \cdot \phi_{2/zz} & \phi_{2/zz} \cdot \phi_{3/zz} \\ \phi_{3/zz} \cdot \phi_{2/zz} & \phi_{3/zz} \cdot \phi_{3/zz} \end{bmatrix} dz \cdot \underline{C} = \int_0^\ell \begin{bmatrix} \phi_2 \\ \phi_3 \end{bmatrix} \cdot p \, dz\]

Three Terms Approximation

Extending to three terms:

\[V(z) = [\Phi_2 \quad \Phi_3 \quad \Phi_4] \cdot \begin{bmatrix} C_2 \\ C_3 \\ C_4 \end{bmatrix}\]

Stiffness matrix:

\[\underline{K} = EJ \cdot \int_0^\ell \begin{bmatrix} \text{2 terms } \underline{K} & \Phi_{2/zz} \cdot \Phi_{4/zz} \\ & \Phi_{3/zz} \cdot \Phi_{4/zz} \\ sym & \Phi_{4/zz} \cdot \Phi_{4/zz} \end{bmatrix} dz\]

Load vector:

\[\underline{f} = \int_0^\ell \begin{bmatrix} \text{2 term} \\ \phi_4 \end{bmatrix} \cdot p \, dz\]

Linear Load Case

Load distribution:

\[q(z) = p\left(-\frac{z}{\ell} + \frac{1}{2}\right)\]

Right-hand side of PVW:

\[RHS = \int_0^\ell \delta \underline{V}^T \cdot q(z) \, dz = \delta \underline{C}^T \cdot \int_0^\ell \underline{\Phi}^T \cdot p\left(-\frac{z}{\ell} + \frac{1}{2}\right) dz\]

Problem 2: Trigonometric Approximation Method

Apply trigonometric shape functions to solve the cantilever beam problem with constant distributed load \(q(z) = p\).

Solution

Trigonometric Shape Functions

Shape function definition (satisfies boundary conditions):

\[\phi_i(z) = \sin\left(\frac{i\pi z}{\ell}\right)\]

Displacement series:

\[V(z) = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} C_i \cdot \sin\left(\frac{i\pi z}{\ell}\right) = \underline{\Phi} \cdot \underline{C}\]

First derivative:

\[\phi_{i/z} = \frac{\pi i}{\ell} \cdot \cos\left(\frac{i\pi z}{\ell}\right)\]

Second derivative:

\[\phi_{i/zz} = \left(\frac{\pi i}{\ell}\right)^2 \cdot \left(-\sin\left(\frac{i\pi z}{\ell}\right)\right)\]

One Term Approximation

PVW equation:

\[\int_0^\ell \delta V_{/zz} \cdot EJ \cdot V_{/zz} \, dz = \int_0^\ell \delta V \cdot q(z) \, dz\]

Substituting:

\[\delta C_1 \cdot \int_0^\ell \Phi_{1/zz} \cdot EJ \cdot \Phi_{1/zz} \cdot dz \cdot C_1 = \delta C_1 \cdot \int_0^\ell \Phi_1 \cdot p \cdot dz\] \[EJ \left(\frac{\pi}{\ell}\right)^4 \cdot \int_0^\ell \sin^2\left(\frac{\pi z}{\ell}\right) dz \cdot C_1 = \int_0^\ell \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{\ell}\right) dz \cdot p\]

Evaluating integrals:

\[\underbrace{\int_0^\ell \sin^2\left(\frac{\pi z}{\ell}\right) dz}_{\ell/2} \qquad \underbrace{\int_0^\ell \sin\left(\frac{\pi z}{\ell}\right) dz}_{2\ell/\pi}\]
One Term Trigonometric Result: \[\boxed{C_1 = \frac{4\ell^4}{\pi^5 EJ} \cdot p}\]

General Case: \(n \rightarrow \infty\)

PVW in matrix form:

\[\cancel{\delta \underline{C}^T} \cdot \int_0^\ell \underline{\Phi}_{/zz}^T \cdot EJ \cdot \underline{\Phi}_{/zz} dz \cdot \underline{C} = \cancel{\delta \underline{C}^T} \cdot \int_0^\ell \underline{\Phi} \cdot p \, dz\]

Stiffness matrix elements:

\[K_{ij} = EJ \cdot \int_0^\ell \phi_{i/zz} \cdot \phi_{j/zz} \, dz = EJ \cdot \int_0^\ell \sin\left(\frac{i\pi z}{\ell}\right) \cdot \sin\left(\frac{j\pi z}{\ell}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{i\pi}{\ell}\right)^2 \left(\frac{j\pi}{\ell}\right)^2 dz\]

Orthogonality property:

\[\begin{cases} i = j & \rightarrow \frac{\ell}{2} \\ i \neq j & \rightarrow 0 \end{cases}\]
Key Result: \(\underline{K}\) is diagonal due to orthogonality of trigonometric functions!

Load vector elements:

\[f_i = \int_0^\ell \phi_i \, dz \cdot p = \int_0^\ell \sin\left(\frac{i\pi z}{\ell}\right) dz = \begin{cases} 0 & \text{if } i \text{ is EVEN} \\ \frac{2\ell}{\pi i} & \text{if } i \text{ is ODD} \end{cases}\]

This is the area of the shape function - even functions have zero net area due to symmetry.

Problem 3: Inclined Beam with Force and Rotation (Exam 13/06/2023)

Analyze an inclined beam member using polynomial approximation for vertical displacement (2nd order) and rotation (1st order).

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(F = 1000 \text{ N}\)
  • \(GJ = 10^{10} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(EJ = 10^{10} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}\)
Note: "ochio al segno della rotazione" - attention to the sign of rotation

Solution

Polynomial Approximation Setup

Vertical Displacement (2nd Order):

\[V_1(z_1) = a_0 + a_1 \cdot z_1 + a_2 \cdot z_1^2\] \[V_2(z_2) = b_0 + b_1 \cdot z_2 + b_2 \cdot z_2^2\]

Z-Rotation (1st Order):

\[\theta_1(z_1) = C_0 + C_1 \cdot z\] \[\theta_2(z_2) = 0\]

Compatibility Conditions

BEAM 1 - Clamp:

\[V_1(0) = 0\] \[V_{1/z}(0) = 0 \quad \text{(x-rotation)}\] \[\theta_1(0) = 0 \quad \text{(z-rotation)}\]

This gives:

\[a_0 = a_1 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad V_1(z) = a_2 \cdot z_1^2\] \[C_0 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \theta_1(z) = C_1 \cdot z_1\]

BEAM 2 - BEAM 1 Connection:

\[V_2(0) = V_1(\ell) \quad \rightarrow \quad b_0 = a_2 \cdot \ell^2\] \[V_{2/z_2}(0) = \theta_1(\ell) \quad \rightarrow \quad b_1 = C_1 \cdot \ell\]

Note: if \(\theta_1(\ell) > 0 \rightarrow V_{2/z_2} > 0\) because of how \(\bar{V}\) is defined.

Final displacement form for beam 2:

\[V_2(z) = a_2 \cdot \ell^2 + C_1 \cdot \ell \cdot z + b_2 \cdot z_2^2\]

Principle of Virtual Work

Internal virtual work (three components):

\[\delta W_i = \underbrace{\int_0^\ell \delta V_{1/zz} \cdot EJ \cdot V_{1/zz} \cdot dz_1}_{\text{Bending Beam 1}} + \underbrace{\int_0^\ell \delta \theta_{1/z} \cdot GJ \cdot \theta_{1/z} \, dz_1}_{\text{Torsion Beam 1}} + \underbrace{\int_0^\ell \delta V_{2/zz} \cdot EJ \cdot V_{2/zz} \, dz_2}_{\text{Bending Beam 2}}\]

External virtual work:

\[\delta W_e = -\delta V_2(\ell) \cdot F = -(\delta a_2 + \delta C_1 + \delta b_2) \cdot \ell^2 \cdot F\]
Key Insight: We can rewrite the PLV as a system of three equations because the three components of internal work are energetically decoupled.
Coefficient Beam 1 Beam 2
\(a_2\) bending rigid translation (no work)
\(C_1\) torsion rigid rotation
\(b_2\) — bending

Solving the Three Decoupled Equations

Equation 1 (Bending, Beam 1):

\[\delta a_2 \cdot a_2 \cdot \int_0^\ell 2 \cdot EJ \cdot 2 \, dz_1 = -\delta a_2 \cdot \ell^2 \cdot F\] \[a_2 = -\frac{F\ell}{4EJ}\]

Equation 2 (Torsion, Beam 1):

\[\delta C_1 \cdot C_1 \cdot \int_0^\ell GJ \, dz_1 = -\delta C_1 \cdot \ell^2 \cdot F\] \[C_1 = -\frac{F\ell}{GJ}\]

Equation 3 (Bending, Beam 2):

\[\delta b_2 \cdot b_2 \cdot \int_0^\ell 2 \cdot EJ \cdot 2 \, dz_2 = -\delta b_2 \cdot \ell^2 \cdot F\] \[b_2 = -\frac{F\ell}{4EJ}\]

Matrix Form:

\[\delta \begin{bmatrix} a_2 \\ C_1 \\ b_2 \end{bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{bmatrix} \int_0^\ell 4EJ \, dz_1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \int_0^\ell GJ \, dz_1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \int_0^\ell 4EJ \, dz_2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} a_2 \\ C_1 \\ b_2 \end{bmatrix} = \delta \begin{bmatrix} a_2 \\ C_1 \\ b_2 \end{bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \ell^2 \cdot F\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{V_2(\ell) = -150 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 4: Two-Member L-Frame Structure

Analyze a two-member frame structure with axial and transversal displacements using compatibility conditions and the Principle of Virtual Work.

Displacement Fields:

  • Axial displacement:
    • Member 1: \(W_1 = a_0 + a_1 \cdot z_1\)
    • Member 2: \(W_2 = b_0 + b_1 \cdot z_2\)
  • Transversal displacement:
    • Member 1: \(V_1 = c_0 + c_1 \cdot z_1 + c_2 \cdot z_1^2\)
    • Member 2: \(V_2 = d_0 + d_1 \cdot z_2 + d_2 \cdot z_2^2\)

Solution

Compatibility Conditions

Translation at the clamp:

\[a_0 = b_0 = c_0 = d_0 = 0\]

Rotation at the clamp:

\[c_1 = d_1 = 0\]

Displacement continuity at joint:

\[V_1(\ell) = W_2(\ell) \quad \rightarrow \quad b_1 = c_2 \ell\] \[V_2(\ell) = W_1(\ell) \quad \rightarrow \quad a_1 = d_2 \ell\]

Let \(c = c_2\) and \(d = d_2\), then:

\[W_1 = d \cdot \ell \cdot z_1\] \[W_2 = c \cdot \ell \cdot z_2\] \[V_1 = c \cdot z_1^2\] \[V_2 = d \cdot z_2^2\]

Principle of Virtual Work

Internal virtual work:

\[\int_0^\ell \delta W_{1/z_1} \cdot EA \cdot W_{1/z_1} \, dz_1 + \int_0^\ell \delta W_{2/z_2} \cdot EA \cdot W_{2/z_2} \, dz_2 +\] \[\int_0^\ell \delta V_{1/z_1z_1} \cdot EJ \cdot V_{1/z_1z_1} \, dz_1 + \int_0^\ell \delta V_{2/z_2z_2} \cdot EJ \cdot V_{2/z_2z_2} \, dz_2\]

External virtual work (force at 45°):

\[= \left(\delta W_1(\ell) + \delta W_2(\ell)\right) \frac{P}{\sqrt{2}}\]

Computing Derivatives

Function Derivative Virtual
\(W_{1/z_1} = d \cdot \ell\) \(\delta W_{1/z_1} = \delta d \cdot \ell\)
\(W_{2/z_2} = c \cdot \ell\) \(\delta W_{2/z_2} = \delta c \cdot \ell\)
\(V_{1/z_1z_1} = 2c\) \(\delta V_{1/z_1z_1} = 2 \cdot \delta c\)
\(V_{2/z_2z_2} = 2d\) \(\delta V_{2/z_2z_2} = 2 \cdot \delta d\)

Assembling the System

Substituting into PVW:

\[\underline{\delta d \cdot d \cdot \int_0^\ell \ell^2 \, EA \, dz_1} + \underline{\delta c \cdot c \cdot \int_0^\ell \ell^2 \, EA \, dz_2} + \underline{\delta c \cdot c \cdot \int_0^\ell 4 \, EJ \, dz_1} + \underline{\delta d \cdot d \cdot \int_0^\ell 4 \, EJ \, dz_2}\] \[= (\delta c + \delta d) \cdot \frac{\ell^2 \cdot P}{\sqrt{2}}\]

Matrix form:

\[\delta \begin{bmatrix} c \\ d \end{bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{bmatrix} \int_0^\ell \ell^2 EA \, dz_2 + \int_0^\ell 4EJ \, dz_1 & 0 \\ 0 & \int_0^\ell \ell^2 EA \, dz_1 + \int_0^\ell 4EJ \, dz_2 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} c \\ d \end{bmatrix} = \delta \begin{bmatrix} c \\ d \end{bmatrix}^T \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \frac{P\ell^2}{\sqrt{2}}\]

Matrix dimensions:

\[\underbrace{\delta \underline{C}^T}_{1 \times 2} \quad \underbrace{\underline{K}}_{2 \times 2} \quad \underbrace{\underline{C}}_{2 \times 1} \quad = \quad \underbrace{\delta \underline{C}^T}_{1 \times 2} \quad \underbrace{\underline{f}}_{2 \times 1}\]

Solving the System

Two equations:

\[(EA \ell^3 + 4EJ\ell) \cdot d = \ell^2 \frac{P}{\sqrt{2}}\] \[(EA \ell^3 + 4EJ\ell) \cdot c = \ell^2 \frac{P}{\sqrt{2}}\]

Due to symmetry: \(c = d\)

\[c = d = \frac{P\ell}{\sqrt{2}(EA\ell^2 + 4EJ)}\]
Final Answer - Displacement at Joint: \[\boxed{W = W_1(\ell) = \frac{P\ell^3}{\sqrt{2}(EA\ell^2 + 4EJ)}}\]
