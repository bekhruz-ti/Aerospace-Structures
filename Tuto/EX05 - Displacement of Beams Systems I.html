Principle of Virtual Work (PCVW) Applications

Problem 1: Cantilever Beam Displacement

Find the vertical displacement \(v\) at the free end of a cantilever beam subjected to a point load using the Principle of Complementary Virtual Work (PCVW).

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1000 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(F = 6000 \text{ N}\)
  • \(E = 200 \text{ GPa}\)
  • \(J_{xx} = 500{,}000 \text{ mm}^4\)

Solution

Step 1: Internal Actions (Real System)

Cutting the beam and analyzing internal forces with coordinate \(z_1\) measured from the free end:

\[ \begin{cases} T_{z1} = 0 \\ T_{y1} = -F \\ M_{x1} = -F \cdot z_1 \end{cases} \]

The elastic problem is solved.

Step 2: Virtual System

To find displacement \(v\), apply a unit virtual load at the point of interest:

\[ \begin{cases} T'_{z1} = 0 \\ T'_{y1} = -1 \\ M'_{x1} = -z_1 \end{cases} \]

Step 3: Apply PCVW

External Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_e = 1 \cdot v\]

Internal Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_i = \frac{1}{EJ_{xx}} \int_0^{\ell} M'_{x1} \cdot M_{x1} \, dz_1 = \frac{1}{EJ_{xx}} \int_0^{\ell} (-z_1) \cdot (-F \cdot z_1) \, dz_1\] \[= \frac{1}{EJ_{xx}} \int_0^{\ell} F \cdot z_1^2 \, dz_1 = \frac{1}{EJ_{xx}} \left[ \frac{1}{3} F z_1^3 \right]_0^{\ell} = \frac{F \ell^3}{3 E J_{xx}}\]

Step 4: Equate Virtual Works

\[\delta W_e = \delta W_i\] \[v = \frac{F \ell^3}{3 E J_{xx}}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{v = 13.33 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 2: Beam with Spring Support (Exam 06/09/2021)

Find the vertical displacement \(V_C\) at the midpoint C of a beam with a pin support at one end and a spring support at the other end, subjected to a uniform distributed load.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 1200 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(p = 12 \text{ N/mm}\)
  • \(EJ = 10^{12} \text{ N} \cdot \text{mm}^2\)
  • \(K = 750 \text{ N/mm}\)

Note: The system is isostatic.

Solution

Step 1: Reaction Forces (Real System)

Equilibrium equations:

\[ \begin{cases} R_{zA} = 0 \\ R_{YA} + R_{YB} - p \cdot 2\ell = 0 \\ \circlearrowleft: R_{YB} \cdot 2\ell = 2p\ell^2 \end{cases} \]

Solving:

\[R_{YA} = p\ell, \quad R_{YB} = p\ell\]

Spring Internal Force (Interface Equilibrium):

\[\boxed{F = -R_{YB}}\]

Step 2: Reaction Forces (Virtual System)

Apply unit load at point C:

\[R'_{YA} = R'_{YB} = \frac{1}{2}\]

Step 3: Internal Actions - Segment 1 (from A to C)

REAL
\[ \begin{cases} T_{z1} = 0 \\ T_{y1} = p \cdot z_1 - p\ell \\ M_{x1} = \frac{1}{2} p z_1^2 - p\ell \cdot z_1 \\ \quad = p \left( \frac{1}{2} z_1^2 - \ell z_1 \right) \end{cases} \]
VIRTUAL
\[ \begin{cases} T'_{z1} = 0 \\ T'_{y1} = -\frac{1}{2} \\ M'_{x1} = -\frac{1}{2} z_1 \end{cases} \]

Step 4: Internal Actions - Segment 2 (from C to B)

REAL
\[ \begin{cases} T_{z2} = 0 \\ T_{y2} = p(\ell + z_2) - p\ell \\ M_{x2} = \frac{1}{2} p (\ell + z_2)^2 - p\ell(\ell + z_2) \\ \quad = \frac{p}{2} \left( z_2^2 - \ell^2 \right) \end{cases} \]
VIRTUAL
\[ \begin{cases} T'_{z2} = 0 \\ T'_{y2} = 1 - \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \\ M'_{x2} = z_2 - \frac{1}{2}(\ell + z_2) \\ \quad = \frac{1}{2}z_2 - \frac{1}{2}\ell \end{cases} \]

Step 5: Apply PCVW

Note: The virtual work of \(R'_{YB}\) is negative because its sign is opposite to the one of \(F\), thus of \(V_B\).

External Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_e = 1 \cdot V_C - R'_{YB} \cdot V_B = V_C - \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{F}{K} = V_C - \frac{p\ell}{2K}\]

Internal Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_i = \frac{1}{EJ} \int_0^{\ell} M'_{x1} \cdot M_{x1} \, dz_1 + \frac{1}{EJ} \int_0^{\ell} M'_{x2} \cdot M_{x2} \, dz_2\] \[= \frac{p}{EJ} \left( \int_0^{\ell} \left( -\frac{1}{2} z_1 \right) \left( \frac{1}{2} z_1^2 - \ell z_1 \right) dz_1 + \int_0^{\ell} \frac{1}{4} \left( z_2 - \ell \right) \left( z_2^2 - \ell^2 \right) dz_2 \right)\] \[= \frac{p}{EJ} \left( \int_0^{\ell} \left( -\frac{z_1^3}{4} + \frac{\ell z_1^2}{2} \right) dz_1 + \frac{1}{4} \int_0^{\ell} \left( z_2^3 - \ell^2 z_2 - \ell z_2^2 + \ell^3 \right) dz_2 \right)\] \[= \frac{p}{EJ} \left( \left[ -\frac{z_1^4}{16} + \frac{\ell z_1^3}{6} \right]_0^{\ell} + \left[ \frac{z_2^4}{16} - \frac{\ell^2 z_2^2}{8} - \frac{\ell z_2^3}{12} + \frac{\ell^3 z_2}{4} \right]_0^{\ell} \right)\] \[= \frac{p}{EJ} \left( -\frac{\ell^4}{16} + \frac{\ell^4}{6} + \frac{\ell^4}{16} - \frac{\ell^4}{8} - \frac{\ell^4}{12} + \frac{\ell^4}{4} \right) = \frac{5}{24} \frac{p\ell^4}{EJ}\]

Step 6: Solve for \(V_C\)

\[\delta W_i = \delta W_e\] \[\frac{5}{24} \frac{p\ell^4}{EJ} = V_C - \frac{p\ell}{2K}\] \[V_C = \frac{5}{24} \frac{p\ell^4}{EJ} + \frac{p\ell}{2K}\]
Final Answer: \[\boxed{V_C = 14.78 \text{ mm}}\]

Problem 3: 3D Truss Structure

Find the horizontal displacement \(U_A\) and rotation \(\varphi_A\) at point A of a three-dimensional truss structure with distributed self-weight loading.

Given Data:

  • \(\ell = 750 \text{ mm}\)
  • \(\rho = 0.08 \text{ N/mm}\) (distributed weight)
  • \(E = 200 \text{ GPa}\)
  • \(G = 77 \text{ GPa}\)
  • \(F = 1000 \text{ N}\)
  • Circular cross-section: diameter = 30 mm

Solution

Step 1: Section Properties

For the circular cross-section with radius \(r = 15\) mm:

\[A = \pi r^2 = 706.86 \text{ mm}^2\] \[J_{xx} = J_{yy} = \frac{\pi r^4}{4} = 39{,}761 \text{ mm}^4\]

Polar Moment of Inertia:

\[J_p = J_{xx} + J_{yy} = 79{,}522 \text{ mm}^4\]

Note: For an axisymmetric section, the Torsional Stiffness is \(GJ_p\). For other types of sections, \(J_p\) must be corrected to consider the real distribution of shear and warping: \(J_p \rightarrow J_p^*\), giving Torsional Stiffness \(GJ_p^*\).

Step 2: Internal Actions - Member 1 (Vertical)

Distributed load varies linearly: \(f(z_1) = \frac{\rho}{\ell} \cdot z_1\)

REAL
\[T_{z1} = -F\] \[T_{y1} = -\frac{1}{2} \frac{\rho}{\ell} z_1 \cdot z_1 = -\frac{\rho z_1^2}{2\ell}\] \[M_{x1} = -\frac{1}{2} \frac{\rho z_1^2}{\ell} \cdot \frac{1}{3} z_1 = -\frac{1}{6} \frac{\rho}{\ell} z_1^3\]
VIRTUAL 1
\[T'_{z1} = -1\]
VIRTUAL 2
\[M''_{x1} = -1\]

Step 3: Internal Actions - Member 2 (Horizontal)

Distributed load: \(f(z_2) = \frac{\rho}{2\ell} \cdot z_2 + \rho\)

REAL
\[ \begin{cases} T_{z2} = 0 \\ T_{y2} = -\frac{1}{2}\rho\ell - \rho z_2 - \frac{1}{4} \frac{\rho}{\ell} \cdot z_2^2 \\ T_{x2} = -F \\ M_{z2} = \frac{\rho\ell}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{3}\ell = \frac{1}{6}\rho\ell^2 \\ M_{y2} = F \cdot z_2 \\ M_{x2} = -\frac{1}{2}\rho\ell \cdot z_2 - \rho \frac{z_2^2}{2} - \frac{\rho}{2\ell} z_2 \cdot \frac{1}{3} z_2 \end{cases} \]
VIRTUAL 1
\[ \begin{cases} T'_{x2} = -1 \\ M'_{y2} = z_2 \end{cases} \]
VIRTUAL 2
\[M''_{z2} = 1\]

Step 4: PCVW for \(U_A\) (Virtual System 1)

External Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_e = 1 \cdot U_A\]

Internal Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^{\ell} T'_{z1} \cdot \frac{T_{z1}}{EA} dz_1 + \int_0^{2\ell} M'_{y2} \cdot \frac{M_{y2}}{EJ} dz_2\] \[= \int_0^{\ell} (-1) \left( -\frac{F}{EA} \right) dz_1 + \int_0^{2\ell} z_2 \cdot \frac{F z_2}{EJ} dz_2\] \[= \left[ \frac{F}{EA} z_1 \right]_0^{\ell} + \left[ \frac{1}{3} \frac{F z_2^3}{EJ} \right]_0^{2\ell} = \frac{F\ell}{EA} + \frac{8}{3} \frac{F\ell^3}{EJ}\]

Setting \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\):

Displacement \(U_A\): \[\boxed{U_A = 141.68 \text{ mm}}\]

Step 5: PCVW for \(\varphi_A\) (Virtual System 2)

External Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_e = 1 \cdot \varphi_A\]

Internal Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^{\ell} M''_{x1} \cdot \frac{M_{x1}}{EJ} dz_1 + \int_0^{2\ell} M''_{z2} \cdot \frac{M_{z2}}{GJ_p} dz_2\]

Setting \(\delta W_i = \delta W_e\):

\[\varphi_A = \int_0^{\ell} (-1) \cdot \left( -\frac{1}{6} \rho z_1 \cdot \frac{1}{\ell} \right) \frac{1}{EJ} \cdot dz_1 + \int_0^{2\ell} 1 \cdot \frac{1}{6} \rho \ell \cdot \frac{1}{GJ_p} dz_2\] \[= \frac{1}{24} \frac{\rho \ell^3}{EJ} + \frac{1}{3} \frac{\rho \ell^3}{GJ_p}\]
Rotation \(\varphi_A\): \[\boxed{\varphi_A = 2.016 \times 10^{-3} \text{ rad}}\]

Alternative Solutions to Problem 2

Method I: Include Spring in Deformable Structures

In this approach, the spring work is included in the INTERNAL virtual work.

External Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_e = 1 \cdot V_C\]

Internal Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_i = \int_0^{\ell} M'_{x1} \frac{M_{x1}}{EJ} dz_1 + \int_0^{\ell} M'_{x2} \frac{M_{x2}}{EJ} dz_1 + R'_{YB} \cdot \frac{R_{YB}}{K}\]

Method II: Alternative Virtual System

Virtual System: Apply unit load \(\uparrow 1\) at A and unit load \(\downarrow 1\) at C.

In this way, the right part of the structure is unloaded in the virtual system → NO virtual work by the forces in the spring.

Internal Virtual Work:

\[\delta W_i = 1 \cdot U_C + 1 \cdot U_A + \ell \cdot \varphi_A\] \[= 1 \cdot U_C + 1 \cdot 0 + \ell \cdot \varphi_A\]

\(\varphi_A\) is the REAL rotation of point A and it is unknown. We should create a second virtual system to compute it.

→ Not so convenient
